When generating reports or documents, follow these guidelines:

**Report Structure:**
1. **Title & Date** — Clear, descriptive title with generation date
2. **Executive Summary** — 3-5 sentences for busy readers
3. **Table of Contents** — For reports with 3+ sections
4. **Main Sections** — Organized logically with clear headings
5. **Data & Visuals** — Use tables, lists, and structured data
6. **Conclusions** — Key takeaways and implications
7. **Appendix** — Raw data or detailed references

**Writing Style:**
- Professional but accessible
- Active voice preferred
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Bold key terms and important numbers

**Data Presentation:**
- Always use markdown tables for comparative data
- Include units and time periods
- Round numbers appropriately (don't over-precision)
- Provide context for numbers (% change, benchmarks)

**Quality Checklist:**
- Facts are sourced and verifiable
- No contradictions within the report
- Recommendations are specific and actionable
- Report can stand alone without additional context

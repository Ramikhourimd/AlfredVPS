# Oren Commits to Including Rami in Cross-Functional Meetings

## Context

Rami expressed frustration that clinical perspectives are not integrated into company decision-making, citing a specific marketing planning incident where recommendations were made without clinical input. The group acknowledged this gap. The steering committee was proposed as the long-term structural solution, but an interim commitment was also needed.

## Options Considered

1. **No immediate action** — Wait for the steering committee to be formally established
2. **Immediate inclusion** — Oren ensures Rami is included in relevant cross-functional meetings starting now
3. **Formal veto process** — Establish a clinical review gate for all company decisions

## Decision

Oren committed to ensuring Rami is included in relevant cross-functional meetings as an interim measure while the steering committee structure is formalized. This is a verbal commitment, not a formal policy change.

## Rationale

The steering committee will take time to define (charter, scope, frequency). In the interim, the governance gap persists. A direct CEO commitment to include the clinical lead in cross-functional discussions provides immediate relief while the structural solution is developed.

## Consequences

- Depends on Oren's follow-through — no enforcement mechanism exists
- Does not define which meetings are "relevant" — leaves discretion to Oren
- May create meeting overload for Rami (already stretched across multiple roles)
- Serves as a bridge until the steering committee provides a structural mechanism
- If this commitment is not honored, it will reinforce the pattern Rami complained about and erode trust

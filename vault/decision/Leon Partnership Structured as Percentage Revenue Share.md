# Leon Partnership Structured as Percentage Revenue Share

## Context

With the decision to operate the UK clinic under Leon's CQC registration, the commercial arrangement between Telia'z and Leon needed to be defined. The options were a flat fee for CQC use, a per-patient fee, or a percentage revenue share.

## Options Considered

1. **Flat fee** — Fixed monthly payment for CQC access regardless of patient volume
2. **Per-patient fee** — Fixed amount per patient routed through Leon's practice
3. **Percentage revenue share** — Leon receives a percentage of revenue from patients flowing through his practice

## Decision

The arrangement is structured as a percentage share — patients are transferred to Leon and he receives a percentage of the revenue. This was described by Adiel as "transferring patients to him as a percentage arrangement."

## Rationale

A percentage model aligns Leon's incentives with patient volume growth. It reduces Telia'z fixed costs during the uncertain early launch phase and gives Leon ongoing benefit proportional to the service's success.

## Consequences

- Leon has financial incentive to support the partnership and patient flow
- Telia'z avoids fixed costs before patient volume is established
- Commercial terms are directly tied to the Leon contract that is pending signature
- Revenue per patient is reduced by Leon's percentage cut, affecting unit economics

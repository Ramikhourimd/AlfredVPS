# Use Percentage Revenue Arrangement With Leon

## Context

The Leon partnership requires a commercial structure defining how revenue flows between Telia'z and Leon's CQC-registered practice. Leon provides the regulatory umbrella (CQC registration) and patient entry point (website), while Telia'z provides the clinical assessment platform and psychiatric services.

## Options Considered

1. **Fixed fee per patient** — Leon receives a flat rate per patient routed through his practice
2. **Percentage of revenue** — Leon receives a percentage of revenue from patients assessed under his CQC
3. **Retainer plus variable** — Monthly retainer to Leon plus per-patient variable component

## Decision

The agreement structure is a percentage arrangement — Leon receives a percentage of revenue from patients who flow through his practice into the Telia'z assessment pathway.

## Rationale

A percentage model aligns incentives: Leon benefits from higher patient volume, which also benefits Telia'z. It avoids fixed costs before patient volume is established, reducing launch risk. The specific percentage is being negotiated in the contract that Leon reviewed over the weekend of Feb 15, 2026.

## Consequences

Revenue per patient is reduced by Leon's percentage. The commercial model needs to remain viable within NHS tariff pricing constraints (~£360 intake, ~£180 follow-up). This arrangement is specific to Track 1 (Leon partnership) and does not apply to Track 2 (own CQC) or the OMG/GP Confed pathway, which will have its own revenue share model.

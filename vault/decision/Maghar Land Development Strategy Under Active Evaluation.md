# Maghar Land Development Strategy Under Active Evaluation

## Context

The Khouri family owns a ~1 dunam land plot in Maghar (Khallet al-Sharif neighborhood) valued at approximately 4 million NIS. During a September 2025 family gathering, multiple development strategies were actively discussed but no commitment was made.

## Options Considered

1. **Build residential apartments** — Develop the land for family housing and/or rental units. Requires survey, zoning approval, and significant construction investment. Could directly solve the housing need.
2. **Sell outright** — Previous interest at 2.5-3M NIS per dunam; current estimated value ~4M NIS with the right buyer (someone wanting to build apartments). Provides liquidity for purchasing elsewhere.
3. **Joint venture with developer** — Partner with a developer who builds on the land in exchange for units or revenue share. Reduces capital requirement but introduces dependency on a partner.

## Decision

No decision has been made. All three options remain under discussion. The family is in an information-gathering phase — needing a survey (moded), takhrit documentation (~250 NIS), and site photos/video before any option can be properly evaluated.

## Rationale

The decision is deferred because prerequisite information (survey, zoning details) hasn't been gathered yet, and the olive tree retention strategy adds complexity to the timeline.

## Consequences

Delay in deciding leaves the family in a rental search while holding a high-value appreciating asset. Each month of inaction means continued rental expenses against a backdrop of rising property values.

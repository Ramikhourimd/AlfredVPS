## Context

During the leadership meeting on 2026-02-22, a significant debate emerged about the appropriate C-level medical role for Telia'z. Rami advocated for a CMO position that would carry medical authority across the entire company, while Oren initially leaned toward a COO model with shared services.

## Key Arguments

### For CMO Role
- Medical authority is needed at the company level, not just within the clinic
- Regulatory requirements in telepsychiatry demand medical oversight at the highest level
- The CMO would oversee medical quality, compliance, and innovation across all operations
- Rami's background (physician, MBA, AI expertise) uniquely positions him for this role

### For Alternative (COO) Approach
- Oren initially preferred a unified operational structure with shared services
- Concern about creating silos between medical and operational functions
- Desire for a single company identity rather than separate divisions

## Emerging Consensus

By the end of the meeting, the group moved toward recognizing the need for medical authority at the company level. The exact title and scope remain to be finalized, but the principle of separating medical leadership from clinic operations management was broadly accepted.

## Implications

- Rami's role will be redefined from 4 current functions to 2 primary areas: CMO responsibilities + Innovation leadership
- A separate Medical Director (part-time, 30%) will be hired for the clinic
- The CMO role will have authority over medical standards, practitioner quality, and regulatory compliance across all Telia'z operations

## Open Questions

- Exact scope boundaries between CMO and clinic Medical Director
- Reporting lines: Does the Medical Director report to CMO or clinic operations?
- How does the CMO role interact with the shared services model?

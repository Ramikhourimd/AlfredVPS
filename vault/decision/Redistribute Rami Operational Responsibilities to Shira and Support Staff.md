# Redistribute Rami Operational Responsibilities to Shira and Support Staff

## Context

Rami Khouri acknowledged during the 2025-03-27 KPI meeting that he had become a bottleneck for clinic operations. He was absorbing operational challenges himself but lacked time to resolve them, while Shira lacked the authority to handle them independently. This created a deadlock: problems were identified but not resolved.

## Options Considered

1. **Maintain status quo** — Rami continues handling operations alongside CMO duties. Rejected because the bottleneck was worsening as the clinic grew.
2. **Hire a full-time operations manager** — Bring in external management. Not chosen at this stage.
3. **Redistribute to existing staff with formal authority** — Give Shira both authority and accountability as clinic manager, redistribute day-to-day tasks to Rena, Avital, and Ori.

## Decision

Formally redistribute Rami's operational responsibilities:
- **To Shira**: Primary operational management, KPI ownership, team supervision (with clinic manager title and authority)
- **To Rena, Avital, and Ori**: Day-to-day operational tasks that Rami previously handled as bottleneck
- **Rami retains**: CMO-level clinical oversight, strategic direction, and escalation point

## Rationale

The root cause was an authority-capacity mismatch: Rami had the authority but not the time; Shira had the proximity but not the authority. By formally granting Shira the clinic manager role with KPI-linked compensation, both the authority gap and the capacity bottleneck are addressed simultaneously.

## Consequences

- Rami freed to focus on CMO, medical director recruitment, and strategic initiatives
- Shira gains formal authority but also formal accountability via KPIs
- Day-to-day operations distributed across three additional staff members
- Risk: Shira's part-time capacity may be tested as clinic scales

# NHS ADHD Pathway Entry Is Exclusively Via GP Referral

## Context

The UK NHS ADHD patient pathway designed with OMG Healthcare specifies that patients enter the service exclusively through GP referral — not through self-referral, direct access, or other entry points. This was mapped during the Rami-Adiel pathway design session.

## Options Considered

1. **GP Referral only** — patients referred by their GP into the combined Telia'z/OMG service
2. **Self-referral** — patients accessing the service directly (as in some private models)
3. **Mixed entry** — both GP and self-referral pathways

## Decision

GP referral is the sole entry point for the NHS ADHD pathway. The patient is referred by their GP to the combined Telia'z/OMG service pipeline.

## Rationale

NHS service delivery models typically require GP referral for specialist services. The OMG partnership operates within the NHS framework, and GP referral aligns with commissioning expectations and clinical governance requirements. The private track (Leon) may use different entry points.

## Consequences

- Patient volume is entirely dependent on GP referral rates and awareness
- Requires GP education and engagement to drive referrals
- Limits direct patient acquisition for the NHS track (unlike the private Leon track)
- Aligns with ICB commissioner expectations for NHS-funded services

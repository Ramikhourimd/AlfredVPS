# Pursue Dual-Track CQC Strategy for UK Launch

## Context

Telia'z needs CQC registration to operate clinically in the UK. Obtaining their own CQC is a lengthy process. Rather than relying on a single pathway, the team is pursuing two tracks simultaneously to maximize speed and strategic positioning.

## Options Considered

1. **Single track — Leon CQC only** — Faster launch but limited to private/Right to Choose patients through one GP's practice
2. **Single track — Own CQC application only** — Full independence but slow (months to years)
3. **Dual track — Leon CQC + own CQC via NHS pathway** — Faster launch via Leon while building toward independent NHS capability

## Decision

Pursue both tracks simultaneously. Track 1 (Leon partnership) provides immediate launch capability for private patients. Track 2 (own CQC via NHS, working with Susan and Dolifa Ioja) builds toward independent NHS referral capability.

## Rationale

Leon's existing CQC enables a fast market entry (weeks, not months). But depending solely on a single GP's CQC creates fragility — Leon is a single point of failure. The second track via NHS provides a path to independent operations and NHS referral volume, which is where the scale opportunity lies (Leeds alone has a 25-year ADHD waiting list).

## Consequences

Resource split across two regulatory workstreams. Need dedicated attention to both tracks — Adiel managing Leon relationship, Susan/Adiel advancing NHS CQC. Risk of neither track completing on time if attention is divided.

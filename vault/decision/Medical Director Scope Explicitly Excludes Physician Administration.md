# Medical Director Scope Explicitly Excludes Physician Administration

## Context

The Feb 22 leadership meeting needed to define clear boundaries between the three clinic management pillars to prevent role overlap and authority conflicts. A key ambiguity was whether the Medical Director manages physicians holistically or only clinically.

## Options Considered

1. **Medical Director manages all physician matters** — clinical and administrative (scheduling, work hours, contracts)
2. **Medical Director manages clinical matters only** — professional protocols, consultations, clinical decisions. Administrative aspects handled by Operations Manager.

## Decision

The Medical Director role explicitly excludes administrative and scheduling management of physicians. The Medical Director handles professional/clinical supervision only — protocols, medical consultations, and clinical quality decisions. Administrative aspects of physician management (scheduling, work hours, coordination) fall under the Clinical Operations Manager.

## Rationale

Both meeting notes independently emphasize this boundary with identical language: the Medical Director "Does NOT handle administrative/scheduling aspects of physicians." This explicit exclusion prevents the Medical Director from accumulating operational authority that would undermine the parallel three-pillar model. The 30% time allocation also makes it impractical to add administrative responsibilities.

## Consequences

- Clear handoff points between Medical Director and Operations Manager for physician-related matters
- Weekly coordination meetings between the two roles become essential (already decided)
- Risk: physicians may receive conflicting guidance if coordination breaks down
- The Operations Manager (Shira's current scope) retains physician scheduling authority

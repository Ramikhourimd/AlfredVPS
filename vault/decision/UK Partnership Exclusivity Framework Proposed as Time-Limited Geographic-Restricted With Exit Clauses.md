# UK Partnership Exclusivity Framework Proposed as Time-Limited Geographic-Restricted With Exit Clauses

## Context

During the Telia'z Company Fun Day on September 21, 2025, an informal but substantive business conversation took place between Rami and colleagues about the UK expansion deal negotiation. The UK partners were pushing for strong exclusivity terms, and the Telia'z team needed to define their counter-position.

## Options Considered

1. **Broad exclusivity** — Grant the UK partners exclusive rights across all of the UK with no time limit. Favored by the UK side.
2. **Time-limited, geographically restricted exclusivity with exit clauses** — Rami's proposed framework. Limited to one year, restricted geography, with milestone-based exit conditions.
3. **No exclusivity** — Retain full flexibility. Not discussed as viable given the UK partners' requirements.

## Decision

Rami articulated the negotiation position: "Our position is that we can do exclusivity for a year, limited geography with certain conditions — if something doesn't happen within X time, we can exit." The framework combines three protective mechanisms:
- **Time limit**: One year maximum
- **Geographic restriction**: Limited to specific regions rather than all UK
- **Exit clauses**: Milestone-based triggers allowing Telia'z to exit if the partnership fails to deliver within defined timeframes

The commercial model under discussion was percentage-based revenue sharing rather than flat fees.

## Rationale

The UK partners were described as enthusiastic ("They keep saying yes yes yes") but not delivering tangible progress ("but we're not really seeing it happen"). This gap between verbal commitment and operational reality justified a cautious exclusivity framework that protects Telia'z's ability to pursue alternative UK partnerships if the current track stalls.

## Consequences

This negotiation posture preserves Telia'z's strategic flexibility while giving UK partners sufficient exclusivity to commit resources. The one-year time limit aligns with the typical proof-of-concept window for healthcare partnerships. The geographic restriction enables pursuing multiple UK regions through different partnership models simultaneously — which is exactly what evolved into the multi-track approach (Leon, GP Confederation, OMG).

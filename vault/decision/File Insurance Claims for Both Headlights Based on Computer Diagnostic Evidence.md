# File Insurance Claims for Both Headlights Based on Computer Diagnostic Evidence

## Context

During a body shop visit on 2025-10-09, Rami had the vehicle's computer diagnostics checked. The diagnostics confirmed both headlights had been previously repaired — a fact Rami was not previously aware of. The inspection revealed the prior repair was done improperly using brute force rather than the manufacturer's computer-guided disassembly procedure.

## Options Considered

1. **File claims for both headlights** — Based on diagnostic evidence of prior negligent repair
2. **Negotiate with prior repair shop** — Attempt to get them to redo the work
3. **Accept the damage** — Absorb the cost personally

## Decision

File insurance claims for both headlights (left and right) based on computer diagnostic evidence proving the damage was caused by negligent prior repair.

## Rationale

- Vehicle computer confirmed prior repair was performed using brute force, not the proper tool-assisted procedure
- Both headlights show damage consistent with forceful disassembly (confirmed by multiple technicians)
- Silicone sealant was also improperly applied with visible gaps and inconsistent spread
- The damage pattern is entirely attributable to the prior repair shop's negligence, not normal wear

## Consequences

- Two insurance claims are active (one per headlight)
- Rami has documented evidence from computer diagnostics and technician assessment
- The prior repair shop's negligence is established through independent technical evaluation

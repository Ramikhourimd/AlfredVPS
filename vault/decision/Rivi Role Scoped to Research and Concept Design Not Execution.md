# Rivi Role Scoped to Research and Concept Design Not Execution

## Context

Rivi Idelman was drifting into execution work (building solutions, handling operational details) beyond her intended role. Rami needed to reaffirm the boundary between concept design and implementation.

## Options Considered

1. **Rivi as researcher + concept designer only** — produces problem definitions, research reports, and conceptual proposals; execution handled by product/dev team
2. **Rivi as full-stack innovation lead** — handles both research and implementation of solutions

## Decision

Rivi's role is explicitly scoped to **research and concept design**. She is responsible for problem identification, data collection, literature review, and conceptual proposals structured as academic-style documents. Implementation (building videos, developing features, configuring systems) routes to the appropriate execution team: product features to Yael, system improvements to Ohad/Stav, operational items to Shira/Renee.

## Rationale

- Rivi's strengths are in understanding problems deeply and gathering insights from staff
- Execution work was consuming time that should go to research
- The concept-to-execution handoff via structured documents ensures quality and traceability
- Rivi can add a "recommendations" appendix for implementation ideas, clearly marked as beyond her scope

## Consequences

- All Rivi's deliverables must follow academic research structure: background, methodology, results, discussion, recommendations
- Clear routing chain established for actionable items
- Need to coordinate with Shira to avoid staff communication overlap

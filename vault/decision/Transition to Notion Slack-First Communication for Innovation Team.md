# Transition to Notion/Slack-First Communication for Innovation Team

## Context

The innovation team's communication was spread across multiple channels without a clear primary platform. As the team formalized workflows (top-down gated concept design, structured meeting cadences), the need for a consolidated communication system became apparent.

## Options Considered

1. **Continue with existing mixed channels** — email, ad hoc messaging, various platforms
2. **Notion/Slack-first approach** — Slack for real-time communication, Notion for structured documentation and project tracking

## Decision

Transition the innovation team to Notion/Slack as the primary communication and documentation platforms. Slack for real-time messaging, Notion for structured project work.

## Rationale

Consolidating onto Slack/Notion aligns with the broader workflow formalization effort. Slack provides threaded, searchable communication. Notion provides structured documentation. Both integrate well and support the team's need for asynchronous collaboration given distributed roles and half-time schedules.

## Consequences

- Requires verifying Slack DM privacy before Rivi will fully adopt (task created)
- Team members need onboarding to new communication norms
- Historical context in other channels may be lost unless migrated

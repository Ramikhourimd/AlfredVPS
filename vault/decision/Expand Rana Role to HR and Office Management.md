# Expand Rana Role to HR and Office Management

## Context

Rana currently serves as chief secretary handling daily operational tasks (phone calls, scheduling, patient management). The clinic is growing rapidly and needs someone to handle HR functions (recruitment coordination, onboarding, staff lifecycle). Meanwhile, Rana's frequent last-minute absences leave the office understaffed.

## Options Considered

1. **Keep Rana in current role, hire separate HR person** — More expensive; Rana already has institutional knowledge
2. **Transition Rana to HR/management, hire replacement secretary** — Leverages Rana's experience while solving daily coverage gap (chosen)
3. **Add technology to reduce secretary workload** — Shira's preference; valid but doesn't solve HR gap

## Decision

Gradually transition Rana from hands-on secretarial work to HR and office management responsibilities:
- Take charge of recruitment coordination and first-contact with new hires
- Manage office task delegation and daily operations oversight
- Handle staff lifecycle (onboarding, scheduling, compliance)
- Reduce involvement in routine phone calls and patient triage

Simultaneously hire one full-time secretary to absorb Rana's daily operational tasks.

## Rationale

Rana has direct contact with psychiatrists and staff daily, making her a natural fit for HR coordination. The transition must be gradual — not an overnight switch. Rami wants Shira to operate at a higher oversight level (tracking updates and results) rather than being pulled into daily secretarial operations.

## Consequences

- Need to hire one full-time secretary position immediately (budgeted from next month)
- Rana's transition is gradual, not immediate
- Shira will need to decide on hiring approach: one full-time or two half-time employees
- Technology investments (automated reminders, WhatsApp bot, centralized phone) may still be needed alongside

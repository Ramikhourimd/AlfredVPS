# Separate Clinic Into Independent Clinical and Administrative Units

## Context

The clinic has been operating with overlapping responsibilities between the clinical director (Shira) and the chief secretary transitioning to administrative manager (Rana). Shira was managing both clinical operations and administrative staff coordination (secretary meetings, contract follow-ups, HR-adjacent tasks). This dual responsibility created bottlenecks and prevented both roles from operating at full effectiveness.

## Options Considered

1. **Maintain current structure** — Shira continues to oversee both clinical and administrative functions. Rejected: creates bottleneck and prevents Shira from focusing on clinical development and product coordination.
2. **Hierarchical model (Rana reports to Shira)** — Administrative function subordinate to clinical. Rejected: creates dependency, slows administrative decisions, frustrates Rana's desire for autonomy.
3. **Two independent parallel units** — Clinical (Shira) and Administrative (Rana) operate as peer functions with no hierarchical relationship. Chosen: reduces dependency, enables specialization, aligns with broader district model.

## Decision

The clinic will operate as two independent functional units:
- **Clinical unit** (Shira): clinical staff management, clinical flow, clinical communication, product development coordination
- **Administrative unit** (Rana): office management, HR coordination, secretary team, onboarding, financial reporting, staff agreements

Neither unit reports to the other. Both will report to the incoming clinic manager (Basel) starting December 2025.

## Rationale

- Eliminates bureaucratic dependency between clinical and administrative tracks
- Allows Shira to focus on clinical quality and forward-looking product work
- Enables Rana to exercise full autonomy in her administrative domain
- Rana has demonstrated readiness by proactively asking for independent decision-making authority
- Aligns with the broader three-pillar district model for clinic operations

## Consequences

- Shira steps back from weekly secretary meetings and administrative follow-ups
- Rana gains authority to hire secretary staff independently
- Information flow between units shifts from "reporting" to "updating" — Rana updates Shira as courtesy, not obligation
- Transition is gradual, not immediate — responsibilities transfer one at a time
- Until Basel arrives, there is no direct oversight of Rana's administrative function

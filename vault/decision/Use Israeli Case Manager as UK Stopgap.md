# Use Israeli Case Manager as UK Stopgap

## Decision

Bassem, an Israeli case manager at Telia'z, will serve as the interim case manager for the UK clinic launch. He will work remotely from Israel, managing UK patients until local English case managers are hired and onboarded.

## Context

The UK clinic launch through Leon's CQC license requires a case manager to handle patient coordination. Hiring locally in England takes time, and the launch timeline (target: March 31, 2026) cannot wait for local recruitment to complete.

## Alternatives Considered

1. **Wait for local UK hire** — Would delay the launch significantly
2. **Launch without a case manager** — Not feasible; case management is core to the clinical model

## Implications

- Bassem needs to adjust to UK working hours and patient expectations
- UK patients will interact with a remote case manager initially
- This is explicitly a temporary arrangement until local hires are onboarded
- Dekkel confirmed UK case managers will eventually be hired locally in England

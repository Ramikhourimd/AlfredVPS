# Build Dream Clinical System Prototype to Inform Annual Development Roadmap

## Context

During the 2026-02-18 meeting, Rami proposed a strategic initiative to envision the most advanced possible clinical management system. Rather than incremental feature requests, the approach starts from an aspirational vision and works backward to a practical roadmap.

## Options Considered

1. **Incremental feature planning** — Continue adding features based on individual requests and pain points
2. **Dream prototype approach** — Survey all existing clinical systems, build a composite model combining the best features, brainstorm beyond what exists, then use the result to inform the annual development roadmap
3. **Competitive analysis only** — Study competitors without the prototyping step

## Decision

Adopt the dream prototype approach: survey the market comprehensively, build a composite best-of-breed model, add novel innovations assuming no budget constraints, and use the resulting prototype concept as the foundation for the annual development roadmap.

## Rationale

Starting from an unconstrained vision creates a more ambitious target and prevents the team from self-limiting based on perceived technical or budget constraints. The prototype serves as both a north star for product development and a communication tool for stakeholders.

## Consequences

- Rivi leads the initial market research phase
- Ohad may contribute academic and competitive research
- The prototype will require significant research investment before producing tangible output
- The annual development roadmap will be shaped by this vision rather than by ad-hoc feature requests

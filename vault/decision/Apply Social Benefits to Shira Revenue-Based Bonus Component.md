# Apply Social Benefits to Shira Revenue-Based Bonus Component

## Context

During the 2025-09-01 contract negotiation call between Rami and Alex Taliaz, Shira had requested that social benefits (pension contributions / הפרשות סוציאליות) be applied to the bonus component of her compensation — not just her base salary. Rami acknowledged he was unfamiliar with this practice in the corporate world but advocated approving it to close the contract negotiation.

## Options Considered

1. **Apply social benefits to bonus** — Increases total compensation cost slightly but removes a blocker to Shira signing
2. **Reject social benefits on bonus** — Standard corporate practice, but risks prolonging unsigned contract

## Decision

Rami asked Alex to approve applying social benefits to the bonus component. Rami's position: "I don't really understand this, I'm not familiar with it in the corporate world. In my opinion, it's possible to give her this." The goal was to close the matter so Shira would sign.

## Rationale

The financial impact is marginal (social benefit contributions on a bonus capped at 1,500 NIS/month), but the symbolic value of granting Shira's request removes friction from the contract signing process. Prolonging the negotiation over this point was seen as counterproductive.

## Consequences

Combined with the bonus percentage doubling (0.5% → 1%), this creates a more attractive compensation package. However, it sets a precedent that bonus components include social benefits, which may affect future compensation negotiations with other staff.

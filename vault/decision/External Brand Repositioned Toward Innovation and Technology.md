# External Brand Repositioned Toward Innovation and Technology

## Context

Telia'z marketing narrative had been primarily clinical and service-oriented. As the Innovation Program matured and the Psychiatry Academy prepared for its first cohort, the question arose of how to position Telia'z externally — particularly for clinician recruitment and partner engagement.

## Options Considered

1. **Continue clinical-service positioning** — Market Telia'z based on patient outcomes and clinical excellence alone
2. **Shift to innovation/technology positioning** — Lead with AI, tech-enabled psychiatry, and innovation as the primary brand differentiator

## Decision

Adi Lavi agreed that the marketing narrative is shifting toward innovation and technology positioning. The Psychiatry Academy and the broader Innovation Program become central to how Telia'z presents itself to potential clinician recruits and external partners.

## Rationale

The innovation program provides a genuine differentiator in a competitive clinician recruitment market. Tech-enabled psychiatry and structured training (Psychiatry Academy) offer a value proposition that distinguishes Telia'z from traditional telepsychiatry employers. The brand shift aligns marketing with the actual organizational investment in innovation.

## Consequences

- Marketing materials will emphasize innovation, AI tools, and professional development opportunities
- The Psychiatry Academy must deliver substantive content before external promotion (per existing assumption)
- External partnerships (Arab healthcare training program, guest speaking) reinforce the innovation brand
- Creates an expectation among recruits that innovation is a real, ongoing program — not just marketing language

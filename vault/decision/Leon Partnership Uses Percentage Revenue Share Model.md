# Leon Partnership Uses Percentage Revenue Share Model

## Context

With the decision to operate under Leon's CQC registration, the team needed to define the commercial arrangement. Leon is a UK family doctor providing his CQC license and practice website as the patient entry point; Telia'z provides the clinical assessment platform and psychiatrist services.

## Options Considered

1. **Fixed fee to Leon** — pay Leon a flat monthly or per-patient fee for use of his CQC
2. **Percentage revenue share** — Leon receives a percentage of revenue from patients flowing through his practice
3. **Employment/subcontract model** — Leon employed or contracted at a day rate

## Decision

The agreement is structured as a percentage arrangement — Leon receives a proportion of revenue from patients who are transferred through his CQC-registered practice into the Telia'z assessment flow.

## Rationale

A percentage model aligns incentives: Leon benefits from patient volume growth, reducing the need for guaranteed payments before revenue materializes. It also keeps Telia'z's fixed costs low during the launch phase when patient volume is uncertain.

## Consequences

- Leon is incentivized to support patient acquisition (his website is the entry point)
- Telia'z avoids fixed costs during the ramp-up period
- The exact percentage terms are in the contract pending signature
- If patient volume is low initially, Leon's compensation is also low — potential relationship risk

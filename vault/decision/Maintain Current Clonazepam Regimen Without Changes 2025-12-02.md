# Maintain Current Clonazepam Regimen Without Changes 2025-12-02

## Context

Female outpatient on Clonazepam (Clonex) presents for follow-up. She reports significant subjective improvement — confirmed by both patient and her husband. The question of medication adjustment (tapering or changing) was discussed.

## Options Considered

1. **Begin gradual Clonazepam taper** — Patient is improving, and doctor had previously indicated a desire to reduce medication over time
2. **Maintain current regimen unchanged** — Preserve the stability that has been achieved

## Decision

Maintain the current Clonazepam regimen without any changes. Doctor explicitly stated: "We are not going to change the medication now" and warned that "any small change could potentially cause a setback."

## Rationale

- Patient's improved state is directly attributed to consistent medication adherence
- The risk of destabilization from premature tapering outweighs the benefit of dose reduction
- Patient's work absence and recent burn incident suggest she is still in a fragile adjustment period
- Future tapering remains a long-term goal, contingent on sustained stability and return to occupational engagement

## Consequences

- Patient remains on current Clonex dosage (1 morning + 1 evening; noon dose prescribed but not consistently taken)
- Next follow-up should reassess medication adherence including the noon dose
- Work return compliance is now a prerequisite condition for any future medication reduction discussions

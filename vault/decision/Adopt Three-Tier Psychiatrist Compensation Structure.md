# Adopt Three-Tier Psychiatrist Compensation Structure

## Context

Rami and Basel needed a standardized compensation framework for psychiatrists that accounts for experience level and session type. The previous system had ad-hoc rates per psychiatrist, creating inconsistencies and payroll complexity.

## Options Considered

1. **Option A** — Flat rate for all psychiatrists regardless of experience
2. **Option B** — Three-tier progressive structure (Shalav Alef/Bet/Gimel) with defined rates per session type

## Decision

Adopted a three-tier (Shalav) compensation structure using a 0.75 multiplier for prorated session types:

- **Tier 1 (Shalav Alef):** Intake 225 NIS/45min; Children follow-up 148.5 NIS/30min
- **Tier 2 (Shalav Bet):** Intake 262.5 NIS; Follow-up with multiplier 173.25 NIS
- **Tier 3 (Shalav Gimel):** Intake 400 NIS; Follow-up 200 NIS (specialist tier — where Ahmed and Wael currently sit)

All strategic follow-up sessions standardized at 250 NIS across tiers.

## Rationale

Creates predictable, scalable compensation that differentiates by experience without requiring individual negotiation per psychiatrist. The 0.75 multiplier provides mathematical consistency across session types and durations.

## Consequences

- New hires can be slotted into tiers based on experience level
- Existing overpaid staff grandfathered at current rates (per separate decision)
- Payroll reconciliation simplified with standardized rate tables
- Tier 3 specialist rate creates a ceiling that may need review as market rates change

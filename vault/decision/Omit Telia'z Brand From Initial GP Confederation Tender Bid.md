# Omit Telia'z Brand From Initial GP Confederation Tender Bid

## Context

GP Confederation Leeds is preparing a tender bid for ADHD services. Telia'z is the technology and clinical partner, but naming them in the bid has procurement and governance implications — the partnership contract would need to be finalised before the bid, and the tender evaluation might scrutinise an international partner differently.

## Options Considered

1. **Name Telia'z explicitly** — Full transparency about the partnership in the bid
2. **Reference capabilities without naming** — Describe technology and psychiatry supervision without identifying Telia'z as the specific partner
3. **Submit bid first, formalise partnership after award** — Reference capabilities generically, confirm partner details post-contract-award

## Decision

The tender bid will reference technology capabilities and psychiatry supervision model without necessarily naming Telia'z as the partner. Partnership details would be confirmed after contract award.

## Rationale

This approach allows the bid to proceed on GP Confed's timeline without being blocked by the Telia'z partnership contract finalisation. It also lets the bid be evaluated on service merit rather than partner scrutiny.

## Consequences

- GP Confed can submit the bid without waiting for Telia'z contract finalisation
- Risk that the awarded contract terms may not align with Telia'z's needs
- Telia'z has less control over how their capabilities are represented in the bid
- Post-award partner disclosure could face governance challenges

# Prefer Rate Grandfathering Over Reductions for Overpaid Staff

## Context

During the 2025-12-04 staff rate review session, Rami and Basel discovered that some staff members are being paid above their designated rate tier — for example, one psychiatrist receiving 400 NIS/hour when their scale places them at 350 NIS/hour.

## Options Considered

1. **Option A** — Reduce the overpaid staff member's rate to match the correct tier
2. **Option B** — Grandfather the higher rate and allow natural correction over time

## Decision

Grandfather the higher rate. Do not lower anyone's existing compensation, even when it exceeds the formally correct tier.

## Rationale

Rami explicitly stated: "better to carry someone than to reduce them." Lowering compensation damages morale and trust, which is counterproductive during a period of organizational restructuring and growth. The cost of the overpayment is small relative to the retention and morale risk of a reduction.

## Consequences

- Some staff may remain above-scale indefinitely until rate tiers catch up
- New hires will be onboarded at correct tier rates, creating temporary rate inconsistencies across similar roles
- Basel must track which staff are above-scale for budgeting accuracy

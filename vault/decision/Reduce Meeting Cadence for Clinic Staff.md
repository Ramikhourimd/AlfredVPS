# Reduce Meeting Cadence for Clinic Staff

## Context

Rami observed that Sunday and Monday meetings cover the same topics, creating redundancy. The one-on-one meeting format was needed under the previous organizational structure but may no longer be necessary.

## Options Considered

1. **Keep both Sunday and Monday meetings** — Redundant coverage
2. **Consolidate to one meeting per week, one-on-ones monthly** — Reduces overhead while maintaining contact (proposed)

## Decision

Rami suggested reducing to weekly clinical meetings with one-on-one sessions monthly. Basel to decide the exact cadence that works for his workflow. Exceptions for ad-hoc topics (academia, protocols, licensing matters).

## Rationale

Under the new organizational structure with Basel as operations manager, the previous meeting cadence is no longer needed. Most content overlaps between the two days.

## Consequences

- Staff need fewer scheduled meeting commitments
- Basel has flexibility to set cadence
- Ad-hoc topics (academia, protocols) handled separately

# Operate UK Clinic Under Leon CQC License as Launch Strategy

## Context

Telia'z needs CQC (Care Quality Commission) registration to operate in the UK. Obtaining their own CQC is a longer process, but Leon — a UK family doctor — already has CQC registration. This creates an opportunity for a fast-track launch.

## Options Considered

1. **Option A: Operate under Leon's existing CQC** — Partner with Leon, send patients through his registered practice. Fast to activate once contract is signed.
2. **Option B: Obtain Telia'z own CQC registration** — Full independence but takes months. Being pursued in parallel as a second track via NHS/referral pathway.
3. **Option C: Wait for own CQC before launching** — Slower, lower risk, but delays revenue and learning.

## Decision

Pursue Option A as the primary launch strategy (fast track under Leon's CQC) while continuing Option B in parallel as a medium-term path to independence.

## Rationale

Speed to market is critical. Leon already has the regulatory shell. Telia'z contributes the clinical protocol (ADHD SOPs), the technology platform, the case management capability, and the patient acquisition engine. Leon provides the regulatory cover and his existing practice infrastructure.

## Consequences

- Dependent on Leon's cooperation and contract terms
- All non-ADHD SOPs remain Leon's existing procedures
- Need to share Telia'z ADHD-specific SOPs with Leon
- Must integrate with UK prescription requirements through Leon's practice
- Leon needs access to Telia'z admin and psychiatrist system views

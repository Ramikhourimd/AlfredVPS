# Adopt RACI Framework for Task Management at Telia'z

## Context

As Telia'z scales beyond the startup phase, tasks are getting confused across team members. Basel Kanaaneh raised the RACI methodology in discussion with Roy Shur — each task should have a responsible owner, people consulted in discussions, and people who need to be informed. Alex Taliaz previously dismissed RACI as "too much structure" for the team.

## Options Considered

1. **RACI framework** — Structured task ownership with clear responsibility, consultation, and information roles
2. **Continue informal coordination** — Maintain current ad-hoc, everyone-runs-at-everything approach

## Decision

The team acknowledged they have reached a stage where structured task management is necessary. The informal approach is causing task confusion as the organization grows. This is a draft decision — formal implementation pending Basel's operational onboarding.

## Rationale

- New tasks are "getting confused across everyone"
- The company has been in a fast-paced run mode where "everyone is busy" but accountability is unclear
- Basel's background brings structured operational processes that the team now recognizes as necessary
- Previous resistance from Alex reflected an earlier stage where the team was smaller

## Consequences

- Requires buy-in from leadership (Alex previously resistant)
- Each task/initiative will need clearly defined owner, consulted parties, and informed parties
- May initially slow decision-making but should improve execution quality

# Medical Director and Operations Manager Coordinate Weekly

## Context

The three-pillar clinic management model creates parallel authority tracks: Medical Director (clinical), Clinical Operations Manager (operational), and Administrative Manager. Since none reports to the other, a risk exists that clinical and operational tracks could become siloed — the Medical Director making protocol decisions without operational awareness, and the Operations Manager making scheduling decisions without clinical input.

## Options Considered

1. **Hierarchical reporting** — One role reports to the other, creating a single chain of command
2. **Ad-hoc coordination** — The two roles communicate as needed, no structured interface
3. **Weekly coordination meetings** — Formalized regular sync between Medical Director and Operations Manager

## Decision

The Medical Director and Clinical Operations Manager collaborate through weekly coordination meetings. This was explicitly stated during the Feb 22 meeting as the mechanism for keeping the two parallel tracks aligned.

## Rationale

Since the two roles are explicitly parallel (neither reports to the other), a structured meeting cadence prevents drift. Weekly frequency matches the operational tempo of a growing clinic where scheduling, protocols, and staffing decisions frequently intersect. The 30% Medical Director position (approximately 10 hours/week) makes weekly meetings a proportional investment in coordination.

## Consequences

- Clinical and operational decisions are aligned on a weekly basis
- Neither role can unilaterally make decisions that significantly impact the other's domain
- Pod leaders have a clear escalation path: clinical issues to Medical Director, operational issues to Operations Manager, cross-cutting issues surface at the weekly sync
- This mechanism partially addresses the CMO-COO authority tension at the clinic level by formalizing collaboration rather than hierarchy

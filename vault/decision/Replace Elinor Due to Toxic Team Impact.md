# Replace Elinor Due to Toxic Team Impact

## Context

Elinor is a senior psychiatrist at Clinic Israel who is creating a toxic environment. She complains publicly in group chats, tells case managers that things are "not right," and refuses to work under certain conditions. Her behavior undermines morale, especially among new interns who are still forming their impression of the clinic culture.

## Options Considered

1. **Address behavior through management conversation** — Attempt to correct the behavior. Implicitly rejected given the severity.
2. **Replace Elinor once a suitable candidate is confirmed** — Find a replacement first, then remove her from the team.

## Decision

Basel Kanaaneh will find a replacement psychiatrist. Once confirmed, Elinor will be removed from the team. Rami explicitly authorized: "Do what you need to do."

## Rationale

The toxic behavior is actively damaging team morale and contradicts the clinic's concurrent effort to recruit new staff. Staff satisfaction reports (via Rivi Idelman) already indicate some staff would not recommend working at the clinic — allowing a senior clinician to publicly undermine confidence compounds this problem.

## Consequences

- Temporary capacity reduction during transition period
- Requires finding a qualified replacement before action (to avoid gaps)
- Expected improvement in team morale and retention
- Sets precedent that toxic behavior from senior staff will not be tolerated

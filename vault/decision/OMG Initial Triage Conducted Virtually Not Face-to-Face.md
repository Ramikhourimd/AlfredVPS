# OMG Initial Triage Conducted Virtually Not Face-to-Face

## Context

The NHS ADHD pathway requires an initial triage step where patients are screened for inclusion/exclusion using health inequity and high risk factors. OMG Healthcare handles this triage stage. The question was whether this should be done at a physical clinic or remotely.

## Options Considered

1. **Face-to-face at OMG clinic** — Patients attend a physical appointment for triage screening
2. **Phone/virtual triage** — OMG staff conduct triage remotely by phone or video call
3. **Hybrid model** — Default to virtual with face-to-face option for complex cases

## Decision

OMG triage is conducted by phone or virtual call, not in-person at a clinic. The pathway design explicitly specifies this: "Conducted by phone or virtual (not in-person at clinic)."

## Rationale

Virtual triage scales more efficiently and aligns with the telepsychiatry model. It reduces the need for clinic capacity at the triage stage, reserving physical infrastructure for later clinical stages if needed.

## Consequences

- Faster patient processing at the triage stage
- Creates tension with the known constraint that ICB commissioners heavily weight face-to-face services in procurement bids
- May need to be revisited if the tender scoring penalises virtual-first approaches
- OMG's physical clinic infrastructure is underutilised at the triage stage

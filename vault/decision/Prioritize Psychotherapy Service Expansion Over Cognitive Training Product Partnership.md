# Prioritize Psychotherapy Service Expansion Over Cognitive Training Product Partnership

## Context

Rami's brother Levi works at a cognitive training company that approached Telia'z for a revenue-sharing marketing partnership. Rami evaluated the opportunity during a strategic discussion on 2025-11-09.

## Options Considered

1. **Option A** — Accept the cognitive training company's proposal to market their product through Telia'z with revenue sharing
2. **Option B** — Focus on the research data angle — the company collects valuable data that could support Telia'z's clinical research
3. **Option C** — Use the partnership exploration as a pathway to bring psychotherapy services into the clinic's offering

## Decision

Rami deprioritized the product resale model (Option A), expressed primary interest in the research data angle (Option B), and identified psychotherapy service expansion (Option C) as the most strategically valuable direction. The cognitive training product itself was not the priority.

## Rationale

- Maccabi patients are unlikely to pay out-of-pocket for cognitive training services
- Regulatory and contractual sensitivity exists given HMO agreements — reselling third-party services introduces compliance risk
- The research data the company collects could support Telia'z's clinical evidence base
- Expanding into psychotherapy services addresses a real clinical gap and aligns with the clinic's growth trajectory
- Rami's filter for partnerships: research value and clinical service expansion over product resale

## Consequences

- The cognitive training partnership was deprioritized as a revenue opportunity
- A meeting was still arranged to learn about session shortening approaches (rebranded cognitive training vs psychotherapy)
- Psychotherapy service expansion remains an open strategic initiative without a formal SPEC or timeline

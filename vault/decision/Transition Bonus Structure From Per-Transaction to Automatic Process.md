## Decision

Transition the employee bonus structure away from per-transaction (Makhirot) incentives toward automatic, process-based outcomes for [[project/Telia'z Clinic Israel]].

## Context

The current per-transaction bonus model rewards employees for each individual private patient sale. While this drives sales activity, it creates tunnel vision where employees focus exclusively on closing deals rather than broader operational goals like patient retention, care quality, and workflow efficiency.

[[person/Oren Taliaz]] articulated the principle: bonuses are attention-direction mechanisms. Whatever behavior you incentivize with bonuses is where employees will concentrate their effort, potentially at the expense of other important responsibilities.

## Rationale

- Per-transaction bonuses encourage short-term deal hunting over long-term patient relationships
- Automatic processes ensure good outcomes happen systematically, not through individual heroics
- Aligns with the broader [[decision/Phase Out Makhirot Incentives After December 2025]]
- Supports the organizational maturation from startup-style individual incentives to scalable process-driven operations

## Impact

- Affects all staff currently receiving Makhirot bonuses
- Requires designing replacement incentive structures tied to process outcomes
- May face initial resistance from high-performing individual sellers

## Next Steps

- Design the replacement incentive structure
- Define measurable process outcomes to incentivize
- Communicate timeline and rationale to affected staff
- See [[task/Communicate Makhirot Phase-Out to Staff]]

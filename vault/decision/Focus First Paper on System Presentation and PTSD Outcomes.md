# Focus First Paper on System Presentation and PTSD Outcomes

## Decision

The first scientific paper will be tightly scoped to three components:
1. System presentation (high-level, not technical deep-dive)
2. Patient demographics and diagnosis distribution post-October 7th
3. Correlation between time from trauma to first treatment and clinical improvement

## Rationale

Dekkel emphasized avoiding scope creep. While the dataset supports many possible analyses (military service correlations, predictive models, comorbidity analyses), running too many statistical tests would require Bonferroni corrections that could undermine statistical significance. The team agreed to reserve deeper analyses for subsequent papers, using this first paper to establish the dataset and platform.

## Context

- Dataset: 3,100+ patients treated after October 7th
- ~65-67% diagnosed with PTSD
- Helsinki committee (IRB) approval obtained from Maale HaCarmel
- Paper will have maximum 4-5 figures

## Alternatives Considered

- Broader scope including military service correlation, predictive modeling — rejected as too many analyses for one paper
- Deep technical system description — rejected in favor of high-level overview

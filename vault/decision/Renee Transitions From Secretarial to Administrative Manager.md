# Renee Transitions From Secretarial to Administrative Manager

## Context

Renee (Rana Khouri) has been performing hands-on secretarial and clinical staff management work. The Nov 2025 discussion established her as responsible for clinical staff (psychiatrists, case managers, secretaries). By the Feb 22 meeting, her role was formalized upward: she becomes the Administrative Manager pillar in the three-pillar district model — responsible for HR, secretarial oversight, onboarding, and compliance rather than direct secretarial operations.

## Options Considered

1. **Keep Renee in hands-on role** — continue current operational capacity
2. **Promote to Administrative Manager** — elevate to management pillar with oversight responsibility
3. **External hire for Administrative Manager** — bring in someone new for the pillar role

## Decision

Renee transitions from hands-on secretarial/clinical staff work to the Administrative Manager role — one of three parallel management pillars in the clinic district model. She will oversee HR processes, secretarial team management, onboarding, and compliance rather than performing these tasks directly.

## Rationale

Renee already holds institutional knowledge of clinical staff operations, recruitment processes, and the HR interface with Li Yamin. Promoting from within preserves this knowledge and fills the Administrative Manager pillar without an additional external hire. The Nov 2025 role delineation (Renee handling clinical staff, Li handling payroll/contracts) laid the groundwork for this elevation.

## Consequences

- Requires 2+ secretarial hires to replace Renee's direct operational output
- Creates clear reporting line: secretarial team reports to Renee as Administrative Manager
- Renee's scope expands from clinical staff to all clinic administrative functions
- Transition is blocked until replacement secretaries are onboarded

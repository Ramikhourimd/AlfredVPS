# Phase Out Mekhirot Incentive by End of December 2025

## Context

The clinic secretaries currently receive a "mekhirot" (sales/attendance) incentive. This was introduced when secretaries had patient-sourcing duties, but that context no longer applies. The incentive has become disconnected from actual job responsibilities.

## Options Considered

1. **Remove immediately** — Abrupt removal, risk of staff dissatisfaction
2. **Phase out by end of December 2025 with replacement** — Gradual transition, communicate in advance, replace with performance-based KPIs (chosen)
3. **Keep as-is** — Rejected, as the incentive no longer matches the role

## Decision

Phase out the mekhirot incentive by end of December 2025. Communicate to secretaries that:
1. The mekhirot incentive is not related to their current role scope
2. It will end at the close of December 2025
3. A new performance-based bonus tied to actual work will replace it

## Rationale

The original context (patient sourcing) has changed. The incentive needs to be tied to measurable, relevant KPIs to serve both as motivation and accountability. Gradual transition avoids surprising staff.

## Consequences

- Rana Khouri and other secretaries need to be informed proactively
- A new KPI-based bonus structure must be designed before January 2026
- Basel needs time to understand what metrics are appropriate

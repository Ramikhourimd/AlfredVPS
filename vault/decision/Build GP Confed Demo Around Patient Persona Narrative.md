# Build GP Confed Demo Around Patient Persona Narrative

## Context

During the GP Confederation demo rehearsal on 2025-02-11, the team needed to decide how to structure the platform demonstration for NHS stakeholders. The demo environment existed but lacked clinical data, and the presentation approach had not been finalized.

## Options Considered

1. **Feature walkthrough** — Show each platform capability sequentially (admin view, questionnaire, reports, scheduling)
2. **Patient persona narrative** — Build the entire demo around a single realistic patient journey from referral to discharge letter

## Decision

Adiel recommended building the demo around a patient story with realistic clinical data (ASRS, DIVA, PHQ-9, GAD-7 scores). Rami was assigned to prepare the clinical content and patient persona. The demo would walk through the full intake-to-letter workflow using this single patient case.

## Rationale

A patient narrative makes the workflow tangible for clinical audiences (GPs, governance leads) who think in terms of patient journeys, not software features. Showing actual scale names and scores adds clinical credibility. This approach aligns with the broader strategy of positioning as a clinical service rather than a technology vendor.

## Consequences

Requires manual population of the demo environment with realistic ADHD assessment data. Demo preparation becomes dependent on CMO (Rami) providing clinically accurate content. The narrative approach reinforces the service-first positioning already decided for GP Confederation engagement.

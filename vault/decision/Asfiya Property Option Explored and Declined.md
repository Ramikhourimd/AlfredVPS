# Asfiya Property Option Explored and Declined

## Context

Marwan Rohana showed Rami a property in Asfiya (Isfiya), connected to someone named Alaa. The property had been purchased by a man named Saber (Abu Abdullah) for 300,000 NIS and was now listed at 1.7 million NIS. It was located near a Druze holy site (maqam) and surrounded by Druze neighbors.

## Options Considered

1. **Pursue the Asfiya property** — Potentially affordable given the 300K purchase history, though now listed at 1.7M NIS
2. **Decline and continue Haifa-area search** — Stay focused on the primary search area

## Decision

The property visit was not followed up on. Rami expressed concern that the family wanted to be in a specific neighborhood (Dar Hal area), suggesting the Asfiya location did not meet the family's community or geographic preferences.

## Rationale

The property's location in a predominantly Druze community did not align with the family's preference for proximity to an Arab community. Additionally, Asfiya is outside the Haifa metropolitan focus area, and the specific neighborhood (Dar Hal area) was not where the family wanted to be.

## Consequences

This decision narrows the active search area and reinforces the pattern that community fit and location outweigh property price advantages. A property with significant appreciation (300K → 1.7M NIS) was declined based on community and location criteria.

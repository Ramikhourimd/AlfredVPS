# Require Part-Time Psychiatrist Hour Escalation Timeline Commitment at Onboarding

## Context

The clinic's workforce scaling model depends on part-time psychiatrists gradually increasing their clinical hours. However, the existing pattern shows psychiatrists frequently stagnating at their initial reduced hours (e.g., 20-25 hours/month) and framing their participation as discretionary. A specific case — psychiatrist Eliezer — crystallized this into a formal management decision.

## Options Considered

1. **Option A** — Accept psychiatrists at reduced hours with no escalation commitment, hoping they voluntarily increase
2. **Option B** — Require an upfront written timeline commitment for reaching target hours as a condition of onboarding, even if the timeline extends 6 months

## Decision

Option B. Rami approved Eliezer's 25-hour start but explicitly required that he commit to a specific timeline for reaching 40 hours. The commitment must be concrete (e.g., "I will reach 40 hours by [date]"), even if the date is 6 months away. This timeline provides patient continuity planning and workforce capacity visibility.

## Rationale

The decision responds to a known pattern: psychiatrists who join at reduced hours tend to remain at those hours indefinitely (see [[contradiction/Psychiatrist Hour Increase Commitments vs Observed Stagnation Pattern]]). By requiring the timeline upfront — before the psychiatrist is embedded in the system — the clinic gains a documented commitment that can be referenced later. Without it, management has no leverage beyond informal requests.

## Consequences

- Onboarding process must include an explicit hour-escalation agreement as a documented artefact
- May deter some candidates who cannot commit to a timeline
- Provides Shira and operations team with capacity projections for patient scheduling
- Creates a trackable metric: timeline commitment vs actual hours delivered

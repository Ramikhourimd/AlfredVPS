# Retain Olive Trees on Maghar Land as Protective Measure

## Context

Israeli authorities counted the olive trees on the Maghar family land plot and notified the family that the land needs to be cleared. This created a decision point: comply with the clearance notice or retain the trees.

## Options Considered

1. **Clear the olive trees** — comply with the authority's notification, preparing the land for potential development
2. **Retain the olive trees** — keep them in place as a deterrent against authority interference with the land

## Decision

Retain the olive trees on the land. A family member explicitly prefers to keep the olive trees in place as a protective measure — the reasoning being that while the olive trees remain, authorities are less likely to interfere with or reclaim the land.

## Rationale

The olive trees serve a dual purpose: (1) they are a cultural and agricultural asset, and (2) they function as a practical deterrent against land appropriation. The presence of active cultivation signals ongoing use and stewardship of the land, making administrative action against the plot less likely.

## Consequences

- The land cannot be immediately cleared for construction
- Any future development would require addressing the olive tree issue first
- The land remains in a holding pattern — protected but not actively developed
- This decision may delay any housing development on the plot

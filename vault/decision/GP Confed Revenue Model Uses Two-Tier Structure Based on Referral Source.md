# GP Confed Revenue Model Uses Two-Tier Structure Based on Referral Source

## Context

The GP Confederation Leeds partnership requires a commercial arrangement that works within NHS tariff constraints while providing revenue flow back to GP Confed for local GP projects. Two distinct patient referral sources create different economic dynamics.

## Options Considered

1. **Flat percentage across all referrals** — simpler but doesn't account for the different economics of local vs national referrals
2. **Two-tier percentage based on referral source** — different rates for Leeds-based vs Right to Choose patients, reflecting different cost structures and volumes
3. **Fixed fee per patient** — predictable but doesn't scale with volume

## Decision

Two-tier revenue share model with different percentages for each referral stream:
- **Leeds-based referrals:** percentage of revenue back to GP Confed from within existing NHS tariffs
- **Right to Choose referrals:** different (potentially more favorable) percentage to GP Confed, reflecting national patient access

## Rationale

Jim Barwick explicitly stated commercial viability is a prerequisite: "If we can't work out the commercial bit, we don't go any further." The two-tier structure acknowledges that Leeds referrals operate within local NHS tariff constraints, while Right to Choose referrals may have different economics. Revenue flowing back to Confed funds local GP projects, creating institutional buy-in. GP Confed Director of Finance to be brought in to model the specifics.

## Consequences

- Must not increase costs to ICB beyond current tariff levels
- Telia'z must demonstrate it can operate profitably within NHS tariff pricing (~£360 intake, ~£180 follow-up)
- Internal GP Confed governance processes required before partnership can be formalized
- Right to Choose referrals could become the primary revenue driver if Leeds volume is slow to ramp

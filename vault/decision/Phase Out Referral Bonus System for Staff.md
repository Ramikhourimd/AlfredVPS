# Phase Out Referral Bonus System for Staff

## Context

The clinic's staff bonus system was originally based on "mekhirot" (referrals/sales) — rewarding employees for bringing in new patients. This model made sense when the clinic was growing and referrals were a key metric. However, the business has matured and the referral-based incentive no longer aligns with actual work.

## Options Considered

1. **Keep the referral bonus system** — Continue as-is despite misalignment with current operations
2. **Phase out by end of December and replace with KPI-based incentives** — Sunset the old system gracefully, design new incentives tied to actual work performance

## Decision

Phase out the referral-based (mekhirot) bonus system by end of December 2025. Replace with a new incentive structure tied to meaningful work KPIs.

## Rationale

- The referral model no longer reflects how the clinic generates patients
- Staff were receiving bonuses for metrics they could not meaningfully influence
- A KPI-based system better motivates actual performance
- Gradual phase-out (communicate now, end in December) avoids staff shock

## Consequences

- Staff must be informed that December 2025 is the last month for the current bonus
- A replacement incentive system must be designed before January 2026
- Risk of staff dissatisfaction during transition — communication must be handled carefully
- Rana's compensation structure will need specific attention

# Consolidate Residential Patient to Seroquel Monotherapy

## Context

During the 2025-12-02 psychiatric assessment, Rami Khouri evaluated a residential patient currently on three overlapping psychotropic medications: Seroquel (quetiapine), Zyprexa (olanzapine), and an antidepressant. The patient experiences excessive side effects including orthostatic hypotension, sedation, and general treatment fatigue.

## Options Considered

1. **Option A: Maintain current regimen** — Keep all three medications as-is. Rejected: excessive polypharmacy with overlapping mechanisms and unnecessary side effect burden.
2. **Option B: Seroquel monotherapy** — Increase Seroquel, gradually taper Zyprexa, discontinue antidepressant. Selected: reduces side effects while maintaining mood stabilization.
3. **Option C: Zyprexa monotherapy** — Keep Zyprexa, taper Seroquel. Rejected: patient already had Seroquel reduced by facility psychiatrist at patient's request with worsened outcome.

## Decision

Consolidate to **Seroquel (quetiapine) as the sole mood stabilizer**:
- Increase Seroquel dosage
- Gradually taper and discontinue Zyprexa (olanzapine)
- Discontinue the antidepressant
- Monitor blood pressure (lying to standing) for orthostatic changes

## Rationale

- Both Seroquel and Zyprexa are atypical antipsychotics — using both is pharmacologically redundant
- Antidepressants can destabilize bipolar disorder; the patient needs mood stabilization, not antidepressant therapy
- Reducing to one medication minimizes side effects and improves adherence
- Seroquel has mood-stabilizing properties suitable for bipolar depression
- Goal is "one pill to take" rather than multiple overlapping medications

## Consequences

- Recommendation delivered via referral letter to facility's treating psychiatrist
- Rami cannot prescribe directly due to the patient already being under residential facility care
- Gradual taper required — abrupt discontinuation of Zyprexa not recommended
- Blood pressure monitoring needed during transition
- Success depends on facility psychiatrist's willingness to follow recommendation

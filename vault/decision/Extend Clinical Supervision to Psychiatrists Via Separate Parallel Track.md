# Extend Clinical Supervision to Psychiatrists Via Separate Parallel Track

## Context

Biweekly clinical supervision sessions with Prof. Moli Lahad were already running successfully for case managers. The question arose whether psychiatrists — who face their own form of clinical isolation in the fully remote model — would also benefit.

## Options Considered

1. **Add psychiatrists to existing case manager sessions** — Rejected. The professional hierarchy and different clinical concerns would change group dynamics and reduce openness.
2. **Create a separate parallel track for psychiatrists** — Chosen. Psychiatrists would have their own supervision sessions with Moli Lahad, allowing role-specific discussion.
3. **No extension** — Not considered seriously, as both Ori and Shira saw clear need.

## Decision

Extend burnout/clinical supervision sessions to psychiatrists via a separate parallel track with Prof. Moli Lahad, distinct from the existing case manager sessions.

## Rationale

Psychiatrists face isolation-driven burnout similar to case managers but have different clinical concerns and professional dynamics. Mixing the groups would compromise the psychological safety that makes supervision effective. A separate track preserves the intimacy and role-specificity of each group.

## Consequences

- Additional cost for Moli Lahad sessions (second track)
- Scheduling complexity increases (two groups to coordinate)
- Psychiatrists gain a structured peer support channel that didn't exist before
- May improve psychiatrist retention by addressing isolation-driven burnout

# Commission Next-Generation System Vision Exercise

## Context

With Shachar rebuilding the clinical platform from scratch, there is a window to define what the most advanced possible version of the system could look like. Rather than incrementally improving the current design, Rami proposed a blue-sky exercise to inform the yearly development roadmap.

## Options Considered

1. **Incremental improvement roadmap** — List features to add to the current rebuild plan
2. **Unconstrained vision exercise first, then roadmap** — Design the ideal system assuming all tools available, then derive priorities
3. **External consultant engagement** — Hire outside expertise to define the vision

## Decision

The innovation team will lead a brainstorming exercise to envision the most advanced possible clinical platform, assuming all tools and technologies are available. The output becomes a conceptual prototype/vision that informs a phased yearly development plan.

## Rationale

Starting from an unconstrained vision prevents anchoring on current limitations. The team can survey competing clinical platforms, identify best-in-class features, and design innovative additions. This produces a more ambitious and coherent roadmap than incremental planning.

## Consequences

- Rivi leads the research and concept design
- Ohad provides literature and research support
- Yael contributes product perspective
- Rami provides strategic direction and approval
- Requires surveying existing clinical management platforms first
- Output: concept document followed by prioritized yearly development plan

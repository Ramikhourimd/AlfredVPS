# Rami Owns On-Call Project Management End-to-End

## Context

After the first real on-call alert activation revealed multiple gaps — missed training, unrecognized phone numbers, process failures — [[person/Dekkel Taliaz]] and [[person/Oren Taliaz]] clarified accountability in the [[conversation/On-Call Incident Review Leadership Meeting 2025-09-22]].

Several issues fell through the cracks: on-call psychiatrists were not formally trained, practice drills were never conducted, and technical issues went unresolved. The root cause was unclear ownership of the operational (non-clinical) aspects of the on-call service.

## Options Considered

1. **Rami owns end-to-end** — Including verifying that all delegated tasks (training, system setup, drills) are completed, even if performed by others.
2. **Split ownership** — Clinical under Rami, operational/technical under Roy Shur and Shachar, with no single accountable person.
3. **Create new project manager role** — Hire or assign someone specifically for on-call service management.

## Decision

Option 1: [[person/Rami Khouri]] owns the on-call project end-to-end. He is accountable for ensuring all aspects — clinical, operational, and technical — are completed. Delegation is permitted, but Rami must verify execution and proactively flag any gaps to leadership.

## Rationale

- The on-call service was treated as a "closed project" from leadership's perspective — it was launched and expected to run under Rami's management
- Splitting ownership without a single accountable person created exactly the gaps that led to the incident
- Rami is closest to the day-to-day operations and the clinical staff

## Consequences

- Rami must track all delegated tasks (to Roy, Shachar, Shira) and confirm completion
- If Rami is unavailable (e.g., traveling), he must ensure coverage or flag the gap in advance
- Rami proposed bringing Elinor into operational management as a structural support — see [[task/Bring Elinor Into On-Call Operational Management]]
- Leadership (Oren, Dekkel) will be informed but not involved in day-to-day execution

# Launch Psychiatry Academy as Training and Recruitment Platform

## Context

Telia'z faces simultaneous challenges: recruiting qualified clinicians, retaining existing staff, and differentiating itself in the telepsychiatry market. The residency pipeline model addresses cost, but doesn't address the lack of a structured professional development offering.

## Options Considered

1. **Psychiatry Academy (structured training program)** — Formal cohort-based program training clinicians in technology-assisted psychiatry, doubling as a recruitment tool and retention mechanism
2. **Ad hoc training via onboarding only** — Continue current approach of per-employee onboarding without a branded program
3. **External training partnerships** — Partner with existing psychiatric education providers

## Decision

Launch a Psychiatry Academy with the first cohort in March 2026. Closed registration in early March, training sessions begin mid-March. The academy will train existing staff to be "technology-enabled clinicians" and position Telia'z as a place where clinicians learn to work at the frontier of clinical AI.

## Rationale

Rami proposed this during the 2026-02-15 team meeting as part of a broader strategy addressing three goals simultaneously:
1. Upskill existing staff in technology-assisted clinical practice
2. Use the academy as a recruitment differentiator (attract clinicians who want cutting-edge training)
3. Build Telia'z brand as an innovation leader in telepsychiatry

Critically, Rami emphasized that the academy must deliver substantive value before being marketed externally — it needs defined curriculum, resource allocation, and a clear offering. It must not be treated as a mere branding exercise.

## Consequences

- Requires curriculum development and resource allocation before March 2026
- Creates a new retention lever beyond compensation
- May increase short-term operational load (staff time for training delivery)
- If successful, reduces dependence on pure compensation-based recruitment

# Guardian Claims Require Court Order Documentation

## Context

During the 2025-11-10 operations meeting, Rami discussed the policy for handling parental consent in the children clinic, specifically when one parent claims sole guardianship.

## Options Considered

1. **Accept verbal sole-guardian claims** — take the parent at their word. Rejected due to legal liability.
2. **Require court order documentation** — demand official court documentation before accepting sole guardian status. Selected as the legally safe approach.
3. **Require any written proof** — accept attorney letters or other documentation. Not chosen — only court orders provide definitive legal authority.

## Decision

When a parent claims to be the **sole guardian** of a child patient, the clinic **must** require a court order as documentation before proceeding with treatment under sole-guardian consent. Standard shared custody situations follow normal two-parent consent procedures.

## Rationale

Accepting verbal sole-guardian claims without documentation creates significant legal liability. If the other parent contests, the clinic could face legal action for treating a child without proper consent. Court orders are the only definitive proof of sole guardianship.

## Consequences

- May delay treatment start for children with complex custody situations
- Protects the clinic from legal liability
- Clear policy for intake staff to follow
- Must be communicated to all children clinic staff

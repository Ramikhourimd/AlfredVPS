# Target Family Doctors for Outreach Instead of Mental Health Centers

## Decision

Focus clinic outreach and marketing efforts on family medicine doctors (GPs) rather than mental health centers.

## Context

During the November 10, 2025 operations review, Rami and Ori discussed the outreach strategy, particularly in light of Adi Lavi's field marketing activities. The current approach needed strategic direction on whom to target.

## Rationale

- **Mental health centers are competitors**, not potential referral partners. They provide overlapping psychiatric services and have no incentive to refer patients elsewhere.
- **Family doctors are the primary referral pipeline.** Patients typically present to their GP first with mental health concerns, and the GP decides where to refer.
- **GPs need what the clinic offers**: quick access to psychiatric evaluation, medication management, and clinical ADHD diagnosis — services that reduce the GP's burden of managing complex psychiatric cases.
- **High volume potential**: Each family doctor practice sees hundreds of patients, a fraction of whom need psychiatric referral.

## Implementation

- Adi Lavi's field outreach to be coordinated with Ori Shukron
- Geographic territories to be divided to prevent overlap
- Messaging to emphasize clinic capabilities relevant to GP needs: rapid psychiatric access, medication management expertise, clinical diagnosis
- Mental health centers to be excluded from outreach target list

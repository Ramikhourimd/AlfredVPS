# Standardize All Follow-Up Sessions at 250 NIS

## Context

During the 2025-12-04 fee schedule review, Rami and Basel discovered inconsistent follow-up rates across psychiatrists. Some were being paid 200, others 250, with no clear logic for the difference.

## Options Considered

1. **Keep individual rates** — Each psychiatrist maintains their current follow-up rate per their signed agreement
2. **Standardize to 250 NIS** — All psychiatrists receive the same 250 NIS rate for standard follow-up sessions (istradik), including children sessions

## Decision

Standardize all psychiatrist follow-up ("ma'akav" / "istradik") sessions at 250 NIS, including for children's sessions. This applies across all tiers.

## Rationale

- Eliminates confusing rate discrepancies between staff (Atef at 200 while others at 250)
- Simplifies payroll calculations and agreement management
- Reduces perceived unfairness among staff
- Aligns with unified agreement ("heskem ahid") initiative

## Consequences

- Atef's follow-up rate must be raised from 200 to 250 NIS
- All existing agreements need verification against this standard
- Slightly increases payroll costs for under-paid staff but creates consistency

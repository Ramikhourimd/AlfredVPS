# Retain Only Text Summaries Not Audio or Video From Clinical Sessions

## Context

The clinical platform records and processes patient sessions. A decision was needed about whether to retain audio and video recordings alongside the text summaries generated by the AI pipeline.

## Options Considered

1. **Save audio/video alongside summaries** — Full fidelity record for research and quality assurance
2. **Save only text summaries** — Minimize storage, overhead, and consent requirements

## Decision

Clinical audio and video files are not saved. Only real-time text summaries are retained. The fast clinical flow is prioritized over archival completeness.

## Rationale

Three factors drive this decision: (1) the AI text analysis captures approximately 95% of clinically relevant signals from text alone, making video's marginal ~2% accuracy gain insufficient to justify retention; (2) patient consent requirements for recordings create bureaucratic overhead that slows the clinical workflow; (3) storage costs and compliance burden for audio/video archives are disproportionate to their value.

## Consequences

- Significantly reduced storage costs and compliance burden
- Faster clinical flow without recording consent friction
- Research is limited to text-based analysis (no retrospective audio/video analysis possible)
- If the 95% text accuracy claim proves optimistic, clinical nuance may be lost
- Aligns with the existing decision to store transcripts as database text, not files

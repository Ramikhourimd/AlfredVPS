# Patients Returning After Six Months Require Full Re-intake

## Context

During the 2025-11-10 operations meeting, Rami raised the question of how to handle patients who return to the clinic after an extended period of absence. The clinic had no formal policy for this scenario.

## Options Considered

1. **Resume from last session** — treat as a continuing patient with access to previous records. Rejected because clinical picture may have changed substantially after 6 months.
2. **Require full re-intake** — treat as a new patient requiring complete clinical assessment. Selected as the safest clinical approach.
3. **Abbreviated re-assessment** — shorter evaluation updating key clinical data. Not chosen — insufficient for a 6-month gap.

## Decision

Patients who return to the clinic after **6 or more months of inactivity** must undergo a **full re-intake process**, including complete intake paperwork and clinical evaluation. Previous records remain available for reference.

## Rationale

After 6 months, a patient may have:
- Changed medications or treatment providers
- Developed new conditions or diagnoses
- Experienced significant life changes affecting mental health
- Had their previous treatment plan become obsolete

A full re-intake ensures clinical accuracy and patient safety.

## Consequences

- Additional intake time and cost for returning patients
- Potential billing question — can re-intake be billed as new intake? (To be confirmed with Nirit)
- Clearer clinical picture for the treating team
- Reduced risk of treatment based on outdated information

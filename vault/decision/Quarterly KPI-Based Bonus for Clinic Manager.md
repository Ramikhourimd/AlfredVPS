# Quarterly KPI-Based Bonus for Clinic Manager

## Context

With Shira taking on the clinic manager role and KPIs defined around financial utilization, a compensation incentive structure was needed to align her performance with clinic financial health.

## Options Considered

1. **Monthly bonus** — More frequent feedback but enables gaming (e.g., shifting costs between months)
2. **Quarterly bonus** — Balances responsiveness with reliability; Alex's recommended cadence
3. **Semi-annual or annual bonus** — Too infrequent for operational feedback

## Decision

Quarterly bonus measurement tied to clinical compensation as a percentage of revenue:
- Below 50%: bonus begins
- Approaching 35%: maximum bonus
- Linear scale between the two points
- Shira has full autonomy on HOW to achieve the target (reduce hours, optimize scheduling, reallocate staff)

Additionally, Shira would define sub-KPIs for her team (Rena, Avital), potentially with a team-wide bonus pool she distributes.

**Status: Draft** — Bonus NIS amounts not yet defined. Alex took this away to finalize.

## Rationale

Quarterly cadence prevents month-to-month gaming while keeping the incentive timely. Giving Shira autonomy over the means (not just the ends) enables creative optimization rather than checkbox compliance. Team bonus pool encourages Shira to build her own management structure.

## Consequences

- Specific bonus NIS amounts still need definition by Alex
- Historical utilization baseline must be calculated first to set realistic starting targets
- Rena and Avital sub-KPIs to be defined by Shira
- May overlap with existing task for Rana's bonus structure design

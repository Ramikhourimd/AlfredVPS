# Launch UK Clinic Through Leon GP Partnership Using His CQC Registration

## Context

Telia'z needed to enter the UK market but does not hold its own CQC (Care Quality Commission) registration, which is required to provide clinical services in England. Obtaining independent CQC registration is a lengthy process.

## Options Considered

1. **Option A** — Partner with Leon, a UK family doctor who already holds his own CQC registration, routing Telia'z patients through his practice
2. **Option B** — Pursue independent CQC registration for Telia'z to receive NHS referrals directly (longer-term)

## Decision

Launch UK operations through Leon's existing CQC registration as the primary path (Track 1), while advancing independent CQC/NHS registration as a secondary longer-term track (Track 2). Leon's practice serves as the operational shell; patients arrive at his site and go through Telia'z assessment flow.

## Rationale

Leon already has CQC registration, eliminating the largest regulatory blocker. He reviewed the partnership proposal and met with Yael to see the patient flow design. Only the ADHD SOP is Telia'z proprietary; all other clinical protocols fall under Leon's existing practice.

## Consequences

- UK launch timeline compressed: target mid-March campaign start, system launch March 31
- Telia'z dependent on Leon's contract signing and CQC compliance
- Only ADHD protocol is Telia'z-owned; other SOPs are Leon's responsibility
- Telia'z retains limited operational control compared to independent registration
- Second track (direct CQC via Susan/Daolifa Eujah) continues in parallel for future independence

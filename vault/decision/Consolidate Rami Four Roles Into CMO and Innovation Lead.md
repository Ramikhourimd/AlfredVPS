# Consolidate Rami Four Roles Into CMO and Innovation Lead

## Context

Rami currently performs four overlapping functions: (1) CEO substitute, (2) Clinic Medical Director, (3) Head of Clinical Operations, and (4) Innovation/AI Lead. This diffusion of responsibilities creates ambiguity in authority, accountability, and time allocation. The Feb 22 leadership meeting identified the need to resolve this.

## Options Considered

1. **Maintain status quo** — Rami continues all four functions. Rejected due to unsustainability and role ambiguity.
2. **Single focused role (CMO only)** — Rami focuses exclusively on clinical oversight. Rejected because it would lose innovation momentum.
3. **Two clear roles: CMO + Innovation Lead** — Rami holds company-level medical authority and leads AI/product innovation. Other functions handed off.

## Decision

Consolidate Rami's four current functions into two clearly scoped roles: CMO (company-level medical authority, quality oversight, regulatory compliance) and Innovation Lead (AI integration, product development, CTO collaboration). CEO substitute and day-to-day Medical Director responsibilities to be transferred to other team members.

## Rationale

Two roles preserve Rami's core strengths (clinical vision + innovation) while eliminating the unsustainable spread across four functions. Hiring a Medical Director for the clinic enables handing off day-to-day clinical oversight. CEO substitute function is temporary and should be formalized differently.

## Consequences

- Requires hiring a Medical Director for the clinic (to absorb day-to-day clinical oversight)
- Requires defining clear boundaries between CMO and Operations Manager authority
- Innovation Lead role needs a product manager hire for execution capacity
- CEO substitute function needs formal succession/delegation protocol

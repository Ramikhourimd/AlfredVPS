# Delay UK Case Manager Hiring Until Patient Volume Confirmed

## Context

UK clinic launch requires case managers, but patient volume is uncertain until the clinic is actually operational and marketing campaigns begin driving traffic. Hiring staff prematurely creates fixed costs without guaranteed revenue.

## Options Considered

1. **Hire UK case managers immediately** — Ready from day one but financial risk if patient volume is low
2. **Use Israeli stopgap, hire UK staff when volume is confirmed** — Lower risk, slower transition to local team
3. **Don't hire at all, rely on Israeli staff permanently** — Already rejected as long-term strategy

## Decision

Do not hire UK-based case managers until patient volume is confirmed through actual operations. Use Israeli case manager (Bassem) as English-speaking stopgap for initial patients. Only trigger UK recruitment once demand is validated.

## Rationale

Financial prudence — Adiel was firm that hiring before volume confirmation is premature. The Israeli stopgap (Bassem has done this before in English) bridges the gap at minimal incremental cost since he's already on payroll. This also avoids recruiting and then potentially having insufficient work for new hires.

## Consequences

Initial UK patients will be managed by someone in a different timezone. Quality of case management may differ from a locally-based manager. Recruitment will lag behind demand — when volume ramps up, there will be a delay before UK case managers are onboarded.

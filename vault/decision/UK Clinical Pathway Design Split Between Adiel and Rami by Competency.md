# UK Clinical Pathway Design Split Between Adiel and Rami by Competency

## Context

The UK NHS ADHD pathway requires detailed clinical specifications for each patient flow stage. Defining this pathway requires both business/operational structure and clinical expertise, held by different people.

## Options Considered

1. **Rami designs full pathway end-to-end** — rejected because Rami is at full capacity and flagged he needs focused, specific deliverables
2. **Adiel designs full pathway** — rejected because clinical specifications require medical expertise
3. **Split by competency** — Adiel maps structural flow in Excel; Rami fills in clinical content per stage

## Decision

Adiel structures and maps each stage of the patient flow in an Excel spreadsheet. Rami then fills in the clinical requirements, specifications, and medical content for each stage. This division allows Rami to contribute focused clinical input without managing the overall flow design.

## Rationale

Rami explicitly stated he is at full capacity and needs focused, specific deliverables rather than open-ended process management. By splitting the work, Adiel handles the organizational/operational mapping while Rami provides discrete clinical inputs per stage — matching each person's bandwidth and expertise.

## Consequences

- Clinical pathway design depends on both Adiel completing the structural mapping AND Rami filling clinical content
- If Rami's bandwidth drops further, clinical specifications stall even if the structural flow is ready
- This pattern could be replicated for other international market pathway designs

# KPIs Decomposed Into Multiple Sub-Projects Not Single Initiative

## Context

Rivi wanted to advance KPI work using validated questionnaires for patient and staff satisfaction, treating it as a single project. KPIs span clinical, operational, and satisfaction domains — too broad for one project.

## Options Considered

1. **Single monolithic KPI project** — one initiative covering all KPI domains
2. **Decomposed sub-projects** — clinical KPIs, operational KPIs, and satisfaction KPIs as separate workstreams

## Decision

KPIs must be broken into multiple sub-projects (clinical KPIs, operational KPIs, satisfaction KPIs). Complex patient project takes priority over KPI work. KPIs are important but should not be treated as a single monolithic project. Maximum 3 active projects at a time for Rivi.

## Rationale

A monolithic KPI project would consume one of Rivi's three project slots while spanning domains that are best tackled independently. Decomposition allows prioritization and prevents scope creep.

## Consequences

- KPI work advances incrementally as sub-projects, not as one large initiative
- Complex patient project has explicit priority over KPI work
- Each KPI sub-project competes for one of Rivi's 3 project slots
- Prevents the KPI effort from becoming an unbounded scope sink

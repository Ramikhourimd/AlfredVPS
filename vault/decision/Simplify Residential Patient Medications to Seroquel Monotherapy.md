# Simplify Residential Patient Medications to Seroquel Monotherapy

## Context

An elderly male residential facility patient was on three concurrent psychotropic medications: Seroquel (quetiapine), Zyprexa (olanzapine), and an antidepressant (likely escitalopram). This polypharmacy was producing excessive side effects including orthostatic hypotension without clear clinical benefit. The patient expressed significant dissatisfaction with his treatment and side effect burden.

## Options Considered

1. **Option A: Maintain current triple regimen** — Continue Seroquel + Zyprexa + antidepressant. Rejected due to overlapping side effects and lack of clinical rationale for two atypical antipsychotics plus an antidepressant simultaneously.
2. **Option B: Consolidate to Seroquel monotherapy** — Increase Seroquel to therapeutic dose, gradually taper Zyprexa, discontinue antidepressant. Selected as optimal approach.
3. **Option C: Switch to Zyprexa monotherapy** — Not considered optimal as patient had better historical tolerance of Seroquel and the facility psychiatrist had already been adjusting Seroquel dosing.

## Decision

Consolidate the patient's psychiatric medications to Seroquel (quetiapine) as the sole agent, serving as both mood stabilizer and antipsychotic. Gradually taper and discontinue Zyprexa (olanzapine) and the antidepressant.

## Rationale

- The patient's presentation requires a mood stabilizer, not antidepressants — the antidepressant is not indicated for his clinical profile
- Two atypical antipsychotics (Seroquel + Zyprexa) with overlapping mechanisms create unnecessary side effect burden
- Seroquel has mood-stabilizing properties at therapeutic doses, making it suitable as a single agent
- Reducing to one medication minimizes side effects (particularly orthostatic hypotension) and simplifies the regimen for a patient who is already resistant to treatment

## Consequences

- Recommendation letter to be delivered to facility psychiatrist for implementation
- Requires careful titration — gradual Seroquel increase while tapering Zyprexa to avoid withdrawal effects
- Blood pressure monitoring (supine to standing) needed during transition period
- Patient may need closer monitoring during medication transition

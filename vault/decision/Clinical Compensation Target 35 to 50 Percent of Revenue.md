# Clinical Compensation Target 35 to 50 Percent of Revenue

## Context

To define a measurable financial KPI for the clinic manager role, Alex Taliaz established benchmark ranges for clinical compensation as a percentage of total revenue, drawing on service industry experience and telemedicine-specific knowledge.

## Options Considered

1. **Fixed percentage target** — Set a single number (e.g., 45%) as the target
2. **Range-based target with bonus scale** — Define an acceptable range with progressive incentives

## Decision

Clinical compensation (all clinical staff costs excluding Rami) should fall between 35% and 50% of clinic revenue:
- **35%** = Excellent / best achievable in telemedicine
- **50%** = Maximum acceptable
- **Above 50%** = Failing — no bonus territory

Historical reference: When Rami managed the clinic, the ratio was approximately 45% (65% of the 70% clinic share), confirming the range is realistic.

## Rationale

Telemedicine has lower overhead than in-person clinics, enabling a tighter compensation-to-revenue ratio. Alex confirmed from his industry knowledge that 35% is genuinely achievable but exceptional, while 50% represents the upper boundary of viability. The range gives Shira clear goalposts without being unrealistically tight.

## Consequences

- Shira must be given visibility into revenue numbers (Alex agreed she needs to know)
- The metric includes ALL clinical costs: psychiatrist pay, therapist pay, insurance, management salaries
- Bonus structure maps directly to this range

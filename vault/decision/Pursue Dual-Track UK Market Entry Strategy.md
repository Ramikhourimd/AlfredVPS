# Pursue Dual-Track UK Market Entry Strategy

## Context

Telia'z needs CQC registration to operate in the UK. Rather than betting on a single pathway, the team is pursuing two parallel regulatory tracks simultaneously to de-risk market entry.

## Options Considered

1. **Single track via Leon** — operate solely under Leon's existing CQC registration, accept full dependency on one partner
2. **Single track via own CQC** — apply for own CQC registration via NHS referral pathway, accept longer timeline before launch
3. **Dual-track approach** — pursue both simultaneously, Leon as fast-launch and own CQC as strategic long-term path

## Decision

Pursue both tracks in parallel. Track 1 (Leon partnership) provides immediate market entry under his existing CQC registration. Track 2 (own CQC via NHS referral pathway, advanced through Dolifa Ioja and Susan) provides long-term regulatory independence.

## Rationale

Track 1 enables a fast launch targeting March 2026 without waiting for CQC application processing. Track 2 is strategically important for independence from a single partner and access to NHS referral volume. Running both in parallel reduces single-point-of-failure risk.

## Consequences

- Requires managing two regulatory relationships simultaneously
- Track 2 progress (Dolifa Ioja meetings, Susan conversations) continues alongside Track 1 launch preparations
- If Track 1 succeeds, Track 2 provides an upgrade path; if Track 1 stalls, Track 2 becomes the primary pathway

# Structure Employee Experience Research as Academic Methodology

## Context

Rivi had been collecting qualitative staff feedback through conversations but lacked a structured framework to organize, measure, and track findings over time.

## Options Considered

1. **Continue informal qualitative collection** — gut-feel reports and bullet-point summaries
2. **Academic research methodology** — structured surveys, measurable variables, correlation analysis, periodic measurement

## Decision

All employee experience research must follow academic research methodology: define the problem, state hypotheses, choose methodology, collect measurable data via periodic surveys, analyze results with statistical rigor, and produce structured reports with recommendations.

## Rationale

- Qualitative impressions cannot be tracked over time or correlated with interventions
- Measurable data enables evidence-based decisions about which interventions actually reduce burnout
- Structured reports are more persuasive to leadership and enable accountability
- Rami can provide automated literature review support via Google Sheets + AI pipeline

## Consequences

- Rivi must build a periodic survey instrument with quantifiable dimensions
- All reports follow: background, methodology, results, discussion, recommendations
- Google Sheets used as input for automated overnight literature review
- Enables longitudinal tracking of staff satisfaction metrics

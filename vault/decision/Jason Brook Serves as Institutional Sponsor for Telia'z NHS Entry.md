# Jason Brook Serves as Institutional Sponsor for Telia'z NHS Entry

## Context

Telia'z needed an institutional pathway into the NHS ecosystem. As an Israeli technology company with no UK healthcare track record, direct access to NHS procurement and GP Confederation partnerships was not achievable without a credible NHS insider vouching for the organization.

## Options Considered

1. **Cold approach to NHS bodies** — Direct outreach without institutional sponsorship
2. **NHS insider introduction** — Leverage a credible NHS figure to make introductions and validate the proposition

## Decision

Jason Brook, an ICB (Integrated Care Board) Medical Director, actively connected Telia'z with GP Confederation Leeds and facilitated the January 2025 partnership meeting. Rebecca Wilson (DigiSave) had previously helped Telia'z obtain DTAC compliance, and Jason used this compliance as a credibility signal when making the introduction. A virtual meeting with Jim Barwick (GP Confederation) preceded the January in-person meeting, also facilitated by Jason.

## Rationale

Jason Brook's ICB Medical Director role gives him institutional authority within the NHS structure. His endorsement carries weight with GP Confederation leadership and provides Telia'z with legitimacy that would take years to build independently. The DTAC compliance (achieved with Rebecca Wilson's help) served as the tangible credential backing the introduction.

## Consequences

Telia'z's NHS pathway is partially dependent on Jason Brook's continued support and institutional position. If Jason changes roles or withdraws support, the GP Confederation relationship would need to be sustained on its own merits. This also means the GP Confederation track was initiated through a personal relationship rather than a formal procurement process, which could create complications if governance scrutiny increases.

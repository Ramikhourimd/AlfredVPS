# CMO Role Positioned at Company Level Above Individual Districts

## Context

The Feb 22 leadership meeting debated the scope and governance level of the CMO role. With the clinic restructuring into a "district model" and international expansion planned (UK first), the question arose whether the CMO role should sit within the Israeli clinic district or at the company level overseeing clinical standards across all districts.

## Options Considered

1. **Company-level CMO** — CMO operates above individual clinic districts, setting clinical standards, protocols, and quality frameworks that apply across all locations (Israel, UK, future)
2. **Clinic-level Medical Director** — Clinical leadership scoped to individual clinic operations, with no company-wide clinical governance authority

## Decision

The CMO role is positioned at the company level, operating above individual clinic districts. The Medical Director role (30% position) handles clinic-level clinical supervision within each district, while the CMO ensures clinical governance, standards, and quality frameworks apply organization-wide.

## Rationale

International expansion requires consistent clinical standards across all locations. A clinic-level clinical lead cannot enforce standards in other jurisdictions. The company needs a single clinical authority who can define protocols that travel across borders — particularly critical for a medical company entering the regulated UK market.

## Consequences

This creates a two-tier clinical governance structure: company-level CMO (strategic, standards, cross-district) and district-level Medical Director (operational, day-to-day clinical supervision). The CMO role becomes essential infrastructure for scaling, not merely a clinical management position. This also strengthens the case for CMO veto authority on clinical matters company-wide.

# External Vendor or Call Center Proposed as Case Management Stopgap

## Context

The Israel clinic is operating at an 8% intake conversion rate (945 new patients, only 77 getting intake appointments), severely limiting the availability of Israeli case managers for UK stopgap duties. The team has already decided to use Israeli staff as an interim measure, but capacity constraints make this increasingly difficult.

## Options Considered

1. **Israeli case managers as stopgap** — Already decided, but constrained by Israel clinic capacity crisis
2. **External vendor or call center** — Outsource case management to a third-party provider
3. **Wait for local UK recruitment** — Already planned as long-term solution, but depends on patient volume

## Decision

Alex Taliaz proposed exploring an external vendor or call center as an alternative stopgap for case management capacity. This would supplement or replace the Israeli staff stopgap if Israel clinic demands prevent staff reallocation.

## Rationale

The 8% intake conversion rate means Israeli case managers are fully absorbed by domestic demand. An external vendor could provide English-speaking case management without competing for the same resource pool.

## Consequences

If pursued, this introduces a third staffing model (Israeli stopgap, vendor, local hire) and adds vendor management complexity. Quality control of the clinical intake process would need to be maintained across an external provider unfamiliar with Telia'z SOPs.

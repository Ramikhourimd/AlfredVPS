# Clinic Provides Clinical ADHD Diagnosis Only Not Formal MOH Assessment

## Decision

The clinic provides **clinical ADHD diagnosis** — a psychiatrist evaluation sufficient to initiate medication treatment — but does **not** provide formal MOH-compliant ADHD assessment reports.

## Context

During the November 10, 2025 operations review, Rami and Ori clarified the scope of ADHD-related services the clinic offers. There had been ambiguity about whether the clinic performs formal ADHD assessments.

## Rationale

The formal MOH-compliant ADHD assessment is a distinct, extensive process requiring:
- Computerized attention tests (CPT, MOXO-type)
- Standardized questionnaires and rating scales
- 2-3 hours of dedicated evaluation time
- Specialized reporting format

This is fundamentally different from a clinical psychiatric evaluation that results in an ADHD diagnosis and treatment plan. The clinic's psychiatrists are qualified to make clinical diagnoses and prescribe treatment, which is the core service offered.

Additionally, Maccabi health fund prohibits charging patients additional fees for assessment services, making the extended formal assessment economically unviable for Maccabi patients.

## Implications

- Patient communications and intake materials must clearly state that the clinic provides clinical diagnosis, not formal assessment reports
- Referral sources (especially family doctors) should understand what the clinic delivers
- Patients needing formal MOH assessment reports should be directed to appropriate assessment centers
- Adiel and other staff communicating with patients must use precise language about the service scope

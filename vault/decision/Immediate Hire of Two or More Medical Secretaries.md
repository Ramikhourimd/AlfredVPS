# Immediate Hire of Two or More Medical Secretaries

## Context

The medical secretary team is at critically low capacity: Alice is barely working, Amda is going on maternity leave, and Renee needs to transition from hands-on secretarial work to the Administrative Manager role. Without replacement hires, the clinic cannot maintain basic operational functions (appointment scheduling, patient coordination, diary management).

## Options Considered

1. **Hire 2 secretaries immediately** — minimum to cover departures and enable Renee's transition
2. **Hire 3+ secretaries** — build toward the 5 FTE target for year-end scaling
3. **Delay hiring** — wait for organizational chart finalization before adding headcount

## Decision

Hire at least 2 additional medical secretaries immediately, with a trajectory toward 5 full-time secretarial positions by end of 2026. This hiring proceeds independently of the broader organizational chart finalization.

## Rationale

Secretarial capacity is a hard operational constraint — without adequate coverage, the clinic cannot schedule appointments, respond to patients, or support psychiatrist diary management. Unlike management-level hires (which depend on org chart finalization), secretarial positions are operationally unambiguous and can be filled immediately.

## Consequences

- Renee can begin transitioning to Administrative Manager role
- Pod-based diary management model becomes feasible
- Recruitment and onboarding costs (~5 FTE by year-end)
- Requires decision on pod-dedicated vs shared secretarial pool model

# Eliminate CPI-Indexed Track Exposure in Restructuring

## Context

The current mortgage contains a variable CPI-indexed track where the principal has grown from 260,000 to 267,000 NIS despite 5.5 years of regular payments — a clear case of negative amortization driven by CPI indexation. This track is the most structurally problematic component of the four-track mortgage.

## Options Considered

1. **Eliminate CPI-indexed exposure entirely** — Restructure to remove the CPI-indexed track, replacing it with more predictable components
2. **Reduce CPI-indexed exposure** — Partial rebalancing to lower CPI risk while retaining some indexed benefit
3. **Retain current structure** — Accept CPI risk as part of the diversified track mix

## Decision

Eliminate the CPI-indexed track exposure as part of the Option A restructuring. The restructured mortgage will replace the inefficient CPI-indexed component with tracks that do not suffer from negative amortization.

## Rationale

The CPI-indexed track is actively eroding principal repayment — after 5.5 years of payments, the principal has grown by 7,000 NIS rather than shrinking. This directly conflicts with Rami's goal of total cost reduction. Removing this track is essential to achieving the ~200,000 NIS total savings target under Option A.

## Consequences

- Eliminates the negative amortization problem on the CPI-indexed track
- Reduces exposure to future CPI inflation risk
- May increase exposure to other rate types (fixed or Prime) depending on replacement track composition
- The specific replacement track structure will be determined during Bank Hapoalim negotiation

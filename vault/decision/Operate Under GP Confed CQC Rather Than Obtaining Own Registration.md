# Operate Under GP Confed CQC Rather Than Obtaining Own Registration

## Context
Telia'z needs CQC (Care Quality Commission) registration to deliver clinical services in the UK. Obtaining their own CQC registration would take months and delay market entry.

## Options Considered
1. **Option A — Operate under GP Confed CQC** — Embed Telia'z clinicians within GP Confed's existing CQC-registered service as an integrated capability (not subcontractor)
2. **Option B — Obtain own CQC registration** — Apply independently, which Adiel noted was a goal "over the course of the next year"
3. **Option C — Subcontract model** — Formal subcontract from Confed, but Sean flagged CQC compliance concerns with this approach

## Decision
Start with Option A (embed under Confed CQC) for speed to market, with a plan to pursue own CQC registration within 12 months.

## Rationale
Fastest path to market given imminent tender. GP Confed already has credibility, CQC registration, and local relationships. Telia'z brings clinical workforce and technology — complementary rather than competing.

## Consequences
- Telia'z is dependent on GP Confed for regulatory coverage initially
- Clinical governance must be clearly mapped for the embedded model
- Revenue share must account for the governance burden on GP Confed

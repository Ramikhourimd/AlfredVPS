# Double Shira Bonus Percentage From Half to One Percent of Revenue

## Context

Shira's unsigned employment contract at [[org/Telia'z]] Clinic Israel included a revenue-based bonus set at 0.5% of total clinic revenue, capped at 1,500 NIS/month. At the clinic's current revenue of approximately 400,000 NIS/month, this produced only 200 NIS/month in bonus — too small to be meaningful. Rami discussed the issue with the lawyers and proposed a change to Alex.

## Options Considered

1. **Keep 0.5% with higher cap** — Would not fix the low base amount at current revenue levels
2. **Double to 1% with same cap** — Doubles the bonus at current revenue, approaches cap faster as revenue grows
3. **Restructure bonus entirely** — Too complex; the goal was to close the contract quickly

## Decision

Double the bonus percentage from 0.5% to **1% of total clinic revenue**, keeping the existing cap at 1,500 NIS/month. Review the cap at year end based on Shira's performance in growing clinic revenue.

Additionally, Rami requested that **social benefits (pension contributions)** be applied to the bonus component, pending Alex's approval.

## Rationale

The original 0.5% rate made the bonus feel token rather than motivational. At 1%, the bonus is still modest (400 NIS/month at 400K revenue) but reaches the cap faster as Shira grows revenue — aligning her incentive with clinic financial performance. The cap remains as a safety net until year-end review provides data on effectiveness.

## Consequences

- Lawyers ([[person/Liron Megidash]] at [[org/NB Law]]) must update the employment contract
- Social benefits on bonus still pending Alex's decision
- Year-end cap review committed to
- Contract must be signed promptly to maintain Shira's momentum

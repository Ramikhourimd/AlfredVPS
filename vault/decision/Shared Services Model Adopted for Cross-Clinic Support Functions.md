# Shared Services Model Adopted for Cross-Clinic Support Functions

## Context

The district model treats each clinic as a semi-autonomous unit with three management pillars (Clinical Operations, Medical Director, Administrative). However, certain support functions — HR, legal, accounting, compliance, and potentially secretarial coordination — need to serve multiple districts without being duplicated in each one. The Feb 22 meeting discussed how to structure these cross-cutting functions.

## Options Considered

1. **Shared services at parent company level** — HR, legal, compliance, and back-office functions centralized under the parent company, serving all districts
2. **Fully localized support per district** — Each district hires its own HR, admin, and compliance staff independently
3. **Hybrid model** — Some functions shared (legal, accounting), others localized (HR, secretarial)

## Decision

The team adopted a shared services model where support functions (HR, legal, compliance, back-office) operate at the parent company level and serve all clinic districts. The exact boundary between shared and localized functions remains under discussion.

## Rationale

At current scale (one clinic), duplicating support functions is wasteful. Centralized services ensure consistency, reduce overhead, and provide a scalable template for international expansion. As districts grow, functions can be progressively localized based on demand.

## Consequences

The decision to adopt shared services is made, but the unresolved boundary question creates immediate friction — particularly around Renee's allocation (clinic-exclusive vs shared) and secretarial staff assignment. Each boundary decision becomes a precedent for how future districts will be supported. The boundary must be formally defined before the model can be operationalized.

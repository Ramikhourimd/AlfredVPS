# Survey Existing Clinical Systems Before Designing Next-Gen Prototype

## Context

Rami proposed a next-generation clinical system prototype initiative during the 2026-02-18 meeting. The question was whether to design from scratch based on internal needs or to first survey the market.

## Options Considered

1. **Design from internal needs only** — Build prototype based on staff feedback and known pain points
2. **Survey-first approach** — Map all existing clinical systems, combine best features, then add novel innovations
3. **Academic literature review** — Start from research on clinical system effectiveness

## Decision

Follow a survey-first approach: map all existing clinical systems and their capabilities, build a composite model combining the best features, then brainstorm innovative additions beyond current market offerings. Design the prototype assuming unlimited technical capability.

## Rationale

Understanding the competitive landscape prevents reinventing existing solutions and ensures the prototype leapfrogs rather than duplicates. Rivi leads the initial research; Ohad may contribute academic references.

## Consequences

The prototype timeline extends by the duration of the market survey phase. The resulting design will be more informed but may also be more constrained by existing paradigms. The unlimited-budget assumption enables ambitious thinking but requires later scoping for realistic implementation.

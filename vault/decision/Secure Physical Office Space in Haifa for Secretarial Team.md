## Decision

Secure a dedicated physical office space in Haifa for the [[project/Telia'z Clinic Israel]] administrative and secretarial team.

## Context

The administrative team (secretaries, and potentially the incoming clinic manager) currently works remotely or in distributed locations. This creates coordination challenges, makes supervision difficult, and limits team cohesion. The discussion concluded that a physical office in Haifa is necessary for operational efficiency.

## Requirements

- **Size**: Sufficient for the current administrative team plus near-term growth
- **Location**: Haifa, accessible by public transit
- **Parking**: Available parking for team members who drive
- **Accessibility**: Wheelchair accessible, ground floor or elevator
- **Amenities**: Kitchen area, at least one meeting room, professional appearance
- **Cost**: To be evaluated against co-location benefits

## Strategic Considerations

- The incoming new manager should have significant input on final office selection
- [[person/Dekkel Taliaz]] described available facilities in Haifa with relevant features
- Co-location of existing remote team members was debated: forcing vs offering flexibility
- Hybrid work model may need to be accommodated
- Office should serve both current needs and near-term (6-12 month) growth

## Co-location Strategy

- Secretary team benefits most from physical co-location (shared workflows, real-time coordination)
- Managers and clinical staff may continue hybrid or remote arrangements
- The office serves as a coordination hub, not a mandatory daily workplace for all

## Next Steps

- See [[task/Find Physical Office Space in Haifa for Administrative Team]]
- Get incoming manager input before finalizing location
- Evaluate cost options and lease terms
- Plan move-in timeline

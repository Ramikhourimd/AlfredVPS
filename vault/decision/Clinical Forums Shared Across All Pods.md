# Clinical Forums Shared Across All Pods

## Context

With the adoption of the pod model, a risk emerged: if clinical discussions (training, protocols, case consultations) are confined within pods, knowledge becomes siloed. Psychiatrists in one pod would not benefit from clinical insights in another.

## Options Considered

1. **Pod-contained clinical discussions** — Each pod handles its own clinical meetings, case reviews, and protocol discussions
2. **Shared clinical forums across pods** — Clinical matters are discussed in cross-pod forums while pods remain operational units only

## Decision

Pods are strictly operational units (scheduling, workload, communications). Clinical matters — training, protocols, case discussions, joint meetings between psychiatrists and case managers — are handled through shared forums that span all pods. This ensures clinical knowledge flows across the entire clinic.

## Rationale

Clinical knowledge should not be fragmented by operational boundaries. A case discussion relevant to one psychiatrist is likely relevant to others regardless of pod assignment. Shared forums also enable the Medical Director to maintain clinical oversight across the entire clinic rather than pod by pod.

## Consequences

- Pod leads handle operational matters only; clinical matters flow through the Medical Director and shared forums
- Dual participation required: practitioners attend both pod meetings (operational) and cross-pod clinical forums
- Prevents clinical quality divergence between pods
- Increases coordination overhead but protects clinical consistency

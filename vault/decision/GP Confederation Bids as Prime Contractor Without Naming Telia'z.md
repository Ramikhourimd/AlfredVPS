# GP Confederation Bids as Prime Contractor Without Naming Telia'z

## Context

The Leeds ADHD tender requires a formal bid from a recognized NHS provider entity. Telia'z is an Israeli company without UK healthcare provider credentials. GP Confederation is an established NHS entity with existing ICB relationships.

## Options Considered

1. **GP Confed bids solo, references technology partner unnamed** — Partnership details confirmed after contract award
2. **Joint bid naming Telia'z explicitly** — Full transparency but introduces foreign entity into NHS procurement
3. **Telia'z bids directly** — Not feasible without UK provider credentials

## Decision

GP Confederation submits the tender as prime contractor. The bid references AI/technology capabilities and psychiatry supervision without explicitly naming Telia'z. Partnership details confirmed after contract award.

## Rationale

Sean noted the bid must address face-to-face service preference, technology/AI capabilities, psychiatry supervision, and workforce scalability. GP Confed as named bidder leverages NHS credibility while keeping partnership details for post-award negotiation.

## Consequences

Telia'z has no direct contractual relationship with the ICB — they are a sub-provider to GP Confed. This creates dependency on GP Confed for all NHS revenue streams and limits direct influence on contract terms.

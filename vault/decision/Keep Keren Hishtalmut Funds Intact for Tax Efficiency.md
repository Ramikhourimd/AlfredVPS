# Keep Keren Hishtalmut Funds Intact for Tax Efficiency

## Context

During a financial planning discussion on 2025-09-23, Rami and his accountant reviewed options for the approximately 20,000 NIS in keren hishtalmut funds (~15,000 NIS accessible). The question was whether to withdraw, transfer to other instruments, or leave the funds in place.

## Options Considered

1. **Keep in keren hishtalmut** — Avoids 47% tax, preserves as tax-sheltered savings
2. **Open osek murshe and route funds through it** — Creates separate business entity but has complications if it's the sole income source
3. **Transfer to pensions/savings (kranot/amana)** — Possible but creates visibility issues if it's the sole income

## Decision

Keep money in the keren hishtalmut. Do not withdraw or transfer.

## Rationale

The 47% tax rate on withdrawal makes any extraction punitive. The accountant's strong recommendation was to preserve the funds in their current tax-advantaged vehicle. Opening an osek murshe was considered but dismissed due to complications when it would be the sole income source for the company.

## Consequences

- Funds remain locked but tax-sheltered
- No immediate liquidity gain from keren hishtalmut
- Avoids significant tax liability
- Future review needed if income structure changes

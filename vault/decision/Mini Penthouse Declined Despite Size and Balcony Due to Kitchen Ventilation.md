# Mini Penthouse Declined Despite Size and Balcony Due to Kitchen Ventilation

## Context

During property viewings on 2025-10-09, a mini penthouse (126 sqm + 37 sqm balcony, converted from 5 to 4 rooms) was evaluated. The property had several strong positives — generous size, large balcony rated as a "strong positive," and the owner was relocating (potentially favorable negotiation). However, the kitchen had no window; the only opening faced the neighbor's bathroom wall, blocking both light and airflow.

## Options Considered

1. **Accept the property** — The size, balcony, and mini penthouse configuration met most family requirements
2. **Decline due to kitchen issue** — The kitchen ventilation and light deficiency was identified as a fundamental flaw

## Decision

The property was effectively declined. The kitchen's lack of a window and its orientation facing a neighbor's bathroom wall made it unacceptable despite other strong attributes.

## Rationale

Kitchen ventilation and natural light have been established as dealbreaker criteria. This property demonstrated that even when multiple other criteria are met (adequate size, exceptional outdoor space, reasonable configuration), a kitchen ventilation failure alone is sufficient to disqualify. The family is unwilling to compromise on daily living quality in the kitchen — a space used multiple times daily.

## Consequences

- Confirms kitchen ventilation as a true hard filter, not merely a preference
- Establishes that even "strong positive" features (37 sqm balcony) cannot compensate for a hard-filter failure
- Narrows the search funnel further by validating the non-negotiable nature of this criterion

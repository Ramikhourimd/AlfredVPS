# Require Garage Certification Before Diesel 4x4 Purchase

## Context

During a pre-purchase inspection of a diesel 4x4 with 153,000 km on 2025-11-03, the professional inspector identified multiple engine concerns: excessive crankcase blowby, oil contamination across the entire engine and undercarriage, signs of hood removal and front bumper repair, and an unreadable engine number. The seller's handler offered an alternative explanation (loose dipstick cap) but could not account for all findings.

## Options Considered

1. **Proceed with purchase based on seller's explanation** — Accept the dipstick cap explanation and buy without further verification
2. **Send to professional garage for engine diagnosis** — Have the engine professionally evaluated before committing to purchase
3. **Walk away from the deal** — Decline based on inspection red flags

## Decision

Option 2 — The vehicle must be taken to a professional garage for engine diagnosis before any purchase decision is made. The inspector explicitly stated he cannot certify the engine and will not bear responsibility for its condition.

## Rationale

The inspector identified findings (blowby, oil everywhere, hood removal signs) that could indicate serious issues (engine replacement, front collision, worn internals). The seller's explanation is plausible but does not address all observations. A garage inspection shifts liability: if they certify it as sound, the garage takes responsibility.

## Consequences

- Purchase delayed pending garage availability and diagnosis
- If garage confirms engine is healthy, purchase can proceed with garage backing the certification
- If garage finds serious issues, the deal is off — preventing a costly mistake

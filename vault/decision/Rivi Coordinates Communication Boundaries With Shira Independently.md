# Rivi Coordinates Communication Boundaries With Shira Independently

## Context

Both Rivi and Shira interact with the same clinical staff but for different purposes — Rivi collects feedback for innovation and product improvement research, while Shira manages clinical operations. Shira raised concern about potential overlap and confusion for staff. Rami needed to establish clear boundaries without micromanaging the coordination.

## Options Considered

1. **Rami mediates directly** — Rami arranges a three-way meeting and defines boundaries himself
2. **Rivi handles independently** — Rivi coordinates with Shira directly, without Rami present

## Decision

Rami explicitly directed Rivi to handle the coordination with Shira independently — establishing clear delineation between innovation research conversations and operational management communications without Rami serving as intermediary.

## Rationale

This reinforces Rivi's professional autonomy and prevents Rami from becoming a bottleneck in team coordination. It also signals trust in Rivi's ability to manage stakeholder relationships, consistent with her role as a research lead rather than a subordinate requiring supervision.

## Consequences

- Rivi must proactively reach out to Shira and establish clear boundaries
- Staff should understand: Rivi collects feedback for innovation/product improvement; operational issues go to Shira/Renee
- If the coordination fails, Rami may need to intervene later

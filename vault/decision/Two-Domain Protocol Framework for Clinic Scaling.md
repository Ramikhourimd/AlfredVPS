# Two-Domain Protocol Framework for Clinic Scaling

## Context

As Telia'z Clinic Israel scales toward 7,000+ patients, the team needed a structured framework to organize all operational features, protocols, and KPIs. The existing Sunday review meetings focused only on surface metrics (new patients, marketing ROI, monthly revenue), missing the deeper operational indicators needed for sustainable growth.

## Options Considered

1. **Feature-first approach** — List all product features, then group into clinical/administrative categories
2. **Goal-first approach** — Define the desired end state ("what does being efficient look like in 6 months?") and work backward to required protocols and metrics

## Decision

Adopt a two-domain protocol framework with a goal-first approach:
- **Domain 1: Clinical Protocols** — Treatment response, remission rates, hospitalization reduction, ER visit reduction, cost reduction on public health system
- **Domain 2: Administrative Protocols** — Complaints handling, communication improvement, staff training/onboarding, patient satisfaction, staff retention, capacity optimization

Each domain feeds into a KPI hierarchy that connects clinical excellence to revenue growth through better HMO contract terms.

## Rationale

The goal-first approach ensures protocols are anchored in measurable outcomes rather than being an inventory of features. The two-domain split reflects the fundamental separation between clinical governance (under the Medical Director) and operational management (under Operations Manager), aligning with the district model already decided for the organizational restructuring.

## Consequences

- The protocol framework must be formalized into a document before it can be presented to leadership
- A persuasion deck is needed to shift leadership from surface metrics to the deeper KPI framework
- Both domains need owners: clinical protocols under the VP Medical, administrative under Operations Manager (Basel)

# Phase Out Makhirot Incentives After December 2025

## Context

The clinic has been providing "makhirot" (sales/referral-based incentives) to secretarial staff. Originally, these incentives were introduced when secretaries played an active role in patient acquisition (bringing in metupalim). The context has since changed — the secretarial role is now primarily administrative and patient-facing support, not sales-oriented.

## Options Considered

1. **Continue makhirot as-is** — misaligned with actual job responsibilities
2. **End makhirot immediately** — disruptive and demoralizing
3. **Phase out makhirot end of December 2025, replace with KPI-based bonus** — balanced transition

## Decision

Adopt Option 3: Makhirot end after December 2025. A replacement performance-based incentive structure will be designed, tied to actual job KPIs rather than sales metrics.

## Rationale

- The original rationale for makhirot no longer applies
- Abrupt removal would hurt morale; December provides a clear transition point
- Replacement incentive maintains motivation while aligning with actual responsibilities

## Consequences

- Staff must be informed clearly (Shira's responsibility) before end of December
- Basel and Rami need to design the replacement KPI-based bonus (separate task)
- Rana specifically needs a new incentive structure for her administrative manager role

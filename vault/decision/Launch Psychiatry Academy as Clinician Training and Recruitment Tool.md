# Launch Psychiatry Academy as Clinician Training and Recruitment Tool

## Context

Telia'z needed a mechanism to both upskill existing clinicians on tech-enabled psychiatry and create an attractive value proposition for recruiting new clinicians. Rami proposed a structured training program (Psychiatry Academy) serving this dual purpose.

## Options Considered

1. **Informal onboarding only** — Continue ad-hoc training for new clinicians without formal structure
2. **External training partnerships** — Partner with academic institutions for clinician training
3. **Internal Psychiatry Academy** — Build a proprietary training program that both upskills staff and serves as a recruitment differentiator

## Decision

Launch an internal Psychiatry Academy with first cohort enrollment closing by early March 2026 and training sessions starting mid-March. The program explicitly serves dual purposes: (1) recruitment value proposition positioning Telia'z as a place where clinicians learn cutting-edge tech-enabled practice, and (2) quality improvement through structured onboarding and upskilling.

## Rationale

The innovation team's marketing narrative is shifting toward innovation/technology positioning (per Adi Lavi). A structured academy provides concrete substance behind that marketing — aligning with the assumption that value must be delivered before external promotion. It also addresses the training gap identified in staff feedback.

## Consequences

- Program content must be built before external marketing (Rami's explicit caution)
- Creates ongoing commitment to maintain and update training materials
- Positions Telia'z competitively in clinician recruitment market
- Requires coordination between innovation team and marketing (Adi)

# Refinance for Total Cost Savings Over Monthly Reduction

## Context

During the RiseUp consultation on 2026-01-26, Rami was presented with two refinancing options for his Bank Hapoalim mortgage (current balance ~705,000 NIS). The monthly payment had risen from 3,400 to ~4,600 NIS, and the CPI-indexed track showed negative amortization.

## Options Considered

1. **Option A: Total Cost Savings** — Keep monthly payment at ~4,600 NIS, restructure tracks to save ~200,000 NIS over the loan's life
2. **Option B: Monthly Payment Reduction** — Reduce monthly payment by ~800-850 NIS, but increase total cost by ~100,000 NIS

## Decision

Rami chose Option A — maximize total cost savings (~200,000 NIS) rather than reduce monthly payments. The family can manage the current payment level, so immediate cash flow relief is unnecessary.

## Rationale

- The family can sustain the current ~4,600 NIS monthly payment without hardship
- 200,000 NIS in total savings is significantly more valuable than ~850 NIS/month relief
- The primary motivation is optimizing the mortgage, not easing monthly cash flow

## Consequences

- Monthly payments remain at approximately current levels (~4,600 NIS)
- Significant long-term savings (~200,000 NIS) through track restructuring
- CPI-indexed exposure to be eliminated, stopping negative amortization

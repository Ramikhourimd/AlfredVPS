# Clinic District Model With Three Parallel Management Tracks

## Context

Basel and Leah presented a proposed organizational structure for the clinic. The group discussed whether management should be hierarchical (one person above all) or split into parallel tracks with domain expertise.

## Decision

Adopt a three-pillar parallel management structure for the clinic:
- **Clinical Operations Manager** (currently Shira): Day-to-day operations, KPIs, scheduling, case manager coordination
- **Medical Director** (30% position, to be hired): Clinical protocols, physician supervision, medical consultations
- **Administrative Manager** (currently Renee): HR, secretarial staff, onboarding, procurement

These three roles are peers, not hierarchical. Coordination happens through regular meetings and shared reporting to Basel (Operations Manager).

## Rationale

Separating clinical, operational, and administrative responsibilities prevents bottlenecks, ensures appropriate domain expertise in each area, and scales better as the clinic grows. The pod model further supports this by creating manageable team sizes within each track.

## Consequences

- Need to hire a Medical Director (30% physician position)
- Must establish regular coordination meetings between the three managers
- Basel serves as the integration point, not a fourth manager above them
- Pod structure supports scaling within each track

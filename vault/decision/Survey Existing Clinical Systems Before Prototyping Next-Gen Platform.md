# Survey Existing Clinical Systems Before Prototyping Next-Gen Platform

## Context

Rami proposed a team-wide initiative to envision the most advanced possible clinical management system. Rather than designing from scratch based on internal assumptions, the approach starts with comprehensive market analysis.

## Options Considered

1. **Internal brainstorm first** — Design based on team's clinical experience and known pain points
2. **Market survey first** — Map all existing clinical systems, build composite model, then innovate beyond
3. **Parallel tracks** — Survey and brainstorm simultaneously

## Decision

Option 2: Sequential approach starting with exhaustive market survey, building composite model of best features, then brainstorming innovations beyond current market offerings, and finally creating a full-featured prototype concept assuming unlimited technical capability.

## Rationale

Starting with a market survey prevents reinventing features that already exist elsewhere. The composite model creates a baseline of "table stakes" functionality, and innovation effort is focused on genuinely novel additions. The unlimited-budget framing encourages ambitious thinking without premature feasibility filtering.

## Consequences

- Research phase required before any design work begins
- Rivi leads initial market research; Ohad may contribute academic/literature references
- Output serves as basis for annual development roadmap
- Risk: research phase could take significant time before actionable prototype emerges

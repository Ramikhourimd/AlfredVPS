# Target Patient Discharge Rates of 10-30-30-30 Percent

## Context

Clinic Israel faces a severe capacity crisis: 945 new patients registered in February but only 77 (8%) received intake appointments. The clinic must increase throughput by actively managing patient duration.

## Options Considered

1. **No active discharge — rely on natural churn (~15%)** — Insufficient given demand
2. **Active discharge framework with graduated targets** — 10% post-intake, 30% per follow-up stage
3. **Cap new patient intake** — Unacceptable to business

## Decision

Rami proposed and the team tentatively agreed to target: 10% discharge after intake, 30% after 1st follow-up, 30% after 2nd follow-up, 30% after 3rd follow-up. This is still draft — requires literature review, data validation, and business alignment before finalization.

## Rationale

The targets aim to shift the clinic from a retention-heavy model to an intake-heavy model, reducing average treatment duration to 4-6 months. This enables the clinic to serve the growing patient pipeline without proportional capacity expansion.

## Consequences

- Requires literature review and data analysis before implementation
- May need changes to questionnaire for early discharge screening
- Psychiatrist decision support tools needed
- Must validate financial impact with Dekkel and Basel
- Risk: discharging too early could harm patient outcomes

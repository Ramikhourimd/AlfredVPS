# Basel Assumes Operational Processes Previously Run by Rami

## Context

Upon joining Telia'z as Operations Manager in late 2025, Basel immediately began taking ownership of operational processes and meetings that Rami had been running in addition to his clinical duties. This transition was organic rather than formally planned — Basel identified disorder and acted on it from day one.

## Options Considered

1. **Gradual handoff** — Rami continues running operations while Basel shadows and learns
2. **Immediate assumption** — Basel takes direct ownership of operational meetings and processes

## Decision

Basel immediately assumed ownership of meetings Rami previously ran, began clarifying role divisions between Rana and Shira, and started working on agreements, protocols, and organizational structure. Both Rami and Oren viewed this positively as evidence of Basel's capability and initiative.

## Rationale

Rami's four-role burden was unsustainable, and operational tasks were consuming time needed for clinical and innovation work. Basel's willingness to take ownership immediately — rather than waiting for formal authority — demonstrated exactly the initiative the organization needed. This also validated the decision to hire Basel despite being a recent addition.

## Consequences

- Rami begins offloading operational burden, supporting the eventual role consolidation
- Basel gains operational credibility quickly, reinforcing his position in the restructuring
- Creates precedent for Basel's authority that informs the Feb 2026 district model discussions
- Risk of information gaps: Rami reports hearing about changes secondhand (e.g., "four secretary hires" rumor)

# PA Hiring Should Begin With Trial Period

## Context

Rami and a close family advisor discussed how to approach hiring Amani Rohana as a personal organizational assistant. Rather than committing to a full employment arrangement immediately, the advisor recommended a staged approach to validate the working relationship.

## Options Considered

1. **Immediate full hire** — Bring Amani on board directly with a defined contract and salary
2. **Trial period first** — Start with a 1-2 week trial to test the working arrangement before full commitment

## Decision

The advisor recommends — and Rami accepts — a trial period approach: "Take a week or two to try it out" before formalizing the PA role. This allows both parties to validate expectations, working style compatibility, and role scope before committing to ongoing employment.

## Rationale

The advisor emphasizes that this must be a genuine working arrangement, not a performative setup. A trial period allows Rami to present clear expectations and Amani to evaluate whether the role suits her, reducing risk for both sides. It also gives Rami time to finalize his own role split definition — a prerequisite for clearly scoping the PA role.

## Consequences

- Lower commitment risk for both Rami and Amani
- Forces Rami to define concrete deliverables and weekly structure before the trial
- Creates a natural checkpoint before financial commitment (13,000 NIS bruto/month)
- May delay full onboarding if trial reveals misalignment

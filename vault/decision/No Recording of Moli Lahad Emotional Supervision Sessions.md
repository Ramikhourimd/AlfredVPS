# No Recording of Moli Lahad Emotional Supervision Sessions

## Context

Biweekly clinical supervision/training sessions were launched with Prof. Moli Lahad for case managers. The team debated whether to record sessions for summarization and analysis.

## Options Considered

1. **Record and generate full summary** — Transcribe sessions, keep full record
2. **Record with anonymized summary pipeline** — Transcribe, auto-generate anonymous thematic summary, delete raw transcription (Rami's proposal)
3. **No recording, rely on personal notes** — Preserve emotional safety; participants take their own notes (Shira's position)

## Decision

Tentatively leaning toward no recording, pending confirmation with Moli Lahad and case managers. The emotional safety and group trust established by a no-recording environment was considered the primary value. A task was created to formally check consent with all parties.

## Rationale

- Recording was not in Moli Lahad's original contract
- Emotional safety requires participants to know nothing is being recorded
- The experiential value of presence cannot be captured in a summary
- Shira argued that participants who want to retain practical tips should take personal notes
- The commitment to attend (treating sessions as mandatory, not optional) is itself part of the therapeutic framework

## Consequences

- No automated analysis of recurring team issues from supervision sessions (limits Rami's ability to identify systemic patterns)
- Practical tools and techniques must be recalled by participants individually
- If recording is eventually approved, the anonymized pipeline concept is ready to implement

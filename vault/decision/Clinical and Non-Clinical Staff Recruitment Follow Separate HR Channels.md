# Clinical and Non-Clinical Staff Recruitment Follow Separate HR Channels

## Context

With Renee taking on clinical staff management responsibilities and Li Yamin handling company-level HR, a clear division was needed for how new hires are processed to prevent confusion and duplication.

## Options Considered

1. **All hiring through Li Yamin** — Single HR channel for all staff, regardless of type
2. **Split by clinical vs non-clinical** — Renee manages clinical staff recruitment, Li Yamin handles company-level/management hires
3. **All hiring through Renee** — Clinic-centric HR function

## Decision

Clinical staff hiring (psychiatrists, case managers, secretaries) is managed by Renee — she defines the role, then Li Yamin routes it to the lawyer for contracts. For non-clinic roles (e.g., general manager level), Rami coordinates directly with Li Yamin, bypassing Renee entirely.

## Rationale

Renee has direct operational knowledge of clinical staffing needs. Li Yamin maintains the legal/payroll infrastructure. The split prevents either from becoming a bottleneck for the other's domain.

## Consequences

- Creates two parallel hiring pipelines that must stay coordinated
- Non-clinical hires bypass clinic-level oversight entirely
- As Basel takes over operations, this channel division may need renegotiation
- Li Yamin's "intermediary" role (between agreement and payroll) remains essential regardless of who initiates hiring

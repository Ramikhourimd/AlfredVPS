# Innovation Output Routing Split by Domain to Yael Shira and Basel

## Context

The Innovation Program produces outputs spanning multiple organizational domains — product features, operational improvements, and cross-functional initiatives. Without clear routing, innovation outputs risk landing on Rami's desk regardless of domain, creating a bottleneck.

## Options Considered

1. **All outputs route through Rami** — Simple but creates bottleneck at Innovation Lead
2. **Domain-based routing to responsible owners** — Distributes execution to people with domain authority
3. **Route everything to Basel as Operations Manager** — Overloads a single new hire

## Decision

Innovation outputs are routed by domain:
- **Product features** → Yael Marciano (Product Manager)
- **Operational issues** → Shira (Clinical Operations Manager)
- **Cross-functional items** → Basel (Operations Manager)

## Rationale

Each routing target has the domain authority and operational context to act on the output. This prevents Rami from becoming a bottleneck between innovation research and execution, while ensuring outputs reach people who can implement them.

## Consequences

- Rivi (concept designer) must classify her outputs by domain before handoff
- Three different people may need to coordinate if an initiative spans multiple domains
- Basel's cross-functional routing may overlap with Shira's operational routing for ambiguous items

# Li Yamin Given 1-3 Month Patience Window Before Role Reevaluation

## Context

Li Yamin has a documented pattern of task deflection and boundary enforcement that blocks administrative processes — most recently refusing to hand employment agreements to Basel Kanaaneh (new Operations Manager). This pattern repeated across interactions with Shachar (6 months prior), Rami, and now Basel. Both Rami and Oren agree the issue is structural (role misfit), not a coaching problem.

## Options Considered

1. **Immediate role change or termination** — Remove Li from the role given the repeated pattern
2. **Patience window with clear expectations** — Give Li 1-3 months to cooperate under explicit guidance, then reevaluate
3. **Role redesign** — Redirect Li toward product design work (her preference) while hiring a dedicated HR person

## Decision

Oren chose Option 2: He spoke with Li on the afternoon of 2025-12-02, asking her to be patient for 1-3 months, cooperate with Basel, and transfer all documents properly. He framed this as essential for Basel's success in centralizing operations. As an interim measure, external service provider communication was rerouted through Tal.

## Rationale

Despite the pattern, both founders (Oren, Dekkel) are generally satisfied with Li's other work. An abrupt change could disrupt ongoing processes. The patience window gives Li a defined opportunity to adapt while Basel establishes his operations role. If the pattern persists after the window, a structural change becomes justified with documented evidence.

## Consequences

- Basel must work around Li's resistance for 1-3 months
- External communication rerouted through Tal (already implemented)
- If Li does not improve, the next step is role change or replacement — with documentation to support the decision
- Timeline: Reevaluation expected by March 2026

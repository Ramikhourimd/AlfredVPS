# Clalit and Leumit Targeted as 2026 HMO Expansion Partners

## Context

During the December 2025 leadership planning session, Alex, Oren, and Dekkel set 2026 goals with an overwhelming financial growth focus. Among these goals was the explicit targeting of new HMO (kupot cholim) partnerships to drive patient volume expansion. Rami reports these targets were handed down roughly a week prior without meaningful clinical input.

## Options Considered

1. **Expand to Clalit and Leumit** — Target Israel's two largest HMOs beyond current partnerships, significantly increasing patient volume and revenue potential
2. **Consolidate existing HMO relationships** — Deepen service quality and operational readiness within current partnerships before expanding to new ones

## Decision

Leadership chose to target Clalit and possibly Leumit as 2026 HMO expansion partners, as part of an aggressive patient acquisition and revenue growth strategy.

## Rationale

Financial growth targets for 2026 require patient volume increases beyond what existing HMO partnerships can deliver. Clalit is Israel's largest HMO, and Leumit offers additional volume. The departure of a Clalit South psychiatrist (leaving ~350 patients without a provider) may present an immediate entry opportunity.

## Consequences

This decision intensifies growth pressure on the clinic before operational stabilization is complete. Rami has argued that rapid expansion without protocols, quality checks, and staff training increases legal exposure and degrades service quality. Successful execution requires simultaneous scaling of secretarial capacity, clinician hiring, and operational processes — precisely the areas Rami identifies as under-resourced.

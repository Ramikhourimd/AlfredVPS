# Rivi Role Defined as Researcher and Concept Designer Not Executor

## Context

Rivi Idelman had been drifting into execution-level work (detailed specifications, building deliverables, managing production) alongside her research and concept design responsibilities. Rami observed she was "overwhelmed" and not clear on how to package her findings for downstream teams.

## Options Considered

1. **Researcher/Concept Designer** — Rivi focuses on problem definition, structured research, concept documents, and recommendations. Handoff to execution teams (Ohad/Stav/Yael) for implementation.
2. **Full-cycle owner** — Rivi would own both research AND execution for each initiative. Rejected due to scope overload and role confusion.

## Decision

Rivi's role is explicitly defined as **researcher and concept designer**. She produces structured research outputs (academic methodology format: background, methodology, results, discussion, recommendations) and concept documents. She does NOT execute or build solutions.

## Rationale

- Separation of research from execution prevents scope creep and burnout
- Academic research methodology ensures findings are measurable, trackable, and actionable
- Clear handoff points prevent confusion about responsibility
- Rivi's natural strengths align with research and analysis, not production management
- Rivi can optionally include a "recommendations" section that goes beyond her core scope, clearly labeled

## Consequences

- All of Rivi's outputs should follow research report structure
- Handoff routing: system features → Yael Marciano; innovation concepts → Stav Zamir/Ohad Edri; operational gaps → Shira/Renee (NOT through Rami)
- Staff should contact Rivi only for improvement ideas, not operational complaints
- Rami will provide a methodology template and worked example to anchor Rivi's output format

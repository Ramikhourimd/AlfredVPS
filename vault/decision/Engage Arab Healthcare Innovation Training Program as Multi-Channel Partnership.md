# Engage Arab Healthcare Innovation Training Program as Multi-Channel Partnership

## Context

During a networking conversation on 2025-09-01, Rami Khouri met organizers of a medical innovation training program targeting Arab healthcare professionals in northern Israel. The program had completed 3 cohorts of 10-15 participants each, with a 4th starting, covering AI integration in medicine across 7 sessions over 3 months.

## Options Considered

1. **Single touchpoint** — Guest speak once and maintain loose contact
2. **Multi-channel engagement** — Guest speaking, sending staff, reviewing syllabus, exploring course collaboration
3. **No engagement** — Focus on internal training only (Psychiatry Academy)

## Decision

Pursue multi-channel engagement with the training program, including: guest speaking at a session, enrolling Telia'z staff in cohort 4, reviewing the program syllabus for alignment opportunities, and exploring a deeper course collaboration with Zaher Elias.

## Rationale

The program's established track record (3 successful cohorts, competitive selection, partnerships with SET and a startup incubator) and its focus on AI in medicine align directly with Telia'z innovation positioning. Multi-channel engagement maximizes the relationship value — staff development, recruitment pipeline, knowledge exchange, and positioning Telia'z as an innovation leader in the Arab healthcare professional community.

## Consequences

- Resource commitment: Rami's time for guest speaking, staff time for cohort participation, coordination effort for syllabus review and collaboration exploration
- Potential returns: recruitment pipeline, staff upskilling, brand positioning, possible joint curriculum development
- Risk: program may not maintain quality or relevance as it scales

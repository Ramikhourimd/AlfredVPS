# Top-Down Gated Concept Design Framework for Innovation

## Context

Rivi Edelman's innovation role needed clearer boundaries to prevent scope creep and role confusion. Her natural tendency to take on execution alongside concept work was creating unsustainable workload and unclear ownership. Additionally, Dr. Khouri needed to prepare for reduced availability post-paternity leave, requiring Rivi to operate more autonomously.

## Options Considered

1. **Flat autonomous model** — Rivi owns end-to-end from concept through execution. Risk: scope creep, unclear handoff points, execution outside expertise.
2. **Top-down gated model** — Rivi owns concept design (problem definition, research, solution options); Dr. Khouri gates approval before execution handoff. Execution routes to appropriate owners (Yael for product, Shira for operations, Basel for cross-functional).
3. **Purely advisory model** — Rivi only provides input on request. Risk: underutilizes her capabilities and initiative.

## Decision

Adopt the **top-down gated concept design model** with a WIP limit of 3 concurrent projects.

## Rationale

- Clear separation of concept/research phase from implementation prevents scope creep
- Gating mechanism ensures quality control and strategic alignment
- Defined routing paths (Yael, Shira, Basel) ensure execution lands with the right owner
- WIP limit prevents overcommitment within 0.5 FTE constraint
- Enables Rivi's autonomous operation during Dr. Khouri's reduced availability

## Consequences

- Rivi can work independently within the concept design boundary
- All execution requires explicit Dr. Khouri approval before proceeding
- Communication shifts to Notion/Slack for structured tracking
- Weekly review cadence (Sunday 16:00-18:00) provides regular checkpoint

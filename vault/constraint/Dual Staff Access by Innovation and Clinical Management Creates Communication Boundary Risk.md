# Dual Staff Access by Innovation and Clinical Management Creates Communication Boundary Risk

## Constraint

Both the Innovation Team Lead (Rivi Idelman) and Clinical Management (Shira) interact with the same clinical staff pool for different purposes. Rivi collects qualitative feedback for innovation research and product improvement. Shira handles operational clinical management communications. Without explicit delineation, staff may conflate the two channels, creating role confusion and potentially biasing research data with operational complaints.

## Source

Shira raised the concern about communication overlap during the 2026-02-18 planning period. Multiple session notes from that date reference the need for boundary coordination between innovation research conversations and clinical management communications.

## Implications

- Staff may route operational complaints through Rivi's innovation research channel, contaminating qualitative data
- Clinical staff may feel over-interviewed if both functions approach them without coordination
- Innovation research findings may be biased by staff conflating research conversations with management feedback opportunities
- Requires active coordination between Rivi and Shira to maintain clear separation of purpose

## Expiry / Review

Active as long as both roles interact with the same staff population. Should be reviewed if either role changes scope or if a formal delineation protocol is established and confirmed effective.

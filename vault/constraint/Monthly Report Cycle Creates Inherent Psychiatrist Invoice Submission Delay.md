# Monthly Report Cycle Creates Inherent Psychiatrist Invoice Submission Delay

## Constraint

Monthly session reports only become available at month-end. Psychiatrists must then review the reports, add unreported hours, and submit for Rami's approval before invoices can be generated. This sequential process creates an inherent multi-day delay between month-end and invoice submission, which cascades into delayed payments when psychiatrists miss the approval window.

## Source

Raised by Adiel Levin and discussed during the 2025-09-01 compensation call. Oren Taliaz asked Rami to push psychiatrists on timely submission. Rami explained the structural limitation: the review-add-approve cycle takes time, and the data simply isn't available until month-end.

## Implications

The delay is structural, not behavioral — it cannot be fully eliminated by pressuring psychiatrists. Tightening the cycle requires either: (1) making reports available earlier (system change), (2) reducing the review window (risk of errors), or (3) allowing provisional submissions with corrections (process change). Delayed invoices cause payments to be deferred to the next approval round, creating cash flow impact on individual psychiatrists.

## Expiry / Review

Active until the reporting and invoice submission process is automated or restructured. Connected to broader payroll data fragmentation issues.

# Early Repayment Balance of 705000 NIS Sets Refinancing Baseline

## Constraint

The current mortgage early repayment balance is approximately 705,000 NIS (down from the original ~790,000 NIS taken in May 2020). This figure establishes the minimum principal that must be addressed in any refinancing restructuring. All savings projections, track restructuring options, and monthly payment calculations are anchored to this baseline figure.

## Source

Bank Hapoalim mortgage statement, referenced in the RiseUp consultation on 2026-01-26. Both consultation notes cite this figure consistently.

## Implications

- Any refinancing proposal must restructure at least 705,000 NIS across the new track structure
- The ~85,000 NIS reduction from original principal (790,000 to 705,000) over 5.5 years reflects a low effective amortization rate, partly due to negative amortization on the CPI-indexed track
- Early repayment penalties or fees may apply to specific tracks, potentially reducing the net amount available for restructuring
- This figure will change over time — the longer the refinancing process takes, the more this baseline shifts

## Expiry / Review

This figure is a point-in-time snapshot from January 2026. It should be re-verified with Bank Hapoalim when the actual refinancing application is submitted, as ongoing payments and CPI adjustments will have altered the balance.

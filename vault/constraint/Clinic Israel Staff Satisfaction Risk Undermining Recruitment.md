# Clinic Israel Staff Satisfaction Risk Undermining Recruitment

## Constraint

Multiple reports (gathered by Rivi Idelman and surfaced by Adi Lavi) indicate that some current clinic staff — including case managers — would not recommend working at Telia'z to others. When prospective employees see job postings and ask existing staff, they are being told not to join. This creates a direct counter-effect to recruitment campaigns.

## Source

Internal feedback collected by Rivi Idelman. Raised in team meeting 2026-02-15. Scope and severity not yet fully assessed — may be isolated or more widespread.

## Implications

- Recruitment campaigns may be undermined by negative word-of-mouth from existing staff
- External marketing of Telia'z as an innovative workplace rings hollow if internal experience doesn't match
- Rami argues stabilization (reducing staff burnout, improving processes, hiring support) must precede aggressive growth messaging
- Internal marketing plan needed to improve clinician engagement and sense of belonging
- Shira serves as primary relationship manager but may be stretched

## Expiry / Review

Active until internal satisfaction survey results show improvement. Should be monitored monthly via Rivi's engagement data.

# Clinical Practice 18-20K Target Assumes Full Weekly Availability

## Constraint

The projected 18,000–20,000 NIS/month clinical practice income requires Rami to work the full standard schedule of 8 hours per week across all weeks of the month, with no leave days and no holidays reducing availability. Any week missed directly reduces monthly income.

## Source

During the 2025-09-23 financial planning discussion, the projected income was described alongside the qualifier: "Standard schedule: 8 hours per week when available (no leave days, no holidays)." The current observed income of ~8,000 NIS for 21 hours in one month (vs. the ~32 hours possible at full utilization) demonstrates the gap between actual and maximum income.

## Implications

- The 18-20K figure is a theoretical ceiling, not a reliable monthly floor for financial planning
- Jewish/Israeli holidays, annual leave, sick days, and conference attendance all reduce available weeks
- Realistic annual average may be 15-25% below the maximum depending on leave patterns
- Financial planning (loan capacity, investment commitments) should use a conservative figure (e.g., 14-16K NIS/month) rather than the 18-20K ceiling
- This constraint compounds with the observation that payment does not scale linearly with hours worked within a given period

## Expiry / Review

This constraint persists as long as Rami's clinical practice operates under the hospital unit bituh allocation model. Should be reassessed if the compensation structure changes or if a formal hourly rate is established.

# Previous Land Sale Attempts Failed Due to Broker Trust Issues

## Constraint

Past attempts to sell the Maghar land plot through intermediaries failed due to trust breakdowns. A family member named Wejdi previously brokered a sale attempt, bringing interested Jewish buyers (including a contact named Yitzhak Darcot). The deal collapsed because of suspicion that Wejdi was taking undisclosed commissions, creating a communication and trust breakdown.

## Source

Family gathering discussion on 2025-09-22. Family members recounted the failed deal attempt as context for why the land remains unsold despite buyer interest.

## Implications

- Future sale attempts will likely face family skepticism toward intermediaries and brokers
- Direct buyer relationships or transparent commission structures may be required
- The trust issue adds friction to the "sell the land" option in the development strategy evaluation
- Family may prefer dealing through known, trusted channels rather than intermediaries
- This history reinforces the pattern of the family relying on social networks rather than formal channels (consistent with broader housing search behavior)

## Expiry / Review

This constraint persists as a behavioral pattern. Review if a trusted intermediary is identified or if the family decides to use formal real estate channels with transparent fee structures.

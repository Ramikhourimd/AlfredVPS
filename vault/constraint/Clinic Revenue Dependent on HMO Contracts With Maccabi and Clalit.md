# Clinic Revenue Dependent on HMO Contracts With Maccabi and Clalit

## Constraint

The Telia'z Clinic Israel's patient pipeline and revenue are tied to contracts with major Israeli HMOs — specifically Maccabi and Clalit. These contracts define the volume of referrals, reimbursement rates, and service requirements that shape the clinic's scaling trajectory.

## Source

Telia'z Clinic Israel project description: "contracts with major HMOs (Maccabi, Clalit)."

## Implications

- Scaling targets (7,000+ patients) depend on maintaining and expanding HMO contracts
- Financial viability of the restructured organization depends on contract terms
- Secretary and pod capacity planning must align with HMO referral volumes
- Any changes to clinical workflows or AI integration must comply with HMO service agreements
- International expansion cannot rely on the same HMO contracts — new payer relationships required

## Expiry / Review

Active and ongoing. Review when HMO contracts come up for renewal or when new payer relationships are established.

# Affordable Purchase Properties Require Renovation Capital Beyond Purchase Price

## Constraint

Properties in the sub-1M NIS range that the family can potentially afford are not move-in ready. They require significant renovation investment — electrical work, finishing, and layout improvements — meaning the true acquisition cost substantially exceeds the listed price.

## Source

During the Property Investment Discussion (2025-10-09), a property at ~850K NIS was evaluated. The family noted it needs "electrical work (cables mentioned), and other improvements" with "additional money needed for renovations." This mirrors the family building viewing from the same day, where unfinished units needed up to 6 months of work.

## Implications

The family faces a consistent pattern: affordable properties (whether rental units in a family building or purchase candidates around 850K) require capital investment and time before occupancy. This creates a hidden cost layer that must be factored into any purchase decision. It also means the family cannot escape their current unsatisfactory living situation immediately via the purchase path — a renovation timeline of months is likely.

## Expiry / Review

This constraint applies to the current market segment being evaluated. Should the family's budget increase (e.g., through Maghar land sale proceeds), market-ready properties may become accessible, making this constraint less relevant.

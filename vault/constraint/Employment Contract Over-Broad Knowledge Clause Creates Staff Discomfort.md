# Employment Contract Over-Broad Knowledge Clause Creates Staff Discomfort

## Constraint

The employment contract contains a clause requiring staff to acknowledge familiarity with all relevant state laws, Maccabi/Clalit regulations, and policy changes. Rivi flagged this as unreasonable — no individual can realistically claim knowledge of all applicable regulations.

## Source

Rivi raised this during the 2026-03-17 innovation meeting. Rami explained the practical intent: the company commits to informing staff of relevant regulations; staff commit to following them. He invited Rivi to propose alternative wording.

## Implications

- May create signing hesitation among new or prospective clinical staff
- Legal intent and practical reading diverge — could cause trust issues
- If other staff share this concern, it could affect recruitment and onboarding
- Resolution path exists: Rivi to propose revised wording

## Expiry / Review

Active until contract language is revised. Rivi has been invited to propose alternative wording.

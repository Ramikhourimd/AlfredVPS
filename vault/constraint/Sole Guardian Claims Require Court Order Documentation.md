# Sole Guardian Claims Require Court Order Documentation

## Constraint

When a parent claims sole guardianship of a child patient, the clinic **cannot** proceed with treatment based on single-parent consent until a court order confirming sole guardianship is provided. This is a non-negotiable requirement to protect the clinic from legal liability.

## Source

Established by Rami Khouri during the 2025-11-10 operations meeting as a mandatory policy for the children clinic. Applies to all intake staff handling child patients.

## Implications

- Intake process must include a guardianship verification step for child patients
- Treatment may be delayed for patients whose parents claim sole guardianship without documentation
- Staff must be trained to request court orders tactfully
- Standard shared custody situations are not affected — both parents can consent normally
- Only sole guardianship claims trigger the court order requirement

## Expiry / Review

Permanent policy. Review if Israeli family law changes regarding guardianship documentation requirements.

# Genealogy Research Limited to Oral Tradition

## Constraint

The Khouri Family Genealogy project currently relies entirely on oral history from Majdi Shaddad as its primary evidence base. No primary historical documents (church records, Ottoman registers, land deeds, census data) have been consulted or cited. All dates, migration routes, and family connections are from oral tradition passed through multiple generations.

## Source

This is an inherent methodological constraint. The note "Christian Migration From Hauran to Galilee and Khouri Family Origins" explicitly states the information is "not verified against primary historical sources." The genealogical claims span approximately 300 years (1725–present), well beyond living memory.

## Implications

- All findings carry inherent uncertainty — dates are approximate, place names may be imprecise, and connections between branches are unverified
- The project cannot currently distinguish between established fact and family legend
- Key claims (Hauran origin, 1725 date, three-branch structure) could be strengthened or challenged by documentary evidence
- Potential primary sources to pursue: Rameh church records, Ottoman millet registers, British Mandate census data, Zahleh/Hauran church archives

## Expiry / Review

This constraint remains active until primary documentary sources are located and consulted. Review when: (1) church records from Rameh become accessible, (2) Ottoman-era records for the Galilee region are consulted, or (3) other family members provide independent documentary evidence.

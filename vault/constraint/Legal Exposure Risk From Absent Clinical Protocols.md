# Legal Exposure Risk From Absent Clinical Protocols

## Constraint

The clinic currently lacks formalized clinical protocols, quality checks, and operational procedures at a scale commensurate with its complexity (~60 KPIs identified as needed). Without these, the organization faces material legal exposure — a single malpractice lawsuit could cost millions and threaten the business. Expanding patient volumes before protocols are in place multiplies this risk proportionally.

## Source

Rami raised this in his Dec 5 debrief, framing it as one of the strongest arguments against premature growth. He characterizes the clinic as "basically a hospital" in terms of operational complexity, but without hospital-grade protocols. This constraint is grounded in Israeli medical regulation and general malpractice liability.

## Implications

- Growth without protocols increases legal exposure linearly with patient volume
- Rapid hiring leads to lower-quality staff on small contracts with no institutional commitment — a documented pattern in healthcare literature
- A single adverse event without documented protocols could result in catastrophic financial and reputational damage
- Protocol development requires dedicated time and resources that compete with growth initiatives

## Expiry / Review

This constraint is persistent until clinical protocols are formally developed, documented, and implemented. Should be reviewed when the Clinical Operations Manager (Shira) role is fully operational and protocol development begins.

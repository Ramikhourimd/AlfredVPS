# ICB Commissioners Prefer Face-to-Face Services Over Virtual

## Constraint
ICB commissioners in Leeds will heavily weight procurement bids that offer face-to-face clinical services over purely virtual models. This is a stated preference from decision-makers, not a regulatory requirement.

## Source
Sean Rejabris stated during the meeting: "Something we know from the commissioners is they're going to heavily weigh preference to providers who are offering a face-to-face service over virtual."

## Implications
- The joint service bid must include a face-to-face component, even if the scalable backbone is virtual
- GP Confed can provide the local face-to-face presence (GPs, nurses) while Telia'z provides virtual psychiatry
- The model needs hybrid capability — face-to-face for initial/complex cases, virtual for scale
- Contradicts the 25-year waiting list reality where virtual is the only scalable solution

## Expiry / Review
May evolve as NHS England increasingly recognizes virtual service necessity. Dr. Lucy Clemens sits on the national ADHD task force, which is pushing toward primary care integration.

# Clinic Cannot Absorb New HMO Agreements Without Operational Stabilization

## Constraint

The Telia'z Clinic Israel cannot onboard additional HMO agreements (e.g., Leumit, expanded Clalit) until the current operation is stabilized. Stabilization requires: recruiting quality psychiatrists with strategic hiring (not rushed, small-contract hires), training existing staff, establishing operational protocols, building tracking for ~60 KPIs, and addressing secretarial capacity shortages.

## Source

Rami's professional assessment as CMO based on operational visibility. He estimates the clinic is "already basically a hospital" in complexity but lacks the management infrastructure to match.

## Implications

- Business development may sign agreements leadership cannot operationally fulfill
- Rapid hiring to meet volume targets leads to lower-quality staff on small contracts with no commitment — well-documented pattern in healthcare literature
- Without stabilization, legal exposure increases (no protocols, unchecked summaries, unmonitored quality)
- Marketing generating demand the clinic cannot serve is worse than no marketing

## Expiry / Review

Should be reviewed after Q1 2026 when stabilization progress can be assessed. If key hires (medical director, additional secretaries) are made and protocols are established, constraint may be relaxed.

# 47 Percent Tax on Keren Hishtalmut Early Withdrawal

## Constraint

Withdrawing funds from keren hishtalmut (education fund) or converting them to cash triggers a 47% income tax rate. This applies to any form of early withdrawal or conversion.

## Source

Israeli income tax regulations governing keren hishtalmut withdrawals. Confirmed by accountant during the 2025-09-23 financial planning session.

## Implications

- Makes withdrawal of keren hishtalmut funds economically unviable in most scenarios
- Strongly favors keeping funds in the keren hishtalmut until the tax-free maturity period
- Any financial planning involving these funds must account for this penalty
- Effectively locks ~20,000 NIS (~15,000 accessible) as long-term savings

## Expiry / Review

This is a standing tax regulation. Review if Israeli tax law changes regarding keren hishtalmut withdrawal rules. The rate may change with future tax reforms.

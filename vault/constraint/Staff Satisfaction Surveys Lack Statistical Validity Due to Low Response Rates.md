# Staff Satisfaction Surveys Lack Statistical Validity Due to Low Response Rates

## Constraint

The clinic's staff satisfaction survey program has response rates too low to produce statistically meaningful individual scores. As of the 2025-12-04 review: only approximately 16 surveys had been sent since program launch, and individual clinician scores are based on as few as 2 responses out of ~200 patients (e.g., Elinor's "perfect" 10/10 score from 2 respondents). At these volumes, individual scores cannot reliably distinguish between genuine satisfaction differences and random noise.

## Source

Rami Khouri reviewed the survey data during the 2025-12-04 office session and flagged the low response rates as undermining the usefulness of the satisfaction metric. The data showed wide variation in sample sizes across clinicians, making comparative scoring unreliable.

## Implications

- Individual clinician satisfaction scores cannot be used for performance evaluation or HR decisions until response volumes increase significantly
- The survey distribution mechanism itself may be broken — 16 total surveys across months of operation suggests a systemic delivery problem, not just patient apathy
- Any KPI framework that includes patient satisfaction as a metric is currently operating on unreliable data
- Before investing in score analysis, the clinic needs to fix the survey delivery pipeline and establish minimum response thresholds

## Expiry / Review

This constraint is resolvable by fixing the survey distribution mechanism and establishing mandatory post-session survey prompts. Review when total response count exceeds 50 per clinician.

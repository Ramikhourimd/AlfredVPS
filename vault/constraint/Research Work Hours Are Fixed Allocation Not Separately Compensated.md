# Research Work Hours Are Fixed Allocation Not Separately Compensated

## Constraint

Research team members (including clinical staff contributing to research tasks such as Rivi Idelman) work within fixed hour allocations. Research activities are not separately compensated beyond the existing employment arrangement.

## Source

Discussed by Rami Khouri during the 2025-11-11 patient data research meeting with Rivi Idelman. Rami clarified that research hours come from a fixed allocation and are not paid as additional work.

## Implications

Research tasks must compete with clinical and operational responsibilities for team members' time. This limits the pace of data collection, analysis verification, and paper revision. Tasks assigned to clinical staff (obtaining gold standard summaries, reviewing psychiatrist complaints, building data tables) may experience delays when clinical duties take priority.

## Expiry / Review

Active for the duration of the research paper project. May change if external research funding is obtained or organizational policy is revised.

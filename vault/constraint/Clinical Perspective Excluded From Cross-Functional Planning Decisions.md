# Clinical Perspective Excluded From Cross-Functional Planning Decisions

## Constraint

Rami reported that cross-functional company decisions — specifically marketing planning — are being made without clinical input. He cited a specific incident where marketing recommendations were developed without consulting the clinical leadership. This is not a one-time oversight but a structural governance gap: no formal mechanism requires clinical review of decisions that affect patient-facing operations.

## Source

Raised by Rami during the Feb 22 leadership meeting. He expressed frustration that "recommendations are made without clinical input," citing the marketing planning incident as a concrete example. This complaint directly triggered the steering committee proposal and Oren's commitment to include Rami in cross-functional meetings.

## Implications

- Marketing claims about medical services may not align with clinical reality or regulatory requirements
- Hiring decisions (e.g., clinician profiles, caseload assumptions) may not reflect clinical judgment
- Scheduling and KPI decisions may optimize for throughput at the expense of clinical quality
- Without structural change, the pattern will recur — individual commitments to "include Rami" are vulnerable to operational drift
- The steering committee proposal addresses this structurally, but its scope and authority remain undefined

## Expiry / Review

This constraint should be reviewed once the steering committee is formally established and has held its first meeting. If the committee successfully ensures clinical input in cross-functional decisions, the constraint transitions to "expired." If the committee is established but the pattern persists, the constraint remains active and escalates.

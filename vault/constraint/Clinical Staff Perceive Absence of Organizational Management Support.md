# Clinical Staff Perceive Absence of Organizational Management Support

## Constraint

Clinical staff at Telia'z perceive a fundamental absence of organizational management backing. One psychiatrist described the team as feeling like "orphaned children without parents" — a perception that clinical staff manage everything alone without adequate organizational support. This goes beyond specific operational gaps (like unanswered WhatsApp messages or inadequate patient pre-screening) to a broader perception that there is no organizational layer actively guiding, supporting, or advocating for clinical staff.

## Source

Qualitative staff interviews conducted by Rivi Idelman, reported during the 2026-02-18 working session with Rami Khouri. The "orphaned children" quote came from a psychiatrist and was corroborated across multiple staff interviews.

## Implications

This perception constrains staff retention, recruitment messaging, and organizational change initiatives. Any new system or process (task system, scheduling changes, AI tools) will be received against this backdrop of perceived abandonment. If staff don't believe management is present and supportive, operational improvements may be met with skepticism rather than engagement. Addressing this requires visible management presence and advocacy, not just tool improvements.

## Expiry / Review

Should be reassessed after any organizational changes that increase management visibility to clinical staff. The internal task system rollout and group supervision expansion are two interventions that could shift this perception.

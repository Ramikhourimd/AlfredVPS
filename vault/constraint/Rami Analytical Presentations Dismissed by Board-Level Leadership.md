# Rami Analytical Presentations Dismissed by Board-Level Leadership

## Constraint

When Rami presents data-driven plans to board-level leadership, his methodology and calculations are immediately challenged rather than substantively engaged. During the December 2025 planning session, Alex challenged patient number calculations, staffing derivations, and basic methodology at the outset — preventing Rami from advancing to the substance of his proposals.

## Source

Rami's debrief of the 2025-12-04 leadership planning session with Alex, Oren, and Dekkel. The pattern was described as "The Presentation Collapse" — Alex immediately dismantled the analytical foundation before the strategic content could be discussed.

## Implications

- Rami cannot use his preferred communication method (evidence-based frameworks, quantitative models) to influence company direction
- Strategic proposals (stabilization, health score thresholds, premium branding) never receive substantive evaluation
- Creates a feedback loop: Rami builds more elaborate analytical frameworks → leadership dismisses them → Rami feels unheard → builds more frameworks
- Forces Rami toward parallel independent planning rather than collaborative decision-making
- Board presentations on restructuring and innovation face the same credibility risk

## Expiry / Review

This constraint is structural and interpersonal — it persists as long as the presentation dynamic between Rami and Alex remains unchanged. External validation (e.g., Ziv's advisor role) may provide a workaround.

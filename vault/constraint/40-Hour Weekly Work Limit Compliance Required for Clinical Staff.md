# 40-Hour Weekly Work Limit Compliance Required for Clinical Staff

## Constraint

Clinical staff at Telia'z Clinic Israel must comply with a 40-hour weekly work limit. Session reporting must accurately reflect hours worked within this cap. Discrepancies in hour reporting — whether over-reporting or working beyond the limit — create compliance risk.

## Source

During the 2025-12-04 payroll data review, a discrepancy was flagged in psychiatrist Noa Hinden's reporting related to the 40-hour limit (approximately 500 NIS discrepancy). Basel Kanaaneh was tasked with verifying the data and reminding Noa about correct hour reporting compliance. This suggests the 40-hour cap is a hard regulatory or contractual constraint, not merely a guideline.

## Implications

- Payroll processing must verify that reported hours do not exceed the 40-hour weekly threshold
- Psychiatrists who also claim extended/double intake sessions (e.g., Sal Shikum billing) may inadvertently exceed the limit
- The constraint interacts with the known issue of psychiatrists underreporting hours — if actual hours exceed 40 but only some are reported, compliance appears met while the true labor situation is masked
- Part-time staff with multiple employers may approach the limit through combined positions

## Expiry / Review

Ongoing regulatory requirement. Should be reviewed if employment classification models change or if the clinic transitions any staff from contractor to employee status.

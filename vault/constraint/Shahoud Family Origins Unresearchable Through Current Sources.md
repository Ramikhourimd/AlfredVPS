# Shahoud Family Origins Unresearchable Through Current Sources

## Constraint

When Rami Khouri asked about the Shahoud (شحود) family and their origins, Majdi Shaddad could only report that he had encountered the name in military records but had no substantive information about their origins or family connections. This creates a research dead end for the Shahoud line within the current oral history framework.

## Source

During the 2025-12-02 conversation, Rami specifically asked about the Shahoud family. Majdi's response — recognizing the name only from military records — indicates this family falls outside his ~45 years of Shaddad-focused genealogical research. The limitation compounds the broader constraint that the project relies on oral tradition.

## Implications

Any investigation into Shahoud family origins would require sources beyond Majdi Shaddad's knowledge base. Potential avenues include: Ottoman military records (where Majdi encountered the name), church parish records, or other family historians. This constraint may be overcome if new informants or documentary sources are identified.

## Expiry / Review

Review if: (1) Majdi locates the specific military record referencing the Shahoud name, (2) another informant with Shahoud family knowledge is identified, or (3) archival research into Ottoman-era military records becomes feasible.

# Organizational Communication Relies on Informal Channels Producing Distorted Information

## Constraint

Telia'z lacks formal communication channels for operational decisions and personnel changes. Information about significant organizational changes — hiring plans, role transitions, structural decisions — flows through informal conversations and rumors rather than structured announcements or documented decisions.

## Source

Rami explicitly complained about hearing organizational changes secondhand: "Rami is already hearing rumors (e.g., 'four secretary hires') that turn out to be inaccurate or exaggerated." He requested a joint meeting with Oren and Basel specifically to get direct, accurate information rather than relying on distorted secondhand accounts. The Dec 2 management discussion also reveals that major assessments (Dekkel's capabilities, Ziv's contribution, company identity crisis) are discussed informally between two people rather than in structured leadership forums.

## Implications

1. Decision-makers operate on different versions of reality
2. Personnel planning cannot be validated because no single authoritative source exists
3. Rami's exclusion from information flow compounds his broader exclusion from decision-making
4. The restructuring itself risks being undermined if participants cannot verify what was actually agreed

## Expiry / Review

Resolves when formal communication protocols are established as part of the organizational restructuring.

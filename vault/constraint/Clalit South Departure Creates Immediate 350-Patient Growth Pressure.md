# Clalit South Departure Creates Immediate 350-Patient Growth Pressure

## Constraint

A psychiatrist serving Clalit South is departing, leaving approximately 350 patients without a provider. Leadership (Alex, Oren, Dekkel) views this as an immediate growth opportunity that must be captured — creating pressure to expand capacity before the clinic has stabilized its operational infrastructure.

## Source

External market event identified during leadership planning discussions in early December 2025. The opportunity was cited alongside Clalit agreement progress and HMO expansion targets as part of the 2026 financial goals.

## Implications

- Creates a concrete, time-sensitive argument for immediate growth that overrides stabilization concerns
- Provides leadership with a specific patient volume number to anchor expansion targets
- Forces the growth-vs-stabilization debate into a decision with real patients at stake
- Rami's counter-argument (stabilize first) appears to cost the company 350 patients if followed
- The pressure is compounded by simultaneous targeting of Leumit as another expansion vector

## Expiry / Review

This constraint is time-bound — the 350 patients will find alternative care within weeks to months. The growth pressure it creates, however, reflects a recurring pattern: leadership using market opportunities to override operational readiness concerns.

# Staff Satisfaction Survey Data Statistically Unreliable at Current Response Rates

## Constraint

The clinic's staff satisfaction survey program has produced statistically meaningless data. Only ~16 surveys were sent total since program launch. Individual psychiatrist scores are based on as few as 2 responses out of ~200 patients (Elinor: 10/10 from 2 responses). At these response rates, individual satisfaction scores cannot be used for performance evaluation, compensation decisions, or quality assurance.

## Source

Rami reviewed the survey data during the 2025-12-04 office session and explicitly flagged the low response rates as undermining the usefulness of the satisfaction metric. The review revealed: Elinor had a perfect 10/10 score but only 2 responses; Ahmed, Saeed, and Elias scored below 9 but sample sizes were unclear.

## Implications

- No individual clinician satisfaction score is actionable at current volumes
- The clinic cannot use satisfaction data to support or challenge decisions about staff (e.g., the Elinor replacement decision cannot cite satisfaction data)
- Survey distribution mechanism may be broken — 16 surveys total across ~200 patients per doctor suggests systemic delivery failure
- Any KPI framework relying on patient satisfaction as a metric is currently measuring noise, not signal

## Expiry / Review

Review when survey response rates exceed a minimum statistical threshold (suggested: 30+ responses per clinician before scores become reportable).

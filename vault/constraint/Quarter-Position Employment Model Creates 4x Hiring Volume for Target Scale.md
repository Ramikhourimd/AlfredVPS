# Quarter-Position Employment Model Creates 4x Hiring Volume for Target Scale

## Constraint

Psychiatrists and case managers at Telia'z Clinic Israel predominantly work quarter-time positions (~25%). To reach the projected staffing need of approximately 44 FTE (24 psychiatrists + 21 case managers), the clinic must recruit and onboard approximately 176 individual clinicians. This 4x multiplier between FTE requirements and actual headcount significantly increases hiring, onboarding, agreement processing, and payroll administration complexity.

## Source

Rami presented data-driven projections at the 2025-12-04 strategic leadership meeting, based on analysis of Maccabi patient data from the clinic database. The projection showed ~7,786 year-end active patients requiring ~44 FTE positions, which at quarter-time positions translates to ~176 individual hires.

## Implications

- Recruitment pipeline must process 4x the volume implied by FTE targets
- Each hire requires individual employment agreements (already identified as bottleneck)
- Payroll complexity scales with headcount, not FTE (each person has unique rate, schedule, agreement)
- Onboarding and supervision capacity must absorb 176 individuals, not 44
- The existing constraint on inconsistent Excel payment formats compounds with higher headcount

## Expiry / Review

This constraint persists as long as the quarter-position employment model is the norm. If the clinic shifts to higher FTE percentages per clinician, the multiplier would decrease.

# Municipal Costs for Purchased Property Exceed Current Rental Budget

## Constraint

Arnona (municipal property tax) for a ~320 sqm property in an established Haifa-area neighborhood is approximately 1,800 NIS/month. Annual water costs were cited at approximately 7,000 NIS (~583 NIS/month). Combined municipal and utility costs alone total roughly 2,400 NIS/month — exceeding the family's entire discussed rental budget of 1,200-1,500 NIS/month.

## Source

Neighborhood residents during a social visit discussion on 2025-09-23. Arnona figure cited for a specific 320 sqm property. Water figure may be annual or reflect a notably high billing period.

## Implications

- On the purchase path, municipal costs alone would exceed the family's current rental expenditure, meaning total housing cost (mortgage + arnona + utilities) would be dramatically higher than current outlay
- This adds significant weight to the rent-vs-buy analysis — even if purchase financing were solved, ongoing costs are substantial
- Arnona scales with property size, so the family's minimum ~120 sqm requirement would carry proportionally lower arnona (~675 NIS/month estimated), but still a material ongoing cost
- Any purchase budgeting must account for these recurring costs beyond the purchase price itself

## Expiry / Review

Arnona rates are set by municipalities and adjust periodically. These figures should be verified against current municipal rate schedules when a specific property is under serious consideration.

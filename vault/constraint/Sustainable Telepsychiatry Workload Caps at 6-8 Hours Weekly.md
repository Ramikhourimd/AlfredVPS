# Sustainable Telepsychiatry Workload Caps at 6-8 Hours Weekly

## Constraint

Clinical staff (both case managers and psychiatrists) consistently report that telepsychiatry work becomes unsustainably draining beyond 6-8 hours per week. Below this threshold, staff manage the intensity reasonably well; above it, burnout escalates significantly.

## Source

Qualitative interviews conducted by Rivi Idelman with multiple case managers and psychiatrists at Telia'z Clinic Israel during February 2026. Corroborated across both role types independently.

Specific data points:
- One psychiatrist (Sa'id) found 3-4 hours daily across 5 days far more draining than 1.5 hours daily — even the same weekly total concentrated into one long block would be unacceptable
- A case manager described feeling "much more empathetic and attentive" at 5 hours versus 9 hours of work
- Another case manager reported leaving Telia'z work days feeling more exhausted than residency days despite shorter hours

## Implications

- Scheduling must respect this threshold when assigning clinician shifts
- Recruitment projections should assume lower per-clinician capacity than traditional clinic models
- Attempts to increase individual clinician throughput beyond this threshold risk burnout, quality degradation, and turnover
- Staff who exceed this threshold may need compensatory support (group supervision, reduced consecutive days)

## Expiry / Review

Should be re-validated if the clinical workflow changes significantly (e.g., AI reduces documentation burden, session formats change, or new support tools are introduced). Review annually or after major platform changes.

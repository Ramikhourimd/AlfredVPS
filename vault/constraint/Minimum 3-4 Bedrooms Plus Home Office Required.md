# Minimum 3-4 Bedrooms Plus Home Office Required

## Constraint

The family requires a minimum of 3-4 bedrooms to accommodate: (1) Rami and his wife, (2) their small daughter, (3) one child on the way, plus a dedicated home office for Rami. Properties below this threshold are not viable candidates.

## Source

Family size and professional requirements. Rami explicitly stated the bedroom count during the family building viewing (2025-10-09). The need for a home office was emphasized across multiple discussions, including the property investment conversation where ground-floor clinic potential was noted as a significant advantage.

## Implications

This minimum size requirement effectively eliminates most apartments under ~110 sqm. The 112 sqm unit at the family building was borderline; the 140 sqm unit was more suitable. Properties at 75 sqm (viewed same day) were dismissed as too small. The home office requirement adds ~15 sqm to the minimum usable area compared to a standard family apartment.

## Expiry / Review

This constraint intensifies over time — as children grow, space needs increase. The investment discussion noted "the current space is not enough because the children have grown." Review when the second child arrives and actual space usage can be assessed.

# Intake-to-Follow-Up Ratio Creates Multi-Month Patient Wait Times

## Constraint

Clinicians continue receiving new intake patients despite having insufficient availability for follow-up appointments. Rivi Idelman reported patients waiting 7+ months for their next appointment. She has been informally opening extra (uncompensated) time slots to maintain continuity of care, but the intake-to-follow-up ratio is structurally unsustainable without additional contracted hours.

## Source

Rivi raised this during the 2026-03-17 innovation team meeting. Rami suggested she may need to stop accepting new patients entirely. Rivi confirmed this is a systemic issue across the clinic, not unique to her caseload.

## Implications

- Clinicians face an impossible choice between patient welfare and contractual boundaries
- Informal workarounds (opening extra slots) create overtime disputes and burnout
- The constraint interacts with Basel's discharge model — if discharge rates don't meet targets, the bottleneck compounds
- Patient care continuity suffers when follow-ups are delayed by months

## Expiry / Review

Structural — persists until either intake volume decreases, clinical hours increase, or discharge model takes effect.

# Ministry of Health Requires Justification for Prolonged Patient Work Absence

## Constraint

The Israeli Ministry of Health will not accept prolonged patient absence from work without proper clinical justification and documentation. Treating psychiatrists must provide documented rationale for any extended work absence, and are expected to actively facilitate patient return to occupational activity when clinically appropriate.

## Source

Israeli Ministry of Health regulations governing psychiatric patient employment and rehabilitation. Referenced during the 2025-12-02 outpatient follow-up where Rami directed a patient to resume attendance on Monday and Wednesday, citing that the Ministry of Health compliance framework requires it and the facility manager had flagged the 2-month absence.

## Implications

- Psychiatrists must document clinical justification when authorizing extended work absence
- Patients cannot be kept on indefinite psychiatric leave without periodic reassessment and return-to-work planning
- The clinic must track work absence duration as part of patient management
- Facility managers serve as a compliance feedback loop — they report prolonged absences that may trigger regulatory scrutiny
- This creates a clinical tension: some patients benefit from continued rest, but regulatory requirements push toward occupational reintegration

## Expiry / Review

Ongoing regulatory requirement — no expiry. Should be reviewed whenever Ministry of Health policies are updated.

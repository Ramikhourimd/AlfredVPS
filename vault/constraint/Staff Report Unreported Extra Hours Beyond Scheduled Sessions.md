# Staff Report Unreported Extra Hours Beyond Scheduled Sessions

## Constraint

Psychiatrists at Telia'z are working unreported extra hours — arriving early to review cases before sessions, spending time on documentation after scheduled blocks, and handling follow-up tasks outside tracked time. This means official workload data understates actual clinical effort.

## Source

Multiple psychiatrist interviews conducted by Rivi Idelman during February 2026 staff experience research. Psychiatrists described preparation time, post-session documentation, and ad-hoc case consultation as routine but untracked.

## Implications

- Official hours-per-week data is unreliable for burnout analysis — actual workload is higher than reported
- The 6-8 hour/week sustainability threshold may refer to scheduled hours, with true effort being significantly higher
- Compensation models based on scheduled session time may undervalue actual work performed
- Staff satisfaction surveys must account for total effort, not just scheduled hours
- Any workload optimization initiative needs to capture the full picture including prep and follow-up time

## Expiry / Review

This constraint persists until Telia'z implements comprehensive time tracking that captures all clinical effort, not just scheduled session blocks. Should be reassessed when the employee satisfaction survey instrument is deployed.

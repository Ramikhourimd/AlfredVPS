# GP Confed Internal Governance Must Approve Partnership Before Execution

## Constraint

GP Confederation Leeds has internal governance processes that must formally approve any partnership arrangement before it can proceed. Jim Barwick was explicit: "If we can't work out the commercial bit, we don't go any further. It's very simple." The commercial model must pass internal finance review and board-level governance approval.

## Source

Jim Barwick stated this directly during the 2025-01-22 partnership meeting. He also indicated the GP Confed Director of Finance needs to be involved in modeling the commercial arrangement. This is standard NHS organizational governance — external partnerships require formal board-level or delegated authority approval.

## Implications

- The timeline for GP Confed partnership is partially outside Telia'z's control — internal governance processes have their own cadence
- Commercial model must be compelling enough to survive internal scrutiny
- GP Confed Director of Finance is a key stakeholder who hasn't been deeply engaged yet (as of Jan 2025)
- This governance gate sits between the partnership agreement and the tender bid — if governance stalls, the tender timeline (aligned to April NHS fiscal year) could slip

## Expiry / Review

This constraint is permanent for the GP Confed track. Every new arrangement or material change will likely require re-approval. Should be reviewed whenever commercial terms are updated.

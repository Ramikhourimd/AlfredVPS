# HMO Growth Requires District-Level Relationship Building

## Constraint

Expanding patient volume within Israeli HMOs (Maccabi, Clalit) requires direct engagement with district-level managers and their deputies. HMO operations are structured around autonomous districts, each with its own budget, territory, and decision-making authority. Central-level agreements alone do not drive patient referrals.

## Source

Observation from Telia'z leadership based on experience with Maccabi North. A deputy district manager directly told the team that the central agreement signing alone is insufficient — they need to engage relevant people at the district level. When the team asked Anat (central Maccabi contact) for a list of district contacts, she did not provide them.

## Implications

- Cannot rely solely on central HMO agreements to drive growth
- Need dedicated field staff or embedded relationship managers at the district level
- Marketing/growth budget must account for this district-level engagement
- Patient acquisition is fundamentally a local, relationship-driven process within HMOs

## Expiry / Review

Ongoing — this is a structural characteristic of the Israeli HMO system.

# Maccabi HMO Transition Reduced Clinical Mission and Professional Variety

## Constraint

The transition from post-October 7th trauma patients (southern Israel) to Maccabi HMO patients fundamentally changed the nature of clinical work. Psychiatrists now describe the caseload as "just another HMO clinic" — routine cases without the professional variety or sense of social mission, but with the added burden of remote work and AI summary requirements.

## Source

Psychiatrist Ilya specifically highlighted this shift during Rivi's staff interviews. When the clinic served trauma patients, there was a sense of meaningful purpose and clinical challenge. The Maccabi HMO caseload feels generic by comparison.

## Implications

Loss of mission-driven work is a retention risk multiplier — it removes one of the few intrinsic motivators that could offset the convenience-only attachment pattern. Any strategy to improve psychiatrist engagement must address the perceived lack of professional challenge and purpose, not just logistical factors.

## Expiry / Review

This constraint is tied to the current payer mix. If Telia'z diversifies patient sources or introduces specialty tracks, it could be re-evaluated.

# Staff Reimbursement Delays Create Cash Flow Burden on Individual Employees

## Constraint

Staff members at Telia'z Clinic Israel are advancing personal funds for company expenses because the company credit card authorization process is too slow or inaccessible. The company Visa card requires CEO-level approval (Oren Taliaz) for transactions, and physical card access is limited to two people (Shira, Basel). When purchases are needed urgently and neither the card holder nor the approver is available, staff pay from personal funds and seek reimbursement later.

## Source

Directly stated in a voice memo fragment from the clinic office on 2025-12-04: a staff member said "I'm currently paying from my own [funds]" (انا هسه بدفع من عندي) as a temporary measure while discussing credit card logistics.

## Implications

- Creates an uncompensated float where employees carry company expenses on personal cash flow
- Disproportionately burdens lower-paid staff who may not have the liquidity to front expenses
- Reimbursement tracking adds administrative overhead that compounds the payroll data fragmentation problem
- As the clinic scales and operational expenses increase, the frequency and size of personal advances will grow
- Risk of employee dissatisfaction if reimbursements are delayed or disputed

## Expiry / Review

Review when the organizational restructuring establishes delegated spending authority or introduces a petty cash / corporate card system with pre-approved limits for operational staff.

# Psychiatrists Working Unreported Extra Hours Outside Scheduled Sessions

## Constraint

Psychiatrists at Telia'z Clinic Israel are routinely working extra hours beyond their scheduled session blocks — arriving early to review cases, staying late for documentation, and performing preparation work that is not captured in the scheduling or compensation system. This unreported labor creates an inaccurate picture of actual workload and contributes to burnout that management may underestimate.

## Source

Identified through Rivi Idelman's qualitative interviews with psychiatrists during early 2026. Psychiatrists reported this as a structural issue: session preparation, pre-session chart review, and post-session documentation require time that falls outside the formally scheduled clinical hours.

## Implications

- Actual hours worked exceed what scheduling data shows, making workload analysis unreliable
- The 6-8 hour sustainable threshold assumption may be inaccurate if unreported hours push real totals higher
- Compensation models based on scheduled hours may undervalue psychiatrist contributions
- Burnout interventions targeting scheduled hours alone will miss a significant workload component
- Any staff satisfaction survey must capture total hours worked including preparation and follow-up

## Expiry / Review

This constraint persists until the scheduling/compensation system accounts for preparation and documentation time. Should be reviewed when: (1) time-tracking improvements are implemented, (2) compensation model is updated, (3) quantitative survey data confirms or refutes the qualitative finding.

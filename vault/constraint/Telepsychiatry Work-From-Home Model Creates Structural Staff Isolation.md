# Telepsychiatry Work-From-Home Model Creates Structural Staff Isolation

## Constraint

The telepsychiatry work model — clinicians working alone from home, facing a screen with patients, without physical co-location with colleagues — creates a structural isolation that cannot be fully resolved within the current operating model. Even when other clinicians work in the same "pod," there is minimal routine interaction.

## Source

This is inherent to the remote clinical work model, not imposed by a specific regulation or policy. It was identified through Rivi Idelman's qualitative interviews with both case managers and psychiatrists in early 2026. One staff member described the feeling as being "orphaned children without parents" — managing everything alone without organizational backing.

## Implications

- Most staff do not feel part of a team despite being organizationally grouped
- WhatsApp group messages frequently go unanswered, reinforcing the isolation
- Informal social connection opportunities are limited to scheduled group sessions
- Moving to structured internal task systems may further reduce informal interaction
- Group supervision (hadracha) partially compensates but does not fully resolve isolation
- Any system redesign must consider social/team-building features, not just task management
- Recruitment messaging about "team environment" may not match staff reality

## Expiry / Review

This constraint persists as long as the work-from-home telepsychiatry model is the primary delivery mode. Should be re-evaluated if: (1) hybrid clinic days are introduced, (2) virtual team-building tools are deployed, (3) staff satisfaction survey data shows improvement in belonging metrics.

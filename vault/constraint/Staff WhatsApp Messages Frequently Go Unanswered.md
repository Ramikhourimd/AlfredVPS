# Staff WhatsApp Messages Frequently Go Unanswered

## Constraint

Clinical staff report writing messages in WhatsApp groups (questions to psychiatrists, technical issues, messages to management) that frequently receive no response. This creates a perception of abandonment and undermines team cohesion. One staff member described feeling like "orphaned children without a parent."

## Source

Reported across multiple staff interviews conducted by Rivi Idelman, shared during the 2026-02-18 meeting. Both case managers and psychiatrists described this pattern.

## Implications

- Staff feel unsupported and uncertain about how to proceed when questions go unanswered
- The problem is structural, not individual — WhatsApp groups lack accountability mechanisms for response
- Moving to an internal task system may solve the accountability gap but needs explicit response-time expectations
- The emotional impact (abandonment, isolation) is disproportionate to the operational issue, suggesting deeper team cohesion problems
- Any replacement communication system must include read receipts, assignment, or escalation to prevent the same pattern

## Expiry / Review

Review when the internal task system is implemented. Measure whether response rates improve.

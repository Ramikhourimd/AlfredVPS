# Israeli Company Car Benefits Fully Grossed Up as Salary

## Constraint

Company car benefits provided by employers in Israel are fully grossed up as salary additions at the employee's marginal tax rate. A car valued at 7,000 NIS/month (including insurance, fuel, maintenance) is treated as a 7,000 NIS net salary increase, which must be grossed up 1:1 — making the employer cost significantly higher than the benefit value.

## Source

Israeli Tax Authority closed all previous loopholes for off-payroll vehicle benefits. This applies regardless of whether the vehicle is owned, leased, or provided as a fringe benefit. The same grossing-up principle applies to other in-kind benefits such as Ten Bis meal vouchers.

## Implications

- Company cars are no longer a tax-efficient compensation method in Israel
- Leasing does not provide a workaround — the benefit is still treated as income
- For Rami's compensation structuring: adding a car benefit through the company would cost more than Rami paying privately
- This constraint directly impacts the financial planning for any salary restructuring or benefit additions at Telia'z

## Expiry / Review

Ongoing — Israeli tax law. May change with future legislation but trend has been toward closing more loopholes, not opening them.

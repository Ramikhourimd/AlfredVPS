# Psychiatrists Feel Unsupported With Difficult Patients Due to Inadequate Pre-Screening

## Constraint

Psychiatrists report frustration around receiving difficult or aggressive patients without adequate prior screening or organizational support. One psychiatrist described the team as feeling like "orphaned children without parents" — a perception that clinical staff must manage everything alone without institutional backing. This creates both a clinical risk and a retention risk.

## Source

Qualitative interviews with psychiatrists, conducted by Rivi Idelman and reported during the 2026-02-18 meeting. Multiple psychiatrists expressed this concern independently.

## Implications

- Difficult patients arriving without screening creates safety and burnout risk for psychiatrists
- The "orphaned" perception suggests systemic underinvestment in clinical support infrastructure
- Improving pre-screening protocols and providing clear escalation paths could address this
- Without resolution, this contributes to psychiatrist dissatisfaction and potential turnover

## Expiry / Review

Review when clinical supervision protocols and patient screening processes are formalized. This constraint persists as long as the organizational support gap exists.

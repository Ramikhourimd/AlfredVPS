# Alex Challenges Rami Data Methodology Blocking Plan Approval

## Constraint

When Rami presents clinical operations plans to Alex (board member/investor), Alex immediately challenges the patient number calculations, staffing derivations, and basic methodology — before engaging with the substance. This pattern creates a structural barrier: Rami's plans cannot gain board-level approval if the data methodology is questioned at the opening.

## Source

During a 2025-12-04 leadership planning session with Alex, Oren, and Dekkel, Rami attempted to present his clinic plans. Alex's immediate response was to challenge the calculation methodology rather than evaluate the strategic direction. Rami described this in his 2025-12-05 early morning debrief as a "presentation collapse" — the meeting devolved into methodology arguments before reaching substantive discussion.

## Implications

This constraint means Rami cannot simply prepare better content — he must also pre-validate his data methodology with someone Alex trusts (possibly Shira for financial projections, or an external analyst) before presenting. It also explains why the board presentation task has a dependency on "financial projections from Shira" — Shira's numbers carry methodological credibility that Rami's do not, at least in Alex's eyes.

Combined with the existing constraint that Sunday meetings exclude clinical operations, Rami has no established forum where his plans receive substantive evaluation rather than methodological challenge.

## Expiry / Review

This constraint should be re-evaluated after the next board presentation. If Rami presents with pre-validated data (from Shira or another trusted source) and Alex engages substantively, the constraint may be situational rather than structural.

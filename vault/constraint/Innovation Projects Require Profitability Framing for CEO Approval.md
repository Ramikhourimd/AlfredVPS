# Innovation Projects Require Profitability Framing for CEO Approval

## Constraint

Innovation and creative projects at Telia'z require explicit profitability framing to receive CEO approval and resource allocation. Pure R&D or creative exploration without a commercial justification is insufficient for green-lighting projects.

## Source

During a creative project alignment call on 2025-09-21, CEO Dekkel Taliaz specifically requested a team alignment meeting to ensure the project would be executed "in a profitable way," with VP R&D Shachar Segev also involved. The emphasis on profitability as a framing requirement — not just a desired outcome — indicates this is an organizational constraint on how innovation work must be justified.

## Implications

- Innovation proposals must include commercial viability arguments alongside technical merit
- Creative exploration phases should include profitability assessment milestones
- The innovation team needs to frame research outputs in terms of business value (cost reduction, revenue generation, competitive advantage) rather than purely clinical or technical improvement
- This may create tension with the research-first approach the innovation program follows, as early-stage research may not yet have clear profitability metrics

## Expiry / Review

This constraint appears to be an ongoing organizational stance rather than a time-limited policy. Should be reviewed if leadership changes or if the organization explicitly adopts a different innovation funding model.

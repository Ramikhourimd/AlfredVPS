# Clinical Practice Private Hours Allocated by Hospital Unit

## Constraint

Rami's clinical private practice hours are formally allocated by the hospital unit via a bituh (license). The initial allocation was 15 hours per month for 8,000 NIS. Working additional hours (e.g., 21 hours in a recent month) did not proportionally increase income — still yielding approximately 8,000 NIS — suggesting a fixed allocation or cap rather than a per-hour rate.

## Source

Hospital unit policy governing private practice privileges (bituh). The allocation structure was described during the 2025-09-23 financial planning discussion with the accountant.

## Implications

Income scaling from clinical practice is constrained by the hospital's allocation system, not just by patient demand. Increasing private practice income to the assumed 18-20K NIS/month target likely requires either a formal increase in allocated hours/rate, renegotiation of the bituh terms, or a structural change in how private practice compensation is calculated. This is a gating factor on the income stabilization assumption.

## Expiry / Review

Should be reviewed whenever the hospital unit reviews bituh allocations, or if Rami negotiates new terms. The gap between hours worked (21) and hours compensated (15) warrants clarification.

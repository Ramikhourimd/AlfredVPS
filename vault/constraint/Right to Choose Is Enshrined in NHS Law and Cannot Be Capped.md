# Right to Choose Is Enshrined in NHS Law and Cannot Be Capped

## Constraint
The Right to Choose programme is enshrined in the NHS constitution and UK law. Patients have the legal right to choose their healthcare provider, and local ICBs cannot cap or limit the number of Right to Choose referrals.

## Source
Jason Brook: "It's in the NHS constitution. You're not allowed to do that. It's enshrined in law." When asked if regions could limit referrals to control costs.

## Implications
- ICBs cannot protect their budgets by limiting Right to Choose
- Creates a perverse incentive: local ICBs pay for patients choosing external providers, blowing budgets
- The only defensive strategy is to build a compelling local pathway that reduces the motivation for Right to Choose
- A strong local ADHD service through GP Confed + Telia'z could attract Right to Choose referrals FROM other areas, generating revenue

## Expiry / Review
Would require legislative change to modify. Current political direction seems unlikely to restrict patient choice further.

# Business Development Can Commit HMO Agreements Without Clinical Capacity Validation

## Constraint

There is no organizational gate requiring clinical capacity validation before business development commits to new HMO agreements (kupot cholim). Leadership (Alex, Oren, Dekkel) can target and sign agreements with Clalit, Leumit, or other HMOs based on financial growth targets without assessing whether the clinic has the psychiatrist capacity, support staff, operational protocols, or quality infrastructure to absorb additional patient volume.

## Source

This is a structural process gap, not an externally imposed constraint. Rami identified it explicitly in his December 5, 2025 frustration debrief: he predicts that when leadership signs a new HMO agreement (e.g., Leumit) in the coming months, he will have to reject it because the clinic cannot absorb more patients. Marketing will generate demand the clinic cannot serve.

## Implications

This constraint creates an operational collision course: business development optimizes for revenue growth (new agreements, patient volume), while clinical operations struggles to maintain quality with current patient loads. Without a clinical capacity checkpoint in the agreement process, the company risks:
1. Signing agreements it cannot honor, damaging HMO relationships
2. Forcing rapid clinician hiring that degrades service quality
3. Legal exposure from overloaded practitioners and inadequate oversight
4. Staff burnout and turnover from unsustainable patient loads

The proposed steering committee (with CMO veto authority) could address this by requiring clinical sign-off before new agreements, but this mechanism does not yet exist.

## Expiry / Review

This constraint persists until a formal clinical capacity review step is added to the HMO agreement process, or until the proposed steering committee governance is implemented with CMO veto authority covering growth commitments.

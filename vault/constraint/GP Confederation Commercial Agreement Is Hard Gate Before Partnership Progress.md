# GP Confederation Commercial Agreement Is Hard Gate Before Partnership Progress

## Constraint

Jim Barwick (GP Confederation) explicitly stated that a commercially viable revenue arrangement is an absolute prerequisite before the partnership can proceed: "If we can't work out the commercial bit, we don't go any further. It's very simple." This is a hard gate — not a preference or a risk, but a stated precondition.

The commercial model must satisfy:
- Revenue flows back to Confed for reinvestment in local GP projects
- Costs to ICB must not exceed current tariff levels (benchmark: £360 intake, £180 follow-up)
- Two separate revenue streams must be modeled: Leeds-based referrals and Right to Choose referrals
- GP Confed Director of Finance must validate the model

## Source

Jim Barwick stated this explicitly during the in-person partnership meeting at GP Confederation offices in Leeds on 2025-01-22. The statement was made in the context of defining next steps — Jim positioned commercial agreement as the first and most critical milestone.

## Implications

- The GP Confed track CANNOT progress past commercial modeling without agreement — clinical pathway design, tender preparation, and governance mapping are all downstream
- Telia'z's assumption that they can operate within NHS tariff pricing is directly tested by this gate
- If the commercial model fails, the entire GP Confed track collapses regardless of clinical or technical readiness
- The two-tier revenue model (Leeds vs Right to Choose) adds complexity — both streams must be commercially viable

## Expiry / Review

This constraint is active until a commercial agreement is reached with GP Confederation. The NHS financial year starting April creates a natural deadline — if commercial terms aren't agreed before tender submission, the opportunity may be delayed by a full year.

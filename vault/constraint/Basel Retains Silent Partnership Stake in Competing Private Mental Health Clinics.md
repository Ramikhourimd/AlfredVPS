# Basel Retains Silent Partnership Stake in Competing Private Mental Health Clinics

## Constraint

Basel Kanaaneh retains approximately 50% ownership in a private clinic company he co-founded, which operates two mental health clinics serving Ministry of Defense patients, Clalit, Leumit, and Meuhedet health fund patients, and NGO referrals. He sold 50% to Ali Awad (owner of Shams Group, a national healthcare consulting firm). Dr. Wasim Reli serves as Medical Director and handles operations. Basel transferred operational management to Wasim due to conflict of interest with his Clalit role, but remains as shareholder/silent partner.

## Source

Disclosed by Basel during the hiring interview on November 9, 2025, attended by Rami Khouri and Dekkel Taliaz. Basel mentioned transferring operational management to avoid conflict of interest.

## Implications

- Basel has a financial interest in private mental health clinics that potentially compete with Telia'z for the same health fund patients (Clalit, Leumit, Meuhedet)
- The Shams Group connection (Ali Awad) creates an indirect relationship between Telia'z and a national healthcare consulting firm
- Any Telia'z expansion into new HMO partnerships (especially Leumit, which Basel's clinics already serve) should consider this overlap
- Basel self-identified the conflict and stepped back operationally, which suggests awareness and willingness to manage the issue

## Expiry / Review

Should be reviewed when Telia'z expands into new HMO partnerships or if Basel's private venture changes scope. No defined expiry — persists as long as Basel holds the stake.

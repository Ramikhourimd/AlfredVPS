# Shira Maccabi-Only Operational Scope Limits Multi-HMO Scaling

## Constraint

Shira's current operational capacity is explicitly limited to managing Maccabi adult patient volume. She cannot scale to handle additional HMOs (Clalit, Leumit) within her existing scope. This means clinic expansion to new HMOs requires either: (a) significantly expanding Shira's capacity and team, (b) creating parallel operations tracks with additional operations managers, or (c) restructuring the Clinical Operations Manager role to be HMO-agnostic with pod leaders handling HMO-specific workflows.

## Source

During the November 9, 2025 organizational discussion between Rami and Dekkel, it was explicitly noted: "Current scope limited to Maccabi volume — cannot scale to handle additional HMOs." This was stated as a fact about Shira's current operational reality, not as a temporary limitation.

## Implications

- The 2026 growth targets (adding Clalit, possibly Leumit) cannot proceed without addressing the operations capacity gap.
- The pod model partially addresses this by distributing management load, but pod leaders still need an operations manager above them.
- Hiring a Medical Director alone does not solve this — operational scaling requires operational capacity expansion.
- The three-pillar model from Feb 2026 maps Shira to Clinical Operations Manager, but this mapping assumes her scope can expand — which contradicts the Nov 2025 assessment.

## Expiry / Review

Should be re-evaluated after Basel's operations structure is fully implemented and the pod model is operational. If pod leaders absorb HMO-specific management, Shira's scope may naturally expand.

# Telepsychiatry Work Intensity Creates Unique Burnout Pattern Distinct From Hours Worked

## Insight

Across four independent session notes from 2026-02-18, a consistent pattern emerges: telepsychiatry burnout is driven by per-hour intensity, not total hours worked. Staff consistently report that a short block of telepsychiatry work is more draining than longer blocks of comparable clinical work (e.g., hospital residency). This suggests telepsychiatry has a distinct burnout signature that cannot be addressed solely through schedule reduction.

## Evidence

- A case manager described leaving Telia'z shifts feeling "far more exhausted and burnt out than after hospital residency shifts, even when residency hours are longer"
- Sa'id (psychiatrist) found 3-4 hours/day across 5 days draining, but 1.5 hours/day sustainable — same weekly total, different intensity distribution
- A case manager reported being "much more empathetic and attentive" at 5 hours versus 9 hours — quality of care degrades with session density
- The combination of constant patient interaction, documentation demands, screen fatigue, and emotional load creates compounding strain
- Group supervision appears to partially mitigate burnout, suggesting social connection counterbalances isolation-driven exhaustion

## Implications

- Scheduling optimization should focus on session density and distribution, not just total weekly hours
- The employee satisfaction survey must measure intensity separately from hours
- Technology improvements (better documentation tools, AI-assisted prep) could reduce per-session cognitive load
- The 6-8 hour/week threshold is likely a proxy for intensity tolerance, not an absolute hours limit
- Recruitment messaging should set realistic expectations about work intensity

## Applicability

Applies to all clinical staff at Telia'z (psychiatrists and case managers). May generalize to other telepsychiatry platforms with similar back-to-back session formats.

# Compensation Inequities Surface Through Data Audits Not Staff Advocacy

## Insight

Compensation inequities at Telia'z Clinic Israel are consistently discovered through periodic data review sessions (management-initiated audits) rather than through staff self-advocacy or formal grievance channels. This means the clinic's compensation fairness depends entirely on the frequency and thoroughness of manual data reviews — problems that are not actively audited remain invisible until they cause departures or incidents.

## Evidence

- **Atef underpayment** (2025-12-04): Atef was being paid below the standard scale for intake sessions despite working more hours than peers. He did not raise the issue — it was discovered during Rami and Basel's payroll data review.
- **Sarah data gap** (2025-12-04): Sarah's payroll Excel was missing from Shira's batch entirely. Her employment status and compensation accuracy were unknown until the data review caught the omission.
- **Systemic discrepancies** (2025-12-04): The broader payroll review revealed that actual payments diverge from signed agreements across multiple staff tiers — none of these were reported by the affected staff members.
- **Elinor/Noa rate demands**: The only staff-initiated compensation discussions occurred when psychiatrists learned about a higher rate tier through peer discovery — not through self-assessment of their own agreement terms.

## Implications

- Compensation audits should be scheduled regularly (quarterly minimum), not triggered by incidents
- The absence of staff complaints about pay should not be interpreted as satisfaction or accuracy
- Automated reconciliation between agreements and actual payments would surface discrepancies without depending on manual reviews
- Staff who discover inequities through peer channels (like Elinor/Noa) respond with demands; staff who don't discover them (like Atef) may simply leave

## Applicability

Applies to all compensation management at Telia'z Clinic Israel, particularly relevant as the clinic scales and adds more clinicians with varied rate structures.

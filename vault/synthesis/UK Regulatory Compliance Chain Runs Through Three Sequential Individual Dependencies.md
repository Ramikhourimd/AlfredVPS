# UK Regulatory Compliance Chain Runs Through Three Sequential Individual Dependencies

## Insight

The UK launch regulatory compliance path runs through three named individuals in strict sequence: Susan (manages CQC registration paperwork), Navin (prepares regulatory documentation), and Leon (holds the CQC license). Each person represents a serial single point of failure — if any one is unavailable or delayed, the entire regulatory track stalls. No backup personnel are documented for any of these roles.

## Evidence

In [[note/Telia'z Team Meeting UK Launch Patient Capacity and Recruitment 2026-02-15]], the paperwork flow is described: "CQC registration paperwork is managed by Susan; Nevin's paperwork is ready" and documents can only be exchanged "after contract signing." In [[note/Telia'z Team Meeting UK Launch and Operations Review 2026-02-15]], the same chain appears: "Navin (regulatory/paperwork person) — all paperwork ready on Navin's side, will exchange documents after contract signing."

## Implications

- Any single person's unavailability (illness, travel, communication gap) blocks the entire regulatory track
- The serial nature means delays compound rather than running in parallel
- No documented contingency if Susan, Navin, or Leon become unavailable
- This fragility is compounded by the existing finding that all UK commercial relationships depend on Adiel as single point of contact

## Applicability

Applies to all UK launch activities gated on CQC compliance. Also relevant as a pattern to watch in future international expansions where regulatory compliance may similarly depend on a small chain of individuals.

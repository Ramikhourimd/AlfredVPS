# Leon Partnership Deliberately Minimizes Clinical Integration Surface to ADHD SOPs Only

## Insight

Across five source records (two meeting notes and three independently created tasks), a consistent and deliberate pattern emerges: the only clinical artifact Telia'z shares with Leon is the ADHD-specific SOPs. All other clinical SOPs — general practice protocols, safeguarding, complaints, record-keeping — remain Leon's own. This is stated identically across records: "all other SOPs are his" / "all other SOPs are Leon's own" / "the ADHD flow is the only one that comes from Telia'z."

This represents a deliberate architectural choice: the clinical integration boundary is drawn at a single, well-defined artifact (ADHD SOPs), keeping the partnership loosely coupled and reducing regulatory complexity.

## Evidence

- Feb 15 meeting note (version 1): "Telia'z needs to share ADHD-specific SOPs with Leon; all other clinical SOPs are his own"
- Feb 15 meeting note (version 2): "SOPs: Telia'z needs to share its ADHD-specific SOPs with Leon; all other SOPs are his"
- Task "Share ADHD SOPs With Leon for UK Clinic": "All other SOPs are Leon's own. The ADHD flow is Telia'z's proprietary development"
- Task "Share ADHD SOPs With Leon for UK Operations": "the ADHD flow is the only one that comes from Telia'z"
- Task "Share ADHD SOPs With UK Partner Leon": "All other clinical SOPs are Leon's own"

## Implications

1. **Reduced regulatory entanglement**: Leon maintains full CQC compliance through his own SOPs; Telia'z only adds the ADHD-specific layer
2. **IP protection**: The ADHD SOPs represent Telia'z's core clinical IP — the boundary makes clear what is proprietary vs. what is Leon's
3. **Scalability**: This minimal-integration pattern could be replicated with other UK GPs without deep clinical integration work
4. **Risk**: If the ADHD SOPs conflict with Leon's existing protocols, the single integration point becomes a single point of failure

## Applicability

This pattern is specific to the Leon/Track 1 partnership model. The OMG and GP Confederation tracks have much deeper clinical integration requirements (triage handoffs, governance frameworks, tender compliance). The minimal-SOP approach may only work for the private patient track.

# Restructuring Discussion Shifted From Financial Founders to Operational Practitioners

## Insight

Between December 2025 and February 2026, the composition of restructuring discussions underwent a significant shift — from financially-oriented founders to operationally-focused practitioners. This participant evolution correlates directly with the transition from abstract financial mandates to concrete structural outcomes.

December 2025 meetings were dominated by Alex, Oren, and Dekkel — founders with a financial lens. They produced revenue targets, patient volume goals, and HMO expansion plans. Rami's clinical input was dismissed ("Alex immediately challenged the patient number calculations"). No operational structure emerged.

The February 22, 2026 meeting included Oren, Rami, Basel, Ziv (advisor), and Leah — but notably excluded Alex. This meeting produced the concrete district model, three-pillar management structure, pod scaling framework, and specific role definitions. The shift from "who controls the budget" to "who builds the structure" unlocked actual organizational design progress.

## Evidence

- **Dec 2025 goal-setting**: Alex/Oren/Dekkel set 2026 financial targets. Rami excluded from design, received targets "roughly a week prior without meaningful clinical input." (Rami Frustration Debrief)
- **Dec 2, 2025 private discussion**: Rami and Oren discuss CEO vacancy and HR issues privately — restructuring still a bilateral conversation. (HR Challenges note)
- **Feb 22, 2026 meeting**: Basel and Leah present district model. Ziv provides external validation. Rami actively participates. Alex absent. Concrete structural decisions emerge. (Both meeting notes)

## Implications

- Restructuring progress requires the people who will implement the structure to design it
- Alex's absence from the Feb meeting removed the pattern of methodological challenges that blocked previous presentations
- Basel and Ziv's presence provided operational credibility and external validation respectively, filling gaps that Rami alone could not
- Future implementation steps (board presentation to Alex) may regress if the participant composition reverts to founder-dominated

## Applicability

- Telia'z board presentation planning: the operational team should present jointly, not Rami alone
- Future restructuring iterations: maintain operational participant composition
- General pattern for founder-led companies: operational design should be led by operators, validated by founders — not the reverse

# CMO Clinical Medication Philosophy Prioritizes Simplification and Stability Over Escalation

## Insight

Across four psychiatric consultations on December 2, 2025, Rami Khouri (CMO) applied a consistent clinical medication philosophy with three distinct principles: (1) consolidate polypharmacy to monotherapy when clinically appropriate, (2) switch drug classes rather than escalate doses when the current class shows tolerance or failure, and (3) maintain stable regimens without adjustment when the patient reports improvement, even when theoretical optimization exists. This conservative simplification philosophy is evidence-based and directly relevant to the clinical protocols being designed under the two-domain protocol framework.

## Evidence

- **Residential patient (polypharmacy consolidation):** Patient on three concurrent psychotropics (Seroquel, Zyprexa, antidepressant). Rami recommended consolidating to Seroquel monotherapy — increasing Seroquel while gradually tapering Zyprexa and the antidepressant. Rationale: Seroquel alone addresses both mood stabilization and sleep, making the other medications redundant.
- **Outpatient SSRI non-responder (class switching):** Patient on Sertraline 50mg for years, previously failed Cipralex. Rather than increasing Sertraline dose, Rami recommended switching to Venlafaxine (SNRI) — a different pharmacological class acting on both serotonin and noradrenaline. Rationale: body has habituated to the SSRI family; a different mechanism can "refresh" therapeutic response.
- **Outpatient Clonex patient (stability maintenance):** Patient on Clonazepam reporting significant improvement. Despite potential for dose optimization, Rami explicitly decided against any changes — "even a small adjustment could destabilize the current positive state." Rationale: current stability is fragile and working; changes carry regression risk.

## Implications

- These three principles (simplify, switch class, maintain stability) should be encoded into the clinical protocol framework being developed under the two-domain model
- Training materials for new psychiatrists should explicitly articulate this philosophy
- The protocol framework's "clinical excellence" domain should measure adherence to evidence-based prescribing patterns, not just volume metrics
- The residency pipeline recruitment model can use this philosophy as a training foundation

## Applicability

Applies to all clinical protocol development at Telia'z Clinic Israel, particularly the clinical domain of the two-domain protocol framework. Also relevant to the Psychiatry Academy training curriculum being planned.

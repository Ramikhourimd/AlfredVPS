# Organizational Restructuring Designed as International Scaling Template

## Insight

The Telia'z organizational restructuring is not merely solving current operational issues — it is implicitly designing a franchise-like template for international replication. Every major structural decision (district model, shared services, CMO vs local Medical Director, pod structure) is being shaped by the requirement that it must work in the UK and beyond, not just in Israel.

## Evidence

Multiple records converge on this pattern:
- **District model** uses the term "district" specifically to imply replicable units, not a one-off structure
- **UK Expansion project** explicitly states the structure "must support replicating the Israeli clinic model internationally"
- **Shared services decision** centralizes HR, marketing, finance, and tech precisely so they can serve multiple districts
- **CMO at company level** is designed to oversee clinical standards "across all clinics"
- **Local Medical Director constraint** acknowledges each country needs its own clinical leadership
- **Pod structure** of ~10 psychiatrists is proposed as a scalable unit that works regardless of location

## Implications

1. Decisions about the Israeli clinic structure carry far more weight than they appear — they set precedent for every future market
2. "Good enough for Israel" is insufficient; the structure must be robust enough to survive different regulatory environments, cultures, and time zones
3. The shared services team needs to be built with multi-country support in mind from day one, not retrofitted later
4. Any deviation from the template for the Israeli clinic creates technical debt that compounds with each new market

## Applicability

This insight applies to all current restructuring decisions and should inform the evaluation criteria for every structural choice: "Does this work as a template, or only for Israel?"

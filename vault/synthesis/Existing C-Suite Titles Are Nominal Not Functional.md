# Existing C-Suite Titles Are Nominal Not Functional

## Insight

The HR system shows a fully populated C-suite — Dekkel as CEO, Oren as CFO, Roy Shur as COO, Rami as CMIO, Adi Lavi as CMO (Marketing), Alex Lellouche as Chief Strategy & Growth Officer, Shachar as VP R&D. Yet the restructuring discussions proceed as if these roles need to be created, defined, or filled. This reveals that the existing C-suite is nominal: titles were assigned during company growth without corresponding functional authority, decision rights, or operational scope. The company has the appearance of professional management structure while actually operating through informal founder dynamics.

## Evidence

1. **Roy Shur as COO**: Listed in both December 2025 and February 2026 rosters, yet the Feb 22 meeting debates COO authority without referencing him. His operational scope is invisible in restructuring discussions.

2. **Dekkel as CEO**: HR lists Dekkel as "CEO & Head Scientist," yet the Dec 2, 2025 discussion between Rami and Oren explicitly identifies a CEO vacuum — Rami claims to perform over 50% of CEO functions while Dekkel focuses on science.

3. **Rami as CMIO**: Already titled "Chief Medical Officer & Head of Innovation" in HR, yet the Feb 22 meeting debates whether to create a CMO role and what authority it should have.

4. **Adi Lavi as CMO (Marketing)**: A "Chief Marketing Officer" exists, yet the restructuring discusses CMO as a medical role — creating title collision between marketing CMO and medical CMO.

5. **Role-defining meetings**: The Feb 22 meeting's core purpose — defining C-level roles and authority — would be unnecessary if existing titles carried real authority.

## Implications

This pattern means that: (1) Formal titles cannot be used as evidence of actual authority or responsibility — always verify through behavioral evidence, not HR data. (2) The restructuring is not adding new roles but rather attempting to make existing nominal roles functional. (3) Any new titles created during restructuring risk the same fate — becoming nominal without enforcement mechanisms. (4) The company likely needs governance documentation (role charters, decision rights matrices) more than it needs new titles.

## Applicability

This insight applies to any organizational restructuring assessment at Telia'z. When evaluating who "holds" a role, always verify through operational evidence (who makes decisions, who is consulted, who has budget authority) rather than HR title data. This also applies to the board presentation planning — presenting the restructuring as "new roles" may be misleading when many roles already exist in title.

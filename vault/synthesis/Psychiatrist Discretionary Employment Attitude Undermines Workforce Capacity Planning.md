# Psychiatrist Discretionary Employment Attitude Undermines Workforce Capacity Planning

## Insight

Across multiple operational records from November 2025 through February 2026, a consistent behavioral pattern emerges: psychiatrists at Telia'z treat their clinic position as a discretionary side activity rather than core employment. This attitude — distinct from burnout or dissatisfaction — fundamentally undermines the clinic's workforce capacity planning, which assumes part-timers will grow into larger positions.

## Evidence

Three independent data points converge:

1. **Atef (Nov 2025)**: Logged only 20 hours/month despite management requests to increase. Explicitly frames his participation as "doing Rami a favor." The language of favor implies voluntarism, not employment obligation.

2. **Eliezer (Nov 2025)**: A newly hired psychiatrist who requested to "start as late as possible while keeping the position reserved." He has concurrent commitments (research project in China, innovation center, Stage B exam) and can only offer 25 hours. His professional identity centers on research and career advancement, not clinical telepsychiatry.

3. **Strategic projections (Dec 2025)**: Rami's data analysis projects needing ~176 individual quarter-position hires. This calculation already accounts for the quarter-position model but does not account for the additional reality that existing quarter-position holders resist growing their hours.

4. **Underreported hours (Dec 2025)**: Psychiatrists underreport working hours — arriving early, reviewing AI summaries after shifts — suggesting they invest more than reported but don't want the hours formally tracked. This paradox (doing more work than reported, yet resisting formal hour increases) suggests the issue is about maintaining perceived freedom, not actual workload.

## Implications

The clinic's scaling arithmetic has a hidden multiplier. If even 30% of hired psychiatrists stagnate at minimal hours rather than growing their positions, the effective hiring target increases proportionally. Combined with the existing recruitment pipeline bottlenecks (careers page delays, marketing dependencies, onboarding complexity), this behavioral pattern creates a compounding capacity deficit.

Potential responses include:
- **Contractual minimum hour escalation clauses** at hire
- **Incentive structures** tied to hour growth (bonuses for reaching hour thresholds)
- **Accepting stagnation as baseline** and adjusting hiring targets upward by 30-50%
- **Differentiating recruitment channels**: targeting psychiatrists seeking primary employment vs. side gigs

## Applicability

Applies to all current and future psychiatrist recruitment at Telia'z Clinic Israel, and potentially to the UK expansion clinic where the same quarter-position model may be adopted. Also relevant for case manager hiring, though evidence is weaker for that group.

# Restructuring Plans Treat Existing Org Chart as Blank Slate

## Insight

Despite the HR system showing six active C-level and VP titles — Dekkel (CEO), Oren (CFO), Roy Shur (COO), Rami (CMIO), Adi Lavi (CMO), Alex (Chief Strategy & Growth Officer), plus Shachar (VP R&D) and Adiel Levin (VP Business Development) — the February 2026 restructuring discussions design the organizational hierarchy from scratch. The team debates whether to create a CMO role (Rami already holds CMIO), whether to separate CMO from COO (Roy Shur already holds COO), and how to structure C-level reporting — all as if no existing title assignments constrain the design space.

This is not merely a title-reality mismatch (already documented). It is a behavioral pattern: the leadership team treats the formal org chart as ceremonial infrastructure with no binding force on organizational design. The restructuring conversation operates in a parallel reality where positions are designed based on operational need and interpersonal dynamics, not formal authority.

## Evidence

1. **Roy Shur (COO)**: Listed as Chief Operating Officer in both December 2025 and February 2026 HR exports. Entirely absent from all restructuring discussions about operational leadership. Basel (a recent hire) is positioned as operations leader without any reference to Roy's existing mandate.

2. **Rami (CMIO)**: Listed as "Chief Medical Officer & Head of Innovation" since at least December 2025. Yet the February 22 meeting treats "CMO" as a role to be created and debates whether Rami should receive it, as though he doesn't already hold the title.

3. **Adi Lavi (CMO)**: Listed as "Chief Marketing Officer" — sharing the CMO acronym with Rami's Chief Medical Officer title. Neither the naming collision nor Adi's existing authority over marketing is addressed in the restructuring discussions.

4. **Dekkel (CEO)**: Holds the CEO title but is assessed by Rami and Oren as lacking strategic planning ability. The restructuring implicitly designs around a CEO vacuum while Dekkel formally occupies the seat.

## Implications

1. **Formal titles carry no organizational weight** — they are HR system artifacts, not authority grants. New hires or external observers relying on the org chart would fundamentally misunderstand who holds power.

2. **Restructuring outcomes are unpredictable** — if the team can design a new structure ignoring existing titles, any agreed-upon structure could similarly be ignored in practice.

3. **International expansion risk** — the UK operation will need clear, legally binding role definitions. A culture where titles are decorative will not translate to a regulated international environment.

4. **Employee expectations are misaligned** — individuals holding C-level titles may believe they have authority that the organization does not operationally recognize.

## Applicability

This pattern is specific to Telia'z's founder-stage culture where relationships and informal authority override formal structure. It directly impacts the organizational restructuring project and will become a critical risk factor for the UK expansion where formal governance structures must be legally sound.

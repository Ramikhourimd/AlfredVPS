# Informal Staff Feedback Channels Outperform Formal Surveys for Actionable Intelligence

## Insight

The clinic's formal staff satisfaction survey program has failed to produce usable data (response rates too low for statistical validity, ~16% participation). Yet informal feedback collected by Rivi Idelman through personal conversations yielded immediately actionable intelligence — specifically, that some staff members are actively discouraging prospective applicants from joining the clinic. This informal finding triggered an urgent response at the 2026-02-15 team meeting, while months of formal survey efforts produced nothing actionable.

## Evidence

- **Formal channel failure:** Staff satisfaction surveys had response rates too low for statistical meaning (~16%). Multiple constraint records document this limitation.
- **Informal channel success:** Rivi Idelman's informal feedback collection surfaced that staff (including case managers) were telling applicants not to work at the clinic. This was reported by Adi Lavi at the 2026-02-15 team meeting.
- **Action differential:** The informal findings immediately triggered task creation (investigate and address staff satisfaction), while formal survey data led to no actions.

## Implications

- The clinic should invest in structured but informal feedback mechanisms (1:1 check-ins, peer conversations) rather than relying solely on formal surveys
- Rivi Idelman's role as informal feedback collector is operationally valuable and should be recognized
- Any future staff satisfaction measurement should supplement surveys with informal intelligence gathering
- Low survey response rates may themselves be a signal of disengagement

## Applicability

Applies to all Telia'z staff engagement and retention initiatives. Particularly relevant as the clinic prioritizes workforce stabilization before aggressive recruitment.

# Clinic Israel Restructuring Spans All Organisational Layers Simultaneously

## Insight

The three active tasks reveal that Telia'z Clinic Israel is restructuring across every organisational layer at the same time: clinical leadership (Medical Director hire), operational management (Basel's operational plan), and frontline staffing (secretary team expansion). This is not a phased rollout — all three layers are changing concurrently.

## Evidence

- **Leadership layer**: Hiring a Medical Director to implement the district model for clinical supervision ([[task/Hire Medical Director for Clinic Israel]])
- **Management layer**: Basel preparing a standalone operational plan with KPIs and staffing timeline ([[task/Basel to Present Clinic Operational Plan]])
- **Frontline layer**: Hiring 2+ secretaries while Renee transitions to management and another secretary goes on maternity leave ([[task/Finalize Secretary Team Structure and Hiring]])

## Implications

- High change management risk — simultaneous changes at every level can create coordination gaps
- Dependencies between layers may surface: e.g., the secretary model (pod vs. shared) may depend on how the district model groups psychiatrists
- Basel's operational plan should account for all three layers and their interdependencies
- Onboarding new staff (Medical Director + secretaries) while restructuring management creates a period of organisational instability

## Applicability

Specific to Telia'z Clinic Israel during the current restructuring period (Q1 2026). The pattern of multi-layer simultaneous change is a general risk factor worth monitoring in any organisational transformation.

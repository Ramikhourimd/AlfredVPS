# Strict Multi-Criteria Filter Creates Extremely Narrow Housing Search Funnel

## Insight

The Khouri family's housing search applies at least 10 simultaneous hard or near-hard filters, creating an extremely narrow funnel where very few properties qualify. Each filter individually is reasonable, but their intersection dramatically limits options — explaining why the search has been prolonged and why the family-network sub-market (with its below-market pricing) is practically the only viable channel.

## Evidence

Filters accumulated across all source records, each demonstrated through specific property rejections or stated requirements:

1. **Arab community proximity** — wife explicitly stated isolation is "the thing that bothers me the most" (hard filter)
2. **Kitchen ventilation / natural light** — Property 2 (126sqm + 37sqm balcony) declined specifically due to kitchen without window (hard filter)
3. **Minimum ~120 sqm** — 75 sqm property rejected as "not logical"; 115 sqm property dismissed as "small" (hard filter)
4. **3-4 bedrooms + home office** — stated requirement for growing family (hard filter)
5. **Open/large kitchen layout** — stated preference during apartment viewing (strong preference)
6. **Balcony privacy** — concern raised about exposed balconies (moderate filter)
7. **Large outdoor space** — 37 sqm balcony rated as "strong positive" (strong preference)
8. **Walkable, lively neighborhood** — empty streets at night cited as a negative (moderate filter)
9. **Street parking** — raised as concern during viewings (soft filter)
10. **Budget constraint** — 1,200-1,500 NIS/month rental OR ~850K purchase (hard filter)
11. **Home clinic potential** — ground floor clinical space noted as "significant advantage" (strong preference)

On 2025-10-09 alone, three properties were evaluated and all were found lacking on at least one hard criterion despite meeting others.

## Implications

- The family-network sub-market (1,200-1,500 NIS/month in family-owned buildings) may be the *only* channel where budget-qualifying properties exist — open market rentals of 8,000-12,000 NIS/month are 5-8x above budget
- Relaxing even one filter (e.g., accepting a kitchen renovation project, or expanding geographic range) could dramatically increase options
- The search is likely to remain prolonged unless the family explicitly prioritizes and trades off between criteria
- The Maghar land development option is the only path that could satisfy all criteria simultaneously (custom build), but faces its own capital and timeline constraints

## Applicability

This funnel analysis applies to any future property evaluation. It can be used to: (1) quickly screen properties before viewing, (2) have an explicit conversation about which filters are truly hard vs. negotiable, (3) assess whether the Maghar build option deserves more serious pursuit given how constrained the rental/purchase market is.

# Remote Telepsychiatry Creates Structural Isolation Beyond Communication Fixes

## Insight

Across four independent meeting notes from staff interviews, a consistent pattern emerges: clinical staff isolation at Telia'z is not primarily a communication tools problem — it is a structural consequence of the remote telepsychiatry work model itself. Staff work alone from home, facing a screen with patients, with minimal peer interaction even when sharing a pod with other clinicians. Improving communication tools (WhatsApp, internal task systems, Slack) addresses symptoms but does not resolve the underlying structural isolation.

## Evidence

1. **"Most staff do not feel part of a team"** — reported across multiple interview notes, consistent across both psychiatrists and case managers
2. **"Orphaned children without parents"** — a psychiatrist's metaphor for feeling unsupported and alone in clinical decisions
3. **Working alongside others doesn't create interaction** — even when paired with another psychiatrist on a case, there is little need for routine discussion
4. **Group supervision is the only reported counterforce** — the single positive intervention mentioned is peer supervision, which introduces structured human contact
5. **WhatsApp messages go unanswered** — but the problem isn't the channel; it's that the work model doesn't create natural reasons for interaction
6. **Internal task system may worsen isolation** — by replacing the informal social function of WhatsApp groups with structured, transactional communication

## Implications

- Communication tool changes (WhatsApp to Slack, task systems) will not solve isolation — they may even worsen it by removing informal social touchpoints
- Interventions must create structured human contact: group supervision, team meetings, peer case discussions, social events
- The 6-8 hour threshold for sustainable work may partly reflect isolation tolerance, not just workload intensity
- Future system design should consider features that create ambient team presence or facilitate spontaneous peer interaction

## Applicability

This pattern likely applies to any remote clinical work model, not just Telia'z. It is especially relevant for:
- Staffing model design (ensuring clinicians have structured peer contact)
- Product roadmap (building social features into the clinical platform)
- Recruitment messaging (acknowledging and addressing isolation proactively)

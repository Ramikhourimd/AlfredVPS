# UK Launch Follows Deliberate Minimum Viable Clinic Strategy

## Insight

Across the February 2026 team meeting records, a consistent pattern emerges: the UK clinic launch is being structured as a minimum viable clinic (MVC) — deliberately accepting known gaps and temporary solutions to get operational quickly, with plans to iterate post-launch. This is not accidental; it is a deliberate strategic choice to prioritize market entry speed over service completeness.

## Evidence

The MVC pattern manifests across five dimensions:

1. **Regulatory**: Operating under Leon's CQC registration rather than obtaining own (faster but dependent)
2. **Staffing**: Single Israeli case manager (Bassem) as stopgap rather than waiting for UK recruitment
3. **Product**: Launching without prescription module or scheduling system — both flagged as critical but not blocking launch
4. **Clinical documentation**: UK report formats still being defined as of Feb 15, targeting same March 31 deadline
5. **Marketing**: Google Ads requiring separate UK approval, campaign preparation dependent on contract signing with only 2-week lead time

Each of these represents a conscious trade-off: speed of market entry over operational maturity. The team acknowledges each gap but proceeds with the timeline.

## Implications

The MVC approach creates a narrow launch window where the service is operational but incomplete. Success depends on:
- Leon's willingness to manually bridge gaps (e.g., prescriptions)
- Low initial patient volume (which the single Israeli case manager can handle)
- Rapid post-launch iteration to add missing features
- Patient tolerance for an incomplete digital experience

If patient volume ramps faster than feature development, the gaps become service failures rather than acceptable MVPs.

## Applicability

This pattern may repeat for future international expansions — understanding whether the UK MVC approach succeeds or fails will inform the expansion playbook. The related synthesis on "converging dependencies creating schedule risk" captures the risk dimension; this synthesis captures the strategic intent behind accepting that risk.

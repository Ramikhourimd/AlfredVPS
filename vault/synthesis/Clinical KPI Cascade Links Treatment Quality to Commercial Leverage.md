# Clinical KPI Cascade Links Treatment Quality to Commercial Leverage

## Insight

Rami articulated a seven-step cascade connecting clinical treatment quality directly to commercial bargaining power with HMOs. This chain is not intuitively obvious to non-clinical leadership but provides the core argument for why investing in clinical protocols (training, safety, quality) produces business results:

1. Better treatment response (תגובת טיפול יותר טובה)
2. Reduced recurrence/relapse rates (הורדה של אחוזי רמיסיה)
3. Fewer hospitalizations (הורדת אחוזי אישפוזים)
4. Fewer ER visits (הורדת פניות למיון)
5. Lower public health system costs (שיפור בעלויות/נטל מערכת בריאות ציבורית)
6. Better HMO contract terms (שיפור בהסכמים)
7. Increased commercial leverage (עליה בלחץ)

This is the "television story" — the narrative that makes clinical quality investments legible to business leadership.

## Evidence

- Directly articulated by Rami during the 2025-12-04 brainstorming session
- Consistent with the hypergrowth model Rami had been developing
- Aligns with existing telepsychiatry outcomes literature showing that better treatment adherence reduces downstream health system costs
- The cascade mirrors value-based care models used in HMO negotiations

## Implications

1. **Board presentations** should always lead with the cascade — clinical quality is not an expense, it is the driver of better contracts
2. **Protocol investment** (training, safety, communication) is justified as revenue-enabling, not cost-adding
3. **KPI tracking** should measure each step of the cascade, not just endpoint revenue
4. **HMO negotiations** should use population health outcomes data as leverage

## Applicability

- Directly applicable to Telia'z Clinic Israel HMO negotiations (Maccabi, Clalit)
- Replicable for international expansion (UK and beyond) as the same cascade logic applies in any value-based care model
- Supports the VP Medical & Innovation role definition by connecting clinical leadership to business outcomes

# External Training Partnerships Serve as Dual Recruitment and Positioning Strategy

## Insight

Across multiple independent initiatives, Telia'z consistently uses external training and education partnerships as a dual-purpose strategy: simultaneously upskilling clinical staff and positioning the organization as an innovation leader for recruitment. This is not a one-off tactic but a repeating organizational pattern.

## Evidence

1. **Psychiatry Academy** (Feb 2026): Launched as a structured training program with explicit dual purpose — upskilling existing clinicians on tech-enabled psychiatry AND creating an attractive recruitment value proposition. First cohort enrollment by early March 2026.

2. **Arab Healthcare Innovation Program** (Sep 2025): Rami engaged a medical innovation training program for Arab healthcare professionals through 4 simultaneous channels — guest speaking, staff enrollment, syllabus review, and course collaboration exploration. Each channel serves both learning and positioning.

3. **AI Course Collaboration with Zaher Elias** (Sep 2025): Exploration of a joint advanced AI course with an external medical professional, combining knowledge sharing with network expansion.

## Implications

This pattern suggests Telia'z views education not as a cost center but as a strategic asset with compounding returns. Future innovation initiatives should consider training/education components as a default design element, not an afterthought. The organization's competitive moat may increasingly depend on being seen as the place where clinicians learn cutting-edge practice.

## Applicability

Applies to all Telia'z Innovation Program initiatives, recruitment strategy, and brand positioning. Particularly relevant when evaluating new partnership opportunities — those with training/education components likely align better with organizational DNA.

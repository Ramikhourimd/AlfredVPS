# Leadership Acquisition Focus Misaligned With Operational Efficiency Requirements

## Insight

A persistent pattern across multiple December 2025 sessions reveals a structural misalignment between Telia'z company leadership and the clinic operations team. Leadership (Dekkel, Oren, Alex) consistently prioritizes acquisition metrics — new patient counts, Facebook campaign ROI, monthly revenue — while Rami and Basel have identified that deeper operational and clinical efficiency indicators are what actually determine the clinic's ability to scale sustainably. This is not a one-time disagreement but a recurring friction visible across strategic meetings, brainstorming sessions, and board presentations.

## Evidence

1. **Strategic meeting (2025-12-04):** Rami described Alex's 2026 goals presentation as a "crossroads" — purely financial targets with clinical sustainability subordinated. "Under clinical excellence, profitability below 40% was listed."
2. **Protocol brainstorm sessions (2025-12-04, multiple):** Rami repeatedly stated the core problem: "The important things we are not tracking — if I can define them now, the year will be much easier." He identified that every Sunday management meeting is frustrating because leadership focuses on surface metrics.
3. **Protocol framework motivation:** The entire protocol framework exercise was motivated by needing to "bridge the gap between operational team knowledge and leadership decision-making priorities." Rami explicitly said: if he cannot articulate this compellingly, leadership will continue with their current approach.
4. **Board presentation task:** A formal task was created to compile the protocol framework into a persuasion document — indicating the misalignment is significant enough to require a formal intervention.
5. **Basel's prioritization decision:** Basel was directed to prioritize international expansion over clinic budget, further demonstrating leadership's growth-first orientation.

## Implications

- The clinic operations team is building a counter-narrative (protocol framework, KPI cascade, clinical-to-revenue causal chain) to redirect leadership attention
- If this persuasion effort fails, operational improvements will be under-resourced while acquisition spending continues
- The misalignment creates execution risk: the clinic is being pushed to grow (7,000+ patients) before operational foundations are stable
- Sunday management meetings will remain ineffective until leadership adopts the operational metrics Rami is proposing

## Applicability

This pattern applies directly to Telia'z Clinic Israel and potentially to Telia'z's broader organizational decision-making culture. Similar dynamics may emerge in any future clinic operations (international expansion) where leadership financial targets outpace operational readiness.

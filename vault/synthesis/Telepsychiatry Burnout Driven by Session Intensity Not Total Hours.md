# Telepsychiatry Burnout Driven by Session Intensity Not Total Hours

## Insight

Across multiple independent staff interviews with both case managers and psychiatrists, a consistent pattern emerges: telepsychiatry burnout is primarily driven by the cognitive intensity per hour of session work, not by total weekly hours. This distinguishes telepsychiatry burnout from traditional clinical burnout where total hours worked is typically the dominant factor.

## Evidence

1. **Case manager vs. residency comparison**: One case manager reported feeling more exhausted after Telia'z shifts than after hospital residency shifts — despite residency sometimes having longer hours. The intensity of back-to-back telepsychiatry sessions was described as "uniquely draining."

2. **Empathy degradation at higher hours**: A staff member described being "much more empathetic and attentive" at 5 hours versus 9 hours — the quality of clinical engagement degrades with session density.

3. **Session concentration matters more than total hours**: Psychiatrist Sa'id found 3-4 hours daily across 5 days far more draining than 1.5 hours daily. Even concentrating the same total weekly hours into one long block would be unacceptable — it's the per-session cognitive load, not weekly volume.

4. **6-8 hour weekly threshold**: Multiple staff independently converged on 6-8 hours per week as a sustainability boundary, regardless of how those hours are distributed.

## Implications

- **Scheduling policy**: Optimize for shorter, spaced sessions rather than long blocks. Spreading hours across more days at lower daily intensity may be more sustainable than fewer days with longer shifts.
- **Capacity planning**: Per-clinician throughput in telepsychiatry is inherently lower than in-person models. Recruitment projections must account for this.
- **Burnout interventions**: Traditional approaches (reducing total hours, adding vacation) may be less effective than reducing per-session density (shorter shifts, breaks between patients, administrative buffer time).
- **AI opportunity**: If AI tools can reduce documentation burden during/after sessions, this could lower the effective intensity per hour and extend the sustainable workload threshold.

## Applicability

Applies to all telepsychiatry staff at Telia'z Clinic Israel. May generalize to other remote mental health platforms, but this synthesis is based specifically on Telia'z staff data from February 2026.

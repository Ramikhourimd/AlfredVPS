# Telepsychiatry Burnout Driven by Intensity Per Hour Not Total Hours

## Insight

Across multiple staff interviews (both case managers and psychiatrists), a consistent pattern emerges: burnout in telepsychiatry is driven primarily by the intensity of each working hour, not by total weekly hours. Staff report disproportionate exhaustion from telepsychiatry compared to traditional clinical settings with longer hours, and shorter concentrated sessions are strongly preferred over longer blocks even at equal weekly totals.

## Evidence

Four independent notes from the 2026-02-18 meeting converge on this finding:

1. **Case manager testimony**: Described leaving Telia'z shifts feeling "more exhausted than residency days" despite residency having longer hours — the unique intensity of back-to-back telepsychiatry sessions is distinctly draining
2. **Psychiatrist testimony (Sa'id)**: Working 3-4 hours daily across 5 days was "very draining," but 1.5 hours daily worked much better — he would not compress the same total into one long day
3. **Self-report pattern**: A case manager noted feeling "much more empathetic and attentive" at 5 hours versus 9 hours — suggesting quality of care degrades nonlinearly with session length
4. **Threshold pattern**: Multiple staff converge on 6-8 hours per week as a manageable threshold; beyond it, exhaustion escalates disproportionately

## Implications

- Scheduling optimization should target shorter, more frequent shifts rather than consolidated blocks
- Staff recruitment messaging should set expectations about intensity, not just hours
- The planned employee satisfaction survey should capture both total hours AND session duration/pattern to validate this quantitatively
- Interventions like group supervision appear to partially buffer the burnout effect, suggesting social support mechanisms matter alongside schedule design

## Applicability

This pattern likely applies across all remote telepsychiatry settings with high-frequency patient contact, not just Telia'z. Could inform broader industry guidance on sustainable telepsychiatry practice design.

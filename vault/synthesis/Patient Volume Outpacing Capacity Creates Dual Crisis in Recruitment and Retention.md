# Patient Volume Outpacing Capacity Creates Dual Crisis in Recruitment and Retention

## Insight

Clinic Israel faces a self-reinforcing crisis: patient demand (945/month) far exceeds intake capacity (77/month, 8%), creating pressure for rapid hiring — but rapid hiring without adequate onboarding and support degrades staff experience, which in turn makes recruitment harder. Evidence from this meeting shows staff actively discouraging new hires from joining, confirming the satisfaction problem is already impacting the recruitment pipeline.

## Evidence

1. **Dashboard data (Feb 2026):** 945 new patients, 77 intakes, 8.1% conversion — a 12:1 demand-to-capacity ratio
2. **Staff satisfaction signals:** Rivi Idelman's data shows some staff telling prospective hires "I wouldn't recommend working here"
3. **Onboarding bottleneck:** 4 new staff in pipeline haven't opened hours yet; onboarding takes too long
4. **Rami's capacity analysis:** The clinic needs to fundamentally shift from retention-heavy to intake-heavy model
5. **Adiel's observation:** At current conversion rates, capacity would need to multiply 25x to serve current demand in-month

## Implications

- **Cannot solve with hiring alone** — need both supply-side (new clinicians) and demand-side (discharge framework) interventions
- **Marketing expansion is counterproductive** until capacity catches up — yet UK launch will add more demand
- **Clinician satisfaction must be stabilized** before recruitment marketing can work — the "academy" and "tech-forward" positioning Rami proposed addresses this
- **The growth-vs-stabilization tension is reaching a critical inflection point** — the February data makes the scale of the mismatch concrete for the first time

## Applicability

Applies to both Clinic Israel operations and UK expansion planning. The UK clinic should be designed with capacity management built in from day one.

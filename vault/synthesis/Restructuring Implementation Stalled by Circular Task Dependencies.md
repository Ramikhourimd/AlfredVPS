# Restructuring Implementation Stalled by Circular Task Dependencies

## Insight

The seven tasks spawned from the Feb 22 leadership meeting form a web of circular dependencies that prevents any single task from being completed independently. The org chart can't be finalized without role definitions; roles can't be defined without the Medical Director hire; the hire can't proceed without the org chart; the financial plan can't be validated without knowing the structure; the board can't approve without the financial plan; and nothing moves until the board approves.

## Evidence

Dependency chain analysis across tasks:
- **Finalize Org Chart** requires: role definitions, resolved staff allocation debates
- **Hire Medical Director** requires: finalized org chart, budget approval
- **Define Rami Role** requires: Medical Director hired (to hand off duties), org chart finalized
- **Review Financial Plan** requires: structure defined (to model costs)
- **Present to Board** requires: financial projections, org chart, role definitions, clinic operational plan
- **Define CMO Role Proposal** requires: agreement on CMO vs COO boundary
- **Define Role Expectations** requires: org chart, shared services boundary decisions

The conversation outcome confirms: "No final decisions were made. Several action items were assigned."

## Implications

Without a deliberate decision to break the cycle — by declaring one task as the "anchor" that proceeds on best-available assumptions — the restructuring risks indefinite delay. The most likely cycle-breaker is the org chart: if leadership commits to a chart based on current conceptual agreements, other tasks can proceed in parallel.

## Applicability

This pattern is common in organizational restructuring. It applies directly to the Telia'z Organizational Restructuring project and indirectly to the UK Expansion (which depends on the Israel model being proven first).

# HealthyMind Academy Delivers Frontier Clinical Training Serving Education Recruitment and Retention

## Insight

The HealthyMind Academy training day (2025-11-12) reveals that the Academy's actual content operates at the clinical frontier — covering QEEG brain mapping, MDMA/psilocybin therapeutics, genetic risk factors and epigenetics, neurostimulation protocols, and sensory processing in autism. This is not standard continuing medical education; it positions Telia'z as offering intellectually stimulating professional development that serves three simultaneous functions: (1) clinical education that improves care quality, (2) recruitment differentiation that attracts clinicians seeking growth beyond routine practice, and (3) retention through professional stimulation that counteracts the documented telepsychiatry burnout and reduced clinical variety.

## Evidence

Six lecture recordings from a single 2025-11-12 training day demonstrate the breadth and depth:

1. **QEEG Brain Mapping and Neurostimulation** — A Beer Sheva clinic processing 1,000 patients/month using QEEG-guided treatment. Covers quantitative EEG, Fourier analysis, normative comparison, and targeted neurostimulation
2. **History of Psychedelics in Psychiatry** — From 1950s research through current clinical trials
3. **MDMA and Psilocybin Therapeutic Mechanisms** — Specific pharmacological mechanisms and clinical application frameworks
4. **PTSD Intensive Treatment and MDMA Clinical Application** — Advanced treatment protocols beyond standard CBT/EMDR
5. **Genetic Risk Factors, Folate Metabolism, and Autism** — Epigenetic lens on environmental risk factors, MTHFR gene variants, heavy metals, plastics
6. **Sensory Processing and Autism in Clinical Practice** — Practical clinical application of sensory processing frameworks

## Implications

- The Academy directly addresses the documented problem of reduced professional variety after the Maccabi transition — clinicians who feel intellectually understimulated by routine Maccabi patients get frontier-level education
- Academy content creates institutional knowledge that differentiates Telia'z from commodity telepsychiatry providers
- The content quality supports the recruitment argument: clinicians joining Telia'z access training they cannot easily get elsewhere
- Group in-person training days also address the documented isolation problem of fully remote work
- The investment in Academy content should be tracked as a retention metric, not just a training expense

## Applicability

- Recruitment marketing: Academy content can be highlighted in job postings and careers page to attract quality clinicians
- Retention strategy: Regular Academy events serve as professional development and social bonding for remote staff
- Clinical quality: Frontier knowledge (QEEG, psychedelics, genetics) may eventually open new service lines or improve diagnostic precision
- The triple function (education + recruitment + retention) makes Academy investment defensible even if any single function alone wouldn't justify the cost

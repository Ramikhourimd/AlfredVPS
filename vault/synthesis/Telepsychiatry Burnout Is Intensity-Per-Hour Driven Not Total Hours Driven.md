# Telepsychiatry Burnout Is Intensity-Per-Hour Driven Not Total Hours Driven

## Insight

Across multiple staff interviews reported in the 2026-02-18 meeting notes, a consistent pattern emerges: burnout in telepsychiatry is driven primarily by intensity per hour of work, not by total weekly hours. The constant flow of back-to-back patient interactions, emotional load, and documentation demands creates a uniquely draining per-hour experience that exceeds even hospital residency in perceived exhaustion — despite residency involving longer absolute hours.

## Evidence

Four overlapping source records from the 2026-02-18 Rami-Rivi meeting capture consistent reports:

1. **Case manager comparison to residency**: One case manager described leaving Telia'z shifts feeling far more exhausted than after hospital residency shifts, even when residency hours were longer. The intensity per hour, not total hours, is the differentiating factor.

2. **Psychiatrist schedule optimization**: Sa'id found 3-4 hours daily across 5 days draining, but 1.5 hours daily sustainable — same weekly total, radically different experience. This points to per-session intensity as the key variable.

3. **Empathy degradation curve**: A case manager reported being "much more empathetic and attentive" at 5 hours versus 9 hours, suggesting a nonlinear degradation in clinical quality as intensity accumulates.

4. **Threshold convergence**: Multiple staff independently converged on 6-8 hours/week as a sustainable boundary, regardless of how those hours are distributed.

## Implications

- Scheduling optimization should focus on session spacing and daily caps, not just weekly totals
- Burnout measurement instruments must capture per-session intensity, not just aggregate hours
- The 6-8 hour weekly threshold assumption may be a proxy for an underlying intensity limit
- Workforce planning should model for more clinicians at fewer hours each, rather than fewer clinicians at more hours
- This pattern likely applies beyond Telia'z to telepsychiatry broadly and may have publication value

## Applicability

- Directly applicable to Telia'z Clinic Israel scheduling and workforce planning
- Informs the staff satisfaction survey instrument design (segmentation by hours and intensity)
- Relevant to Psychiatry Academy curriculum (teaching sustainable practice patterns)
- Potential research publication topic for the innovation team's academic output pipeline

# Information Asymmetry Functions as Informal Power Structure at Telia'z

## Insight

The recurring information flow problems at Telia'z are not merely communication failures — they function as an informal power structure that determines who can influence decisions and who cannot. Multiple source records reveal a consistent pattern: access to information correlates directly with decision-making authority, and restricting information access effectively constrains influence regardless of formal titles or expertise.

## Evidence

Five distinct information asymmetry mechanisms operate simultaneously:

1. **Agenda gatekeeping**: Sunday leadership meetings are structured exclusively around marketing and business development topics. Clinical operations, quality metrics, and staff wellbeing have no standing agenda slot — effectively locking Rami's primary domain out of the regular decision-making forum.

2. **Rumor-based communication**: Rami learns about organizational changes (e.g., "four secretary hires") through secondhand rumors that prove inaccurate. He explicitly requested direct involvement — "asked not to hear about organizational changes secondhand" — yet the pattern persists, leaving him reactive rather than participatory.

3. **Methodology delegitimization**: When Rami presents data-driven clinical plans, Alex immediately challenges his calculation methodology rather than engaging with the substance. This shifts the burden of proof onto the presenter and effectively vetoes proposals without counter-evidence.

4. **Document territoriality**: Li Yamin's pattern of withholding employment agreements from Basel — claiming it's "not her responsibility" — demonstrates information as territorial control. Documents become leverage rather than shared organizational resources.

5. **Commitment without mechanism**: Oren repeatedly commits to including Rami in cross-functional meetings and decision processes, but no structural mechanism (standing invitation, agenda slot, escalation path) is created to enforce these commitments. The verbal promise substitutes for actual access.

## Implications

The information asymmetry pattern means that formal role definitions alone cannot resolve the governance vacuum. Even if Rami receives a CMO title with defined authority, without structural changes to information flow — mandatory clinical agenda items, direct communication channels, shared document access — the same exclusion dynamics will persist under new labels.

Any restructuring plan that addresses titles and reporting lines without addressing information architecture will reproduce the current dysfunction in a reorganized form.

## Applicability

This pattern applies to:
- The CMO role design (what information rights come with the title)
- The shared services boundary design (who has visibility into what)
- The steering committee proposal (agenda-setting power)
- Future international expansion structures (risk of replicating the pattern in new clinics)

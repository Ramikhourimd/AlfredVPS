# Oren Mediation Produces Verbal Commitments Without Structural Change

## Insight

Across multiple records spanning December 2025 to February 2026, Oren Taliaz follows a consistent mediation pattern: he acknowledges problems raised by Rami, makes verbal commitments to intervene, but no structural changes follow. In December 2025, Oren committed to speaking with Li Yamin about her obstruction pattern and to keeping Rami in the loop on organizational decisions. By February 2026, Li Yamin's behavioral pattern remained unchanged and Rami still reported being excluded from cross-functional meetings. Oren then made the same inclusion commitment again. This pattern suggests Oren's mediation style substitutes verbal reassurance for organizational action.

## Evidence

- **Dec 2, 2025**: Oren spoke with Li Yamin around 5 PM, asked her to cooperate and be patient for 1-3 months. Separately committed to keeping Rami informed about organizational changes. (Source: [[conversation/Telia'z Management Discussion HR Leadership and CEO Role 2025-12-02]])
- **Dec 5, 2025**: Three days later, Rami's debrief reveals no structural changes — Sunday meetings still exclude clinical operations, no formal mechanism created for Rami's inclusion. (Source: [[conversation/Rami Personal Debrief on Leadership Frustrations 2025-12-05]])
- **Feb 22, 2026**: Nearly three months later, Rami still excluded from cross-functional meetings. Li Yamin role suitability task still open. Oren commits again to including Rami. (Source: [[note/Telia'z Leadership Meeting 2026-02-22 Organizational Structure and Roles]])
- **Pattern**: Verbal commitment → no structural enforcement → same issue resurfaces → new verbal commitment

## Implications

- Relying on Oren as sole mediator between Rami and the organization will not produce lasting change
- Structural mechanisms (formal meeting agendas, governance committees, role authorities) are needed to replace verbal commitments
- This pattern partially explains why the restructuring has stalled — verbal agreements are treated as action but never translate into organizational change
- The proposed steering committee ([[decision/Steering Committee for Strategic Governance]]) would address this if implemented

## Applicability

Applies to all organizational changes that depend on Oren's mediation rather than formal governance structures. Particularly relevant to Rami's role definition, Li Yamin's role evaluation, and cross-functional meeting inclusion. Also applies to any future restructuring decisions that rely on verbal consensus rather than documented authority.

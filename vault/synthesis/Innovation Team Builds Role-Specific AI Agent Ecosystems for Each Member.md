# Innovation Team Builds Role-Specific AI Agent Ecosystems for Each Member

## Insight

Across three independent team member documentation efforts, a consistent organizational pattern emerges: the Telia'z Innovation Program does not deploy generic AI tools to its team. Instead, it builds deeply personalized AI agent ecosystems for each individual team member, tailored to their specific role, schedule, expertise domains, stakeholder relationships, and recurring workflows.

## Evidence

**Rivi Idelman** received 8 integrated AI skills including a PA that knows her weekly schedule (Sunday=innovation day, Tue/Wed=split clinical-innovation, Monday=1:1 day), enforces her 3-project maximum, and manages her stakeholder tiers across three levels of interaction frequency.

**Ohad Edri** has a documented "three hats" operating model (AI Expert, Developer, Infrastructure Architect) with specific OPTICA framework ownership (4 of 13 steps owned, 8 contributed to), and a clear project ownership matrix across 8 active projects.

**Stav Zamir** has a three-pillar responsibility architecture (Clinical Viability Validation, Clinical Needs Analysis, Implementation & Training) with documented go-to-person expertise areas and evaluation framework ownership.

Each ecosystem reflects the individual's actual working patterns rather than a generic role template.

## Implications

- This approach is highly resource-intensive per person but may yield outsized productivity gains
- Extending to clinical staff (30+ people) would require a scalable toolkit generation process
- The personalization depth creates a new knowledge transfer risk — each toolkit embodies institutional knowledge about the person's role
- Success validation requires measuring individual output before/after toolkit deployment

## Applicability

Directly applicable to the innovation team's three current members. Potentially extensible to clinical management (Basel, Shira) and selected psychiatrists if the approach proves effective. The taliaz-agentic-workspace skill already exists as the scaffolding mechanism for scaling this pattern.

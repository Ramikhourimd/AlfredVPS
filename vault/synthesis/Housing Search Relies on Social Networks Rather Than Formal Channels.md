# Housing Search Relies on Social Networks Rather Than Formal Channels

## Insight

Across all housing search records, property discovery consistently occurs through social networks and personal connections rather than formal real estate agents or listing platforms. The primary channels are: family-owned buildings discovered through community connections, Facebook property listings shared among contacts, word-of-mouth referrals (e.g., "Afnan's sister bought there"), and social visit conversations that surface opportunities.

## Evidence

- **Family building viewing (2025-10-09)**: The property was a family-owned building — discovered through community networks, not a real estate agent
- **Facebook listings (2025-10-09)**: Properties browsed during family conversation were found on Facebook, including a coastal villa
- **Social visit (2025-09-23)**: A rental offer of 600 emerged during casual conversation; properties discussed via Facebook by someone named Yousuf
- **New building visit (2025-10-09)**: Discovered because "Afnan's sister" bought an apartment there and invited family to see it
- **No real estate agent mentioned**: Across all source records, no formal agent, broker, or listing platform (beyond Facebook) is referenced

## Implications

The family's housing search is embedded in social infrastructure. This means:
1. Available options are filtered by the family's social reach, not the full market
2. Pricing may be negotiated relationally rather than at market rates (as confirmed by the sub-market synthesis)
3. Formal listings and agents may surface options the social network misses
4. The search pace is governed by when opportunities surface through contacts, not systematic market scanning

## Applicability

This pattern is consistent with Arab community housing practices in northern Israel where family and community networks are primary channels for property transactions.

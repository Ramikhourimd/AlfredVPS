# Li Yamin Administrative Role Creates Net Workload Increase

## Insight

Li Yamin's HR/administrative function at Telia'z is generating a net increase in workload for the clinic operations team (Rami, Shira, Rana, Basel) rather than reducing it. When Li prepares employment agreements or handles administrative processes and workers don't respond or sign, the unresolved items cycle back to the operations team without resolution. This pattern has persisted for over 10 years and has reached a point where Rami has decided to distance clinic operations from Li's direct involvement.

## Evidence

- **2025-12-04 voice memo**: Rami explicitly states "I decided to distance from [Li] because Li was adding work to us" and describes a pattern where unsigned agreements and unresolved administrative items return to the clinic team
- **2025-12-02 HR discussion**: Earlier conversation about Li's role identified similar concerns about the HR function's effectiveness
- **Pattern**: Li reportedly complains about issues rather than resolving them; expects the clinical/operational team to handle follow-ups that should be within HR scope
- **Duration**: Described as a pattern lasting "more than 10 years"

## Implications

- The decision to distance from Li may accelerate the need for Basel to absorb more administrative functions directly
- Shira is overloaded partly because Li doesn't close administrative loops, creating dependency on Shira as a fallback
- The organizational restructuring should explicitly address where Li's responsibilities end and clinic operations begin
- This reinforces the existing task to evaluate Li Yamin's role suitability

## Applicability

Applies to all Telia'z organizational restructuring decisions, particularly the shared services boundary question and the definition of clinic-level vs company-level HR functions.

# Remote Telepsychiatry Work Creates Compounding Isolation That Standard Team Tools Cannot Address

## Insight

The fully remote telepsychiatry model at Telia'z creates a compounding isolation effect: clinicians work alone from home, interact only with patients through screens, have no physical colleagues nearby, and are losing even the informal social connective tissue of WhatsApp groups as operations formalize into structured task systems. The planned internal tasks system (replacing WhatsApp coordination) will improve operational efficiency but may inadvertently accelerate isolation by removing the last informal social channel.

Group supervision forums appear to partially counteract this — one case manager reported a noticeable reduction in burnout perception after group sessions started. This suggests that the isolation effect is not just about workload but about the absence of professional community and shared experience.

## Evidence

- Staff interviews (via Rivi): "I'm alone in front of my computer with patients" — near-universal sentiment
- WhatsApp messages going unanswered reinforces abandonment feeling
- Psychiatrist described feeling like "orphaned children without parents"
- Group supervision forums showed measurable (subjective) burnout reduction
- Internal tasks system will replace WhatsApp, removing informal social element

## Implications

- Operational efficiency tools (tasks system) need to be paired with intentional social/community mechanisms
- Group forums and peer supervision should be treated as retention infrastructure, not just clinical education
- The "orphaned children" metaphor suggests the issue is not just isolation but perceived lack of organizational care/backing
- Any next-gen system should consider built-in social/community features

## Applicability

Applies to all remote clinicians at Telia'z. Likely to intensify as the clinic scales and individual relationships become harder to maintain. Also directly relevant to UK expansion planning — same model will face same issues.

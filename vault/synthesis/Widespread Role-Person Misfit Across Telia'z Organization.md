# Widespread Role-Person Misfit Across Telia'z Organization

## Insight

Multiple source records reveal that role-person misfit is not an isolated issue at Telia'z but a systemic organizational problem. At least five key personnel are in roles that poorly match their capabilities, preferences, or actual functions. This pattern suggests the restructuring is not just about designing better boxes on an org chart — it requires honest reassessment of whether current people can fill the roles being designed.

## Evidence

1. **Dekkel Taliaz** — Holds CEO title but assessed by both Rami and Oren as operationally capable without strategic planning ability. The CEO role requires exactly the capability he lacks.
2. **Li Yamin** — In an HR collaboration role but prefers product design. Exhibits systematic task deflection and boundary enforcement that blocks organizational processes.
3. **Rami Khouri** — Performing four overlapping roles (CEO substitute, Medical Director, Clinical Operations Head, Innovation Lead) with none formally defined or scoped. Has neither the title nor authority commensurate with his actual function.
4. **Elinor** — Identified as exhibiting toxic workplace behavior requiring intervention. Current role placement enables rather than contains the impact.
5. **Renee** — Transitioning from hands-on secretarial work to Administrative Manager, but the transition is blocked until replacement secretaries are hired, leaving her in a hybrid state.

## Implications

- Designing new organizational roles without addressing current misfit risks placing the same people in equally poor-fit new positions
- The restructuring must include honest capability assessments, not just title reassignments
- Some role-person misfits (Li Yamin, potentially Dekkel) may require personnel changes rather than structural changes
- The pattern explains why multiple restructuring discussions have stalled — the people being asked to fill new roles may not be suited for them
- Training, coaching, or role redesign should be considered alongside structural changes

## Applicability

This pattern is relevant to all restructuring decisions, particularly the finalization of the organizational chart and any new hire planning. Each proposed role should include a candid assessment of whether the intended incumbent matches the role requirements.

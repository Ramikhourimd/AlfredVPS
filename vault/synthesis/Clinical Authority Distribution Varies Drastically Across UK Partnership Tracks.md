# Clinical Authority Distribution Varies Drastically Across UK Partnership Tracks

## Insight

Across the three UK partnership tracks, Telia'z holds fundamentally different levels of clinical authority, creating an asymmetric operational model. This is not merely a difference in CQC registration (which existing records capture well) but a difference in WHO controls WHAT clinically:

- **Leon track:** Telia'z provides ONLY the ADHD-specific SOPs. All other clinical governance, SOPs, and patient management are Leon's. Telia'z has the narrowest clinical authority here — essentially a specialist procedure provider operating within someone else's practice.

- **OMG track:** OMG handles regulatory governance, safeguarding, and initial patient triage. Telia'z provides the FULL psychiatric clinical expertise — case management, psychiatric assessment, AI-generated reports, and treatment recommendations. Broader clinical authority, but OMG controls the patient entry point and governance layer.

- **GP Confederation track:** Telia'z would be embedded within the Confederation's CQC, with the deepest integration — case managers potentially trainable within the Confed workforce, technology embedded in their clinical workflows, and Telia'z participating in tender responses as an unnamed technology/psychiatry partner.

The gradient runs from minimal clinical authority (Leon) to maximal integration (GP Confed), with OMG in the middle.

## Evidence

1. **Leon track** (2026-02-15 team meeting): "All other SOPs are his... only ADHD SOPs from Telia'z." Patients enter through Leon's website. Leon holds all CQC authority.

2. **OMG track** (2025-07-03 pathway design): OMG handles Stage 1 triage (inclusion/exclusion screening). Telia'z handles Stages 2-4 (case management, psychiatric assessment, report generation). OMG holds CQC and governance.

3. **GP Confed track** (2025-01-22 partnership meeting): Case manager role "positioned as trainable within the GP Confed structure." Technology demonstration focused on showing how it integrates INTO their workflow. Rebecca mapping IG requirements for embedded model.

4. **Demo rehearsal** (2025-02-11): Framing explicitly positions Telia'z as supporting existing clinical structures, not replacing them — "how the virtual psychiatry service and technology are utilized by psychiatrists."

## Implications

- **Scaling strategy differs per track:** Leon requires minimal integration effort but gives Telia'z minimal control. GP Confed requires deep integration but gives the most sustainable position.
- **Risk profile differs:** In the Leon track, Leon could theoretically learn the ADHD workflow and drop Telia'z. In the OMG track, the psychiatric expertise is harder to replace. In the GP Confed track, deep embedding creates switching costs.
- **Clinical quality control varies:** Telia'z can ensure clinical quality most directly in the OMG track (full psychiatric service delivery) and least in the Leon track (only SOPs, no direct governance).
- **This asymmetry may be intentional:** The Leon track is designed as a fast launch vehicle, not a permanent operating model. The GP Confed track is the strategic long-term play. The clinical authority gradient matches the strategic timeline.

## Applicability

This pattern applies to any future international expansion where Telia'z pursues multiple partnership models simultaneously. The clinical authority trade-off (speed-to-market vs. depth of integration vs. IP protection) is a recurring strategic decision.

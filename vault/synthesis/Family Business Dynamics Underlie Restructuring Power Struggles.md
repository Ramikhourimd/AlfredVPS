# Family Business Dynamics Underlie Restructuring Power Struggles

## Insight

The Telia'z organizational restructuring is not merely a professional management exercise — it is a family business negotiation. The Feb 22 meeting participants include five members of the Khouri family (Oren, Rami, Basel, Dekkel, Leah), meaning the debates about CMO authority, operational control, and resource allocation carry familial weight beyond their organizational implications.

## Evidence

- Meeting participants: Oren Khouri, Rami Khouri, Basel Khouri, Dekkel Khouri, Leah Khouri — all family members
- Basel described as "the new hire caught between the co-founders' differing visions" — a family member navigating between other family members
- Rami "expressed frustration about feeling sidelined from operational decisions despite being the physician founder" — a familial grievance framed as professional
- Basel and Leah co-authored organizational proposals that Rami counter-proposed — family members on different sides of the design
- Oren "emphasized the need for professional management separate from clinical leadership" — potentially managing family dynamics through structural separation

## Implications

- Decisions about roles and authority cannot be evaluated purely on organizational merit — family relationships influence negotiation dynamics
- Basel's position is uniquely precarious: a new hire who is also family, designing structures that redistribute power between family members
- The push for "professional management" may partly be a mechanism for establishing non-familial governance norms in a family business
- External facilitators or board members (like Ilan Rosenfeld) may play an outsized role in resolving disagreements that family members cannot resolve among themselves

## Applicability

This pattern applies to all Telia'z restructuring decisions and should be considered when interpreting the emotional dynamics behind seemingly rational organizational debates.

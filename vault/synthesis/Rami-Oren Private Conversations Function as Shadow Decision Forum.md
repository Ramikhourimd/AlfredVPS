# Rami-Oren Private Conversations Function as Shadow Decision Forum

## Insight

The most consequential organizational assessments at Telia'z — personnel evaluations (Li Yamin suitability, Dekkel's strategic limitations), governance questions (CEO vacancy), and strategic direction (clinical vs. financial priorities) — occur in private bilateral conversations between Rami and Oren, outside any formal governance structure. The December 2, 2025 conversation demonstrates this pattern: Rami and Oren privately assess Li Yamin's fitness for her role, evaluate Dekkel's leadership capabilities, and explore the CEO question — all without formal documentation or broader stakeholder involvement. The December 5 debrief further reveals that Rami processes strategic decisions privately before they reach any group forum.

This means formal meetings (like the Feb 22 session) function more as ratification or debate arenas for positions already developed in this shadow channel. The actual decision-making power resides in the Rami-Oren dyad, which operates without minutes, formal authority, or accountability structure.

## Evidence

- **Dec 2, 2025 conversation**: Rami and Oren privately assessed Li Yamin (concluding she may be structurally unfit), evaluated Dekkel (operationally capable but lacking strategic planning), and explored the CEO vacancy — none of which appeared in any formal meeting agenda.
- **Dec 5, 2025 debrief**: Rami's frustration recording references prior private discussions with Oren about strategic direction, indicating an ongoing private channel.
- **Feb 22 meeting**: Formal positions presented (district model, CMO role) appear to have been pre-negotiated in private channels before being surfaced in the group setting.

## Implications

- Formal governance (board presentations, leadership meetings) may be performative rather than decisional.
- Other stakeholders (Basel, Shira, Dekkel) participate in a process where key positions have already been shaped privately.
- If Rami-Oren alignment breaks, there is no formal decision-making structure to fall back on.
- The shadow forum creates information asymmetry that compounds the existing organizational communication constraint.

## Applicability

This pattern is critical for understanding power dynamics during the restructuring. Any new governance structure (steering committee, formal decision protocols) must account for and either formalize or replace this shadow channel.

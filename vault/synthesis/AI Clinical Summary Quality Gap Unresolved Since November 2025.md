# AI Clinical Summary Quality Gap Unresolved Since November 2025

## Insight

A persistent pattern emerges across multiple records: the AI clinical summary quality problem — specifically, summaries ignoring intake data — has remained unresolved for over three months (November 2025 to February 2026). The gold-standard example needed to fix prompts has not been obtained. Meanwhile, the team is exploring additional AI-dependent workflows (phone call transcription) that will face similar quality and accuracy challenges.

## Evidence

1. **Gold standard task (Nov 2025):** Task to obtain a reference AI summary from a senior psychiatrist has been open for ~3 months with no outcome recorded
2. **Transcription task (Feb 2026):** New task to explore phone call transcription adds another AI-quality-dependent workflow before the first one is resolved
3. **Account snapshot (Feb 2026):** Confirms both AI summary quality and transcription are active workstreams, with methodology template also still outstanding from Feb 18
4. **Mixed-language constraint (Aug 2025):** Transcription quality issues predate the summary quality issues, suggesting a broader pattern of AI tool limitations in the multilingual clinical context

## Implications

- The innovation program is building new AI-dependent workflows on top of unresolved quality issues in existing ones
- Senior psychiatrist engagement may be a bottleneck — the gold-standard example requires their time and willingness
- A systematic approach to AI output quality (evaluation framework, iterative prompt improvement, quality metrics) may be more effective than waiting for a single gold-standard example
- The team should consider whether the transcription workflow exploration should be sequenced after the summary quality issue is resolved, or whether both can proceed in parallel

## Applicability

Applies to all AI-assisted clinical workflows within the Telia'z Innovation Program, particularly any that depend on accurate natural language processing of multilingual clinical content.

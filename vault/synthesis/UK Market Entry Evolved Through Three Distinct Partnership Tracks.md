# UK Market Entry Evolved Through Three Distinct Partnership Tracks

## Insight

Over 13 months (January 2025 to February 2026), the UK market entry strategy evolved through three distinct partnership tracks, each serving a different strategic purpose. Rather than a single-partner dependency, the team pragmatically diversified across: GP Confederation Leeds (NHS tender pathway), OMG Healthcare (NHS referral pathway with physical infrastructure), and Leon (immediate CQC launch for private/Right to Choose patients). This pattern reveals an emergent "portfolio approach" to market entry where multiple partnership tracks reduce single-point-of-failure risk.

## Evidence

**Track 1 — GP Confederation Leeds (January 2025):**
- In-person meeting at GP Confed offices, Leeds
- Strategy: GP Confed leads ADHD tender bid, Telia'z provides technology and psychiatric services
- CQC: Operate under GP Confed's registration
- Revenue: NHS tariff-based, revenue share with Confed
- Status: Tender preparation underway as of Jan 2025

**Track 2 — OMG Healthcare (July 2025):**
- OMG provides CQC registration, clinical governance, and physical clinic infrastructure
- Strategy: Joint service delivery — OMG handles triage/governance, Telia'z provides psychiatric expertise
- CQC: Operate under OMG's registration
- Adds face-to-face capability (addressing ICB preference)
- Status: Pathway design in progress as of Jul 2025

**Track 3 — Leon Partnership (February 2026):**
- Leon is a UK GP with his own CQC registration
- Strategy: Fastest path to launch — patients through Leon's website into Telia'z flow
- CQC: Operate under Leon's personal registration
- Revenue: Percentage arrangement
- Status: Contract pending signature as of Feb 2026

## Implications

- The team operates with strategic optionality rather than betting on a single partner
- Each track has different timelines, risk profiles, and revenue models
- Resource allocation must be managed carefully — three partnerships require three sets of clinical pathway design, governance frameworks, and operational setup
- The Leon track (Track 3) appears to have become the fastest path to first UK revenue, while Tracks 1-2 serve longer-term NHS scale
- This pattern may repeat in other international markets — diversified partnership portfolio as a market entry strategy

## Applicability

This partnership diversification pattern is relevant to any future Telia'z international expansion. The lesson: rather than seeking one perfect partner, pursue multiple tracks with different time horizons (immediate launch, medium-term NHS, long-term own-CQC). The approach trades operational complexity for reduced dependency risk.

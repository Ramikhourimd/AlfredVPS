# Operational and Clinical Proposal Tracks Remain Unmerged

## Insight

The organizational restructuring is proceeding along two parallel proposal tracks that address different concerns, use different framings, and have not been reconciled into a single coherent design. Track 1 — the Basel/Leah operational proposal — focuses on clinic management mechanics: three-pillar structure, pod scaling, secretarial capacity, and reporting lines. Track 2 — Rami's clinical-strategic framework — focuses on governance principles: physician authority, CMO scope, clinical quality as growth prerequisite, and evidence-based planning. The Feb 22 meeting was the first time both tracks were discussed in the same room, but they remained distinct proposals rather than merging into one. The assumption that they can be "reconciled into a unified chart" remains untested.

## Evidence

- **Track 1 (Operational)**: Basel and Leah presented the three-pillar district model, pod structure, and secretarial scaling plan. This track focuses on HOW the clinic operates — management spans, reporting lines, hiring sequences. It is grounded in Basel's operational experience and Leah's organizational design work.
- **Track 2 (Clinical-Strategic)**: Rami's counter-proposals span CMO authority, stabilization-before-growth, premium branding, and 60 KPIs. This track focuses on WHY and WHAT — what standards must be met, what governance ensures quality, what metrics define readiness. It is grounded in Rami's clinical expertise and external research.
- **The gap**: Track 1 defines roles without addressing clinical authority scope. Track 2 defines authority principles without detailing operational mechanics. The Feb 22 meeting surfaced this gap in the CMO-vs-COO debate but did not resolve it — Rami was tasked with writing a proposal to bridge the two tracks.
- **Risk**: The task "Define CMO Role Proposal" puts the burden of reconciliation on Rami alone, while the assumption "Role Incumbent Defining Own Position Can Produce Objective Proposal" questions whether this is realistic.

## Implications

Until these two tracks merge into a single organizational design, implementation cannot proceed coherently. Operational changes (hiring, pod setup, secretarial scaling) may advance under Track 1 while governance questions (CMO authority, quality gates, clinical veto) remain unresolved under Track 2. This creates a risk of building structure without governance — an operational machine without clinical guardrails.

## Applicability

This two-track pattern is likely to recur during UK expansion, where operational setup (office, staffing, compliance) and clinical governance (standards, protocols, physician oversight) will again need to be designed in parallel. Establishing a merge process now creates a template for future expansions.

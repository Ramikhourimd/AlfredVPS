# AI Three-Step Workflow Is Core Differentiator Across All UK Partnerships

## Insight

Across four different source records spanning 12 months (Jan 2025 to Feb 2026), the same three-step AI-powered workflow is consistently presented as Telia'z's core value proposition — regardless of which UK partnership model is being discussed (Leon, OMG Healthcare, or GP Confederation). The workflow is: (1) digital questionnaire/screening, (2) AI-assisted case manager session, (3) AI-assisted psychiatrist consultation with automated report generation. This consistency reveals that the workflow IS the product. The partnership structures (Leon CQC, OMG governance, GP Confed tender) are regulatory and commercial wrappers around a single, invariant clinical engine.

## Evidence

- **2025-01-22 GP Confed meeting**: Three-step workflow presented with Israel metrics (3.5-day access, 81.3% NPS, 60% time savings). Positioned for NHS ADHD tender.
- **2025-02-11 Demo rehearsal**: Same workflow scripted for GP Confed stakeholders. Four stages mapped: questionnaire → case manager → psychiatrist → AI summary/letter.
- **2025-07-03 OMG pathway design**: Same workflow embedded in NHS patient pathway. OMG handles pre-triage, then patients enter Telia'z's three-step flow.
- **2026-02-15 Team meeting**: Same workflow discussed for Leon partnership. UK-specific adaptations (report formats, prescriptions) are modifications to the workflow's outputs, not its structure.

## Implications

- Product development should prioritize the three-step workflow's UK adaptation (questionnaire localization, report format, prescription integration) over partnership-specific customization
- All UK partnerships share the same technical dependency: the AI platform must work flawlessly for any partnership to succeed
- Failure of the AI platform affects ALL UK pathways simultaneously — there is no diversification of technical risk
- The case manager role is the key human interface and must be trainable regardless of which partner employs them

## Applicability

This pattern applies to all current and future UK partnerships. It also suggests that international expansion to other markets will follow the same model: find a local regulatory wrapper, insert the three-step AI workflow.

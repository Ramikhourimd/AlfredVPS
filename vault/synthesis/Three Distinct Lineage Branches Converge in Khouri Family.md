# Three Distinct Lineage Branches Converge in Khouri Family

## Insight

The Khouri family identity is not a single lineage but a convergence of at least three distinct branches, each with different geographic origins and denominational affiliations:

1. **Shaddad/Migration branch** — The Hauran-Lebanon-Coast-Galilee migration (~1725 onwards), documented extensively by Majdi Shaddad. This is the trunk line, producing three cousin branches in Eilabun, Rameh, and Nazareth.

2. **Khouri/Priestly branch** — The surname itself originates from a specific individual who served as a priest (qasis) in Rameh. The "Khouri" identity is an overlay on the Shaddad lineage, not a separate bloodline, but it represents a distinct social identity within the family.

3. **Maronite/Northern Galilee branch** — Rami's grandmother came from "Drim" (possibly Jish) in the far north, connecting the family to Lebanon's Maronite tradition. This is a separate lineage entering through marriage, adding denominational diversity to what was previously understood as a single Orthodox/Catholic family.

## Evidence

- The Shaddad migration history is documented across three detailed notes and two conversations from the 2025-12-02 discussion with Majdi
- The Khouri surname origin is consistently attributed to a priestly role in Rameh across all sources
- The Maronite branch was disclosed by Rami himself in a casual conversation on 2025-11-09, where he stated he is "quarter Maronite" through his grandmother
- The Arrabah conversation note explicitly identifies this as "a third lineage branch"

## Implications

The genealogy project should track three research threads, not one:
- The Shaddad migration (Majdi's primary research domain)
- The priestly lineage in Rameh (how the Khouri name was established)
- The Maronite connection to northern Galilee (a separate family line requiring its own research)

Each branch may have different documentary sources available — Maronite church records in Jish, Orthodox records in Rameh, and oral tradition for the broader Shaddad migration.

## Applicability

This structural insight applies to how the Khouri Family Genealogy project is organized. Research should be segmented by branch rather than treated as a single linear narrative.

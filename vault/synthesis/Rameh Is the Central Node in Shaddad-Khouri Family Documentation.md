# Rameh Is the Central Node in Shaddad-Khouri Family Documentation

## Insight

Across all six source records in the vault, Rameh consistently emerges as the narratively central and most thoroughly documented location in the Shaddad/Khouri family history. This is not accidental — it results from three converging factors: (1) the Khouri surname originated in Rameh through the priestly role, (2) Majdi Shaddad, the primary genealogical researcher, is based in the Rameh branch, and (3) oral history is naturally most detailed about the narrator's own branch. The Eilabun and Nazareth branches receive significantly less detail in all sources.

## Evidence

- All three conversation records and all three notes describe Rameh as the origin point of the Khouri surname
- Majdi Shaddad — the sole informant — belongs to the Rameh branch
- The Eilabun branch receives only a name mention with no family member details
- The Nazareth branch receives minimal description beyond its existence
- Rameh is the only branch where specific events are described (the priest serving, the surname adoption)

## Implications

- The genealogical record has an inherent Rameh bias — the Eilabun and Nazareth branches are under-documented
- Future research should specifically target informants from the Eilabun and Nazareth branches to balance the record
- Conclusions about the overall Shaddad family should be tested against non-Rameh sources before being treated as comprehensive
- Any family tree document from Majdi will likely have more depth for the Rameh branch

## Applicability

This applies to all research planning for the [[project/Khouri Family Genealogy]] project. It should inform interview priorities and source diversification strategy.

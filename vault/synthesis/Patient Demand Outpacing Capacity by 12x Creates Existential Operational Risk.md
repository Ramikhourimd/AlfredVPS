# Patient Demand Outpacing Capacity by 12x Creates Existential Operational Risk

## Insight

The February 2026 data reveals a fundamental structural mismatch: Clinic Israel receives approximately 945 new patient registrations per month but can only convert 77 (8%) to intake appointments in that same month. This 12:1 demand-to-capacity ratio is not a temporary spike — it reflects the steady-state imbalance between marketing-driven patient acquisition and clinical delivery capacity.

The 7-day average wait time metric is misleading because it only measures patients who received appointments. The 300+ patients with no scheduled appointment at all represent a hidden wait time of 1-2+ months.

## Evidence

- 945 new patients registered in February 2026 (questionnaire completions)
- 77 received same-month intake appointments (8.1% conversion)
- ~600 scheduled for March (forward-loaded)
- ~300 still unscheduled (true waitlist)
- Two new psychiatrists and two case managers hired but not yet active
- Adiel calculated effective conversion rate at 4% when accounting for full-month trajectory

## Implications

1. **Hiring alone cannot solve this**: Even adding 30-40 psychiatrists would take most of 2026, and the demand keeps growing
2. **Patient discharge protocol is essential**: Without actively managing patient lifecycle, existing patients consume all capacity permanently
3. **Patient selection at intake is needed**: Currently no screening for severity, medication-appropriateness, or treatment complexity
4. **Growth-stabilization contradiction intensifies**: Leadership wants more patients while operations cannot serve existing ones
5. **Wait time metrics must be redesigned**: Current dashboard creates false sense of adequacy

## Applicability

This synthesis applies directly to Telia'z Clinic Israel operations and has implications for UK launch planning — the same capacity management framework will be needed internationally.

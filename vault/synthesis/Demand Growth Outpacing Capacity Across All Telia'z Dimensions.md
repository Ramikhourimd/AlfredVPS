# Demand Growth Outpacing Capacity Across All Telia'z Dimensions

## Insight

The Feb 15 2026 team meeting crystallized a pattern visible across multiple datapoints: Telia'z patient demand is growing exponentially while capacity (clinical staff, onboarding pipeline, product features, marketing infrastructure) grows linearly. The 8% same-month intake conversion rate represents a 12x gap between demand and supply. This gap is simultaneously driving: (a) the urgency of the UK expansion (to capture a new market), (b) the crisis in Israel operations (patients waiting months), and (c) the need for fundamental operational change (discharge protocols, academy, faster onboarding).

## Evidence

- 945 new patients in Feb 2026, only 77 served (8.1% conversion)
- ~300 patients with no appointment at all
- 4 staff members hired but not yet treating (onboarding bottleneck)
- UK launch requires simultaneous product development, staffing, and regulatory work
- Employee satisfaction concerns emerging under the pressure

## Implications

The company cannot solve this through hiring alone — it needs to simultaneously: (1) increase throughput per clinician via active discharge, (2) accelerate onboarding from 1 month to 1 week, (3) explore stopgap solutions (vendor case managers, academy cohorts), and (4) improve retention to prevent staff burnout from undermining all other efforts.

## Applicability

This applies to both Clinic Israel (immediate crisis) and UK Expansion (where the same pattern will emerge if demand meets expectations). The patient lifecycle discharge protocol, if validated, could become a transferable model for UK operations.

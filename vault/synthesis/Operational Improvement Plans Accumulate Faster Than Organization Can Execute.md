# Operational Improvement Plans Accumulate Faster Than Organization Can Execute

## Insight

Across 14 months of operational meetings (January 2025 through February 2026), Telia'z Clinic Israel generates detailed operational improvement plans at a rate that consistently exceeds its execution capacity. Plans are designed with specificity (exact fields, alert triggers, workflow steps) but then remain unimplemented for months while new plans are generated in subsequent meetings. The organization's planning velocity significantly outpaces its implementation bandwidth.

## Evidence

1. **Commitment Renewal Workflow** — Identical system requirements (Retool field, expiry alerts, secretary automation, validation workflow) were specified in January 2025 and again in August 2025, seven months later, with no implementation progress. The same person (Alice) was still investigating the same Maccabi terms.

2. **Feature Rollout Protocol** — Psychiatrist non-adoption of the summary feature was identified in January 2025 and remained unresolved in August 2025. The solution (frontal training) was agreed upon multiple times but implementation depended on scheduling sessions that had not occurred.

3. **Protocol Framework** — A comprehensive two-domain protocol framework was designed in December 2025, targeting operational stability in 6 months. The framework's execution depends on leadership buy-in, Shira/Rana capacity, and Basel's coordination — all of which are already stretched.

4. **Onboarding Model** — A three-track onboarding structure was designed in February 2026 but delivery depends on Stav or Shira, who both have full existing workloads.

## Implications

The bottleneck is not planning quality but execution capacity. The organization has strong analytical capability (Rami's clinical and operational insight) but limited implementation bandwidth. This creates a growing backlog of "agreed but not done" improvements that compounds over time. Each unexecuted plan increases operational technical debt while new plans add to the queue.

This pattern may also explain why the same issues resurface across meetings — not because solutions aren't designed, but because they haven't been implemented since the last discussion.

## Applicability

This pattern applies across all operational improvement domains at Telia'z Clinic Israel: platform features, clinical workflows, administrative processes, and organizational restructuring. It is particularly relevant when evaluating the feasibility of new improvement plans — each should be assessed against the existing execution backlog before being added to the queue.

# Innovation Team Operates Research-Strategy-Rigor Triad Division of Labor

## Insight

The Telia'z Innovation Program consistently divides work across three complementary roles: (1) Rivi Idelman handles qualitative research, field interviews, and concept design; (2) Rami Khouri provides strategic direction, builds automated data pipelines, and makes architectural decisions; (3) external advisors (Yaron Goren for evaluation methodology, Ohad Edri for academic references) supply academic rigor and methodological validation. This triad operates across every major initiative — staff experience research, AI model evaluation, and the next-generation system prototype.

## Evidence

- **Staff experience research**: Rivi conducts field interviews with clinical staff, Rami directs her to structure findings as academic methodology and builds automated survey processing pipelines.
- **AI model evaluation**: Rami built the Make.com data pipeline and LLM evaluation framework, Yaron Goren was onboarded as external advisor for methodological rigor.
- **Next-gen system prototype**: Rivi leads market survey research, Ohad provides academic references, Rami sets strategic direction and will build automation.
- **Role definition**: Rami explicitly scoped Rivi to research and concept design (not execution), reinforcing the division.

## Implications

This triad structure allows a half-time Innovation Team Lead (Rivi) to be effective — she focuses on what she does best (qualitative research, creative ideation) while Rami handles strategy and technical automation, and external experts fill the academic credibility gap. The model scales without hiring full-time academic staff but creates a dependency on external advisor availability.

## Applicability

This pattern is relevant to any small innovation team operating within a larger clinical organization. The triad model — internal researcher + strategic technical lead + external academic advisors — may be replicable for other telepsychiatry or healthtech innovation contexts.

# Company Identity Vacuum Drives Organizational Dysfunction at Telia'z

## Insight

The recurring organizational dysfunction at Telia'z — unclear roles, CEO vacuum, structural debates, branding confusion — traces back to a fundamental unresolved question: **what is Telia'z?** Without a clear, shared company identity, every structural decision (org chart, hiring, role definitions, brand positioning) is built on sand. Each stakeholder fills the identity vacuum with their own interpretation: Adi Lavi sees psychiatric services, Dekel sees a scaling startup, Rami sees a healthcare innovation platform.

## Evidence

- Rami reports that "nobody truly knows what our identity is" — including marketing, R&D, and operations staff
- Adi Lavi branded Telia'z as "Telia'z Health" = psychiatric services, which Rami sees as prematurely limiting
- The CEO role discussion is partly a proxy fight: whoever defines the identity controls the company's direction
- Organizational structure proposals (district model, shared services, pod structure) keep stalling because they depend on a company vision that doesn't exist
- Rami believes he is the only person who understands the company's DNA — a fragile single point of knowledge

## Implications

1. Define company identity *before* finalizing organizational structure
2. The CEO question cannot be resolved without first resolving identity
3. Brand decisions (website, marketing, public positioning) are encoding identity choices implicitly — these should be made explicitly
4. The identity should be technology-changes-resilient: "no matter what happens externally, we are still who we are"

# UK Market Entry Requires Three Distinct Partnership Models

## Insight

Telia'z's UK expansion has evolved into three parallel partnership models, each with fundamentally different governance, commercial, and operational characteristics:

1. **Leon Partnership** (Private CQC Umbrella) — Percentage revenue share, patients via Leon's website, Telia'z supplies own case managers and psychiatrists. Fastest to launch but limited to private/Right to Choose patients initially.

2. **OMG Healthcare Partnership** (NHS CQC + Governance) — OMG holds CQC, handles triage and clinical governance/safeguarding, Telia'z provides psychiatric expertise. Physical clinic infrastructure in Leeds. Targets NHS referral pathway.

3. **GP Confederation Partnership** (NHS Tender Vehicle) — GP Confed bids for ADHD contracts with ICB, Telia'z unnamed in initial tender. Revenue share with two streams (local Leeds + Right to Choose national). Case managers potentially embedded from NHS workforce.

## Evidence

- Jan 2025: GP Confed meeting establishes tender partnership with embedded workforce concept
- Feb 2025: Demo rehearsal confirms HealthyMind branding and clinical workflow for GP Confed audience
- Jul 2025: OMG pathway design establishes parallel governance/triage model with physical clinics
- Feb 2026: Leon track confirmed as fastest-to-launch with Israeli stopgap staffing

## Implications

Managing three simultaneous partnerships creates organizational complexity across:
- **Regulatory**: CQC through three different entities
- **Staffing**: Embedded NHS vs. dedicated Telia'z employees vs. Israeli stopgap
- **Revenue**: Percentage share vs. NHS tariff vs. tender-based
- **Patient channels**: Website, NHS referral, GP referral
- **Governance**: Each partner has different compliance requirements

This complexity may outpace team capacity, especially given the CMO bandwidth constraint.

## Applicability

This pattern — multiple partnership models required for regulated healthcare market entry — will likely repeat in other international markets. Key lesson: regulatory entry through existing providers requires adapting to each partner's operating model rather than imposing a single template.

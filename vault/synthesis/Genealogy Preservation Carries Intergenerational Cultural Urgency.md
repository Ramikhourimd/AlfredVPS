# Genealogy Preservation Carries Intergenerational Cultural Urgency

## Insight

The Khouri family genealogy project is not simply one researcher's hobby — it reflects a broader cultural imperative shared across generations. Multiple family members independently express concern that ancestral knowledge is being lost, and they frame preserving it as a moral obligation. This urgency is amplified by the project's total dependence on oral tradition: when the current generation of knowledge-holders is gone, the information goes with them.

## Evidence

Three distinct signals converge:

1. **Majdi Shaddad's 45-year research commitment** (since ~1980): A single individual dedicating decades to genealogical research signals deep cultural value placed on ancestry preservation.

2. **Multi-speaker family discussion (2025-09-21)**: Multiple unnamed family members — not just Majdi — discussed the importance of knowing one's origins ("denying ancestry is shameful"), acknowledged the difficulty ("the origins are old, nothing remains of them"), and expressed hope that "the children's generation will continue the search." This was organic conversation, not prompted.

3. **Oral tradition constraint**: The vault already documents that no primary written sources exist. Combined with the cultural urgency, this creates a time-sensitive preservation problem — the knowledge exists only in living memory.

## Implications

- Rami's genealogy project serves a family-wide need, not just personal curiosity — this may help with engagement and cooperation from other family members
- Recording Majdi's knowledge systematically is time-critical, as he is the primary (possibly sole) deep source
- The next generation's interest, expressed in the 2025-09-21 discussion, could be leveraged to distribute research effort
- Future research sessions should prioritize capturing oral accounts before attempting to verify them against formal sources

## Applicability

Applies to the overall research strategy for [[project/Khouri Family Genealogy]] — specifically the prioritization of source capture over source verification in the near term.

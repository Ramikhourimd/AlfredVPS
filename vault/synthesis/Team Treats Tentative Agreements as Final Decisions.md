# Team Treats Tentative Agreements as Final Decisions

## Insight

The Feb 22 meeting conversation explicitly records that "No final decisions were made" and the outcome was to "continue discussions with clearer role proposals." Yet the subsequent tasks and several decision records treat the meeting's discussion points as settled conclusions. The org chart task assumes the district model is agreed. The board presentation task assumes C-level structure, shared services, and three-pillar management are decided. Decision records reference this meeting as their source with statuses of "final" or "draft."

This pattern — treating directional alignment as binding decisions — creates a risk: if any participant later contests that a particular point was actually decided, the entire downstream task chain and implementation work may need to be revisited.

## Evidence

- **Conversation record**: "No final decisions were made. Several action items were assigned."
- **Task/Finalize Organizational Chart**: Assumes "conceptual agreement on the district model" is sufficient to begin charting
- **Task/Present Clinic and Innovation Plans to Board**: Lists the restructuring outcomes as presentation-ready content
- **Decision records**: Multiple decisions reference the Feb 22 meeting as their source, some marked "final"

## Implications

- The team may be operating on assumed consensus rather than explicit agreement
- A formal decision-ratification step (e.g., written sign-off by Oren and Rami) could prevent future disputes
- The board presentation to Alex may inadvertently "finalize" decisions by presenting them externally before internal agreement is confirmed
- This pattern may explain some of the circular dependency issues identified in other synthesis records — tasks proceed as if prerequisites are met when they are actually still open

## Applicability

This pattern is relevant to any organization where strategic meetings produce directional alignment but not formal decisions. Particularly risky when the meeting participants hold conflicting views (as with Rami and Oren on CMO scope) — silence after a meeting is not the same as agreement.

# UK Partnerships Follow Hub-and-Spoke Model With Israeli Clinical IP Hub

## Insight

Across all three UK partnership tracks (Leon, GP Confederation, OMG), a consistent operating pattern has emerged: Telia'z provides the clinical intellectual property (AI platform, ADHD SOPs, psychiatric methodology, three-step workflow) from Israel as a central hub, while local UK partners provide the regulatory shell (CQC registration, clinical governance, safeguarding) as spokes. The local partner is the legal operator; Telia'z is the clinical engine. This is not a traditional vendor-client relationship — it is a clinical IP licensing model wrapped in a regulatory partnership.

Evidence across all three tracks:
- **Leon**: Operates under Leon's CQC; Leon provides all non-ADHD SOPs; Telia'z provides only ADHD-specific SOPs and the platform. Patients flow through Leon's website into Telia'z assessment.
- **GP Confederation**: GP Confed bids as prime contractor under their own regulatory standing; Telia'z provides technology and psychiatric expertise without being named.
- **OMG**: OMG holds CQC, handles governance/safeguarding and triage; Telia'z provides the psychiatric assessment layer and AI platform.

In every case, the pattern is identical: local regulatory wrapper + Israeli clinical IP core.

## Evidence

1. 2026-02-15 team meeting: "SOPs: Telia'z needs to share ADHD-specific SOPs with Leon; all other clinical SOPs are his own" — clear IP boundary where only ADHD methodology transfers.
2. 2025-01-22 GP Confed meeting: Telia'z not named in tender bid; GP Confed provides the regulatory credibility.
3. 2025-07-03 OMG pathway meeting: "OMG handles the regulatory/governance layer... Telia'z provides the psychiatric clinical expertise."
4. Israeli case managers deployed as UK stopgap — clinical operations run from Israel even for UK patients.

## Implications

This hub-and-spoke model is the de facto international expansion template, even if not explicitly labeled as such. It has strategic implications:

1. **Scalability**: The model can replicate to any country with a local regulatory partner, without Telia'z needing its own registration in each market.
2. **IP Protection**: By sharing only ADHD SOPs (not the full platform or broader methodology), Telia'z maintains its competitive moat.
3. **Dependency Risk**: Telia'z depends entirely on local partners for regulatory standing. If a partner relationship fails, operations in that market stop immediately.
4. **Quality Control**: Clinical quality is managed remotely from Israel, with limited local oversight of the actual psychiatric workflow.

## Applicability

This pattern applies to all current UK partnerships and is likely the template for future international expansion (as supported by the "Israeli District Model is Replicable" assumption). Any new market entry should be evaluated against this hub-and-spoke structure: find a local regulatory partner, transfer ADHD SOPs, deploy the platform, and run clinical operations from Israel initially.

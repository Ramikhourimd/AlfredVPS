# Paper 1 Scope Progressively Narrowed Through Iterative Constraint Addition

## Insight

Across multiple meetings, Paper 1's scope was systematically reduced through a series of independent decisions that collectively formed a pattern of progressive narrowing. Each decision removed content from Paper 1 and either deferred it to Paper 2 or eliminated it entirely. The narrowing followed a consistent logic: reduce methodological complexity in Paper 1 to keep it publishable and focused.

## Evidence

The narrowing happened in layers:
1. **Content split** — Full research split into two papers (methods-heavy content was too much for one)
2. **Figure limits** — Capped at 4-5 figures, then further reduced to 3 methodology figures for Paper 1
3. **Pipeline scope** — Fusion agent and bias agent coverage deferred entirely to Paper 2
4. **Statistical scope** — Number of statistical analyses limited to avoid Bonferroni correction penalties
5. **Results scope** — Paper 1 narrowed to system presentation + PTSD outcomes only
6. **Structural constraint** — Methods section already constitutes ~50% of paper, limiting room for additional content

Each decision was made independently but the cumulative effect is a Paper 1 that covers: system architecture overview, per-source prediction (S1/S2/S3), demographics, timing data, and basic PTSD diagnostic accuracy — nothing more.

## Implications

- Paper 2 has accumulated significant deferred content (fusion, bias agent, cross-diagnosis analysis) which may itself need scoping
- The narrowing pattern suggests the research generated substantially more findings than initially expected
- Future papers in this line of research should plan for multi-paper publication from the outset

## Applicability

Any research project with multi-stage AI pipelines is likely to face similar scope explosion, where each pipeline stage generates enough methodology and results for independent publication.

# Staff Isolation Is Systemic Risk Across All Clinical Roles

## Insight

Isolation is not a side effect of remote work at Telia'z — it is a systemic, structural characteristic of the operating model that affects every clinical role. Case managers, psychiatrists, and even staff who share patient pods report minimal meaningful interaction. The problem compounds across multiple dimensions: physical isolation (working from home), communication isolation (unanswered messages), professional isolation (no regular peer interaction), and emotional isolation (feeling unsupported by management).

## Evidence

- Most staff report "not feeling part of a team" — working alone from home facing a screen
- Even paired psychiatrists on shared cases rarely need routine discussion
- WhatsApp messages frequently go unanswered, creating abandonment feelings
- One staff member described the team as "orphaned children without parents" — managing everything alone without organizational backing
- The planned internal task system may reduce informal social connection further
- Group supervision is the only reported intervention that partially mitigates isolation
- Staff who discovered that colleagues share the same struggles were surprised — they had no visibility into each other's experiences

## Implications

- Isolation is not a complaint to be addressed with a single initiative — it requires structural redesign of the work model
- The employee satisfaction survey must measure isolation as a multi-dimensional construct (social, professional, emotional, operational)
- Technology changes (task system, communication platforms) must explicitly include community features
- Group supervision should be expanded and potentially made mandatory, not optional
- The next-generation clinical system prototype should include collaboration and community features as core requirements, not add-ons
- Recruitment and retention strategies must address isolation proactively — this is likely a primary driver of turnover risk

## Applicability

Applies to all clinical staff at Telia'z Clinic Israel. Likely relevant to any remote-first telepsychiatry operation. The pattern may intensify as the organization scales.

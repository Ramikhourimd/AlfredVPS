# Innovation Program Operates Three Concurrent Strategic Tracks

## Insight

Across all source records, the Telia'z Innovation Program consistently operates on three parallel strategic tracks simultaneously, each with distinct objectives, methods, and stakeholders:

1. **AI Clinical Improvement** — Model evaluation, summary quality enhancement, Make.com data pipelines, prompt engineering, LLM-based rubric evaluation. Led by Rami with Yaron Goren as external advisor and Ahmad Masalha on execution.

2. **Employee Experience Research** — Staff burnout measurement, satisfaction survey instrument design, qualitative interviews, academic research methodology. Led by Rivi Idelman under Rami's direction.

3. **External Positioning & Partnerships** — Psychiatry Academy launch, Arab healthcare training program collaboration, marketing narrative shift toward innovation/technology, guest speaking. Led by Rami with Adi Lavi on marketing.

## Evidence

- Track 1 evidenced in: Innovation Team AI Sprint Meeting 2025-01-16 (model evaluation methodology, Make.com pipeline, Yaron onboarding)
- Track 2 evidenced in: Employee Experience Research notes 2026-02-18 (burnout findings, survey design, role definition)
- Track 3 evidenced in: Psychiatry Academy task, training program collaboration tasks, Creative Project Team Alignment Call 2025-09-21

## Implications

- The three-track structure aligns with the WIP limit constraint of 3 concurrent projects — suggesting the tracks themselves may represent the structural ceiling
- Each track feeds the others: AI improvement creates content for Academy (Track 3), employee research (Track 2) identifies pain points for AI tools (Track 1), external positioning (Track 3) attracts talent validated by employee experience work (Track 2)
- Resource allocation decisions must balance across all three tracks simultaneously

## Applicability

This pattern applies to the overall Innovation Program strategy and resource planning. Understanding the three-track structure helps prioritize when competing demands arise across tracks.

# Housing Evaluation Embedded in Social Gatherings Constrains Search Pace

## Insight

Across all housing search records, property viewings and neighborhood assessments consistently occur during social visits and family gatherings rather than as dedicated, standalone search sessions. On 2025-09-23, a social visit doubled as a neighborhood assessment and property discussion. On 2025-10-09, a family outing encompassed viewings of at least 5 properties (family building units, mini penthouse, investment property, new building, walkthrough of a 75 sqm unit). The housing search is not a distinct activity — it is woven into the family's social calendar.

## Evidence

- **2025-09-23**: Social gathering at a neighborhood included property browsing on Facebook, discussion of rental offers, and detailed neighborhood history assessment — all embedded in casual conversation with guests (speakers 23-31 in one recording, speakers 1-10 in a companion recording from the same event).
- **2025-10-09**: Multiple property viewings conducted as a family trip — at least 5 properties evaluated in a single day, with family members (wife, others) participating in all viewings and discussions.
- **2025-10-09 (new building)**: Visit prompted by social connection ("Afnan's sister bought an apartment and invited us to see it") — property discovery triggered by social event.
- No records show dedicated property search sessions (e.g., scheduled viewings with an agent, systematic open-house visits).

## Implications

- **Search pace is tied to social calendar**: The family cannot accelerate their housing search without either increasing social visit frequency or breaking the pattern to schedule standalone viewings.
- **Evaluation quality is high but infrequent**: When visits happen, multiple properties are assessed thoroughly. But the interval between search events depends on social occasions, not search urgency.
- **Distinct from discovery channel**: The existing synthesis about social networks as discovery channels describes HOW properties are found. This synthesis describes HOW they are evaluated — during social life, not as a separate work stream.

## Applicability

Relevant to any strategy for accelerating the housing search. Suggests that formalizing a dedicated search cadence (e.g., one viewing trip per week) could significantly increase throughput without abandoning the social network advantage.

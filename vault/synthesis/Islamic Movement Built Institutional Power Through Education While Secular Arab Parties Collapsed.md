# Islamic Movement Built Institutional Power Through Education While Secular Arab Parties Collapsed

## Insight

Across two independent social recordings (September and November 2025), a consistent institutional analysis emerges: the Islamic Movement in Israel succeeded where secular Arab political parties failed — not through political rhetoric but through methodical educational institution-building. The Communist Party (Hadash/Jabha) effectively died with no active members remaining. Tajammu (Balad) collapsed amid allegations of financial corruption (millions in stolen cash donations). Meanwhile, the Islamic Movement built Quran memorization schools, Sharia instruction programs, and youth education pipelines that initially attracted students from all backgrounds before gradually embedding movement-affiliated guides and instructors. Their approach is described as "literal" (harfiyya) — methodical, disciplined, and community-embedded — extending beyond religious education into broader public engagement.

## Implications

The institutional vacuum left by secular Arab party collapse means the Islamic Movement is the only organized community-building force in many Arab communities. This has implications for:
- Understanding patient community context in Telia'z's Arab patient population
- Recognizing that political identity loss compounds the clinical identity-based trauma patterns already documented
- The absence of secular civic institutions means mental health services may be filling a community cohesion gap

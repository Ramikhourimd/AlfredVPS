# Village Name Transliteration Is Systematically Inconsistent Across Sources

## Insight

The existing contradiction record about Eilabun's Arabic name is not an isolated case — it is part of a systematic pattern of transliteration inconsistency affecting multiple villages across all source records. This undermines cross-referencing and could introduce errors into any structured genealogical database.

## Evidence

Inconsistencies found across the three research notes and three conversation records:

- **Eilabun**: إلابون / إعبلين / البعنة / اللبيا / معلياس — at least 5 different Arabic renderings (already documented)
- **Suata/Suhmata**: السوطه (note 1) vs السوطة (note 3) vs "al-Suwta" (note 2) vs "Suhmata" (note 2) — different transliterations and possibly different places
- **Rameh**: الرامي (note 1) vs الرامة (notes 2, 3) — different Arabic endings
- **Ma'ilya/Mi'ilya**: معليا (note 3) vs "Mi'ilya" (note 1) — minor but inconsistent across English renderings

## Implications

- Any future structured database or family tree tool must implement fuzzy matching on village names
- A canonical village name lookup table should be created before further research
- The inconsistencies likely reflect different oral pronunciation traditions across the three Shaddad branches (Eilabun, Rameh, Nazareth)
- Without standardization, automated cross-referencing of sources will fail

## Applicability

Applies to any future digitization of Khouri Family Genealogy data, and to evaluating source reliability — transliteration variance may indicate the notes were taken at different times or by different transcribers.

# Clinical Protocol Framework Links Operational Metrics to Business Value

## Insight

The brainstorming session revealed a clear causal chain connecting clinical protocols to business outcomes that the Telia'z leadership team has not yet internalized: structured clinical protocols (treatment response, remission tracking, hospitalization reduction) produce measurable clinical value, which proves the clinic's worth to the public health system, which enables better HMO contract terms, which drives revenue growth. The current leadership focus on surface metrics (new patient count, Facebook campaigns, monthly revenue) misses the underlying operational drivers that determine whether scaling is sustainable.

## Evidence

- Rami and Basel identified that Sunday leadership meetings focus on lag indicators rather than lead indicators
- The gap between "what Rami and Basel know" vs "what the company has decided to do" is the core friction point
- Training and staff retention were identified as force multipliers — each retained employee represents saved recruitment costs and preserved institutional knowledge
- Administrative protocols (complaint handling, communication efficiency, inter-team coordination) were identified as prerequisites for scaling, not afterthoughts

## Implications

The restructuring effort cannot succeed by only changing org charts and roles — it must also change what the organization measures and optimizes for. Without this shift in operational focus, the new structure will be evaluated against the wrong metrics.

## Applicability

Applies to [[project/Telia'z Clinic Israel]], [[project/Telia'z Organizational Restructuring]], and future international expansion where the same protocol framework would need to be replicated.

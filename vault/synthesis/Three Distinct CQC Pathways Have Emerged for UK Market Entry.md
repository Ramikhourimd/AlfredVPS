# Three Distinct CQC Pathways Have Emerged for UK Market Entry

## Insight

While existing decisions describe a "dual-track" CQC strategy, the source records reveal three distinct regulatory pathways have emerged, each serving a different market segment:

1. **Track 1 — Leon's Personal CQC** (private patients, immediate): Telia'z operates under Leon's existing CQC registration. Patients flow through Leon's practice website. Revenue via percentage share. Target: operational by March 2026.

2. **Track 2 — OMG Healthcare / GP Confederation CQC** (NHS patients, medium-term): OMG holds CQC and handles governance/safeguarding. GP Confed bids on NHS tenders. Telia'z provides psychiatric services as an embedded partner (not subcontractor). Revenue via NHS tariff with share back to Confed.

3. **Track 3 — Own CQC Application** (independence, long-term): Telia'z pursuing its own CQC registration through NHS referral pathway. Working with Susan and Dolifa Ioja. This provides regulatory independence from partners.

## Evidence

- 2025-01-22: GP Confed meeting establishes the NHS tender pathway with GP Confed holding CQC
- 2025-07-03: OMG partnership meeting confirms OMG holds CQC for the NHS delivery model
- 2026-02-15: Team meeting confirms Leon Track 1 (contract imminent) alongside Track 2 CQC application via NHS
- Multiple decisions reference "dual-track" but the OMG/GP Confed pathway is functionally a third track with different commercial terms

## Implications

- Each track has different commercial models, different patient populations, and different timelines
- Track 1 (Leon) and Track 2 (OMG/GP Confed) can potentially operate simultaneously for different market segments
- Track 3 (own CQC) is the long-term play for operational independence but doesn't block the other two
- Resource allocation across three tracks may stretch the team, particularly the CMO who must define clinical pathways for each
- The "dual-track" framing in existing decisions may need updating to reflect the actual three-track reality

## Applicability

This pattern applies to any future international expansion where Telia'z may need to pursue multiple regulatory pathways simultaneously — each with different partner structures and patient populations.

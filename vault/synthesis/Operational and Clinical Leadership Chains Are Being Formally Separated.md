# Operational and Clinical Leadership Chains Are Being Formally Separated

## Insight

Telia'z Clinic Israel is deliberately splitting its leadership into two distinct chains: operational leadership (owned by Basel Khouri) and clinical leadership (to be owned by a new Medical Director). This is not an incidental outcome — it is a structural design choice embedded in the district model adoption.

## Evidence

Five records converge on this pattern:
1. **Basel assigned the operational plan** — He owns KPIs, staffing, metrics, and presentation to leadership. This is operational authority.
2. **Medical Director hiring underway** — A separate role is being created specifically for clinical supervision of psychiatrists. This is clinical authority.
3. **District model adopted** — The organisational model inherently separates operational and clinical governance.
4. **Operational plan separated from restructuring** — The deliberate decision to decouple the operational plan from the restructuring discussion reinforces that these are distinct workstreams with distinct owners.
5. **Rami cannot serve as full-time Medical Director** — The constraint that triggered the need for a dedicated clinical leader, catalysing the separation.

## Implications

- The clinic will need explicit governance rules for where operational and clinical authority intersect (e.g., staffing levels affect both clinical quality and operational cost)
- Success depends on the working relationship between Basel (operations) and the Medical Director (clinical) — if they conflict, there is no single tiebreaker below the CMO level
- The dual reporting structure of the Medical Director (CMO for clinical, Operations Manager for coordination) is the formal mechanism connecting these two chains
- This separation pattern could become a template for other Telia'z clinics if it works

## Applicability

- Directly applicable to Telia'z Clinic Israel restructuring
- Potentially a template for other Telia'z clinic operations if the model proves effective
- Relevant to any healthcare organisation separating administrative and clinical governance

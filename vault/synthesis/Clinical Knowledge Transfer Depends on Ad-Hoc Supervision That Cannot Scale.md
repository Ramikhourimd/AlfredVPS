# Clinical Knowledge Transfer Depends on Ad-Hoc Supervision That Cannot Scale

## Insight

Clinical knowledge at Telia'z Clinic Israel — risk assessment frameworks, medication protocols, reporting obligations, regulatory compliance procedures — is transferred exclusively through ad-hoc, one-on-one supervision calls initiated when case managers encounter situations they cannot handle independently. There is no systematic training curriculum, no documented clinical protocol library accessible to staff, and no structured knowledge transfer mechanism beyond Rami Khouri's personal availability.

## Evidence

The December 2025 clinical supervision call demonstrates this pattern clearly: a case manager (Selina) called Rami in an unscheduled call because a patient with suicidal ideation and military weapon access required assessment beyond her training. Rami conducted a real-time teaching session covering the five-level suicidal risk assessment hierarchy, point-in-time danger assessment principles, risk factor differentiation, and military reporting protocols. This clinical knowledge — fundamental to safe practice — was being transmitted person-to-person for the first time during an active patient crisis.

The February 2026 onboarding discussions further reveal that the planned three-track onboarding model assigns clinical training delivery to "either Stav or Shira" without a defined curriculum. The operational and technology tracks have clear deliverables; the clinical track does not.

## Implications

This ad-hoc knowledge transfer model has three compounding risks:

1. **Single point of failure**: If Rami is unavailable during an acute clinical situation, case managers have no fallback for clinical guidance. The constraint that acute supervision depends on single-clinician availability is a direct consequence.

2. **Inconsistent clinical practice**: Each case manager receives different training depending on which situations they encounter and when they call for help. There is no baseline clinical competency standard.

3. **Scaling impossibility**: As the clinic grows toward 7,000+ patients with ~176 quarter-position hires, the volume of ad-hoc supervision calls will overwhelm any individual's availability. The medical director at 30% position cannot absorb this demand.

## Applicability

Applies to Telia'z Clinic Israel's scaling strategy, medical director role definition, case manager onboarding, and clinical quality assurance. Directly relevant to the organizational restructuring and district model implementation.

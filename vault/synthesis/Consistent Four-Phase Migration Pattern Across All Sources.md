# Consistent Four-Phase Migration Pattern Across All Sources

## Insight

All six source records in the vault — three conversations and three research notes, created independently at different times — describe the same four-phase migration pattern: Hauran → Lebanon → Palestinian coast → Galilee villages. The consistency of this narrative across independently authored records strengthens confidence in the broad migration arc, even though specific dates and details vary.

## Evidence

The four phases appear consistently as:

1. **Hauran (~1725)** — Family departs Hauran (or possibly Zahleh)
2. **Lebanon (1725–early 1800s)** — Transit period, ended by Druze-Christian conflicts
3. **Palestinian coast (early 1800s–~1865)** — Trade-based settlement, Christians concentrated on coast
4. **Galilee villages (post-1865)** — Inland settlement in Rameh, Eilabun, Maghar, Mi'ilya, Safed, Tiberias

Each source was authored as a separate vault entry, yet all converge on this same structure. Minor variations exist (village name transliterations, exact dates, inclusion of Suata/Suhmata) but the core pattern is stable.

The alignment with documented historical events — the 1860 Mount Lebanon civil war, the presence of trade-oriented Christian communities on the Palestinian coast, and the establishment of churches in Galilee villages in the 1860s — provides external corroboration for the broad timeline.

## Implications

- The four-phase model can serve as the structural backbone for the genealogy project's narrative
- Research efforts can be organized by phase — each phase likely involves different archives and documentary sources
- Phase 4 (Galilee settlement) is the most verifiable, as it falls within the period of church records and Ottoman registers
- Phase 1 (Hauran origin) is the least certain and most dependent on oral tradition

## Applicability

Applies to the Khouri Family Genealogy project. May also be relevant to other Christian families in the Galilee who share similar migration histories — the pattern of Hauran → Lebanon → coast → Galilee appears to be a broader community migration, not unique to the Shaddad/Khouri family.

# Re-intake Policy for Returning Patients 2025-11-10

## Context

During the 2025-11-10 operations meeting, Rami established a policy for handling patients who return to the clinic after an extended absence.

## The Policy

Patients who return to the clinic after **6 or more months** of inactivity must undergo a **full re-intake process**. This means:
- They are treated as new patients from a clinical assessment perspective
- Full intake paperwork and clinical evaluation are required
- Previous records are available for reference but do not replace the re-intake
- This ensures clinical accuracy — the patient's condition may have changed significantly

## Rationale

After 6 months without treatment, a patient's clinical picture may have changed substantially:
- Medication adjustments may have been made elsewhere
- New diagnoses or conditions may have developed
- Life circumstances affecting mental health may have shifted
- Previous treatment plans may no longer be relevant

## Payment and Billing

The re-intake process raises billing questions:
- Can the clinic bill for a full intake session for a returning patient?
- How does this interact with HMO commitment structures (Maccabi/Clalit)?
- Rami directed that this question be raised with Nirit for clarification
- The re-intake is clinically necessary regardless of billing resolution

## Key Takeaways

1. **6-month threshold** — patients returning after 6+ months get full re-intake
2. **Clinical necessity** — ensures accurate current assessment
3. **Billing TBD** — payment policy to be confirmed with Nirit
4. **Applies to all HMOs** — not specific to one health fund

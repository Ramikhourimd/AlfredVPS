# Psychiatrist Recruitment Impact Model 2025-12-04

## Context

Voice memo recorded by Rami on 2025-12-04 during the clinic protocol framework brainstorming day. Rami outlines a structured 4-layer causal model demonstrating how hiring experienced, board-certified psychiatrists generates measurable KPI improvements. This model is part of the broader effort to formalize the protocol framework and provide data-driven justification for clinical investment decisions.

The framework is: **Input → Mechanism → Intermediate Effect → KPI Impact**.

## The 4-Layer Impact Model

### Layer 1: Input
- **Recruitment of an experienced, board-certified psychiatrist** (פסיכיאטר מוסמך מנוסה)
- Rami positions himself as a case study for this input category

### Layer 2: Mechanism (Direct Clinical Effects)
An experienced psychiatrist produces:
1. **Faster and more accurate diagnosis** — reduced diagnostic uncertainty and iteration
2. **Fewer unnecessary medication changes** — more confident initial treatment selection
3. **Ability to handle complex cases** — reduces need for transfers or referrals
4. **Fewer patient transfers** — complex cases stay within the pod
5. **Fewer documentation errors** — cleaner records, less administrative rework
6. **Fewer free/uncompensated sessions** — fewer sessions lost to corrective care or complaints

### Layer 3: Intermediate Effects (Observable Outcomes)
The mechanisms produce measurable intermediate changes:
1. **Fewer urgent follow-ups** — patients stabilize faster
2. **Fewer case manager requests for transfers** — case managers do not need to escalate
3. **Improvement in PHQ/GAD scores** — validated clinical outcome measures improve
4. **Reduction in medication side effects** — better prescribing means fewer adverse reactions

### Layer 4: KPI Impact (Measurable Business Metrics)
The intermediate effects roll up to dashboard-level KPIs:
- **Clinical:** Diagnostic accuracy, side effect rates
- **Treatment outcomes:** Treatment success rate, regression/relapse rates
- **Operational:** Examination time efficiency, clinical engagement time
- **Financial:** Treatment cost (via the full patient journey)

Rami noted that treatment cost impact flows through the entire care journey ("דרך מסע הכיפה") rather than being a direct per-session metric. He acknowledged this layer may seem straightforward ("banal") but emphasized it as necessary connective tissue for the framework.

## Significance

This model provides the missing **micro-level causal logic** for the macro-level KPI cascade already articulated in the protocol framework discussions. While the existing cascade maps clinical quality → HMO leverage → revenue, this model explains *how* clinical quality is actually produced at the individual practitioner level. It answers: "What exactly does investing in better psychiatrists buy us, and through what mechanisms?"

This is critical for:
1. **Recruitment justification** — quantifying the value of paying premium rates for experienced psychiatrists vs. cheaper junior hires
2. **Pod leader evaluation** — establishing what "good clinical outcomes" means at the pod level
3. **Protocol framework detail** — providing the mechanism layer that connects staffing inputs to KPI outputs

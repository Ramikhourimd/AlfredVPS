- Tax is a concern: "It's a shame for both of us that it would go to income tax"

### Company Car Tax Treatment in Israel
- Detailed discussion of how company cars are taxed after Israeli tax authority closed previous loopholes
- A company car valued at 7,000 NIS/month (including insurance, fuel, everything) is treated as 7,000 NIS net addition to salary
- The employer must then gross up the full amount — making it a 1:1 salary addition at the employee's marginal tax rate
- Historical loopholes (giving cars "off-books") have been hermetically sealed by Israeli tax authority
- Even benefits like Ten Bis meal vouchers (800 NIS/month) are grossed up — Dekkel pays 200-400 NIS overhead on each
- Leasing doesn't help either — "the whole salary is an expense; that's irrelevant"
- Rami asks: if he pays 5,000 NIS/month privately for a car, would having the company pay it cost the same? Answer: No, the grossing-up makes it more expensive through payroll

### Gross-to-Net Calculations
- 20,000 NIS expense: approximately 15,300 NIS bruto (using factor of 1/1.3 × 20)
- This confirms the significant tax wedge in Israeli compensation

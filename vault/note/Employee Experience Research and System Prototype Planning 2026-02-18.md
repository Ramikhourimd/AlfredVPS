# Employee Experience Research and System Prototype Planning 2026-02-18

## Context

Work session between Rami Khouri and Rivi Idelman on 2026-02-18. Basel Kanaaneh had separately expressed to Rami that he enjoys working with Rivi and finds their collaboration productive. This meeting covered three major themes: staff experience findings, research methodology for measuring employee satisfaction, and next-generation system prototype planning.

## 1. Clinical Staff Burnout and Satisfaction Findings

Rivi reported comprehensive findings from her conversations with both case managers and psychiatrists at the clinic:

### Burnout and Work Intensity

- **Both case managers and psychiatrists report burnout**, though in different ways
- Staff working **6-8 hours per week** at the clinic manage the intensity reasonably well; beyond that, burnout increases significantly
- One psychiatrist (Said) reported that working 3-4 hours daily across 5 days was very draining, but reduced to 1.5 hours daily, which works much better — even if total weekly hours were compressed into one long day, he would not do it
- A case manager described leaving Telia'z work days feeling **more exhausted than residency days**, despite residency sometimes having longer hours — something about the intensity of back-to-back telepsychiatry sessions is uniquely draining
- **Group supervision has a noticeable positive effect**: one case manager reported that burnout suddenly decreased once peer supervision sessions started

### Isolation and Lack of Teamwork

- Most staff do not feel a strong sense of being part of a team
- Working from home, alone at a computer with patients, creates a fundamentally isolating experience
- Even having other psychiatrists or case managers in the same pod does not create regular interaction
- WhatsApp group communication is one-directional: people post questions or raise issues and frequently get **no response**, leaving them unsure what to do
- The planned internal tasks system (replacing WhatsApp for operational messages) will improve task tracking but may **further reduce casual team interaction**, amplifying isolation

### Psychiatrist-Specific Concerns

- Psychiatrists feel like **"children without parents"** — a direct quote from one clinician — meaning they feel unsupported and left to handle complex situations alone
- **Difficult/aggressive patients** are a major frustration; psychiatrists feel patients are not screened before reaching them, and they bear full responsibility including risk of license consequences
- **Unreported extra hours**: many psychiatrists work additional hours (prep before sessions, reviewing summaries, rewriting notes) but **no longer report them** because they know the company discourages overtime — so they silently absorb the cost
- Case managers are less affected by unreported hours; their overtime is still within acceptable range for reporting

### Retention Risk

- **Psychiatrists**: Rivi senses many stay primarily because of convenience (work from home, pay) rather than intrinsic commitment. If a better offer came, they would likely leave. This is concerning for long-term retention.
- **Case managers**: Generally more committed to staying, with the exception of one who left due to workload. Most plan to continue, though this may shift when they complete their training programs.

### Mission and Professional Variety Loss

- One psychiatrist (Ilya) highlighted that when the clinic served post-October 7th trauma patients from the south, there was a sense of **social mission and professional challenge** that made the work meaningful
- Since transitioning to Maccabi HMO patients, the caseload feels like **"just another HMO clinic"** — routine cases without the variety or sense of purpose, but with the added burden of remote work and summary requirements

### Follow-Up Session Protocol Gap

- Case managers created an **intake checklist** (with Rivi's input) that they found very helpful for structuring the first patient interaction
- They requested a similar structured guide for **follow-up sessions (3rd and 4th visits)**, where the clinical objectives are unclear — medication side effects are already covered, but there is no defined protocol for what value to add in subsequent follow-ups
- This is an opportunity to better define the follow-up session purpose and potentially leverage it for patient engagement

## 2. Research Methodology Directive

Rami instructed Rivi to restructure her staff experience research using academic methodology:

### Structure Required

Reports must follow academic format:
1. **Background** — Why this is being investigated
2. **Methodology** — How it is being measured (what questions, what sample, what segmentation)
3. **Results** — Data-driven findings
4. **Discussion** — Interpretation and implications
5. **Recommendations** — Actionable proposals

### Measurable Metrics Approach

- Need to move from qualitative impressions ("burnout is high") to **measurable, trackable metrics**
- Build a **structured satisfaction survey** sent periodically to staff
- Survey dimensions should include: financial satisfaction, work-from-home experience, hours/scheduling, workplace atmosphere, sense of belonging, professional development, technology experience, surrounding support systems
- Segment by: role type, morning vs. evening shifts, Shabbat workers, hours per week, number of concurrent patients, tenure

### Automated Research Pipeline

- Rivi should create a **Google Sheets** document where each row contains a research question or topic she wants to investigate
- Share it with Rami, who will build an automated system to run literature reviews overnight and return findings by morning
- Ohad Edri can also support literature review tasks

## 3. Role Boundary Clarification

Rami reinforced the boundaries of Rivi's role as concept designer/researcher (aligning with the previously decided [[decision/Top-Down Gated Concept Design Framework for Innovation]]):

- **Rivi's scope**: Problem identification, research, concept design, structured reports with recommendations
- **Not Rivi's scope**: Building solutions, execution, implementation details (e.g., whether a video is animated or filmed)
- Rivi can add a "Recommendations" section to concept documents with implementation suggestions, but these are explicitly labeled as extra/advisory
- Rivi acknowledged this fits her strengths but expressed desire to also see end products through to completion (review stage, not ownership)

### Staff Communication Routing

When Rivi encounters issues during her research conversations with staff:
- **Clinical operational issues** → Route to Shira
- **Technical/system issues** → Route to Shachar
- **Office/administrative issues** → Route to Renee
- **Ideas and improvement proposals** → Rivi's domain (concept design)
- **Immediate problems** → Tell staff to contact Shira or Renee directly
- Rivi should handle all staff interactions **independently** without Rami present, to establish her own authority
- Need to coordinate with Shira to avoid **duplication of staff outreach** — scheduled meeting for Sunday to clarify boundaries

## 4. Pre-Treatment Patient Video

- Rivi drafted content requirements for a pre-treatment video for patients before their first intake appointment
- She consulted with Renee about common friction points in the initial phone call before intake
- The concept document was sent to the team, with the expectation that execution is someone else's responsibility
- Rami confirmed this is the right approach: concept spec delivered, implementation handed off

## 5. Next-Generation System Prototype

Rami proposed a new initiative: **build a prototype vision of the most advanced clinical system possible**

- Survey all existing systems in the market to understand what is available
- Create a model that combines the best features from all systems
- Add innovative, forward-looking features on top
- Assume unlimited technical capability — design the ideal, then build a roadmap to get there
- This should naturally start with Rivi's research and concept capabilities
- The output would inform a **yearly development plan** for the innovation and product teams

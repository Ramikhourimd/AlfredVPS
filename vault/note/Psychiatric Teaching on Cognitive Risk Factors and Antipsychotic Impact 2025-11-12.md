### Risk Factors for Cognitive Deterioration

Rami outlines multiple factors that influence a psychiatric patient's cognitive prognosis:

**Medication Effects:**
- **Antipsychotic medications (first and second generation):** The presence of antipsychotic treatment reduces risk in some dimensions. Studies show that antipsychotics do not necessarily improve cognitive outcomes either
- **Anticholinergic medications:** Use of anticholinergic drugs *increases* the risk of cognitive deterioration. The treatment approach should minimize anticholinergic burden as much as possible, especially in the relevant domain
- Impatience/restlessness as a symptom reduces certain risk factors

**Substance Use:**
- **Alcohol use:** Environmental alcohol use reduces prognosis (increases risk)
- **Cannabis or similar substances:** Also reduces prognosis (increases risk)

**General Treatment Principle:**
- The overall treatment approach should minimize anticholinergic medication burden wherever possible
- The function of psychiatric medications is "dispersed" — affecting multiple cognitive and clinical domains simultaneously
- This requires careful consideration of each medication's net effect on cognition

### Teaching Framework

The discussion follows a structured teaching approach:
1. Observe the clinical trend (improvement or deterioration)
2. Identify risk factors that could worsen cognition
3. Evaluate current medications for anticholinergic burden
4. Assess substance use patterns
5. Optimize treatment to preserve cognitive function

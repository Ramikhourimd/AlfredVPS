# Staff Management Discussion at Company Fun Day 2025-09-21

## Context

Informal staff management conversation captured during the Telia'z Company Fun Day on September 21, 2025. The discussion took place between at least four participants, with the substantive portions involving what appears to be Rami, Shira, and Elinor (Linor) discussing clinic staffing challenges, psychiatrist reliability, recruitment pipeline, and organizational structure.

This is a separate recording from the leadership speech captured in [[note/Telia'z Company Fun Day Recording 2025-09-21]], focusing specifically on operational staffing matters.

## Holiday Coverage and Immediate Staffing

Elinor (Linor) confirmed she is managing all loose ends and handling complaints during the holiday period until Monday. She reassured the team: "I'm closing all the corners, don't worry." There are several current complaints she is handling alongside broader coverage needs.

## Psychiatrist Absence Crisis — Saeed and Ahmad Accidents

Two psychiatrists were unexpectedly unavailable:
- **Saeed** had a car accident and cancelled his shift. The speaker was not initially aware of his accident — Saeed had sent a message but it was not immediately registered.
- **Ahmad** ([[person/Ahmed Masalha]]) also had a car accident — a separate incident, not the same route.

The handling of their patients required last-minute rearrangement of the on-call schedule to cover all shifts.

## Frustration with Psychiatrist Unreliability

A recurring pattern of last-minute cancellations by psychiatrists was discussed with clear frustration:
- **Anan** and **Eliav** were specifically named as chronic last-minute cancellers — "not coming, not giving answers, not committing"
- The speaker had spoken with Anan on Thursday, asking directly if he would attend, and described this as a repeat of a pattern now in its second year
- The conclusion reached: psychiatrists as a profession face enormous life management challenges, and this systemic unreliability needs to be factored into clinic operations rather than treated as individual failures

This unreliability pattern was discussed also with Elinor (Linor), who had similar observations about the profession.

## New Psychiatrist Recruitment Pipeline

### Jenny — Starting October
- First psychiatrist who covers all three critical needs: treats **children**, treats **adults**, and speaks **Russian**
- Described as "ace" — highly positive assessment
- Ahmad ([[person/Ahmed Masalha]]) is currently responsible for Jenny's integration at Ma'ale HaCarmel, including managing the on-call schedule

### Shirley — Limited to 20 Hours
- Shirley could only commit to 20 hours per week, not the required 40
- This was seen as insufficient: "I'm saying 20 hours, what's that?"
- There are commitment implications beyond just hours — it affects scheduling and reliability

### Elinor (Linor) — From Experience, 40 Hours Difficult
- Elinor shared that from her own experience, she also could not commit to 40 hours
- This is a data point, not an endorsement — the discussion concluded that **insistence on 40-hour commitment is necessary** for new hires

### Firm Stance on 40-Hour Commitment
The discussion concluded that the clinic must insist on 40-hour commitments from psychiatrists. Accepting lower hour commitments leads to chronic understaffing and scheduling fragility.

## Atef — Increasing to 25 Hours

- Atef is increasing from 20 to **25 hours per week** (discussed by phone)
- Described as an "asset" to the clinic
- However, he is chronically unreachable: does not answer WhatsApp messages and does not return phone calls
- This communication gap was compared negatively to Elias ([[person/Elias Ashhadi]]) — "asset that doesn't answer, asset not available"

## Elinor — Highly Positive Assessment

Elinor (Linor) received strong praise:
- Described as "fire" — meaning exceptionally capable and energetic
- Accustomed to working within hierarchical frameworks — "I'm used to working in a hierarchical framework"
- She proactively put important operational matters on the table from her first meeting
- Her work ethic was compared favorably to both Elias and Anan — "she did the work, like Elias did the work"

## Children's Services Manager Search

The need for a dedicated children's services manager was explicitly discussed:
- **Anan** was rejected as unsuitable: "Anan, no" / "you want Anan? He's strange"
- Anan's work was acknowledged ("he did the work") but both speakers agreed he was not managerial material
- Elias was similarly acknowledged as having done the work but with known limitations from the start
- This aligns with the existing [[task/Find New Manager for Children Services]]

## Ahmad's Role at Ma'ale HaCarmel

- Ahmad ([[person/Ahmed Masalha]]) has taken on responsibility at Ma'ale HaCarmel
- He is responsible for Jenny's integration and announces the on-call schedule
- There was some confusion about the extent of his responsibilities — the speaker "didn't know he was responsible for this"
- Ahmad has limited availability: he is completing Stage A residency training, and children's specialization requires five years (not a year and a half)

## Ori's Potential Return

- **Ori** ([[person/Ori Shukron]]) was discussed as potentially returning
- The question was raised: "Is she with us? How do you want to define her part?"
- She would need to be assigned specifically to children's services
- Her role definition remains open

## Psychiatry Academy as Pipeline

The Psychiatry Academy was referenced as a future source for clinic staffing:
- "At some stage this is where the Academy will feed the clinic"
- This aligns with [[assumption/Psychiatry Academy Serves Dual Purpose of Recruitment and Quality]]

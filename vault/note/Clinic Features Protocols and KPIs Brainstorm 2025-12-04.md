# Clinic Features Protocols and KPIs Brainstorm 2025-12-04

## Context

Rami and Basel conducted a brainstorming session to map out all clinic features from a product/operations perspective and organize them into a coherent protocol and KPI framework. The goal is to define what "being efficient" (yatsiv/stable) means for the clinic at scale, and to build a persuasion case for company leadership to change their current operational approach. Rana was present but primarily engaged in a separate side conversation (in Russian).

## Approach: Start from Goals, Not Features

Rami emphasized a critical framing shift: instead of listing features and mapping them to protocols, they should start from the desired end state — "what do we need to be efficient (yatsiv) in 6 months?" — and work backward to determine what protocols and metrics are needed.

The key insight: **there is a gap between what Rami and Basel know needs to happen and what the company has decided to do.** If Rami cannot articulate the "why" convincingly, leadership will continue with their current approach (reviewing basic metrics like new patient counts and Facebook campaign performance each Sunday) and miss the deeper operational indicators.

## Two Protocol Domains

The discussion identified two major protocol domains:

### 1. Clinical Protocols (Klini)
Focused on patient care quality and clinical outcomes:
- **Treatment response** (tguvat tipul) — better response to treatment
- **Remission rates** — reducing relapse/recurrence (hordat achuzei remission)
- **Hospitalization reduction** (hordat achuz ishpuzim)
- **ER visit reduction** (hordat pniyot lamiyun)
- **Cost reduction on public health system** (shipur ba'aluyot, netel al ma'arechet briut)

These clinical metrics serve a business purpose: proving clinical value leads to better HMO contract terms (shipur baheskemim), which increases revenue.

### 2. Administrative/Operational Protocols (Adminstrativ)
Focused on operational efficiency:
- **Complaints handling** (te'anot) — both general and professional complaints (te'anot miktzuiyot)
- **Accumulated structures** (bniyot mitztaber) — structural improvements over time
- **Communication improvement** (shipur tikshort) — strategic communication improvement
- **Communication streamlining** (yiul tikshort) — particularly inter-team communication (bein tzvati)
- **Process management** (yiul tahalich) — streamlining engagement with external bodies (gufim chitzoniyim)
- **Patient satisfaction** (sipuyei ratzon)
- **Revenue increase** (aliya bahakhnasot)
- **Excellence** (metzuyanut)
- **Time excellence** (metzuyanut zman) — reducing wait times

## Protocol Component Mapping

Basel mapped the operational protocol into structured components:
1. **Integration protocol** (protokol integratzia) — onboarding, training, and assimilating new staff
2. **Training** (hakhshara) — broken into:
   - Professional training (miktzuiyot) — leads to patient satisfaction, accumulated knowledge, and better patient retention
   - Ongoing professional development (hakhshara v'tnicha)
   - Setting up orientation for new hires (hakhshara v'tnicha)
3. **Staff retention** (shimur ovdim) — linked to reducing turnover costs
4. **Patient satisfaction** (ratzon metuplim) — both as an outcome metric and a driver
5. **Patient attrition reduction** (azivat metuplim) — reducing patient dropout
6. **Surplus capacity/output** (tsfua, yiter tsfua) — more output with fewer additional resources needed

## KPI Hierarchy: From Clinical to Revenue

The discussion mapped a clear causal chain:
1. **Better clinical outcomes** (treatment response, remission) →
2. **Reduced burden on public health system** (fewer hospitalizations, ER visits) →
3. **Better HMO contract terms** (improved agreements with Maccabi, Clalit) →
4. **Higher revenue** (aliya bahakhnasot)

This chain is the core persuasion argument for the board.

## VP Medical and Innovation Role

Brief mention of the VP Medical and Innovation (Smankal Refui V'Chadshnut) role — this connects to the organizational restructuring. The role is described as needing to bridge vision (chazon) and clinical execution. This connects to Rami's own role definition discussions.

## Strategic Purpose

Rami stated the explicit goal: **"I want to capture everything we know, put it in effect, so I can convince them to change their approach."** 

The current Sunday review meetings focus on surface metrics (new patients, marketing campaigns, monthly revenue). The deeper operational and clinical KPIs identified in this brainstorm are what Rami believes should drive decision-making. Without a compelling framework, leadership will continue the status quo and Rami will have to intervene reactively every month.

## Hypergrowth Model

Rami mentioned referencing a hypergrowth model — a framework comparing how clinics scale (adding clinicians vs. increasing per-clinician capacity). The model distinguishes between hiring more staff (horizontal scale) vs. improving efficiency per staff member (vertical scale). The KPIs need to measure both dimensions.

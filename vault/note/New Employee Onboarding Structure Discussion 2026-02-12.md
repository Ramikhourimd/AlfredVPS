# New Employee Onboarding Structure Discussion 2026-02-12

## Context

A 1:1 clinical management meeting (HM-RAMI-ELIAS series, now discontinued) between Rami Khouri and Elias Jahjah on 2026-02-12. The conversation was conducted in Arabic and focused on designing a structured onboarding framework for new employees joining the clinic.

## Key Discussion Points

### Onboarding Cadence

Rami proposed conducting onboarding sessions on a bi-weekly (every two weeks) basis. Elias agreed the cadence should be once or twice per month. They acknowledged that some months will require a quick orientation when replacing staff, while other months may have no new hires. The cadence should flex based on hiring volume.

### Three-Track Onboarding Model

Rami proposed splitting the onboarding process into three distinct tracks:

1. **Operational Track** — Clinical operations orientation. Can be started by new hires even without completing the full onboarding. Covers workflow, patient handling, and day-to-day clinical procedures.

2. **Administrative Track** — Administrative systems and processes. Can also be started independently. Covers HR processes, agreements, reporting, and administrative tools.

3. **Technology Track** — Technology setup and access. This is the **mandatory must-have** that cannot be skipped or deferred. New employees must have working access to Teams (for video sessions) and HoViD (clinical platform) before they can begin any clinical work. Without technology access, a new hire cannot function — they literally cannot join Teams sessions or access the clinical system.

### Technology as the Critical Prerequisite

Rami emphasized that technology setup is the one truly non-negotiable element. A new clinician arriving without Teams access or HoViD credentials is completely blocked. The operational and administrative tracks can be deferred, but technology must be resolved on day one.

### Delivery Responsibility

Rami discussed who should deliver each onboarding track:
- **Stav Zamir** or **Shira** should walk new hires through HoViD access and platform orientation
- The operational and technology components could potentially be combined into a single onboarding session delivered by Stav or Shira

## Outcome

Agreement on the three-track model with technology as the mandatory prerequisite. Implementation to be coordinated through the operations team.

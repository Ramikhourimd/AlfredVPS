## Key Observations

1. **Wait time warning:** Average wait time of 14 days is flagged with a rising trend. This is an operational risk — longer waits lead to patient attrition and may affect HMO contract compliance.
2. **Capacity gap:** 82 sessions conducted against a 100-session target suggests either insufficient psychiatrist capacity or scheduling inefficiency. The clinic is operating at 82% of its monthly session target.
3. **Conversion funnel:** 125 inquiries yielding 82 conducted sessions represents a 66% end-to-end conversion (inquiry → completed session), though the 92% card figure likely measures inquiry-to-scheduling specifically.
4. **Follow-up ratio on target:** The 85/15 intake-to-follow-up split exactly matches the stated target, suggesting the clinic is successfully managing its patient mix.

## Context

This dashboard was prepared for the Clinic Israel management meeting. The data informs operational decisions around staffing (particularly secretarial capacity for scheduling), psychiatrist availability, and the ongoing organizational restructuring discussions.

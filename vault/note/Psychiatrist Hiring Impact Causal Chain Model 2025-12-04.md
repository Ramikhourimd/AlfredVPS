# Psychiatrist Hiring Impact Causal Chain Model 2025-12-04

## Context

During the in-person protocol framework brainstorming session on 2025-12-04, Rami outlined a four-layer causal chain model demonstrating the downstream impact of hiring experienced, certified psychiatrists. This model follows an **Input → Mechanism → Intermediate Effect → KPI Impact** structure, illustrating how a single staffing decision cascades through clinical and operational dimensions.

This is a concrete worked example of the broader protocol framework being developed for [[project/Telia'z Clinic Israel]], and directly supports the argument in [[synthesis/Clinical KPI Cascade Links Treatment Quality to Commercial Leverage]].

## The Four-Layer Causal Chain

### Layer 1: Input
- **Hiring an experienced, certified psychiatrist** (גיאוס פסיכיאטר מוסמך מנוסה)
- Rami positions himself as an example of this input category

### Layer 2: Mechanism (Direct Capabilities)
An experienced psychiatrist enables:
- **Faster and more accurate diagnosis** — reduces diagnostic uncertainty and delays
- **Fewer unnecessary medication changes** — experienced clinicians are more targeted in prescribing
- **Ability to handle complex cases** — reduces need for external referrals or transfers
- **Fewer patient transfers** — cases stay with the treating psychiatrist
- **Fewer document errors** — more precise clinical documentation
- **Fewer free/unstructured sessions** — more efficient use of session time

### Layer 3: Intermediate Effects (Observable Outcomes)
The mechanisms produce measurable clinical improvements:
- **Fewer urgent follow-ups** — stable patients require less crisis management
- **Fewer case manager referrals for transfers** — case managers spend less time coordinating hand-offs
- **Improvement in PHQ/GAD scores** — validated outcome measures show treatment response
- **Reduction in medication side effects** — better prescribing leads to better tolerability

### Layer 4: KPI Impact (Measurable Business Metrics)
The intermediate effects translate to trackable KPIs across two domains:

**Clinical KPIs:**
- Assisted employment outcomes (תעסוקה מסייעת)
- Diagnostic accuracy rates
- Side effect incidence
- Treatment success rates
- Regression/relapse rates

**Operational KPIs:**
- Examination/assessment time
- Clinical engagement time (time spent on clinical tasks)
- Treatment cost per patient (via the full patient journey)

## Analysis

Rami noted that treatment cost impact flows through the entire patient journey ("דרך מסע הכיפה"), suggesting this is a downstream consequence rather than a direct mechanism. He acknowledged some of these connections may seem "banal" (obvious) but emphasized the importance of making them explicit for leadership who don't intuitively see the clinical-to-commercial link.

This model is a building block for the [[task/Formalize Clinic Protocol Framework Document]] and the [[task/Build KPI Persuasion Deck for Board]] — it provides a concrete, arguable chain from staffing investment to measurable ROI.

# Rami Leadership Frustration Debrief 2025-12-05

## Context

Early morning personal conversation (Rami woke at 1:30 AM) recorded on 2025-12-05. Rami is venting to a close family member or friend about a deeply frustrating leadership planning session the previous day (2025-12-04) where Alex, Oren, and Dekkel discussed 2026 goals.

## Key Points

### 1. Leadership's Financial Tunnel Vision

Alex, Oren, and Dekkel designed the 2026 goals together. The overwhelming focus was financial:
- Expense ratio targets
- Patient volume expansion
- Expansion to additional HMOs (kupot cholim) — specifically targeting Clalit and possibly Leumit
- New patient acquisition numbers

These targets were handed down to Rami roughly a week prior without meaningful clinical input or consultation.

### 2. The Sunday Meeting Pattern

The weekly Sunday leadership meetings are entirely marketing and business-development oriented: new patient counts, referral source analysis (family doctors, campaigns), discussions about new secretaries, the UK expansion, and Clalit agreement progress. There is no space for clinical operations, quality metrics, or staff wellbeing in these meetings.

### 3. Rami's Counter-Argument

Rami has been trying to explain that the clinic needs stabilization before growth:
- Defined nearly 60 KPIs that need tracking — "it's already basically a hospital"
- Needs to recruit better psychiatrists, train staff, and build operational protocols
- Psychiatrist satisfaction, wellbeing, and training investment indirectly translate to revenue — but leadership cannot see the connection
- Legal exposure risk: without protocols and quality checks, a lawsuit could cost millions at any time
- Rapid hiring leads to lower-quality staff on small contracts with no commitment — well-documented in healthcare literature

### 4. The Presentation Collapse

When Rami started presenting his plans, Alex immediately challenged the patient number calculations, staffing derivations, and basic methodology. Rami had prepared extensive material but became so frustrated he stopped — presenting only about 10% of what he had prepared. He told them explicitly: "I cannot continue."

### 5. Fundamental Misunderstanding

Each leader took a different (wrong) interpretation from Rami's frustrations:
- **Dekkel** understood Rami was offended about not being included in meetings (misread: Rami wants strategic involvement, not ego validation)
- **Oren** said "we threw you into the deep end too fast, you shouldn't do this alone" (misread: Rami is capable and wants MORE involvement, not less)
- **Alex** focused on questioning Rami's numbers rather than engaging with the strategic argument

Rami's core frustration: "they don't understand me, and they think they do."

### 6. What Rami Actually Wants

- A CEO who understands clinical value and speaks the language of healthcare operations
- OR: full autonomy to run the clinic independently with a defined budget
- Specifically: a budget, allocated marketing hours per month, no business development pressure for the next 6 months, and development resources that don't take 8 person-months for 2-week tasks

### 7. CTO/Development Frustration

Rami is frustrated with Shachar (CTO):
- Sends a list of development requests; Shachar estimates 8 person-months for work Rami believes could be done in 2 weeks with AI
- Shachar took 2 years to start using AI in his coding environment
- This creates a bottleneck where innovation ideas cannot be prototyped quickly

### 8. Predicted Outcome

Rami expects he will just follow leadership's financial plans to get his budget approved, then independently run the clinic his way. The result will be:
- No connection between what leadership does and what the clinic does
- When they sign a new HMO agreement (e.g., Leumit) in a few months, Rami will have to reject it because the clinic cannot absorb more patients
- Marketing will generate demand he cannot serve
- The disconnect between the company and the clinic will only grow

## Analysis

This conversation reveals that the assumption captured in [[assumption/Protocol Framework Will Convince Leadership to Change Priorities]] may be weakening. Rami attempted to present his structured framework and was shut down by Alex before he could even make his case. The communication problem may be deeper than framing — it may be a fundamental values misalignment where leadership genuinely does not prioritize clinical quality metrics.

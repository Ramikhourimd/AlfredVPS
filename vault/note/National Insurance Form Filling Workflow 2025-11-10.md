# National Insurance Form Filling Workflow 2025-11-10

## Context

During the 2025-11-10 operations meeting, Rami discussed the process of filling out national insurance (Bituach Leumi) forms for patients.

## The Workflow

Clinicians are required to fill out national insurance forms for patients who need disability or welfare documentation:
- These forms require clinical input about the patient's condition
- They take significant time to complete properly
- The forms are a necessary part of patient care but are not billable clinical sessions

## Administrative Time Approval

Rami approved counting national insurance form filling as **administrative time**:
- This acknowledges the work involved in completing these forms
- Staff should log the time spent on NI forms separately
- This prevents the work from being invisible in workload tracking

## Volume Tracking

Rami directed the team to **track the volume** of NI form requests:
- Need data on how many forms are being filled per month
- This helps assess the workload impact on clinical staff
- Volume data will inform staffing and scheduling decisions
- May need to allocate dedicated time slots if volume is high

## Key Takeaways

1. **NI form filling** is recognized as legitimate administrative work
2. **Hours approved** as admin time — not unpaid extra work
3. **Volume tracking** required for workload planning
4. **Data needed** to assess staffing implications

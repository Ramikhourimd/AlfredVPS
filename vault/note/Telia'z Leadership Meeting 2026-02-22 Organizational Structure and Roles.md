# Telia'z Leadership Meeting 2026-02-22 — Organizational Structure and Roles

## Context

In-person leadership meeting on 2026-02-22 with Oren (owner/CEO), Rami (medical lead/innovation), Basel (incoming operations), Ziv (advisor), and others. The meeting focused on defining the organizational structure for Telia'z as it transitions from a single Israeli telepsychiatry clinic to a multi-clinic international operation. This is a pivotal meeting for the organizational restructuring project.

## Key Discussion Topics

### 1. Clinic Management Structure (the "District" Model)

Basel and Leah proposed a three-pillar management structure for the clinic:

- **Clinical Operations Manager (מנהל תפעול קליני)**: Manages day-to-day clinic operations, KPIs, scheduling, case manager coordination, audit and quality processes. Currently mapped to Shira.
- **Medical Director (מנהל רפואי)**: 30% position. Manages psychiatrists professionally — protocols, consultations, clinical decisions. Does NOT handle administrative/scheduling aspects of physicians.
- **Administrative Manager (מנהל אדמיניסטרטיבי)**: Handles HR, secretarial oversight, onboarding, compliance. Currently mapped to Renee.

These three roles are **parallel** — none reports to the other. They all report upward to the clinic-level leadership.

### 2. Pod Model for Scaling

As the clinic grows (potentially to 30+ psychiatrist positions, 50+ case managers), direct management by a single operations manager becomes unsustainable. The proposed solution:

- **Pods**: Groups of ~10 practitioners managed by a pod lead (senior clinician with added management hours, ~10 hours/month)
- Pods are **operational** (scheduling, workload, communications), not clinical
- Clinical matters (training, protocols, case discussions) handled through **shared forums** across all pods
- Pod leads communicate upward to the Medical Director (clinical) or Operations Manager (operational)

### 3. Medical Secretary Team

Current state: 2 full-time secretaries (Alice returning to full-time, Amada full-time until maternity leave), Avital leaving, Renee moving to pure management. Need to hire 2 more immediately.

Projected end of year: 5 full-time secretary positions.

Secretaries handle two functions:
- **Patient-facing**: Appointment scheduling, reminders, complaints, tech support for connecting to platform, general service
- **Staff-facing**: Diary management for clinicians, shift coordination, schedule changes

Discussion on whether to assign secretaries to specific pods (dedicated diary manager per pod) vs. general pool with rotating availability. No final decision reached.

### 4. C-Level Role Debate: CMO vs COO

The most contentious topic. Core tension:

**Rami's position**: The company is a medical operation. Medical decisions permeate everything — marketing, hiring, scheduling, KPIs, growth strategy. Therefore, the person overseeing all clinics must be a physician who understands clinical implications. He cited the legal precedent that Israeli courts (Bagatz) intervene when hospital directors are not physicians. He wants the CMO to have strategic authority and veto power over decisions with medical implications.

**Oren's position**: The company needs to function as one unified entity. Shared services (HR, marketing, finance, tech support) should come from the parent company, not be duplicated in the clinic. He envisions a COO at company level who manages operational consistency across clinics, with the CMO providing clinical oversight and veto on medical matters specifically.

**Ziv's input**: Drew parallels to multinational corporations — start centralized, build local functions only as scale demands. Cautioned against copying hospital structures blindly. Emphasized the difference between medical veto authority and operational management responsibility.

**Emerging consensus** (not fully agreed):
- **CMO at company level**: Ensures medical standards, has veto on clinical matters, reviews all contracts, oversees medical directors at each clinic
- **COO at company level**: Manages operational consistency, shared services, KPI tracking across clinics
- **Medical Director per clinic**: Local clinical authority, protocols, physician management
- **Operations Manager per clinic**: Day-to-day clinic operations (Basel's role for Israel)
- **Steering committee (ועדת היגוי)**: Governance body including CMO, COO, CEO for strategic decisions

### 5. Rami's Role Clarification

Rami has been fulfilling ~4 roles simultaneously: CMO, operations management, clinical direction, and innovation leadership. The meeting attempted to reduce this to 2:

1. **Chief Medical Officer (company level)**: Clinical oversight, veto authority on medical matters, review contracts with HMOs, ensure regulatory compliance, KPI definition for clinical quality
2. **Innovation leadership**: Identifying clinical workflow improvements, AI integration opportunities, piloting new processes, working with product/R&D

Rami expressed frustration that his clinical voice is not sufficiently integrated into company decision-making. He feels recommendations are made without clinical input (citing the marketing planning incident). He wants either formal authority or a structured mechanism to ensure clinical perspective is always present in strategic decisions.

The group acknowledged this gap. Oren committed to ensuring Rami is included in relevant cross-functional meetings. The steering committee concept was proposed as the formal mechanism.

Rami was asked to reflect and come back with his preferred role definition.

### 6. Innovation and CTO Relationship

Brief but important discussion:
- Innovation (Rami's domain) and R&D/CTO (Shachar's domain) need better coordination
- Currently they operate semi-independently which causes gaps
- Rami wants a product manager to serve as interface between innovation research and engineering execution
- The relationship between Innovation features and the platform roadmap needs formalized prioritization
- Shachar indicated he can work in parallel on some innovation items but needs prioritized requirements

### 7. Shared Services vs. In-House

Oren repeatedly emphasized "this is one company" — the clinic should leverage parent company resources rather than building duplicate functions:

| Function | Recommendation | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| HR/People | Parent company (Tal + team) | Clinic defines needs, parent executes onboarding, contracts, etc. |
| Finance/Accounting | Parent company | Clinic reports metrics, parent handles books |
| Marketing | Parent company (Adi) | Adi should be available on-demand for clinic needs, embedded when needed |
| Tech support | Parent company (Roi + team) | Training, troubleshooting, system onboarding |
| Clinical operations | In-house (Basel) | Must be managed within the clinic |
| Medical direction | In-house (Medical Director) | Must be a physician on-site |
| Administration | In-house (Renee) | Day-to-day staff management, but guided by parent HR policies |

Rami pushed back on fully outsourcing HR, arguing that staff need to feel they have a local "address" for issues, especially in a hybrid/remote work environment. Basel supported having local administrative presence.

## Unresolved Items

2. **Pod structure details** — whether to include mixed clinical/operational pods or keep them separate
3. **Secretary team structure** — dedicated per pod or shared pool
4. **Medical Director hiring** — need to recruit 30% position physician
5. **Board composition** — no physician currently on the Board of Directors
6. **Innovation-CTO coordination mechanism** — product manager needed, prioritization framework undefined
7. **Children's services** — currently ~10% of activity, may need separate clinical lead if volumes grow

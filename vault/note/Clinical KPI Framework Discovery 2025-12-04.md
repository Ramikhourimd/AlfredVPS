# Clinical KPI Framework Discovery 2025-12-04

## Context

During the 2025-12-04 clinic operations session, Rami came across a structured clinical KPI framework (likely from industry literature or a course — referred to as "masak business" meaning a business course/track). He became excited about the alignment between these formal KPIs and the protocol framework he and Basel had been developing. Basel was somewhat distracted by incoming emails from Shira, prompting Rami to ask him to focus.

## KPI Categories Identified

### Clinical Quality Metrics
- **Retention Rate:** Percentage of patients who continue treatment after initial sessions. If 100%, it means no one dropped out — unrealistic but directional
- **Recovery Rate:** Clinical outcome measure for treatment success
- **Cut Size:** Case volume metric per clinician
- **Caseload Closure Rate ("kohos"):** Rate at which active cases are resolved and closed

### Operational Quality Metrics
- **ER Visit Rate:** Emergency room visits by active patients — lower is better, indicates treatment stability
- **Supervisor to Clinician Ratio:** Ratio of clinical supervisors overseeing clinicians — reflects quality oversight capacity
- **Clinician Notes Audited:** Percentage of clinical notes reviewed by supervisors — the "battle" Rami described wanting to fight (achieving 100% audit rate)

### Workforce Metrics
- **Retention Rate (Staff):** How many staff stay vs leave
- **Time for Open Roles:** Speed of filling open positions
- **Third Party Satisfaction:** Patient satisfaction scores (see separate note)

### Business Metrics
- **Dimension Rate:** Conversion-related metric
- **Funnel Metrics:** Patient flow from referral to active treatment

## Connection to Protocol Framework

Rami noted that the KPI framework he discovered directly maps to the protocol framework they had been brainstorming earlier that day:
- Clinical KPIs validate the "clinical excellence drives revenue" thesis
- Supervisor audit rate connects to the quality assurance protocols
- Retention and recovery rates are the ultimate outcome metrics that demonstrate value to HMOs (Maccabi, Clalit)
- These KPIs can be used in the board persuasion deck to justify operational investments

## Key Insight

Rami emphasized: "This is all business" — meaning every clinical KPI ultimately feeds the commercial value proposition. High retention, good recovery rates, and low ER visits make the clinic more valuable to HMO partners, justifying higher contract rates and volume commitments.

The protocol work connects to the larger goal of building a formalized clinic protocol framework that can scale operations and demonstrate clinical rigor to HMO partners and company leadership.

## ADHD Diagnosis — Scope Clarification

A critical distinction was clarified:
- **What the clinic provides**: Clinical ADHD diagnosis — a psychiatrist evaluation sufficient to initiate medication treatment
- **What the clinic does NOT provide**: Formal MOH-compliant ADHD assessment reports — these require extensive computerized testing (CPT, MOXO), standardized questionnaires, and 2-3 hours of evaluation time

This scoping is important because some patients and referrers may expect the full formal assessment when referred for "ADHD diagnosis." The clinic should be clear about which service it provides.

### Maccabi Constraint
Maccabi health fund prohibits the clinic from charging patients additional fees beyond the standard session rate for assessment-related services. This means the clinic cannot offer the extended formal assessment as an add-on revenue service for Maccabi patients.

## Adiel — Intake Duration Misrepresentation

Adiel has been telling patients that intake appointments take only 30 minutes. The actual clinical standard requires 45-60 minutes minimum for a proper psychiatric intake. This creates:
- Patient frustration when sessions run longer than expected
- Risk of rushed clinical evaluations if sessions are cut short
- Misalignment between patient expectations and clinical reality

## Outreach Strategy Pivot

Key strategic decision: outreach efforts should target family medicine doctors (GPs) rather than mental health centers.

**Rationale:**
- Mental health centers are competitors, not referral sources
- Family doctors are the primary referral pipeline for patients needing psychiatric services
- GPs see patients first and make the initial referral decision

**Action items:**
- Coordinate Adi Lavi's field outreach with Ori
- Divide geographic territories to avoid overlap
- Focus messaging on what the clinic offers that family doctors need (quick access to psychiatric evaluation, medication management)

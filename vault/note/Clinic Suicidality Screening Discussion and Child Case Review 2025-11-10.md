# Clinic Suicidality Screening Discussion and Child Case Review 2025-11-10

## Context

Voice memo captured on 2025-11-10 during an in-person conversation between Rami and a senior clinician at or near the clinic. The conversation addresses a concerning child intake case from the previous day and establishes protocol guidance for suicidality screening.

## The Child Intake Incident

Rami describes a case from the previous day (2025-11-09) involving Ahmad (Ahmed Masalha), the child psychiatrist:

1. **Parent intake (by case manager):** During the parent intake, parents reported their child had made a single suicidal statement several months ago, followed by multiple psychiatric evaluations with no current suicidality identified.
2. **Child intake (by Ahmad):** When Ahmad spoke directly with the child, the situation was drastically different — the child reported having an active suicide plan (wanting to hang himself) and had accessed his father's firearm. This was a completely different picture from what the parents described.
3. **Ahmad's reaction:** Ahmad expressed concern that the clinic should take suicidality more seriously and suggested not sending such patients to psychiatrists without additional screening. He also proposed that the case manager (team lead) should see the child directly before referral to psychiatry.

## Clinical Guidance Provided

The senior clinician (speaker 2) provided clear direction:

- **Case manager role boundary:** The case manager's role does NOT include ruling out suicidality. This is not standard practice anywhere. A child who arrives at the clinic could become suicidal at any point, and the psychiatric evaluation is designed to handle this.
- **No workflow change needed:** The suggestion that case managers should screen children for suicidality before psychiatric referral was rejected. The current workflow remains appropriate.
- **Reinforced screening during parent intake:** However, during the parent intake with parents/caregivers, case managers should strengthen their suicidality-related questioning by explicitly asking about:
  - Whether there have been any incidents (past suicidal statements or behavior)
  - Current suicidal thoughts
  - Past suicide attempts
  - Self-harm behaviors
  - The child's talk about death
- **Action needed:** Anan (a team member, possibly a clinical lead) should explain this protocol clarification to Ahmad directly.

## Key Takeaways

1. **No change to case manager workflow** — case managers do NOT screen for suicidality; that remains the psychiatrist's domain
2. **Strengthen parent intake suicidality questions** — ask specifically about current thoughts, past attempts, self-harm, and death talk
3. **Anan to clarify with Ahmad** — explain the protocol rationale so Ahmad understands the workflow boundary
4. **Case underscores the gap between parent reporting and child reality** — parents may significantly underreport suicidal ideation, reinforcing the importance of the psychiatric intake as the definitive assessment

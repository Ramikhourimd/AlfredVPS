# Basel First Impressions and Telia'z Brand History 2025-09-21

## Context

Two related conversations captured during the [[event/Telia'z Company Fun Day 2025-09-21]] on September 21, 2025. The first segment features [[person/Basel Kanaaneh]] being asked about his initial impressions after joining [[org/Telia'z]]. The second segment involves a group discussion about the Telia'z brand's visual identity history.

## Basel's First Impression — "One Big Mess"

Someone prompted Basel directly: "Tell us, what was the first experience? Where did you land? A bunch of crazy people, a bunch of... what's the vibe you feel?"

Basel's response was characteristically blunt: **"One big mess"** (בלגן אחד גדול).

The follow-up question probed how this "mess" reconciles with the company's technology foundation. Basel's one-word answer — **"Gold"** (זהב) — suggested that despite the surface chaos, the underlying technology platform is valuable.

Another attendee then quipped dismissively: "Treatment, treatment, treatment. That's bullshit. Yalla, yalla" — possibly expressing impatience with the clinical operations side vs. the tech.

This exchange captures a common perception among newer [[org/Telia'z]] team members: the company's operational layer appears chaotic, but the technology and clinical platform beneath it is genuinely impressive. It aligns with the [[assumption/Basel Khouri Is the De Facto Operational Leader of Clinic Israel|assumption about Basel's operational leadership role]] — he is now tasked with bringing order to this "mess."

## Telia'z Brand Logo and Design History

A separate nostalgic conversation about the Telia'z visual brand unfolded:

- **The X symbol**: An attendee mentioned "the X" — a symbol that was once part of the Telia'z logo. Others confirmed they remembered it, suggesting it was used in earlier branding iterations but has since been replaced by the current circle-based logo
- **Current logo**: Described as "just circles of the logo, without 'Telia' written" — indicating the brand moved to a more minimal, abstract identity
- **Past weekly printouts**: Someone recalled a period when creative printouts were produced weekly — "you used to make me printouts every week, what fun that was" — suggesting an era of more hands-on, artisanal marketing materials
- **Stone-based designs**: A reference to "we made them in stone" — possibly stamped or engraved materials

## Shachar's Creative Credit

The conversation pivoted to giving credit to [[person/Shachar]]:

- **"Lots of credit to Shachar"** (הרבה קרדיט לשחר) — an explicit acknowledgment of Shachar's contribution to the company's creative and design output
- Shachar's response was humble: "Really, there's nothing to say"
- Historical context: Shachar was previously involved in the company's branding and creative work, including the stamp/seal design referenced earlier
- The conversation suggests Shachar played a foundational role in establishing the Telia'z visual identity during the company's earlier stages

## Work vs. Vacation Philosophy

A brief exchange touched on work-life perspective:

- Someone noted they were on vacation/holiday at the moment
- The response: "First you change the place in the world — that's not vacation, that's very, very good"
- Counter: "Better than a week of vacation"
- This reflects a company culture where meaningful work at Telia'z is framed as intrinsically rewarding, not just employment

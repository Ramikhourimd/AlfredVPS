# Case Manager Session: Patient Mental Health and Autism Screening 2025-11-11

## Context

Recorded case manager session at the Telia'z clinic with an anonymous patient. The session covers autism/Asperger's screening results, self-harm, suicidal ideation, anxiety, and discontinued antidepressant medication. The patient prepared written notes in advance because they find it difficult to articulate thoughts verbally during sessions.

## Autism/Asperger's Screening Results

- Patient completed an autism screening assessment showing "signs of autism" and "many typical signs of autism"
- Patient scored 7 on a scale where 80% of people with Asperger's score above 6
- Patient identifies with traits: being overly straightforward, others perceiving directness as offensive, difficulty with social nuance
- Patient previously saw psychologist "Taliel Benjamin" (partial name) for 4 appointments and requested a summary letter
- Referral to additional services was promised but no follow-up received yet — patient currently not seeing anyone for autism assessment

## Suicidal Ideation and Self-Harm

Patient read from prepared written notes describing their internal experience:

- **Suicidal ideation**: Patient frames suicidal thoughts as rational response to persistent mental suffering — "My brain is part of my body and that is not temporary. It is a permanent problem."
- **Self-harm pattern**: Cuts when angry as a way to release rage; has escalated and de-escalated — fainted once from deep cut, now uses metal key which causes bruises and minor bleeding
- **Recent self-harm**: Hurt themselves "just a few days ago" due to anger
- **Rooftop incident**: Was on rooftop "looking over the edge" at 3-4 AM, "admiring the courage of others who have taken their own lives"
- **Ambivalence**: Reports fluctuating self-perception — "Sometimes I hate myself and sometimes I love myself. I love myself for hating myself and I hate myself for loving myself."
- **Minimization pattern**: After crises, patient questions whether they are "exaggerating" but acknowledges "bad feelings always come back eventually"

## Anxiety and Medication History

- Patient was prescribed antidepressants which significantly spiked anxiety
- Discontinued medication on own initiative and felt less anxious afterward
- Multiple professionals expressed disapproval of stopping medication, which the patient found offensive
- Patient believes residual anxiety persists from the antidepressant period — "now I'm scared of that anxiety and so it feeds itself"
- Panic attacks described as feeling "like the end is near" and "I'm going to die" — hospital confirmed these as anxiety attacks
- Anxiety drives suicidal ideation: "I'm not necessarily a suicidal person until I feel anxious. The anxiety is unbearable."

## Sleep and Energy

- Sleep described as only reprieve from mental suffering: "Sleep is the only time I get a break from my own mind"
- Persistent fatigue regardless of sleep duration
- No motivation to exercise, no intention of seeking motivation
- Morning dread upon waking as "everything comes back"

## Case Manager Intervention

The case manager (speaker 2) begins a cognitive reframing approach near session end:
- Distinguishes between "brain" (permanent) and "thoughts" (changeable activity of the brain)
- Encourages patient to "fight the thoughts and not yourself"
- Challenges patient's belief that nothing can change because their brain is the problem
- Session transcript cuts off during this therapeutic intervention

## Clinical Risk Assessment Notes

This session presents **high-risk indicators** that would typically warrant escalation:
- Recent rooftop incident with suicidal contemplation
- Active self-harm within past week
- Expressed admiration for completed suicides
- Anxiety-driven suicidal ideation pattern
- Discontinued medication without clinical supervision
- Currently not receiving specialist follow-up for autism assessment
- Nocturnal pattern of crisis episodes (3-5 AM)

# Employee Experience Research and Innovation Role Definition Meeting 2026-02-18

## Context

Work session between Rami Khouri and Rivi Idelman on 2026-02-18. Rivi reported back on her field research with clinic staff, and Rami provided strategic direction on how to formalize her research methodology and clarify her role boundaries within the innovation team.

## Key Findings: Staff Burnout and Satisfaction

### Case Manager and Psychiatrist Burnout
- Both case managers and psychiatrists report significant work intensity and burnout
- Staff working 6-8 hours per week at the clinic seem to manage better; longer hours correlate with higher burnout
- One psychiatrist (Sa'id) reported that working 3-4 hours daily across 5 days was far more draining than 1.5 hours daily — shorter concentrated sessions work better even at the same weekly total
- After the clinical supervision forums (hadracha) began, at least one case manager reported a noticeable reduction in burnout, suggesting group support mechanisms help

### Isolation and Teamwork Gap
- Most staff do not feel a strong sense of teamwork — they work alone from home facing a screen
- Even when paired with another psychiatrist on a case, there is little need for routine discussion
- The move to an internal tasks system (replacing WhatsApp) is beneficial for task tracking but may further reduce the informal social interactions that WhatsApp groups provided
- Risk: increased isolation as communication becomes more structured and less informal

### Unanswered Communication
- Staff report writing messages in WhatsApp groups (questions to psychiatrists, technical issues, messages to Renee) that go unanswered
- This creates a feeling of abandonment — one staff member said: "I feel like we are orphaned children without a parent — we have to take care of everything ourselves and there is no one above telling us what should happen"

### Unreported Extra Hours
- Psychiatrists are working extra hours (preparation before sessions, reviewing AI summaries, rewriting documentation) but not reporting them
- They know that reporting will trigger questions about efficiency, so they absorb the extra work silently
- Case managers also do summary reviews but this is less problematic as their hours are less constrained

### Psychiatrist Retention Risk
- Many psychiatrists, especially the more experienced ones, stay primarily because of convenience (work from home, pay) rather than passion or professional growth
- Rivi senses they would leave if a better offer came along — not stated explicitly but felt in conversations
- Case managers appear more committed to staying (except for workload-driven departures)

### Loss of Mission After Maccabi Transition
- One psychiatrist (Ilya) noted that the transition from post-October 7th trauma work (Clalit South) to routine Maccabi patients removed both the professional diversity and the sense of social mission
- Regular HMO patients feel routine; the work becomes "just another health fund clinic but with worse conditions" (remote, isolated)

### Follow-Up Session Protocol Gap
- Follow-up sessions (3rd, 4th visits) lack a defined protocol — staff improvise what to discuss
- Intake and first/second follow-ups have clearer goals (side effects, dosage), but later sessions have no structured framework
- An intake checklist Rivi created and distributed to case managers was well-received and brought clarity — staff requested something similar for follow-up sessions
- The pre-treatment patient video should also address why follow-up sessions matter, to set patient expectations

## Research Methodology Directive

Rami instructed Rivi to treat her staff feedback work as academic research, not informal reporting:
- **Structure**: Background, methodology, results, discussion, recommendations
- **Measurability**: Convert qualitative impressions into measurable scales/metrics
- **Questionnaire**: Build a periodic staff satisfaction questionnaire covering: financial satisfaction, work comfort, home-work dynamics, hours, atmosphere, professional growth, technology, surrounding support
- **Segmentation**: Correlate satisfaction with variables (morning/evening shifts, weekday/weekend, case load, seniority, role type)
- **Delivery**: Rivi to create a Google Sheet with research questions; Rami will build an automated system to process them overnight and return structured research findings

## Role Clarification

Rami reinforced the gated concept design framework:
- **Rivi's scope**: Research, problem definition, concept design, recommendations
- **Not her scope**: Execution, implementation, building solutions
- Concept documents can include a "recommendations for implementation" section as an addendum
- When staff raise operational issues, Rivi should redirect to Shira (clinical) or Renee (administrative) — she only collects innovation/improvement ideas
- Staff should not direct operational complaints to Rivi; she should clarify her role when meeting them

## Pre-Treatment Patient Video
- Rivi drafted a concept spec based on conversations with Renee about common patient drop-off points
- Needs to be reviewed by Rami for format guidance (concept doc, not production spec)
- Goal: Set patient expectations about the treatment journey, especially the value of follow-up sessions

## Next-Generation System Prototype
- Rami proposed starting a collaborative exercise to envision the most advanced possible system
- Approach: Map all existing system capabilities, brainstorm new innovative features, assume unlimited tools
- Create a visual/conceptual prototype, then build a yearly development plan to reach that vision
- Rivi should start by surveying existing competitor/peer systems and gathering literature
- For literature reviews, Rivi should request support from Ohad Edri

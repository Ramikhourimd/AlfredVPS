# Vehicle PPF Coating Process Discussion 2025-10-09

Voice memo recorded on October 9, 2025 — a conversation in Hebrew between Rami (Speaker 1) and a PPF (Paint Protection Film) installer/technician (Speaker 2) at a coating shop. This is one of several PPF-related recordings from the same day.

## Context

Rami visited a PPF installer to discuss the coating process. The technician explains how their workflow handles spoiler removal and surface preparation. Rami mentions he had the coating done a week after purchasing the vehicle.

## PPF Coating Process Explained by Technician

The installer (Speaker 2) described the following procedure:

1. **Spoiler removal:** The shop (a separate body/paint shop) removes the spoiler from the vehicle before coating begins.
2. **Surface inspection and cleaning:** Once the spoiler is removed, the technician inspects and cleans the surface underneath, checking for any "dos gadi" (dents or damage) that may be hidden.
3. **Photo documentation:** The technician photographs the clean spoiler to confirm it is free of defects before re-applying the PPF film.
4. **Damage liability:** If the shop causes damage during spoiler disassembly, they issue a work order ("seder shidur b'hashbacha") documenting the damage. In that case, the technician does NOT proceed with PPF application until the damage is resolved — the shop replaces or repairs the damaged part first.
5. **Re-application:** Once the surface is confirmed clean, the technician applies the new PPF film and returns the original spoiler piece to the customer.

## Key Concern Raised

The technician emphasized a practical problem: if he himself were to remove the spoiler and accidentally damage the coating underneath, he would have no repair materials or spare parts on hand. That is why the disassembly is handled by a separate body shop that can take responsibility for any damage. He expressed concern that damage during disassembly is a realistic risk.

## Timeline Note

Speaker 1 (Rami) stated: "I did this a week after I bought the car" — confirming the PPF was applied shortly after vehicle purchase. This places the original PPF application in the context of either the previous vehicle or the early ownership period.

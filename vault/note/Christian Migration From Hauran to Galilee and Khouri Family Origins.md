# Christian Migration From Hauran to Galilee and Khouri Family Origins

## Context

This note consolidates oral history shared by [[person/Majdi Shaddad]] — a Shaddad family member from Rameh who has been researching the family tree since approximately 1980. The information was recorded during a conversation with [[person/Rami Khouri]] on 2025-12-02. All dates and details are from oral tradition and genealogical research, not verified against primary historical sources.

## Migration Timeline

### Phase 1: Hauran Origins (~1725)
The Shaddad family (Dar Shaddad) left their original location — either Hauran (southern Syria) or possibly Zahleh (Lebanon) — around 1725. The exact origin point is debated within the family; Majdi was told it was either Hauran or Zahleh.

### Phase 2: Lebanon Period (1725–early 1800s)
The family moved from Hauran to Lebanon. During the early 1800s, sectarian conflicts between the Druze and Christian communities in Mount Lebanon escalated, leading to further displacement of Christians.

### Phase 3: Coastal Palestine (early 1800s–1865)
Christians who fled Lebanon settled along the Palestinian coast and worked primarily in trade. By 1865, there were almost no Christians living in the inland Galilee villages.

### Phase 4: Settlement in Galilee (~1865 onwards)
Circumstances (details unclear) forced coastal families to move inland to the Galilee villages. Key settlements included:
- **Suata** (السوطة)
- **Rameh** (الرامة) — became the Shaddad/Khouri family's primary village
- **Mi'ilya** (معليا)
- **Maghar** (المغار) — church established around 1860-1865
- **Safed** (صفد)
- **Tiberias** (طبريا)

## The Three Shaddad Branches

The Shaddad family split into three branches, all considered cousins (awlad al-amm):
1. **Eilabun branch** (الجبيا/إعبلين) — Shaddad family members settled in Eilabun
2. **Rameh branch** (الرامة) — Majdi's branch; this is where the Khouri surname originated
3. **Nazareth branch** (الناصرة) — Shaddad family members settled in Nazareth

## Origin of the Khouri Surname

The name "Khouri" (خوري) derives from "qasis" (قسيس), meaning "priest" in Arabic. A member of the Shaddad family served as a priest (qasis) in the village of Rameh. Over time, the descendants became known as "Dar Khouri" (the house of the priest) — the children of the Khouri. This is a common pattern in Levantine Christian communities where families take their surname from an ancestor's ecclesiastical role.

## Diaspora Connections

Majdi's research has identified family members who emigrated from the region:
- **Yacoub al-Asif** — was at a church house (possibly a seminary or church institution) in Beirut
- **Boulos** (Paul) — living in Chile at the time of recording

These diaspora connections are part of a broader pattern of Levantine Christian emigration to Latin America and other regions.

## The Shahoud Family

During the conversation, Rami asked Majdi about the Shahoud (شحود) family. Majdi noted:
- He had encountered the Shahoud name in a military book/records
- He was unable to determine their region of origin
- Further research is needed on this family name

## Research Status

Majdi has been actively building the family tree since approximately 1980. He maintains contact with relatives across branches and invites family members to visit during holidays to share information. The research is ongoing and primarily based on oral history and personal records.

# Psychiatrist Recruitment Impact Causal Framework 2025-12-04

## Context

Voice memo from Rami recorded during the 2025-12-04 brainstorming day, articulating a structured causal chain model for evaluating the downstream impact of recruiting experienced, certified psychiatrists. This framework connects a single input (hiring) through multiple mechanism layers to measurable KPI outcomes. The model was developed as part of the broader [[note/Clinic Protocol Framework and KPI Strategy 2025-12-04|protocol framework and KPI strategy work]].

Rami noted he was specifically working on the "recruitment case" (גיוס) as he walked through the framework.

## The Four-Layer Causal Framework

### Layer 1: Input
- **Recruitment of a certified, experienced psychiatrist** — The foundational investment that drives all downstream effects.

### Layer 2: Mechanism (How it creates value)
An experienced psychiatrist produces:
- **Faster, more accurate diagnosis** — Reduced time to correct diagnosis
- **Fewer unnecessary medication changes** — More targeted pharmacotherapy from the start
- **Ability to handle complex cases** — Fewer referrals out or escalations
- **Fewer patient transfers** — Less need to reassign patients between clinicians
- **Fewer documentation errors** — Higher quality medical records
- **Fewer "free clauses" (תניות חופשיות)** — Reduced unstructured/unbillable clinical activity

### Layer 3: Intermediate Effects (Observable changes)
The mechanisms produce measurable intermediate outcomes:
- **Fewer urgent follow-ups** — Patients stabilize faster
- **Fewer case manager referrals for transfers** — Less administrative burden from patient reassignment
- **Improvement in PHQ/GAD scores** — Better patient-reported outcomes on standardized measures
- **Reduction in side effects** — More appropriate prescribing from the start

### Layer 4: KPI Impact (Bottom-line metrics)
The intermediate effects flow into tracked KPIs across two domains:

**Clinical KPIs:**
- Diagnostic accuracy
- Treatment success rates
- Side effect burden on treatment outcomes
- Regression rates (patient relapse)

**Operational KPIs:**
- Examination time (time per session)
- Clinical engagement time (active treatment time)
- Treatment cost (through the patient care journey)

## Analysis

This framework demonstrates Rami's approach to connecting upstream clinical quality decisions to downstream business metrics — a core principle in the [[synthesis/Clinical Excellence to Revenue Causal Chain as Scaling Lever|clinical excellence to revenue causal chain]]. By tracing a single input (hiring quality) through mechanism, intermediate effects, and KPI impact, it provides a structured argument for investing in experienced psychiatrists rather than simply maximizing headcount.

The model is notable for including both clinical outcomes (PHQ/GAD, side effects) and operational efficiency metrics (session time, engagement time) in the same framework, reinforcing the idea that clinical and operational performance are linked.

Rami acknowledged that "treatment cost" as a KPI may need further work to define precisely, noting it would flow "through the patient care journey" (דרך מסע הכיפה).

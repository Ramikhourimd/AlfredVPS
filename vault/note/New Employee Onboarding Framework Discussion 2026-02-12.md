# New Employee Onboarding Framework Discussion 2026-02-12

## Context

A 1:1 clinical management meeting between Rami Khouri and Elias Jahjah (meeting series HM-RAMI-ELIAS, now discontinued) focused on designing the onboarding structure for new clinic employees. The discussion was conducted in Arabic.

## Key Points

### Onboarding Cadence
- Rami proposed that onboarding sessions should occur once a month or once every two months, depending on hiring volume
- Elias confirmed he could handle one or two sessions per month from his side
- The cadence would flex based on how many new hires need orientation in any given month

### Three-Track Onboarding Model
Rami outlined a model that separates onboarding into three distinct tracks:

1. **Operational Track** — Can begin without formal orientation. Covers day-to-day clinical workflows and procedures
2. **Administrative Track** — Can also begin without formal orientation. Covers HR processes, agreements, scheduling
3. **Technology Track (MANDATORY)** — This is the absolute must-have for day one. New staff cannot function without access to:
   - **Microsoft Teams** — the primary communication and video platform for remote sessions
   - **HoViD** — the clinical record system that is essential for all clinical operations

### Who Delivers Onboarding
- Rami identified either **Stav** (Stav Zamir) or **Shira** (Shira Lachman) as the right people to lead the operational + technology onboarding combined sessions
- The idea is to combine operational and technology orientations into a single session led by one of these staff members

### Rationale
- The critical failure mode is a new clinician arriving and not knowing how to log into Teams, which directly blocks them from seeing patients
- Operational and administrative knowledge can be picked up gradually, but technology access is a hard prerequisite
- Clinical onboarding (protocols, supervision) is a separate concern from operational/technology onboarding

# Experienced Psychiatrist Impact Causal Model

Rami articulates a structured four-layer causal impact framework for understanding the value of hiring experienced, board-certified psychiatrists at Telia'z Clinic Israel. This model maps the chain from input to measurable KPI outcomes.

## The Four-Layer Framework

### Layer 1: Input
- **Hiring an experienced, board-certified psychiatrist** — the investment action

### Layer 2: Mechanism (Direct Capabilities)
The experienced psychiatrist produces these direct operational effects:
- **Faster and more accurate diagnosis** — reduced diagnostic uncertainty
- **Fewer unnecessary medication changes** — more confident initial prescriptions
- **Ability to handle complex cases** — reduces need for transfers or second opinions
- **Fewer patient transfers** — complex cases handled in-house
- **Fewer documentation errors** — clinical experience reduces paperwork mistakes
- **Fewer unstructured consultations** — efficient use of clinical time

### Layer 3: Intermediate Effects (Clinical Outcomes)
The mechanisms cascade into measurable clinical improvements:
- **Fewer urgent follow-ups** — stable patients require less unplanned contact
- **Fewer case manager requests for transfers** — reduced escalation load
- **Improvement in PHQ/GAD scores** — better patient-reported mental health outcomes
- **Reduction in medication side effects** — better prescribing leads to fewer adverse reactions

### Layer 4: KPI Impact (Business Metrics)
The intermediate effects ultimately impact core business KPIs:
- **Employment assistance outcomes** — patients return to work faster
- **Clinical diagnostic accuracy** — measurable accuracy rate
- **Side effects impact on treatment success** — fewer dropouts due to side effects
- **Treatment regressions** — fewer patients needing to restart treatment
- **Operational: examination time** — more efficient per-session time
- **Operational: clinical engagement time** — optimized clinician hours
- **Treatment cost** — overall cost reduction through the entire care journey

## Rami's Commentary

Rami notes that treatment cost impact flows through the entire care journey ("derech masa hakipa") rather than being a direct single-step outcome. He acknowledges the framework is relatively straightforward but necessary to formalize for leadership presentation and KPI framework design.

## Significance

This causal model provides the logical backbone for the clinic's KPI framework. It connects the hiring decision (input) to the board-level metrics (KPI impact) through a traceable chain. This is directly relevant to:
- The protocol framework document being prepared for the board
- The KPI persuasion deck
- The hiring justification for the medical director role

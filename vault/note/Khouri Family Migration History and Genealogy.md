# Khouri Family Migration History and Genealogy

## Context

On 2025-12-02, Majdi — a relative from the Shaddad family who has been researching the family tree since the 1980s — shared a detailed oral history of the Khouri/Shaddad family migration and genealogy. This was recorded as a voice memo. Majdi is building a comprehensive family tree and connecting relatives across branches and the diaspora.

## Migration Timeline

### Phase 1: Hauran Origin (c. 1725)
The Shaddad family ("Dar Shaddad") left their original location around 1725 and settled in Hauran (southern Syria). Some accounts suggest they may have come from or passed through Zahleh (Lebanon) before reaching Hauran.

### Phase 2: Lebanon Transit (c. 1725–1860s)
From Hauran, the family moved to Lebanon. During the early-to-mid 1800s, sectarian conflicts erupted between Druze and Christian communities in Lebanon (historically known as the 1860 Mount Lebanon civil war and related tensions). These conflicts drove Christian families, including the Shaddads, to seek safety elsewhere.

### Phase 3: Coastal Palestine — Trade Era (c. 1860s)
Christians migrating from Lebanon initially settled along the Palestinian coast, where they engaged in trade. Around 1865, there were almost no Christians in the Galilee villages or rural areas — the community was concentrated on the coast.

### Phase 4: Settlement in Galilee Villages (post-1865)
Changing circumstances (likely economic pressures or new opportunities) pushed the coastal Christian traders into the interior Galilee villages. They settled in: Suhmata (al-Suwta), Rameh (al-Rama), Ma'ilya, Eilabun, Maghar, Safed, and Tiberias.

The Maghar church was established around 1860-1865, marking the beginning of formal Christian community presence in that village.

## The Three Shaddad Branches

The Shaddad family split into three cousin branches that settled in different Galilee towns:

1. **Eilabun branch** — Shaddad family in Eilabun (البعنة/اللبيا)
2. **Rameh branch** — Shaddad family in Rameh (الرامة) — this is Rami's branch
3. **Nazareth branch** — Shaddad family in Nazareth

Majdi states these three branches are all "awlad al-amm" (paternal cousins) — descendants of the same ancestor.

## Origin of the Khouri Surname

The surname "Khouri" (خوري) derives from "qasis" (قسيس), meaning priest. In the village system, each community had a priest. The priest's descendants became known as "Dar Khouri" (House of the Priest). In Rameh specifically, the priestly family became known by the Khouri name, which persisted as a hereditary surname.

The sequence: Shaddad (clan name) → a family member becomes the village priest → descendants called "awlad al-Khouri" (children of the priest) → surname becomes Khouri.

## Diaspora Connections

Majdi has traced two notable diaspora members:
- **Yacoub al-Aseef** — settled near a church/monastery in Beirut
- **Boulos** — settled in Chile (a common destination for Levantine Christian emigration in the late 19th/early 20th century)

## Research Status

Majdi has been conducting this genealogical research since approximately 1980. He has been building a family tree and connecting with relatives across the three branches. He mentioned having found the Shahoud family name in military records — the Shahoud family may be connected to the broader Shaddad/Khouri clan, though the exact relationship and origin are unclear.

## About Shahoud

The speaker's grandfather's family name was Shahoud (شحود). Majdi says he encountered this name in a military book/record. The geographic origin of the Shahoud family is unknown to Majdi. This is an open research thread.

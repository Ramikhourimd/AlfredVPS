# Company Fun Day Salary Planning and Identity Discussion 2025-09-21

## Context

Casual conversation captured during the [[event/Telia'z Company Fun Day 2025-09-21]] on September 21, 2025. The conversation is between [[person/Rami Khouri]] (Speaker 2) and a female colleague who handles HR, admin, and onboarding tasks at [[org/Telia'z]] (Speaker 1). This is a separate recording from the [[note/Telia'z Company Fun Day Recording 2025-09-21|leadership speech recording]] and the [[note/Staff Management Discussion at Company Fun Day 2025-09-21|staff management discussion]].

The recording naturally splits into two halves: work logistics and a cultural identity discussion.

## Work and Logistics

### October Salary Processing

Speaker 1 notes that October presents scheduling challenges for salary processing. Yom Kippur falls at the start of the month, followed immediately by Sukkot, meaning the effective working month begins around the 5th. She needs to plan the salary cycle accordingly.

### Onboarding Tal Goldshmid

Speaker 1 describes the hiring process for [[person/Tal Goldshmid]]:
- Someone reached out to Tal (she did not submit a CV herself).
- Tal is approximately 25–26 years old.
- Speaker 1 conducted a Zoom interview with her on Wednesday.
- Tal came to the office on Thursday to meet [[person/Dekkel Taliaz]].
- Speaker 1 says she "really liked her" and is waiting to hear back from Dekkel and [[person/Oren Taliaz]].
- Speaker 1 will need to onboard ("overlap") the new hire once she starts.

This provides backstory to Tal's hiring at Telia'z, prior to her taking on the Office Administrator role.

### Shira Running Late

[[person/Shira]] messaged Speaker 1 to say they would arrive late, around 14:30. Speaker 1 was not surprised and adjusted the tour start by half an hour.

### Tour Delay

A scheduled tour (likely part of the Fun Day activities) was pushed back. Speaker 1 mentions the tour was supposed to start at 17:00 but needed to be shifted due to the late arrivals.

## Personal and Cultural Discussion

### Ramallah Modernity

Speaker 1, who relocated from Haifa to her village during COVID, describes Ramallah as a modern city where one can move around freely. She qualifies that the Old City area requires more modest dress, but nightlife with bars exists. Rami compares this to the contrast between Ramat Gan and Bnei Brak — a modern area adjacent to a more religiously conservative one. They agree that Ramallah has both a modern and a more traditional/religious side, similar to Israeli cities.

### Identity Discussion

[[person/Alex Taliaz]] made a comment describing the [[org/Telia'z]] team as "Palestinians and Israelis." This triggered a deep conversation about identity labels:

- **Rami's response**: He told Alex "I'm Syrian, habibi" (אני סוריה, חמוד), pushing back on the Palestinian label. He explains his family is Christian from Syria, tracing back to the 1700s — consistent with the [[project/Khouri Family Genealogy]] records documenting the family's Syrian Christian origins.
- **Terminology debate**: They discuss whether "Palestinian," "Israeli Arab," or "Arab" is the appropriate term. Rami notes that terminology matters — he identifies as "a Christian from Syria" rather than using national labels. He also questions why he should accept "Israeli Arab" or "Palestinian" when neither fully captures his identity.
- **Speaker 1's perspective**: She draws a parallel to calling herself Moroccan — an ethnic/heritage label rather than a political one. She argues that Palestinian identity is not exclusively Muslim, noting that Christians are also Palestinian.
- **Golda Meir reference**: Speaker 1 brings up that Golda Meir called herself Palestinian, because before the establishment of the State of Israel, everyone living in the region was considered Palestinian. She uses this to illustrate that "Palestinian" was historically a geographic rather than ethnic term.
- **Religious vs. national identity**: They explore whether "Christian" is an identity marker Rami uses. He says he does not define himself as Christian per se, but uses it as part of "Christian from Syria" to describe his family heritage rather than as a religious identity.

This conversation complements but differs from the [[note/Social Conversation Arab Identity The Chosen and Patriotism 2025-09-21|separate identity discussion]] from the same day, which focused on The Chosen TV series, cultural appropriation, and Druze/Bedouin identity. Here the focus is specifically on the Palestinian/Israeli/Syrian/Christian label debate triggered by Alex's comment.

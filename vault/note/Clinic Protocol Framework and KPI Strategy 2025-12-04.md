# Clinic Protocol Framework and KPI Strategy 2025-12-04

## Context

Rami and Basel held an in-person brainstorming session to define the operational protocol framework for [[project/Telia'z Clinic Israel]]. The session was motivated by a critical gap: Rami and Basel understand what operational changes are needed, but the company's current practices (reviewed in weekly Sunday meetings) focus on surface metrics (new patients, Facebook campaigns, monthly revenue) rather than the deeper operational and clinical indicators that drive sustainable growth. Rami needs to formalize and document this knowledge to convince leadership (Dekkel, Oren) to change direction.

A third person (speaker [3], likely [[person/Rana Khouri]]) was present but engaged in a separate background conversation and did not participate in the main discussion.

## Hypergrowth Model

Rami presented a comparison of two hypergrowth models for the clinic. The key challenge is measuring the right KPIs for scaling — not just counting psychiatrists or tracking hiring flow, but measuring deeper operational efficiency metrics that indicate readiness to scale.

The critical insight: there is a **gap between what Rami and Basel know needs to happen vs. what the company has decided to do**. If Rami cannot articulate and present this clearly, leadership will continue with their current approach, and every Sunday meeting will remain a frustrating misalignment. The goal of this session was to capture and structure this knowledge for persuasive presentation.

## Protocol Framework

The discussion defined two main protocol categories:

### 1. Clinical Protocols (קליני)

Clinical protocols govern patient care quality and outcomes. Key subcategories discussed:

- **Treatment response** (תגובת טיפול) — Better treatment response rates
- **Remission rates** (רמיסיה / אחוזי רמיסיה) — Reducing relapse, improving sustained remission
- **Hospitalization reduction** (הורדת אחוזי אשפוזים) — Fewer patients needing inpatient care
- **ER visit reduction** (הורדת פניות למיון) — Fewer emergency department presentations
- **Cost/burden reduction** (הורדת עלויות / נטל על מערכת בריאות ציבורית) — Demonstrable value to the public health system
- **Better HMO agreements** (שיפור בהסכמים) — Clinical proof → better contract terms with Maccabi/Clalit

The clinical metrics tie directly to business outcomes: proving clinical value leads to reduced costs on the public health system, which leads to better contract terms with HMOs, which leads to revenue growth.

### 2. Administrative Protocols (אדמיניסטרטיבי)

Administrative protocols define how the staff operates. These were further broken down into:

| בקשת טענויות מקצועיות | Professional complaints | Handle clinical-professional grievances |
| בקשת בניות מצטבר | Accumulated structural requests | Track and manage operational structure change requests |
| בקשת שילות דלימות | Delivery queries | Handle operational delivery questions |

| ייעול תקשורת | Communication efficiency | Streamline internal communication |
| ייעול תקשורת בין צוותית | Inter-team communication | Improve coordination between clinical teams |

#### Protocol Goals/KPIs

| Goal (Hebrew) | English | Category |
|---|---|---|
| סביבת רצון | Staff/patient satisfaction | Satisfaction |
| עלייה בהכנסות | Revenue increase | Financial |
| הורדת הוצאות | Cost reduction | Financial |
| מצוי זמן | Time optimization | Efficiency |
| שימור עובדים | Staff retention | HR |

### 3. Training and Onboarding (הכשרה ותניכה)

Training and onboarding was identified as a key enabler. It connects to two critical outcomes:

- **Professional development** (הכשרות מקצועיות) — More competent staff
- **Staff retention** (שימור עובדים) — Better-trained staff are more engaged and stay longer
- **Reduced hiring costs** (הורדת הוצאות על גיוס) — Retaining existing staff means less spending on recruitment and training of replacements
- **Attraction** — Being more attractive to better quality candidates

The connection: good training → better satisfaction → lower turnover → lower hiring costs → more institutional knowledge retained.

### 4. Integration Protocols (אינטגרציה)

Basel discussed the need for an integration (onboarding) protocol for new staff that provides clear guidelines from day one, so every new hire knows exactly what the service includes and what it does not. The current lack of structured onboarding means each new staff member has to figure things out on their own, leading to inconsistency, errors, and wasted time.

## Strategic Communication Plan

A key meta-theme: Rami emphasized that having the right protocols is necessary but not sufficient — he also needs to **present this framework persuasively to leadership**. The Sunday meetings currently focus on the wrong metrics, and unless Rami can demonstrate why the company needs to change its operational approach, leadership will continue with the status quo.

Rami framed this as: "I need to take what you and I know, put it in a structured format, so I can convince them to change their current approach. If I can do this now, things will be much easier throughout the year."

Brief mention of the VP Medical and Innovation (סמנכ"ל רפואי וחדשנות) role and its connection to the clinic's vision. The clinical-business integration needs to be understood at leadership level — this is something Rami needs to explain to the leadership team.

# Body Shop PPF Pricing and Insurance Claim Strategy 2025-10-09

## Context

Voice memo recorded on October 9, 2025 at a body shop during a vehicle repair visit. Multiple speakers in Hebrew and Arabic: Rami (Speaker 1), a body shop advisor (Speaker 2), and a technician (Speaker 3). This recording captures a distinct segment from the same day documented across several related notes — taking place at the body shop rather than the service center.

## PPF Coating Pricing Breakdown

Discussion of the PPF (Paint Protection Film) coating that was applied to the vehicle several years prior. The body shop advisor provided a detailed cost breakdown of what PPF application typically runs:

- **Per-part cost:** 1,000 to 1,400 NIS per component (hood, bumper, fenders, etc.)
- **Total estimated cost:** Approximately 5,000 NIS for full-vehicle PPF application
- **Large panel pricing:** Up to 1,900 NIS for the largest panels
- **Context:** The pricing was discussed in Arabic, with the advisor explaining cost structures to help Rami understand the value and expense of what was originally applied

This pricing context is relevant to the ongoing PPF warranty dispute documented in [[note/Vehicle PPF Removal Damage Discovery 2025-10-09]] — understanding the original investment helps frame the frustration over PPF-related damage.

## Phoenix Insurance Claim Strategy

The body shop advisor (Speaker 2) suggested a strategy for filing an insurance claim through Phoenix Insurance (Rami's insurer at the time for his pre-BYD vehicle):

- **Claim target:** Sun visors and mirrors damage
- **Deductible:** 250 NIS (10% of estimated 2,500 NIS claim value)
- **Suggested narrative:** Report the damage as having occurred while the car was parked at a mall/parking lot — describe discovering the damage upon return to the parked vehicle
- **Process:** File a police report first, then submit the insurance claim
- **Recommendation:** The advisor noted this approach could cover the cost of repairs with only the 250 NIS deductible out of pocket

Phoenix Insurance was Rami's insurer before switching to [[org/Bituach Yashir]] for the [[asset/BYD Vehicle]] in November 2025. The BYD insurance quotes comparison from November 2025 confirms Rami "mentioned paying less for his Tesla through Phoenix Insurance."

## Seat Motor Diagnosis

The front right seat motor was identified as non-functional:

- **Issue:** The seat motor does not operate — the seat height adjustment mechanism is not responding
- **Location:** Front right seat, specifically the motor controlling seat height
- **Status:** Needs replacement; the technician confirmed the motor itself needs to be swapped out
- **Warranty consideration:** Previously flagged at the service center for warranty evaluation (see [[note/Vehicle Service Visit Warranty Dispute and Repairs 2025-10-09]])

## Spoiler Non-Original Adhesive Discovery

Upon inspection, the body shop found that the spoiler had been reattached using non-original adhesive:

- **Finding:** When the spoiler was opened/inspected, it was clear the adhesive holding it was not the factory-original material
- **Implication:** The service center indicated they cannot accept the spoiler back for warranty work because of the non-original adhesive
- **Proposed solution:** A technician suggested Rami take the car, remove the spoiler himself, steam-clean all the non-original silicone and adhesive residue from both the spoiler and the car body, and then bring it back clean for proper reattachment
- **Method:** Steam cleaning to remove silicone, followed by fresh adhesive application on properly prepared surfaces

This relates to the suspected adhesive tampering discussed in [[note/Ambient Voice Memo Car PPF Discussion and Casual Fragments 2025-10-09]], where frustration with the PPF installer's work quality was already noted.

- **Scope:** Version update for the steering wheel control module
- **Timing:** Done at the end of the visit before releasing the vehicle

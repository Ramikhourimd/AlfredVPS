# Pre-Purchase Vehicle Inspection Findings 2025-11-03

## Context

Voice memo recorded during a pre-purchase vehicle inspection. An inspector (Speaker 1) examines a diesel 4x4 vehicle with 153,000 km on the odometer and presents findings to the buyer's representative (Speaker 2). A subsequent conversation with others at the dealership (including someone named Omri) discusses the findings and next steps.

## Vehicle Overview

- **Engine type:** Diesel
- **Drivetrain:** Permanent 4x4 (no warranty on 4x4 system)
- **Odometer:** 153,000 km

## Inspection Findings

### Engine (Major Concern)

- **Engine blowing (menape'ach):** When pulling the oil dipstick, excessive vapor/smoke comes out. While some blow-by is normal for diesel engines, the inspector notes the amount is excessive.
- **Engine number unreadable:** Could not identify the engine number due to heavy oil contamination covering the entire engine.
- **Oil everywhere:** Oil present not just around the dipstick area but across both sides of the engine and dripping down along the undercarriage. Needs to be cleaned and re-inspected.
- **Possible engine replacement:** Signs that the hood has been removed and reassembled. Hood removal can indicate prior engine extraction, though it could also be from front-end collision repair.
- **Recommendation:** Must be checked at a garage before purchase. If the garage certifies the engine as sound, they bear responsibility — not the inspector.

### Explanation from Seller's Side

- The vehicle handler (Speaker 2) claims the oil mess is from an improperly replaced dipstick cap. When the car was received, their worker checked the oil and left the air vent cap loose, causing oil to spray. He insists this is not an oil leak and that they inspected the vehicle thoroughly.

### Transmission

- **Status:** Normal — tested during a road drive, shifts on time, no faults detected.

### Brakes

- **Vibration during braking:** Steering wheel vibrates when braking. May need disc replacement or resurfacing.

### Body and Exterior

- **Front bumper:** Repaired and/or replaced.
- **Hood:** Signs of disassembly and reassembly. Multiple possible reasons: frontal collision repair, bumper replacement, or engine removal.
- **Windshield:** Small crack noted by inspector. The seller disputes this, claiming it is merely a scratch, not a crack. Inspector maintains a scratch can develop into a crack.

### Tires and Suspension

- **Rear tires:** Dated from 2021, some sidewall dryness. Can still be used for about another year, though replacement is recommended.
- **Rear alignment:** Good condition.
- **Shock absorbers:** All four checked and in good condition (two front, two rear).

### Other

- **Left side mirror:** Does not adjust vertically (only horizontal adjustment works).
- **Service history:** Inspector recommends checking when the last service was done and continuing on schedule.
- **Oil and coolant monitoring:** Recommended to check oil and water levels regularly between services given the mileage.

## Inspector's Overall Assessment

The inspection report lists findings as "low significance defects" (likuyim ba'alei mashma'ut namukha). The main open question is the engine condition — the inspector explicitly recommends a garage check before any purchase decision. If the garage confirms the engine is sound, then the vehicle is generally acceptable.

## Post-Inspection Discussion

After the formal inspection, the buyer's representative (Speaker 2, referred to as Omri by Speaker 4) discusses the findings privately:
- He believes the engine is fine and that the inspector is exaggerating the windshield issue (scratch vs. crack).
- He plans to go back to the sellers and negotiate/discuss the engine finding.
- He notes the inspection report classification as "low significance defects."
- He expresses confidence the engine concern can be resolved through a follow-up garage check.

## Action Items

1. Take the vehicle to a garage for a thorough engine check before purchase.
2. Wash/degrease the engine and re-inspect for actual oil leaks.
3. Check oil consumption between services.
4. Check when the last service was done.
5. Address braking vibration (disc replacement or resurfacing).
6. Consider replacing rear tires within the next year.
7. Get windshield crack/scratch evaluated.

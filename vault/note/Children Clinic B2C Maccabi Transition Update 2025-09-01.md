# Children Clinic B2C Maccabi Transition Update 2025-09-01

## Pipeline Status

- 37 children currently registered in the [[org/Maccabi Healthcare Services]] children pipeline
- 14 new children entered during August
- Many children were blocked from progressing due to the teacher questionnaire requirement

## Teacher Questionnaire Issue

The intake process required a mandatory teacher questionnaire. Out of approximately 30 children, 29 were stuck at this step because parents could not get the form filled by teachers (summer/school year transition). After consulting with Nurit (children's psychiatrist), the questionnaire was made optional. Anan was tasked with defining which specific cases (e.g., ADHD assessments) should still require it.

## B2C to Maccabi Transition

Children who were previously seen as free/B2C patients are being transitioned to [[org/Maccabi Healthcare Services]]. The process mirrors the adult patient pathway. [[person/Ori Shukron]] communicated to parents that they would receive two more sessions before the transition, receiving positive feedback from parents who appreciated the notice period.

## Questionnaire Gaps

The team identified a lack of standardized digital questionnaires (e.g., Conners, Vanderbilt) that psychiatrists could send directly to parents. Currently, psychiatrists resort to sending screenshots of health fund forms, which secretaries then email to parents manually. A more streamlined solution with in-app questionnaire delivery was requested.

## Project Management Challenges

- Eitan was piloting as project manager for the children's clinic project, following [[person/Nadav Blatt]]'s departure
- [[person/Ori Shukron]] and Shira both reported communication difficulties with Eitan (preference for WhatsApp over email/meetings, unclear requests, redirecting to Shachar)
- Rami explained that Eitan's pilot performance feedback would determine whether to expand his role
- The team planned to finalize the B2C closure implementation during the week, contingent on [[person/Shachar]]'s availability before his next vacation

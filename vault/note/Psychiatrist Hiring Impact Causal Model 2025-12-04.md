# Psychiatrist Hiring Impact Causal Model 2025-12-04

## Context

During the in-person protocol framework brainstorming session on 2025-12-04, Rami articulated a structured causal model showing how hiring experienced, board-certified psychiatrists creates a chain of effects that ultimately drives measurable KPI improvements. This model follows an Input → Mechanism → Intermediate Effect → KPI Impact framework.

Rami noted he was using his own hiring ("I happen to be the recruitment case") as the example to illustrate the model.

## The Causal Chain

### Input
- **Recruitment of an experienced, board-certified psychiatrist** (גיאוס פסיכיאטר מוסמך מנוסה)

### Mechanism (Direct Clinical Capabilities)
- Faster and more accurate diagnosis (אבחון מהיר ומדויק יותר)
- Fewer unnecessary medication changes (פחות שינוי תרופות מיותרים)
- Ability to handle complex cases (יכולת לטפל בתיקים מורכבים)
- Fewer patient transfers between clinicians (פחות העברות)
- Fewer documentation errors (פחות טעויות במסמכים)
- Fewer unnecessary follow-up sessions (פחות תניות חופשיות)

### Intermediate Effects (Observable Clinical Outcomes)
- Fewer urgent follow-ups required (פחות מעקבים דחופים)
- Fewer case manager requests for patient transfers (פחות פניות מנהלי תיק להעברות)
- Improvement in PHQ/GAD outcome scores (שיפור במדדי PHQ/GAD)
- Reduction in medication side effects (ירידה בתופעות לוואי)

### KPI Impact (Measurable Business Metrics)
- **Clinical KPIs:**
  - Diagnostic accuracy (דיוק אבחון)
  - Side effects impact on treatment success (תופעות לוואי על הצלחת טיפול)
  - Regression rates (רגרסיות)
- **Operational KPIs:**
  - Examination/assessment time (זמן בדיקה)
  - Clinical engagement time (זמן התעסקות קליני)
- **Financial KPIs:**
  - Treatment cost reduction (עלות טיפול) — through the full patient journey

Rami noted that treatment cost is important but acknowledged it may just be a downstream output of the other metrics rather than a separately measured KPI.

## Significance

This model provides the granular justification for why psychiatrist quality (not just quantity) is a scaling lever. It directly supports the broader "clinical excellence → revenue" causal chain by showing exactly how a single high-quality hire generates measurable value at each level of the funnel.

The model also implicitly argues against a pure horizontal scaling approach (just hiring more psychiatrists regardless of quality) by showing that the mechanisms and intermediate effects only activate when the hired psychiatrist has sufficient experience and certification.

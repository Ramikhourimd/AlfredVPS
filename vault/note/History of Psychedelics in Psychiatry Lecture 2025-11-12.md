# History of Psychedelics in Psychiatry Lecture 2025-11-12

## Context

Voice memo recording from 2025-11-12 capturing a Hebrew-language lecture on the history of psychedelic substances in psychiatry. The presentation is delivered by a physician (likely at a clinical training session or seminar) and covers the fascinating pendulum between law, medicine, and societal acceptance of psychedelics over the past century. The speaker uses slides and follows a chronological narrative structure.

## Key Historical Timeline

### Pre-1900s: Altered States Awareness
- By the 1890s, researchers (primarily German) began studying peyote as a source of mescaline
- The experiences were labeled "the new artificial paradise"

### 1900: Mescaline Isolation
- Arthur Heffter (referred to as "Otto Karl" in the lecture) — one of the founders of modern psychopharmacology — first isolated mescaline from peyote cactus

### 1912: MDMA Synthesis
- Merck pharmaceutical company first synthesized MDMA
- The compound would not see widespread use for another 50-60 years

### 1930s: Early Clinical Use
- Around 1930, Walter Stoll began treating patients with mescaline
- Combined with psychotherapy; early clinical results documented

### 1938: LSD Discovery
- November 1938: Albert Hofmann, a chemist at Sandoz Pharmaceuticals, was researching vasoconstrictive properties of compounds for the respiratory and cardiovascular systems
- He accidentally stumbled upon LSD-25

### 1943: Hofmann's Bicycle Day
- April 19, 1943: After a 5-year gap, Hofmann returned to investigate LSD-25
- He accidentally absorbed a small amount through his fingertip
- Three days later, he deliberately took 250 micrograms of LSD — the first intentional LSD trip
- He rode his bicycle home, profoundly affected by the intense, introspective experience
- Hofmann was optimistic, believing the intense introspective quality would have therapeutic value
- This event (April 19) is celebrated as "Bicycle Day" in psychedelic history

### Late 1940s-1950s: Clinical Exploration Begins
- Sandoz began marketing LSD commercially
- Initial use spanned both psychotherapy and schizophrenia research (exploring psychotomimetic properties)
- Max Rinkel took LSD, studied 100 subjects (patients, therapists, and students), found interesting results
- Rinkel identified connections to adrenaline and noradrenaline mechanisms
- He classified LSD as psychotomimetic (mimicking psychosis), not psychotherapeutic
- The CIA took interest, running Project MK-Ultra with experiments that likely lacked informed consent, before eventually abandoning the work

### 1950s: Serotonin Hypothesis Emerges
- Daniel Freedman (referred to as "Sweetman") proposed the first serotonin mechanism for LSD action
- The link between LSD and serotonin became increasingly clear

### 1954-1958: The Overenthusiasm Phase
- By 1954, LSD was being prescribed for everything: depression, social anxiety, sexual dysfunction, insomnia, writer's block, frigidity, and even autism
- Ronald Sandison introduced the "psycholytic model" — lower doses combined with psychotherapy
- His seminal work with 5 patients was presented at the APA in 1955

### 1958: Peak of Early Research
- The ADCO study: The largest study of the era with 700+ patients, more than 6,000 sessions, reporting very good outcomes
- Claviceps-based LSD research achieved 90%+ success rates that held up to one year — a record that remains unbroken
- Hofmann isolated psilocybin for the first time

### 1960s: The Flowering
- Psychedelic psychiatry flourished internationally
- Israeli psychiatrists conducted experiments combining psilocybin and LSD with psychoanalysis and psychodrama
- This period represents the peak of the first wave before the regulatory backlash

## Key Themes

### The Pendulum
The lecturer emphasizes the recurring cycle: psychedelics rise in medical acceptance, then fall due to regulatory or societal backlash, then rise again. The current era (post-2021) represents yet another upswing.

### Law vs. Medicine
The "fascinating pendulum between law, justice, and medicine" is presented as the central narrative — how substances with demonstrated therapeutic potential are repeatedly caught between medical promise and legal prohibition.

### Neurophysiology
The lecture touches on the serotonin mechanism as the key neurochemical pathway, first proposed in the 1950s and remaining central to psychedelic research today.

### Psychotomimetic vs. Psychotherapeutic
A key tension in the field: whether psychedelics mimic psychosis (psychotomimetic model, favored by early researchers like Rinkel) or facilitate therapy (psycholytic/psychedelic model, advanced by Sandison and others).

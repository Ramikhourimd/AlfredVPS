# New Employee Onboarding Framework and Technology Requirements 2026-02-12

## Context

Meeting between Rami Khouri and Elias Jahjah on 2026-02-12, part of the HM-RAMI-ELIAS 1:1 clinical management series (now discontinued). The discussion focused on how to structure and schedule onboarding for new employees joining Clinic Israel.

## Key Discussion Points

### Onboarding Cadence

Rami proposed establishing a regular onboarding cadence — either monthly or bi-monthly (every two weeks). Some months may require faster turnaround depending on hiring volume. Elias confirmed he could support once or twice per month from his side.

### Three-Track Onboarding Framework

Rami proposed separating onboarding into three distinct tracks that can run independently:

1. **Technology Access (Mandatory prerequisite)** — New employees must have access to Microsoft Teams and the HoViD platform before they can function. Without technology access, a new hire arrives and immediately encounters blockers ("I don't know how to get into Teams"). This track is the only hard prerequisite.

2. **Operational-Clinical** — Covers clinical workflows, patient handling, and system usage. Rami noted this can be combined with the technology track and delivered together by Stav Zamir or Shira. The goal is for new hires to understand the basic operational and clinical expectations.

3. **Administrative** — Covers HR paperwork, agreements, and compliance. This can start independently and doesn't block clinical work.

### Delivery Responsibility

Rami suggested Stav Zamir or Shira as the primary onboarding deliverers for the combined technology + operational track. They would walk new employees through HoViD access (which is the "must") and basic operational-clinical orientation in a single session.

### Flexibility

The approach acknowledges variable hiring volume — some months may bring multiple new hires requiring rapid orientation, while other months may be quieter. The bi-weekly cadence provides enough flexibility to catch new hires without excessive overhead.

## Outcome

Agreement on the three-track framework with technology as mandatory prerequisite. Implementation details (scheduling, materials, tracking) to be formalized.

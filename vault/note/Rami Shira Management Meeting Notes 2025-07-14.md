# Rami Shira Management Meeting Notes 2025-07-14

## Context

1:1 management meeting between Rami Khouri and Shira Lachmann (Clinical Director) at Telia'z. Meeting classified as HM-SHIRA-MGMT (HealthyMind Clinical Operations). This was a substantive operational planning session covering compensation, recruitment strategy, office restructuring, and staffing changes.

## 1. Shira's Bonus Structure

Rami discussed the bonus framework with Shira following a conversation with Alex Taliaz:

**Revenue Component (50%):**
- Shira to receive a percentage of overall clinic revenue
- At 1M NIS monthly revenue (break-even target), this should yield approximately 750 NIS/month
- Not capped — if revenue reaches 2M NIS, bonus doubles to 1,500 NIS
- Alex to provide exact percentage calculation
- Rami personally preferred the bonus not be tied to sales/revenue, but deferred to Alex and Shira's preference

**Operational KPI Component (50%):**
- **Wait time management** — keeping patient wait times within defined targets
- **Patient satisfaction** — target 90% satisfaction score
- **Staff satisfaction** — measured regularly, with structured surveys
- Bonus cadence: quarterly (not monthly), which is industry standard
- Total bonus framework: up to 1,500 NIS/month (paid quarterly)

**Shira's concerns:** She expressed confusion about the bonus model changing across multiple discussions, and concern that revenue targets (1M NIS) are not achievable in the near term. Rami acknowledged complexity but emphasized fairness and alignment.

## 2. Clinic Revenue Status

- July 2025 actual revenue: ~335K NIS
- Mid-month created (pipeline): 198K NIS additional
- Target: reach 1M NIS monthly revenue within 3 months
- 1M NIS = break-even point covering all company costs, not just clinic
- Unrealized patient appointments roll forward to next month (revenue potential accumulates)

## 3. Psychiatrist Recruitment — Residency Pipeline Model

Rami presented a major new recruitment strategy: hiring psychiatrists from the beginning of their residency and retaining them through certification.

**Three seniority tiers with pay progression:**

| Stage | Role | Rate |
|-------|------|------|
| Year 1 (pre-hospital clearance) | Case Manager | 120 NIS/hour |
| Year 2+ (post-hospital clearance, pre-Stage A) | Psychiatrist | 300 NIS/hour |
| After Stage A exam | Psychiatrist | 350 NIS/hour |
| After Stage B exam (specialist) | Psychiatrist | 450 NIS/hour |

**Target composition:** Maximum 20% specialists (post-Stage B) on staff to balance quality with cost efficiency. Average hourly cost should converge to ~340 NIS/hour.

**Retention strategy:** Young residents cost less but need more support. Specialists cost more but bring credibility for service tenders. The mix is designed to keep the average cost sustainable.

**Past experience:** Previous experiment with residents Sofian and Hala was not successful — attributed to insufficient support during the demanding first year of residency.

## 4. Psychiatry Academy — Three Training Tracks

Starting September 2025, Rami plans to launch a structured training program:

**Track 1: First-Year Orientation**
- Weekly group sessions for new residents
- Focus: integrating into residency, psychiatry fundamentals, ER rotations, on-call duties
- Goal: reduce anxiety and provide emotional support during the overwhelming first year

**Track 2: Stage A Exam Preparation**
- For residents approaching the Stage A board exam (typically June)
- Requires digital platform with lectures, practice questions, detailed explanations, and mock exams
- Replaces expensive external preparation courses

**Track 3: Stage B Exam Preparation**
- More complex, possibly requiring in-person or patient-based components
- May start later than Tracks 1 and 2
- Rami considering Zoom-based delivery as a starting point

**Infrastructure needed by September:** Instructors, syllabus, digital learning platform with video lectures and exam simulations.

**Recruitment messaging:** Shira can immediately mention these training offerings to prospective psychiatrist candidates as a differentiator.

## 5. Secretary Hiring and Rana's Role Transition

**Current problem:** Rana (chief secretary) has frequent last-minute absences, leaving the office understaffed. Avital alone cannot cover all responsibilities.

**Rami's proposal:**
- Hire one full-time secretary position (starting next month's budget)
- Transition Rana from daily secretarial tasks to HR/office management role
- Rana to handle: recruitment coordination, onboarding, staff lifecycle management, task delegation within office team
- Shira to oversee at a higher level (updates and results) rather than daily operations

**Shira's counter-proposal:**
- Agrees additional staff is needed but advocates for technology investments first:
  - **Automated reminder calls** — currently secretaries spend ~50% of time calling patients for appointment reminders; an automated system (like HMO call systems) would eliminate this
  - **WhatsApp customer service bot** — triage inbound patient messages, route to questionnaires/scheduling without human intervention
  - **Centralized phone system** — currently each secretary has their own phone; if one is absent, calls/messages are missed. A single number with shared access would solve this
- Shira discussed these ideas with Shachar but he was on vacation
- Recommends starting with one half-time secretary who could scale to full-time, combined with technology investment

**Rami's position:** Budget one full position immediately; growth will be faster than expected. Secretary workload depends on registered patients (not just active clinical volume), so the need is already apparent.

## Key Themes

1. **Revenue pressure:** The 1M NIS monthly target frames all decisions — bonus structure, recruitment pace, cost management
2. **Pipeline thinking:** The residency pipeline model is a long-term bet on growing talent internally rather than competing on salary for experienced psychiatrists
3. **Operational independence:** Rami is systematically delegating operational control to Shira and Rana, moving himself toward strategic oversight

# Psychiatric Follow-Up: Clonex Management and Work as Therapy 2025-12-02

## Context

Follow-up psychiatric session conducted by Dr. Rami Khouri with a female outpatient. The session was conducted in Arabic (Palestinian Levantine dialect). A family member was also present during the session. The patient has a long-standing relationship with Dr. Khouri.

## Clinical Assessment

### Patient Status
The patient reports feeling **significantly better** than before. Both the patient and the doctor acknowledge noticeable improvement. The patient's current well-being is attributed to consistent medication compliance — the family member present reinforced this point, noting the patient would not feel as well without taking medication on time.

### Current Medication — Clonex (Clonazepam)
- **Patient-reported regimen**: 1 pill in the morning, 1 pill at night
- **Doctor's note**: A midday dose is also prescribed but the patient denies taking it
- **Dosage discrepancy**: The patient disputes the midday dose; the doctor references it as part of the prescribed regimen
- **Plan**: No medication changes this visit. The doctor explicitly stated the current medications will remain unchanged to preserve the patient's stability. Even small changes could risk regression.

### Recent Incident
The patient described a recent incident while making bread — hot oil or fire fell on her. She reported burns and associated distress. The doctor acknowledged this but redirected focus to the clinical discussion.

### Symptoms and Side Effects
The patient reports pain and itching which she attributes to the medication. She scratches due to discomfort. The doctor does not consider this a significant clinical concern at this time.

## Work Attendance — Therapeutic Mandate

### The Problem
The patient has been absent from work for **two months**. Her workplace manager contacted the clinic noting:
- The patient came to work only once in two months
- There are people waiting for the position
- The **Ministry of Health will not accept** continued absence

### Doctor's Directive
The patient must return to work on **Mondays and Wednesdays** during her available time. The doctor framed this not as an employment issue but as a **clinical intervention**:

> "We want you to work not for money but for your brain/mind."

The doctor emphasized that if the patient does not return to work, the progress made through medication reduction could be lost. Work provides cognitive engagement that is essential to mental health recovery.

### Patient's Response
The patient expressed openness to work but mentioned interest in **secretarial work** and **computer training** as alternatives. The doctor noted that what matters is mental engagement, not the specific type of work. The patient also acknowledged enjoying work in general, but has reservations about returning.

## Family Dynamics and Religious Context

The family member (Speaker [2]) present during the session:
- Reinforced the importance of medication compliance
- Mentioned siblings and family relationships
- Brought up a religious perspective on medication (as Christians, referencing Jesus — affirming that medicine is compatible with faith)
- Discussed meal plans and a restaurant in Tamra
- Praised the patient's father

The religious discussion around medication appears to address a potential concern about whether taking psychiatric medication conflicts with religious beliefs. The doctor and family member both affirmed that medication is appropriate and necessary.

## Clinical Decisions

1. **No medication changes** — Current Clonex regimen maintained to preserve stability
2. **Work attendance required** — Patient must resume Monday and Wednesday attendance, framed as therapeutic intervention
3. **Future medication reduction** — Doctor indicated that with continued improvement and work compliance, gradual medication reduction may be possible over time

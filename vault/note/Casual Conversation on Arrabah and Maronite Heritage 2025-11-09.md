# Casual Conversation on Arrabah and Maronite Heritage 2025-11-09

## Context

Voice memo recording of a casual conversation between Rami (speaker 1) and an unnamed acquaintance (speaker 2) before a meeting on 2025-11-09. Speaker 2 is originally from Arrabah but has lived in Hanifah for eight years. The conversation covered two main topics: Arrabah's reputation and Rami's Maronite heritage.

## Key Points

### Arrabah — Town of Doctors

- Speaker 2 noted that Arrabah was currently in the news (November 2025), related to the town's mayor
- Arrabah is reputed to be the settlement that produces the most doctors relative to its population size — approximately one doctor per 1,000 residents
- Rami initially assumed the demographic was Druze, but speaker 2 clarified that Arrabah has both Muslim and Christian residents (not Druze)
- Rami mentioned he also associates Arrabah with a song ("shir") from the general repertoire

### Rami's Maronite Heritage

- Rami disclosed that he is "quarter Maronite" (reva Maroni)
- His grandmother was from "Drim" — likely Jish (Gush Halav) or a nearby village in the far north of the Galilee, described as "the most north there is"
- Speaker 2 immediately connected Maronites to Lebanon, and Rami confirmed the link
- Rami expressed that he is actively learning about Maronite history: "I'm learning here, bro"
- Speaker 2 also expressed interest in the topic

### Genealogical Significance

This is a new data point for the Khouri Family Genealogy project. Previously documented research focused on the Shaddad family's migration from Hauran and the Khouri priestly origins in Rameh. The Maronite connection through Rami's grandmother adds a third lineage branch — a northern Galilee Maronite line connected to Lebanon. The village "Drim" (possibly Jish or another far-north village) should be investigated further.

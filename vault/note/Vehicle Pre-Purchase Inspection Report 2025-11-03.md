# Vehicle Pre-Purchase Inspection Report 2025-11-03

## Context

Voice memo recorded during a professional pre-purchase vehicle inspection on 2025-11-03. The inspector examined a diesel 4x4 vehicle with 153,000 km on the odometer. The recording captures the inspector's verbal report to the vehicle's handler, followed by a side discussion between the buyer's party about the findings.

## Vehicle Overview

- **Type:** Diesel 4x4 (permanent all-wheel drive)
- **Mileage:** 153,000 km
- **Inspection type:** Pre-purchase general inspection

## Inspection Findings

### Critical Issues (Require Garage Verification)

1. **Engine blowby / excessive crankcase pressure** — When the oil dipstick is removed, an abnormally large amount of fumes escapes. While some blowby is normal for diesel engines, the inspector flagged the quantity as excessive. This requires professional garage diagnosis before purchase.
2. **Engine covered in oil** — Significant oil presence across the entire engine and undercarriage. The vehicle handler attributed this to a previous oil dipstick cap being left improperly seated (the dipstick has a non-standard, non-round opening). The inspector noted oil is present not just near the dipstick area but also on the opposite side and along the entire underside of the vehicle.
3. **Engine number unreadable** — Could not be identified due to oil contamination covering the engine.
4. **Signs of hood removal and front bumper repair/replacement** — Evidence of prior disassembly and reassembly of the hood, along with front bumper repair or replacement. The inspector noted multiple possible explanations: (a) the hood was removed to extract/replace the engine, (b) there was a front-end collision with bumper and hood replacement. The inspector could not determine which scenario occurred.

### Moderate Issues

5. **Steering wheel vibration during braking** — Small vibration felt through the steering wheel when braking. May require brake disc replacement or resurfacing.
6. **Front windshield damage** — Inspector identified a small crack in the windshield. The vehicle handler disputed this, calling it merely a scratch, not a crack. The inspector maintained that even a scratch can propagate into a crack.
7. **Left side mirror malfunction** — Does not adjust vertically (up/down), only adjusts horizontally (left/right).

### Acceptable / Good Condition

8. **Transmission** — Tested during drive, shifts on time, no faults detected.
9. **4x4 system** — Permanent all-wheel drive, functioning (no warranty coverage for 4x4 system).
10. **Rear tires** — Dated 2021, some dryness on sidewalls. Inspector says they are safe for approximately one more year but recommends eventual replacement.
11. **Shock absorbers** — All four checked and in good condition (two front, two rear).
12. **Last wheel alignment** — Within specification.

## Inspector Recommendations

- **Must do before purchase:** Have the engine inspected at a professional garage. If the garage confirms the engine is healthy, the inspector's responsibility ends — the garage takes accountability.
- **Regular maintenance:** Check oil and water levels between service intervals due to high mileage.
- **Engine cleaning:** Wash the engine and reinspect for oil leaks after cleaning, to isolate the true source of oil contamination.
- **Service history:** Verify when the last service was performed and continue with scheduled maintenance.
- **Brake discs:** Have braking vibration diagnosed and repaired as needed.

## Post-Inspection Discussion

After the formal inspection, the buyer's party (Speakers 4 and 5, including someone addressed as "Omri") discussed the findings privately. Key points from this side conversation:
- Frustration with the inspector's findings, particularly the windshield characterization (scratch vs. crack) and the engine blowby concern.
- The inspection report listed findings as "low significance defects" (likunim ba'alei mashma'ut nemucha), which the buyer's party felt was reasonable.
- The group planned to go back to the seller/garage to discuss the engine findings and find a way to verify the engine's condition.
- Speaker 5 (likely the car's handler) emphasized that the engine is not smoking during operation and that some blowby is normal for all diesel engines — expressing confidence the engine is fine.

# Mortgage Refinancing Consultation Summary 2026-01-26

## Context

Rami Khouri attended a mortgage advisory consultation through the RiseUp (רייזאפ) platform on 2026-01-26. The consultation was triggered by RiseUp's algorithm recommending a mortgage refinance review. Rami took his mortgage from Bank Hapoalim in May 2020, with an original amount of approximately 790,000 NIS. The current balance stands at approximately 705,000 NIS.

Rami is both salaried and self-employed (owns a Ltd. company), with part of the mortgage payment coming from the company account.

## Current Mortgage Structure

The mortgage comprises four tracks:
1. **Prime-linked track** — variable rate, likely the primary driver of the payment increase
2. **Fixed non-indexed track** — stable payments
3. **Variable CPI-indexed track** — principal has grown from 260,000 to 267,000 NIS despite 5.5 years of payments (negative amortization due to CPI indexation)
4. **Short-term track** — 14-year duration

Monthly payments have risen from 3,400 NIS to approximately 4,600 NIS (a 1,200 NIS increase), likely driven by Prime rate increases.

## Pain Points

1. **Feeling of lost opportunity** — Rami's primary concern is that he's not optimizing his mortgage ("being a sucker"). The fact that principal is growing on one track despite years of payments reinforces this feeling.
2. **Elevated monthly payments** — While Rami and his wife can manage the 4,600 NIS monthly payment, the 35% increase from the original 3,400 NIS feels excessive.

## Refinancing Options Presented

### Option A: Total Cost Savings (Rami's preference)
- **Total savings:** ~200,000 NIS over the life of the loan
- **Monthly payment:** Stays roughly the same as current (~4,600 NIS)
- **Trade-off:** No immediate cash flow relief, but significant long-term savings

### Option B: Monthly Payment Reduction
- **Monthly savings:** ~800-850 NIS reduction per month
- **Total cost impact:** Increases total cost by ~100,000 NIS
- **Trade-off:** Immediate cash flow relief but more expensive overall

### Option C: Aggressive Savings (mentioned but not pursued)
- **Total savings:** ~300,000 NIS
- **Monthly payment:** Increases by ~1,000 NIS
- **Rami's response:** Not interested in raising the monthly payment

## Key Considerations

- **Property sale planned:** Rami and his wife plan to sell the property in the near future, so the new mortgage mix must minimize early repayment penalties
- **Dual income structure:** The bank will need to recognize Rami's self-employment income during the refinancing process
- **Service tier decision:** RiseUp offers two service levels:
  - **Partial service (2,400 NIS):** Rami negotiates with banks himself using RiseUp's analysis
  - **Full service (6,000 NIS):** RiseUp handles all bank negotiations and presents Rami only the final signing documents
  - Rami leans toward the full service option
- **Money-back guarantee:** RiseUp commits to a refund if they don't meet the defined savings target

## AI Advisor Suggestions (from RiseUp)

1. Present visual comparison (graphs) of current vs. post-refinance mortgage trajectory at next meeting
2. Build simulation showing savings at multiple exit points (3, 5, 7 years) including early repayment penalties
3. Share ongoing bank negotiation updates with Rami for transparency
4. Send educational content about mortgage refinancing in variable-rate environments

# MDMA and Psilocybin Therapeutic Mechanisms Lecture 2025-11-12

## Context

Voice memo recording from 2025-11-12 capturing the second part of a Hebrew-language lecture on psychedelics in psychiatry. The first part (see [[note/History of Psychedelics in Psychiatry Lecture 2025-11-12]]) covered the historical timeline from the 1890s through the 1960s. This segment shifts to the modern therapeutic applications, covering MDMA for PTSD and psilocybin for depression and addiction. The lecture was delivered as part of ongoing clinical education, likely associated with the HealthyMind Academy at Telia'z.

## MDMA Therapeutic Mechanism for PTSD

### Fear Memory Reconsolidation
- Current SSRI-based treatment is described as "orthogonal" — it prevents de facto healing while also enabling consolidation of fear memories
- Serotonin plays a critical role in the creation, consolidation, and retrieval of fear-related memories
- MDMA facilitates therapeutic processing by reducing amygdala reactivity, enabling the patient to revisit traumatic memories with less emotional flooding, less distress, and less dissociation
- Patients gain the ability to access difficult, painful memories with greater self-compassion and self-forgiveness, reducing the self-hatred characteristic of PTSD

### Oxytocin and Critical Windows
- MDMA triggers re-opening of oxytocin-mediated critical periods (similar to those active during the perinatal window)
- This creates a state of increased tolerance, allowing patients to process traumatic material without overwhelming emotional response

### Prosocial and Interpersonal Effects
- MDMA's serotonergic effects enhance prosocial traits and interpersonal qualities
- These qualities improve the therapeutic relationship, creating a powerful synergy between psychopharmacological and psychotherapeutic interventions

### Receptor Pharmacology
- MDMA acts on 5-HT1 receptors — reduces anxiety
- Also acts on 5-HT2A receptors — enables perspective-shifting, seeing things from a different angle

## Psilocybin Pharmacology and Clinical Evidence

### Mechanism of Action
- Psilocybin primarily acts on 5-HT2A receptors
- The lecturer uses the metaphor: "The gardener went to sleep" — referring to the default mode network (DMN)
- 5-HT2A activation leads to increased entropy in brain activity and decreased connectivity of the DMN
- This results in increased novel connectivity patterns, dissolving of self-boundaries, and altered sense of time and space
- Creates opportunities for new neural pathways, new meanings, and creative reframing of life experiences
- The phenomenon of synesthesia under LSD is cited as evidence of this novel inter-neuronal connectivity

### Safety Comparison
- Psilocybin is described as safer than LSD
- Addiction potential characterized as "0.0%" — very low risk
- FDA designated breakthrough therapy status for both MDMA and psilocybin in 2017-2018

### Psilocybin vs. Escitalopram (2021 UK Study)
- Study by Dr. Robin Carhart-Harris (referred to as "Dr. Rozus" in the transcript) in England
- Compared psilocybin to escitalopram (Cipralex) for depression treatment
- Described as a "two-arrow" (short duration) study
- Primary outcomes: Psilocybin showed non-inferiority compared to escitalopram
- Secondary outcomes: Psilocybin showed slight superiority
- The lecturer notes discussing this study directly with the lead researcher

### Psilocybin vs. SSRIs for Emotional Processing
- SSRIs reduce emotional reactivity in depressed patients
- Psilocybin, in contrast, enables patients to be in contact with their emotions rather than dampening them
- This distinction is highlighted as a significant therapeutic difference

### Psilocybin for Addiction
- Evidence cited for cannabis use disorder (2014 studies)
- Evidence for alcohol use disorder
- Evidence for tobacco/cigarette cessation
- The mystical experience was found to be the strongest predictor of therapeutic benefit

## Mystical Experience Measurement

- Validated questionnaires exist for measuring mystical experiences
- Measurement is based on three domains:
  1. **Introversion** — internal experience quality
  2. **Extroversion** — external experience quality
  3. **Interpretation** — meaning-making of internal and external experiences
- The intensity of the mystical experience correlates with therapeutic outcomes across addictions

## Historical Context

- The lecturer notes that psychedelic research has been on an upward trend for over 100 years (with regulatory interruptions)
- Since 2005, the research trajectory has been consistently ascending
- This is attributed to accumulating evidence of efficacy
- For "pure psychiatry" applications (as opposed to addiction), psilocybin's addictive potential is described as extremely low and transient

## Key Researcher Mentioned

- **Lior Roseman** — Referenced as a notable figure in the field, described as working productively despite institutional affiliations
- **Dr. Robin Carhart-Harris** (as "Dr. Rozus") — Led the 2021 psilocybin vs. escitalopram study; the lecturer reports personal contact with him

This lecture directly informs clinical practice at Telia'z/HealthyMind by keeping clinicians updated on emerging psychopharmacological paradigms. Understanding MDMA and psilocybin mechanisms is relevant for:
- Contextualizing PTSD treatment approaches used at the clinic
- Understanding the neurobiological basis of therapeutic interventions
- Evaluating potential future treatment modalities

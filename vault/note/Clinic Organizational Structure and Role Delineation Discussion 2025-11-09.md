# Clinic Organizational Structure and Role Delineation Discussion 2025-11-09

## Context

Voice memo from 2025-11-09 where Rami and Dekkel discuss the organizational structure of the Telia'z Clinic Israel. This conversation took place on the same day as the Basel Kanaaneh hiring interview, and covers role delineation for existing staff ahead of the new general manager joining.

## Role Division: Renee, Li Yamin, and Shira

### Renee (Rana Khouri) — Clinical Staff Management
- Started formal handover process with Li Yamin and Shira
- Responsible for all clinical staff within the clinic: psychiatrists, case managers, secretaries
- Manages recruitment, onboarding, and day-to-day personnel for clinical team
- When a new employee needs a contract, Renee defines the role and Li Yamin routes it to the lawyer
- For non-clinic roles (e.g., general manager level), Rami coordinates directly with Li Yamin, not through Renee

### Li Yamin — HR and Payroll Intermediary
- Sits between "employee has an agreement" and "employee gets paid"
- Handles employment contracts, bank details, payroll coordination
- Manages non-clinical staff (company-level employees, not clinic-direct)
- Must maintain awareness of all active employees even when not directly managing them
- Routes contracts through lawyers when new agreement types are needed

### Shira — Clinical Operations (Maccabi Adults)
- Manages all day-to-day clinical operations for adult patients under Maccabi
- Handles: psychiatrist scheduling, availability, staff hours, complaint management
- Direct communication channel with clinical staff
- Current scope limited to Maccabi volume — cannot scale to handle additional HMOs
- Until the new general manager arrives, Renee reports temporarily to Rami

## New Clinical Manager Candidate

- Leading candidate is currently employed at Clalit
- Needs approximately one month notice period before starting
- **Non-compete risk identified:** Need to check employment agreements with Clalit and Maccabi for non-solicitation clauses that could restrict hiring from these HMOs
- Planned onboarding approach: gradual entry over first month
  - First month: meet all staff, understand the organization, map out who does what
  - Become the go-to address for clinic-related questions
  - Build relationships before taking operational authority

## Ori Shukron — Expanded Role Definition

Ori's responsibilities are broader than initially apparent from the "field visits" task:
- **Protocols:** Responsible for clinical protocols and documentation
- **Medical Education:** Coordinates clinician training sessions
- **Psychiatry Academy:** Building the academy program together with psychiatrists
- **Children's Operations:** Manages the operations side of children's services
- **Field visits/outreach:** Only a small supplementary part of her role, not the core function

## Clinic Scaling: Department/Unit Structure

Discussion about future organizational scaling as the clinic grows beyond Maccabi:
- Current structure where Shira manages all operations cannot expand with additional HMOs (Clalit, etc.)
- Two structural options discussed:
  1. **Team lead model:** Promote experienced case managers to partial management roles alongside Shira
  2. **Department/unit model:** Divide the clinical organization into formal departments or units
- Hospital analogy used: departments are larger (up to 30 beds), units can be single-provider specialized functions
- Consensus: the new general manager should design this structure upon arrival
- Clinicians would serve across HMOs (not separated by payer), but operations management would be split

## Key Takeaway

The conversation reveals a deliberate strategy of defining clear role boundaries for existing staff (Renee, Li, Shira, Ori) before the new general manager arrives. This prevents role confusion during transition and gives the incoming manager a clean organizational foundation to build upon.

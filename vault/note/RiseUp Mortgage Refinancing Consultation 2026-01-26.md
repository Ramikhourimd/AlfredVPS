# RiseUp Mortgage Refinancing Consultation 2026-01-26

## Context

On 2026-01-26 Rami Khouri had an advisory session with a RiseUp (רייזאפ) consultant to explore refinancing his Bank Hapoalim mortgage, originally taken in May 2020. Rami and his wife feel the monthly repayment has risen significantly — from 3,400 NIS to approximately 4,600 NIS — and want to evaluate options to improve the mortgage terms. Rami is both salaried and self-employed (owns a Ltd. company), with part of the mortgage payment coming from the company account.

## Mortgage Overview

- **Original amount:** ~790,000 NIS (May 2020)
- **Current balance for early repayment:** ~705,000 NIS
- **Monthly payment:** ~4,600 NIS (up from 3,400 NIS)
- **Tracks:** Four tracks — Prime-linked, fixed non-indexed, variable CPI-indexed, and a shorter 14-year track
- **Key concern:** On the CPI-indexed track, the principal grew from 260,000 to 267,000 NIS despite 5.5 years of payments

## Pain Points

### 1. Fear of Being a "Sucker" (Freier)
Rami's primary emotional driver is the feeling he's not maximizing savings. He knows refinancing is an option but keeps postponing. The fact that principal is growing on one track despite regular payments reinforces this anxiety.

### 2. Elevated Monthly Payment
The 1,200 NIS increase (3,400 → 4,600) creates financial pressure, though the family can still manage the payment. The increase is likely driven by Prime rate rises affecting the variable-rate track.

## Two Options Presented

### Option A: Reduce Total Cost (~200,000 NIS savings)
- Keep monthly payment roughly the same as current (~4,600 NIS)
- Restructure tracks to eliminate inefficient CPI-indexed exposure
- **Rami's preferred option**

### Option B: Reduce Monthly Payment (down ~800-850 NIS)
- Would increase total cost by ~100,000 NIS over the life of the loan
- Provides immediate monthly cash flow relief
- Rami is not interested in this option — they can handle the current payment

### Third Possibility Mentioned
- Increase monthly payment by ~1,000 NIS to save closer to 300,000 NIS total
- Rami was not enthusiastic about this option either

## Service Tier Decision

RiseUp offers two service levels:
1. **Partial service (2,400 NIS):** Client handles bank negotiations themselves with RiseUp guidance
2. **Full service (6,000 NIS):** RiseUp handles all bank communications, obtains quotes, and presents ready-to-sign deals

Rami is leaning toward the **full-service option (6,000 NIS)** for professional handling of the entire process.

**Money-back guarantee:** RiseUp commits to a refund if they fail to meet the savings target defined at process start.

## Additional Considerations

- **Property sale planned:** Rami and his wife intend to sell the apartment in the not-too-distant future, so the new mortgage mix must minimize early repayment penalties
- **Company income recognition:** The bank needs to recognize Rami's self-employment income (Ltd. company) during the refinancing process
- **Part of payment from company account:** This arrangement needs to carry over or be addressed in the new structure

## AI Advisor Suggestions (from RiseUp platform)

1. Present visual comparison (graphs) of current vs post-refinancing projections, showing how refinancing stops principal growth
2. Build simulation showing savings at various exit points (3, 5, 7 years) including early repayment penalty impact
3. Share ongoing bank negotiation updates for transparency and confidence
4. Send educational content about why periodic mortgage refinancing is wise in a variable-rate environment

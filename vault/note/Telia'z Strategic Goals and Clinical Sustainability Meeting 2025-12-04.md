# Telia'z Strategic Goals and Clinical Sustainability Meeting 2025-12-04

## Context

A pivotal leadership meeting held via Zoom on 2025-12-04, where Rami Khouri confronted the rest of the Telia'z leadership about a fundamental strategic misalignment. The week prior, Alex (a leadership member) had presented 2026 goals and financial targets that Rami felt were purely financially-driven and ignored the clinical sustainability needed for a healthcare operation. Ziv was absent from this meeting. Basel Kanaaneh had recently joined and was helping Rami build the operational plan.

## Key Points Raised by Rami

### 1. Turning Point After Previous Week's Presentation
Rami described the previous week's board presentation as a "crossroads" (צומת) that changed his entire outlook for 2026. The financial targets presented lacked clinical sustainability consideration. Under "clinical excellence," profitability below 40% was listed — which Rami interpreted as evidence that clinical goals were being subordinated to financial ones.

### 2. Data-Driven Patient Projections
Rami presented an analysis based on Maccabi data from the clinic database:
- **Current active patients:** ~1,000
- **Projected year-end active patients:** ~7,786
- **Required FTE positions:** ~44 (24 psychiatrists + 21 case managers)
- **At quarter-positions:** Requires ~4x = ~176 individual hires
- **Monthly patient churn rate:** ~15% (range 11.6% to 18.9% across months May-December)

### 3. Session-to-Patient Ratio Anomaly
A data discrepancy was identified in the session-to-patient ratios:
- Case managers: 1.38 sessions per patient per month
- Psychiatrists: >1.0 sessions per patient per month
- The psychiatrist ratio above 1.0 was flagged as anomalous — most patients should not see a psychiatrist more than once per month
- Likely cause: active patient denominator may not include new intakes in the same month
- Team agreed to investigate the calculation methodology separately

### 4. The Core Argument: Clinical Sustainability Over Financial Targets
Rami's central thesis: **A clinical operation cannot be governed purely by financial metrics.** Key arguments:
- Even if 2026 financial targets are met, without clinical sustainability the operation won't survive into 2027
- Healthcare is fundamentally different from a regular business — legal, regulatory, and clinical quality dimensions must be factored in
- The company needs KPIs for treatment quality, staff wellbeing, patient satisfaction, and organizational readiness — not just revenue and cost reduction
- Rami developed ~60 KPIs covering clinical documentation, triage, symptom tracking, remission rates, ER referral reduction, time to treatment, staff burnout, and growth readiness

### 5. Organizational Isolation and Communication Failure
Rami expressed deep frustration about:
- Being isolated as the only person in leadership who understands the clinical implications of growth decisions
- A complete disconnect between clinic needs and R&D/marketing/development priorities
- Shachar (CTO) spent 3 hours pressuring Rami to link features to financial targets — because that's what leadership mandated
- Adi (marketing) focused on bringing more patients rather than quality/readiness
- Previous week's warnings about clinical sustainability went unheard and nothing changed

### 6. Basel's Positive Impact
Rami credited Basel Kanaaneh with helping build the operational plan and providing clarity. Basel suggested that strong branding (like Clalit) could attract case managers willing to work at 50-60 NIS/hour as employees rather than expensive contractors — potentially saving ~7,000 NIS/year and 6% in expenses.

## Leadership Response

### Oren Taliaz
- Acknowledged the team may have "thrown Rami into deep water" with too many responsibilities
- Proposed that Rami should focus on clinical requirements while leadership handles operational/financial planning
- Suggested building the plan collaboratively rather than having Rami work in isolation

### Dekkel Taliaz
- Acknowledged difficulty understanding the exact source of Rami's frustration
- Proposed a dedicated Sunday meeting to hear Rami's full strategic proposal
- Wanted to understand Rami's plan in detail — what growth rate is safe, what clinical KPIs matter, what the risk thresholds are
- Asked Rami to "drive from the clinical side upward" while leadership "drives from the financial side downward" to find alignment

### Rami's Pushback
- Rejected the proposal to separate clinical from operational: Basel executes plans, but Rami must set the strategy — including secretary management, session timing, budget allocation within the clinic
- Expressed frustration at still being misunderstood: "The most dangerous thing is to think you understood someone who tells you that you don't understand them"
- Ultimately agreed to present his full proposal at Sunday's meeting

## Outcome

- Dedicated strategy session scheduled for Sunday to hear Rami's complete clinical-driven strategy
- Shachar's work plan progressed independently of this meeting
- Fundamental tension remains: leadership approached from financial metrics, Rami insists on clinical sustainability as the foundation

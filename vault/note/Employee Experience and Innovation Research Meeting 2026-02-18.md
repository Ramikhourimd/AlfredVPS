# Employee Experience and Innovation Research Meeting 2026-02-18

## Context

One-on-one working session between Rami Khouri and Rivi Idelman on 2026-02-18 (Zoom). Meeting opened with personal updates (Rami adjusting to second child, Rivi in regular cadence with Basel and Yael). Basel reported being very happy working with Rivi. The conversation then moved to substantive discussion on four major topics.

## 1. Staff Burnout and Employee Experience Findings

Rivi reported qualitative findings gathered from interviews with both case managers and psychiatrists:

### Burnout and Work Intensity
- **Both case managers and psychiatrists** report significant burnout, though in different patterns
- Staff working **6-8 hours/week** cope better; beyond that threshold, exhaustion escalates
- Example: One psychiatrist (Saeed) previously worked 3-4 hours/day across 5 days and found it draining. After reducing to 1.5 hours/day due to exam preparation, he found it much more sustainable — even if the same total weekly hours were compressed into one day, he would not do it
- A case manager noted feeling "much more empathetic and attentive" at 5 hours versus 9 hours of work
- Burnout was described by multiple staff as present and significant, though not everyone articulates it as a primary complaint

### Isolation and Lack of Team Cohesion
- Most staff report **not feeling part of a team** — they work alone from home facing a screen
- The planned internal tasks system (replacing WhatsApp for operational messages) will improve task tracking but may **amplify isolation** by reducing the informal social connection of WhatsApp group chats
- Some staff expressed that things they struggle with, others also experience, but nobody knows because they don't see each other's messages in the groups

### Impact of Group Supervision (Hadracha)
- One case manager reported that burnout **noticeably decreased** after group supervision sessions began
- She was unsure whether it was the group contact itself or the content, but the effect was real

### WhatsApp Communication Gaps
- Multiple staff reported writing messages in various WhatsApp groups and **receiving no response**
- This includes psychiatrist questions, technical issues, and messages to Rena — staff don't know who is responsible for addressing their concerns
- A staff member described the feeling as "we're like abandoned children without parents — we have to take care of everything ourselves and nobody above us says what should happen"

### Maccabi Transition Reduced Clinical Diversity
- Psychiatrist (Iliya) mentioned that moving from post-October 7th trauma patients to regular Maccabi patients reduced his clinical motivation
- Before: diverse, challenging PTSD cases with a sense of social mission
- After: routine mental health clinic work, but with the downsides of remote work and administrative overhead
- Net assessment: "not worth the effort" compared to other options, staying mainly for convenience (pay, WFH)

### Unreported Extra Hours
- Psychiatrists regularly work **unreported extra hours** — preparation before sessions, reviewing summaries after, learning complex case histories
- They stopped reporting these hours because they know the company questions extra time claims
- AI summaries improving may help, but the pattern of under-reporting is established

### Follow-Up Session Ambiguity
- Case managers unclear on **what to do during 3rd and 4th follow-up sessions**
- First and second sessions have clear goals (side effects, adjustments), but later sessions lack defined objectives
- Staff end up improvising — each person develops their own approach
- Case managers asked for structured guidelines for follow-up sessions, similar to the intake checklist Rivi created (which they found very helpful)

### Psychiatrist Retention Risk
- Many psychiatrists appear to stay for **convenience factors** (pay, work-from-home) rather than engagement or mission
- Rivi's assessment: if a better offer comes along, many would leave
- Case managers appear more committed by comparison, though workload concerns exist

## 2. Research Methodology for Employee Experience

Rami instructed Rivi to adopt a structured academic-style approach:

- **Structure reports like research**: background, methodology, results, discussion, recommendations
- **Build measurable scales** — convert qualitative impressions into survey instruments
- **Create periodic employee satisfaction surveys** measuring dimensions: financial, work comfort, atmosphere, professional development, technology, support services
- **Segment analysis** by staff type (psychiatrist vs case manager), work schedule, hours, concurrent caseload
- **Immediate action**: Rivi to create a Google Sheets document where each row is a research question she wants to investigate. Rami will use an automated system to generate literature reviews overnight from these inputs

## 3. Role Boundaries — Researcher/Concept Designer

Rami explicitly reinforced Rivi's role boundaries (consistent with [[decision/Top-Down Gated Concept Design Framework for Innovation]]):

- Rivi's role is **concept design and research**, not execution or implementation
- She should produce structured documents: problem definition, research findings, recommendations
- For the pre-treatment video: she should define the concept (what problem it solves, what it should contain), not produce the video itself
- She can add a "recommendations for implementation" section to any document, but must be clear this is supplementary
- For operational issues surfaced during research (payment problems, system bugs): redirect staff to Shira (clinical), Rena (administrative), or Shachar (technical) — not absorb them herself
- Staff should only approach Rivi with **ideas or improvement suggestions**, not operational complaints

## 4. Next-Generation System Prototype

Rami proposed a new initiative: collaboratively build a **prototype vision** of the most advanced possible clinical management system:

- Start by surveying existing systems in the market
- Build a model combining the best features from all systems
- Add innovative and novel features through brainstorming
- Create a conceptual specification assuming unlimited technical resources
- Use this as the basis for an **annual development roadmap**
- Rivi to lead the initial phase, requesting relevant academic literature and market research from Ohad

## 5. Coordination with Shira

Rivi raised that Shira had asked for clarity on who talks to staff about what, to avoid overlap. Agreement:
- Rivi approaches staff from the **innovation/research perspective** — understanding needs, characterizing problems, designing solutions
- Shira approaches from the **clinical management perspective** — operational issues, performance, compliance
- The distinction needs to be formalized, potentially in a meeting (without Rami, who wants Rivi to manage this boundary independently)

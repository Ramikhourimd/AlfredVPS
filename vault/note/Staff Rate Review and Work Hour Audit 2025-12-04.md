## Individual Rate Review

- One psychiatrist currently receives **400 NIS/hour for production** (clinical work)

- Rami's approach: rather than lowering someone's rate (which hurts morale), it's better to carry the person at the higher rate and let it naturally correct — "better to carry someone than to reduce them"
- Another person should be receiving **75 NIS** for something specific

- **Manal and Naomi**: Were the first two clinicians to start at **150 NIS/hour**, and later received raises. These two have the highest visibility from Rivi at 120
- **Ahmed Masalha**: Receives **2 hours** of compensation (likely for specific admin or clinical function)
- **Said**: Also receives a specific compensation amount
- **Tamer and Rivi**: Combined allocation of **25%** or **17%** (unclear which figure is final)
- **David**: Mentioned in the rate review but specific rate not clearly stated

### Rate Categories
- **120 NIS/hour**: Rivi's rate — appears to be for administrative or coordination work ("avoda masredit" / office work, plus "hadrakha" / training hours)
- **150 NIS/hour**: Starting clinical rate for certain staff
- **157 NIS/hour**: Bassam's rate
- Distinction between **clinical hours** ("hatshnut" / patient-facing) and **masradot** (office/administrative hours) is tracked

## Work Hour Tracking Details

### Clinical vs Administrative Hours
- Important distinction between:
  - **"Shaot hatshnut"** (patient-facing clinical hours)
  - **"Shaot masradot"** (office/administrative hours)
  - **"Shaot hadrakha"** (training/guidance hours)
- Some staff (like Rivi) have hours split across patient-facing and administrative functions
- **Noor**: Has **40 hours** allocation but actually working **34** — needs investigation into where the **37** figure comes from in the system

### System Tracking Concerns
- After a clinical session ends ("sof hatiko"), there's additional time logged for post-session work
- This creates inflated hour counts if not properly filtered
- Some staff have hours appearing in the system that aren't real clinical work — it's post-session admin
- The question of "how many hours actually worked vs how many hours billed" remains an ongoing data quality issue

### Payment Figure Discrepancies
- One employee's total shows **23,800 NIS** but the calculated amount is **25,000 NIS** — a **~1,200 NIS gap** with an additional 7,000+ that needs explanation
- Traced to extra hours (**52 hours**) at a wedding/event — these are "ovef" (extra) hours, not standard clinical
- This employee started early with Rami and may be the 4th hire ("from the earliest days")

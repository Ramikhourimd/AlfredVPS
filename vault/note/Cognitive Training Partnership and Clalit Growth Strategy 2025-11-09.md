# Cognitive Training Partnership and Clalit Growth Strategy 2025-11-09

## Topic 1: Cognitive Training Company Partnership

Rami's brother Levi works at a company that provides cognitive training services to patients. Rami met with Levi and the company's app developer to explore a potential partnership with Telia'z.

**What the company wants:** They are already selling cognitive training services to patients and initially proposed marketing the service through Telia'z, with a revenue-sharing model.

**Rami's assessment:**
- Skeptical that Maccabi patients would pay out-of-pocket for this service
- Concerned about regulatory/contractual sensitivity given the HMO agreements
- Sees the **research angle** as the most interesting aspect — the company collects data that could be valuable
- More interested in bringing psychotherapy services into the clinic's offering than cognitive training

**Psychotherapy discussion:** Rami noted that a company Shachar previously suggested they meet was trying to shorten psychotherapy session times via telemedicine. Rami expressed doubt this is clinically acceptable — psychotherapy cannot be meaningfully shortened the way other services can. However, he was curious to understand their approach, especially if they rebrand it (e.g., "cognitive training" instead of "psychotherapy"). The group agreed to attend the meeting to learn more.

**Key insight:** Rami's primary interest is not the cognitive training product itself, but rather what it reveals about expanding into psychotherapy services and the research data it generates.

## Topic 2: Clalit Patient Acquisition and Growth Strategy

The group discussed how Clalit patient volumes will develop and whether the partnership can be profitable.

**Form 17 history lesson (Maccabi precedent):**
- When Telia'z operated under Maccabi with Form 17 (referral authorization), patient volume was stuck at approximately 13 patients per month for several months
- Once Form 17 was eliminated, volumes jumped to hundreds per month
- This experience suggests that having a referral gate (Form 17) dramatically suppresses patient volume

**Clalit patient acquisition concern:**
- Rami predicts that even if the Clalit contact sends referral emails to family doctors, the GPs will simply ignore them
- Without direct patient access or marketing, Telia'z will not see significant patient volumes from Clalit
- To reach Maccabi-like volumes, the referral barrier must be minimized

**Colleague's optimism (Speaker 1):**
- Expressed calmness about the situation, believing Telia'z will "be inside" and can set their own pace
- Emphasized that marketing investment will drive volume — it depends on how much Telia'z invests
- Flagged a critical gap: profitability calculations to date have NOT included marketing budget, which must be corrected

**Clalit financial terms:**
- Clalit is offering 3 case manager session components (vs. 4 at Maccabi) per patient
- The per-component rate is slightly lower than Maccabi but "still pays nicely"
- Speaker 2 confirmed Clalit agreed to 3 components (not 4) — a 3:1 case manager to psychiatrist ratio
- Profitability analysis (including marketing costs) has not yet been completed

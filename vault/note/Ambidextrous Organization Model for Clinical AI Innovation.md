# Ambidextrous Organization Model for Clinical AI Innovation

## Context

Rami listened to a structured presentation/debate on 2025-12-01 about building an innovation and AI department from the ground up at a growing digital health company like Taliaz. The content covers the ambidextrous organization model — a research-backed approach to structuring innovation alongside traditional R&D.

## Core Thesis: The Ambidextrous Organization

The inherent friction between fast-moving innovation teams and execution-focused R&D teams is **structural, not personal**. The ambidextrous organization model solves this by giving the company "two hands":

- **One hand (R&D):** Expert at executing and optimizing today's business
- **Other hand (Innovation):** Exploring and building the business of tomorrow
- **Same brain (senior leadership):** Coordinating both

Research shows organizations using this model achieve **90% of their innovation goals**.

## Blueprint: Three Core Components

### 1. Structure — The Golden Rule

**The head of innovation must report directly to the CEO.**

This is the single most important structural decision. It:
- Establishes innovation as a **peer to R&D**, not a junior team underneath it
- Sidesteps territorial conflicts that kill progress
- Aligns with industry trends: 83% of physician informatics officers now report directly into the C-suite

### 2. Team — The Five-Person Core

The department has three arms:

| Arm | Purpose | Roles |
|-----|---------|-------|
| **Clinical & Insights** | Sensors in the real world — understand clinician and patient pain points | Clinical informatics specialist |
| **AI & Data** | Technical engine — designs and builds the models | Senior AI/ML engineer, Platform engineer |
| **Projects & Implementation** | Operational hub — manages roadmaps, budgets, delivery | Innovation lead (vision), Product manager (execution) |

This lean five-person team is the core unit for driving change:
1. Innovation Lead — sets the vision
2. Senior AI/ML Engineer — technical core
3. Platform Engineer — technical core
4. Clinical Informatics Specialist — bridge to clinical world (crucial)
5. Product Manager — roadmap and metrics

### 3. Governance — Preventing Conflict

Two tools eliminate turf wars:

**A. Innovation Steering Committee**
- Members: CEO, Head of R&D, Innovation Lead, key clinical leaders
- Function: Sets priorities, allocates resources
- Critical role: **Ultimate referee** — decides which projects belong to innovation vs. R&D

**B. Three Horizons Framework**

| Horizon | Focus | Timeline | Ownership |
|---------|-------|----------|-----------|
| H1 — Core | Incremental improvements to existing products | 0–2 years | R&D team (undisputed) |
| H2 — Adjacent | Extending current capabilities into new areas | 2–4 years | **Shared territory** — steering committee manages |
| H3 — Breakthrough | Disruptive, unproven AI, new business models | 3–7 years | Innovation team (their sandbox) |

## Practical Workflow

The innovation lifecycle flows as:

1. **Insights team** collects real problems from clinicians (ground-up)
2. **Product manager** frames and structures the problem
3. **Steering committee** decides if it gets prioritized
4. **AI team** designs a solution
5. **Development and implementation** team delivers
6. **Usage and impact data** fed back to team for next iteration

Clean flow from need → impact, with a closed feedback loop.

## Key Takeaway

> "The friction you feel between your innovation and R&D teams — it's almost always structural. It's not personal. You've got good people trying their best to do their jobs inside a system that's just not set up for them to win. The solution is to stop blaming people."

## Applicability to Telia'z

This model directly informs several active restructuring decisions:
- **Innovation Lead reporting line** — model says report to CEO (Oren), not under CTO (Shachar)
- **Steering committee** — already proposed for Telia'z governance; this provides the blueprint
- **Three Horizons** — framework for resolving innovation vs R&D territory at Telia'z
- **Five-person team structure** — template for sizing the innovation department
- **Clinical informatics specialist** — validates the need for a clinical bridge role (Rami's current function)

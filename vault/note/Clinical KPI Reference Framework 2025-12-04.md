# Clinical KPI Reference Framework 2025-12-04

## Context

Rami identified this KPI framework while reviewing materials at the clinic office on 2025-12-04. The source appears to be a business curriculum or reference document focused on clinical practice management. These metrics complement the existing KPI targets already defined for the Telia'z Clinic Israel operations dashboard.

## KPI Categories

### Patient Flow Metrics
| KPI | Description | Relevance |
|-----|-------------|-----------|
| Collection rate | Percentage of patients who complete or continue treatment | Core retention indicator |
| Caseload size | Active patients per clinician | Capacity planning |
| ER visit rate | Emergency room utilization by clinic patients | Quality of care proxy; important for Maccabi HMO reporting |
| Retention rate | Patient continuation in treatment program | Treatment effectiveness |

### Clinical Quality Metrics
| KPI | Description | Relevance |
|-----|-------------|-----------|
| Recovery rate | Patient recovery/improvement measurement | Outcome tracking |
| Clinician notes audited (%) | Percentage of clinical notes reviewed by supervisors | Critical quality lever — Rami notes achieving 100% is the "battle" from the previous day's discussion |
| Supervisor-to-clinician hours ratio | Ratio of supervision time to clinical delivery time | Oversight intensity |

### Operational Metrics
| KPI | Description | Relevance |
|-----|-------------|-----------|
| Clinician retention | Staff turnover/retention rate | Workforce stability |
| Time for open roles | Speed of recruitment for vacant positions | Operational continuity |
| Third-party satisfaction | Patient and referrer satisfaction scores | Reputation and referral pipeline |
| Dimension rate | Unclear — possibly outcome dimension scoring | Needs clarification |

## Connection to Existing Dashboard Targets

These KPIs extend the four core targets already established for the clinic operations dashboard. The "clinician notes audited" metric is particularly actionable — Rami links it directly to the clinical audit initiative discussed on 2025-12-03, framing it as the key intervention to reach 100% compliance.

# AI in Medicine Training Program Collaboration Discussion 2025-09-01

## Context

Voice memo transcript from a multi-party networking conversation (in Arabic with Hebrew) recorded on 2025-09-01. Rami Khouri met with organizers of a medical innovation training program targeting Arab healthcare professionals. The conversation explored collaboration opportunities between Telia'z and the program.

## The Medical Innovation Training Program

A structured medical innovation and entrepreneurship program operating in northern Israel (Nazareth area, connected to the French Hospital):

- **Format:** 7 frontal (in-person) sessions over 3 months (October, November, December), typically on Fridays or Sundays
- **Cohorts completed:** 3 cohorts of 10-15 participants each; 4th cohort about to start
- **Participants:** Primarily Arab medical professionals (psychiatrists, psychologists, social workers) but also open to Jewish participants
- **Partners:** Collaborates with SET (a social entrepreneurship organization) and a startup incubator called "North Betkhnin"
- **Curriculum:** Covers the full medical innovation lifecycle — concept development, business model building, team assembly, pitch preparation, startup basics, and digital health innovation
- **Cost:** Currently free (subsidized), exploring sustainability models for future cohorts
- **Selection:** Competitive entry; organizers interview and select motivated participants

## Key Discussion Points

### AI Adoption Gap in Medicine
- One speaker (early AI adopter) took a "Prompt Master" course and has been deeply involved in AI forums since ChatGPT's launch
- Expressed surprise at how few doctors and medical professionals have adopted AI tools, even basic ChatGPT usage
- Emphasized that the real gap is between casual ChatGPT use and building automations/agents for daily medical workflows
- Predicted an emerging "intellectual barrier" — those not adopting AI will fall behind in their thinking approach

### Rami's Engagement Offer
- **Guest speaking:** Rami offered to present at the program, sharing his experience at Telia'z working at the intersection of healthtech and clinical innovation
- **Staff participation:** Rami proposed sending diverse Telia'z staff to participate — psychiatrists, psychologists, social workers, and software engineers — to broaden their understanding of the innovation/startup ecosystem
- **Personal motivation:** Rami expressed a genuine desire to contribute to the Arab medical community, noting that his work is primarily in the center (Tel Aviv) and he doesn't get enough opportunity to engage with Arab professionals
- **Data collaboration:** Mentioned mutual benefit — Telia'z could gain from data perspectives and the program participants could gain from Telia'z's real-world clinical AI experience

### Zaher Elias Connection
- [[person/Zaher Elias]] was mentioned as someone interested in developing AI-specific courses for doctors at the English Hospital (Nazareth)
- Connected through [[person/Maia Elias]] (his niece), who is known to the program organizers
- This was flagged as a potential separate but complementary initiative

### Advanced AI Course Idea
- At the end of the conversation, someone proposed creating an advanced AI-specific course (separate from the general innovation program) covering:
  - Building automations and AI agents
  - Vibe coding
  - Integration with automation platforms (n8n, Make)
  - Building complete products
  - Targeted at more advanced participants who want to go deeper into AI

# Telia'z HR Challenges, Leadership Assessment, and CEO Discussion — 2025-12-02

Private management discussion between Rami Khouri and Oren Taliaz covering urgent HR issues, personnel assessments, and a candid exploration of the CEO role vacancy.

## 1. Li Yamin HR Obstruction

Basel Kanaaneh, newly onboarded as Operations Manager, requested employment agreements from Li Yamin (HR). Li redirected him to Shira instead of providing the documents directly. When Rami emailed Li asking her to hand over all signed agreements and templates, she pushed back — claiming it was not her responsibility and she already has too much work.

**Key concerns:**
- Li has a pattern of deflecting work and "educating" colleagues about boundaries rather than collaborating
- She previously had similar friction with Shachar, who also found her difficult to work with
- Both Rami and Oren agree her behavior is blocking organizational progress
- Li's legitimate complaints: external providers (lawyers, accountants) repeatedly lose documents she sends them, creating redundant work

**Oren's intervention:** Oren already spoke with Li around 5 PM that day. He asked her to be patient for 1-3 months, cooperate with Basel, and transfer all documents properly — framing it as essential for Basel's success in centralizing operations.

**Assessment:** Both agree Li may not be suitable for her role. Rami wants a "root cause" solution, not another one-off conversation. Oren suggests she may lack the temperament for a role requiring constant collaboration under pressure. She wants to be a product designer rather than HR.

## 2. Basel Kanaaneh's Positive Impact

Basel is making immediate changes since joining the clinic:
- Taking ownership of meetings Rami previously ran
- Identifying disorder and acting on it from day one
- Clarifying role divisions between Rana and Shira
- Working on agreements, protocols, and organizational structure
- After a meeting with Anan (children's services), Basel declared the meeting cancelled and said he'd look for a replacement manager — Anan wasn't taking responsibility

**Basel's observation:** "I don't know how you tolerated this situation. People here don't care." Rami told him: "That's your job now. Do what you need to do."

**Contrast with Elinor (previous hire attempt):** Elinor was offered a similar operational role but was scared off by the scope and chaos. Basel took on even more responsibility at lower pay and hit the ground running. The difference is attitude and drive, not compensation.

## 3. Elinor — Toxic Behavior in Clinical Team

Elinor is a senior psychiatrist currently at the clinic displaying problematic behavior:
- Complaining publicly in group chats (not privately)
- Case managers are complaining about her attitude
- She declares things are "not right" and refuses to work under certain conditions
- As a senior psychiatrist, her public complaints undermine morale, especially with new interns present
- Basel has candidates to replace her; she will be let go once a replacement is confirmed

## 4. Shira's Ongoing Limitations

Shira was previously responsible for admin/HR coordination within the clinic but could not handle it:
- She never truly took ownership of agreements and protocols
- Oren believes she "didn't want to do it" rather than being unable
- This is a known, documented issue being addressed separately through Basel's restructuring

## 5. Anan — Children's Services Underperformance

Anan manages children's services but is not performing:
- Rami has been doing 90% of Anan's work
- Basel already cancelled a joint meeting after seeing Anan's lack of engagement
- Basel is actively searching for a replacement manager

## 6. CEO Role Assessment — Rami's Self-Evaluation

**The authority vacuum:** Rami feels he is doing >50% of CEO-level work, particularly in operations, clinical leadership, and organizational culture. He lacks formal authority to match his actual responsibilities. He is excluded from key decisions and hears about them after the fact.

**Alex (current/outgoing CEO) assessment:**
- Alex is essentially an advisor, not an operator
- He doesn't follow through — he'll suggest things and hope they happen
- Rami and Oren constantly need to remind him of commitments
- Stark contrast with Dekel, who is thorough with follow-ups and monthly reviews

**Dekel Taliaz assessment:**
- *Strengths:* Extremely operational, makes things happen, thorough follow-ups, good external presence (media, public representation)
- *Weaknesses:* Short-term focused, constantly firefighting, avoids confronting uncomfortable truths, micromanages (undermines trust in subordinates), lacks long-term strategic planning
- Does CEO-level operational work by default because nobody else does it
- Not suited for scaling — his approach works for a startup but not for a growing organization

**Rami's self-assessment:**
- Believes his leadership style is "manage goals, not people" — hire good managers and oversee them
- Has strong vision for company identity and direction
- Acknowledges he doesn't love operational details (budgets, schedules) but can do them
- Is not fully committed to wanting the CEO role — loves innovation work more
- Key motivator: wants authority to match his actual influence, not the title itself
- Proposed: "If there's ever a decision about replacing Alex, at least discuss it with me before bringing someone external"

**Oren's response:** Acknowledges the situation, commits to keeping Rami informed, but doesn't make commitments about the CEO question specifically.

## 7. Company Identity Crisis

Rami observes that nobody at Telia'z — including Adi Lavi, Adiel, and other team members — truly understands the company's identity:
- People are waiting for someone to define "who we are"
- Is Telia'z a clinical service company? A tech company? A healthcare platform?
- Each person interprets the identity based on their own comfort zone
- Adi Lavi equated Telia'z = psychiatric services on the website, which Rami disagrees with
- Rami advocates keeping the brand umbrella broad: "Telia'z Health by Telia'z" — leaving room for the brand to grow beyond psychiatric services
- Rami believes he is the only person who truly understands the company's DNA and trajectory

**Significance:** This identity vacuum is directly connected to the CEO question — whoever leads needs to define and defend this identity.

## 8. Ziv — Advisor Evaluation

Ziv is an external advisor/consultant currently working with Telia'z:
- Very tech-focused — conversations with Rami gravitate toward AI and data
- Lacks the broad organizational vision needed for structural guidance
- Does not understand the company holistically despite having experience
- Arrangement: working through end of 2025 with a review to decide on continuation and role definition
- Oren acknowledges "I'm still learning what he's not" — Ziv won't prepare presentations or build org charts

## 9. Innovation-R&D Coordination Gap

Rami describes a fundamental coordination problem:
- Innovation (Rami) and R&D (Shachar/CTO) operate without formal oversight
- The work "functions" because Rami and Shachar personally get along
- No one from above manages the interface — no regular joint meetings with leadership
- In a properly structured company, the CEO or CTO would coordinate this
- As the company scales, this informal arrangement will break down

## 10. Recruitment Clarification

Rumor of hiring 4 secretaries was discussed and denied. The actual hiring is more targeted and relates to clinical needs (medical hires), not a large administrative expansion.

## Key Themes

1. **People who block flow must be addressed** — Li, Elinor, Anan all represent friction points
2. **Basel is a culture carrier** — his approach embodies the work ethic Rami wants across the organization
3. **The CEO vacuum is causing drift** — without clear top-level leadership, everyone improvises
4. **Company identity must precede structure** — you can't build the org chart without knowing what the company is
5. **Collaboration over correctness** — both speakers emphasize that being right matters less than keeping things moving

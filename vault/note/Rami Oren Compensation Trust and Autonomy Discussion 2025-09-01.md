# Rami Oren Compensation Trust and Autonomy Discussion 2025-09-01

## Context

This is a second segment of the same-day conversation between [[person/Rami Khouri]] and [[person/Oren Taliaz]] captured in [[conversation/Rami Oren On-Call Compensation Discussion 2025-09-01]]. While the first segment covered budget mechanics and distributions, this segment addresses the philosophical and relational dimensions of the compensation arrangement.

## Key Points

### Oren's Discomfort With Employee-Freelancer Dual Model

Oren opened by confirming agreement on the 24,000 NIS budget ("we'll go with the 24") but expressed deep discomfort with the hybrid employee-freelancer arrangement. He noted:
- The company's lawyer (Daniel) objected repeatedly across approximately seven separate conversations, consistently saying "you don't do something like this"
- The lawyer could not understand why Oren and Alex were even entertaining the arrangement
- Oren acknowledged the legal and audit complexity it created
- Oren's concern: the efficiencies Rami creates should flow to the company, not be captured through a personal freelancer vehicle

### Rami's Perspective on the Opportunity

Rami clarified his position transparently:
- He saw it as an **opportunity** for supplemental income, not as a permanent mechanism
- He was clear from the start it was not a long-term incentive model
- He wanted Oren to never doubt his motives: "I don't want you to ever feel in any situation that I'm trying to sting you — not for one shekel or a million shekels"
- He agreed to drop the company structure and take everything through the payslip

### Oren's Promise of Comprehensive Compensation Package

Oren revealed he is working with [[person/Alex Taliaz]] and [[person/Dekkel Taliaz]] on a comprehensive compensation package for Rami:
- Will include **stock options**, a **bonus mechanism**, and the "full package"
- Oren said: "The goal is to take care of you. I'm telling you this clearly and sharply."
- He asked for time: "Give me some time, because I want to come with something that is very, very right and very, very precise"
- This connects to a conversation from two weeks prior where Oren said he needed to "sit and think about Rami holistically"

### Flexible Budget as Autonomy Tool

Oren proposed a creative mechanism for the on-call budget gap:
- Rami would know the total budget (24,000), manage distributions, and keep whatever remains
- The remainder could be used flexibly: as personal bonus, or to "pamper psychiatrists" with perks, conferences, team activities
- Oren framed this as giving Rami a discretionary clinic fund: "Go, use it for what you need. Give bonuses to whoever you think deserves it."
- Rami valued this concept primarily for the **autonomy** it provided, not the money itself

### Rami's Need for Operational Autonomy

Rami articulated a core professional need:
- "I have a new idea every day, and every time I have to come and ask, get approval — it frustrates me"
- He wants the flexibility to reallocate resources without seeking permission each time
- He explained this is why he preferred a budget model over fixed payments: he constantly adjusts on-call staffing (Ahmad one month, Elinor the next, nobody the month after)

### Oren's Recognition of Rami's Growth

Oren explicitly acknowledged a shift in their dynamic:
- "In the last two-three months, almost everything you bring, I say 'go ahead, proceed'"
- "I see that you come cooked already. You don't come with musings — you come prepared, after processing things"
- "Most things you say sound good, sound right"
- Oren contrasted this with his experience creating an incentive mechanism for "DM" (another executive), noting it took a long time but eventually created strong motivation

### Trust Foundation

Both emphasized that their relationship is built on trust rather than formal agreements:
- Rami: "Our agreement is built more on trust than what was written in the contract six or eight months ago"
- Oren: "I trust you. I'm just showing you the small aspect that is contradictory — you work full-time and then do additional things"
- The tension: Oren trusts Rami's effort but is concerned about time allocation between salaried work and on-call side activities

## Takeaways

1. The on-call arrangement through December 2025 is confirmed at 24,000 NIS monthly budget
2. Rami drops the personal company structure entirely — all payments through payslip
3. Oren is actively developing a proper long-term compensation package (options + bonus)
4. Rami's primary motivator is operational autonomy, not the money itself
5. The relationship has matured — Oren increasingly defers to Rami's judgment on clinic operations

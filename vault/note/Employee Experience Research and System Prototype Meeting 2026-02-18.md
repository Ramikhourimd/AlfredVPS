# Employee Experience Research and System Prototype Meeting 2026-02-18

## Context

One-on-one working session between Rami Khouri and Rivi Idelman on 2026-02-18. Rivi reports on her clinical staff interviews and engagement findings, and Rami provides direction on how to structure the research and clarifies her role boundaries within the innovation team.

## Key Findings: Staff Burnout and Satisfaction

### Case Managers
- Multiple case managers report the work is **intensely draining** — one described leaving Telia'z shifts feeling far more exhausted and burnt out than after hospital residency shifts, even when residency hours are longer.
- The **intensity per hour** is the issue, not just total hours: constant patient interactions, documentation demands, and emotional load create cumulative strain.
- Staff working **6-8 hours per week** manage reasonably; those exceeding this threshold report significantly more burnout.
- One case manager (Sa'id) found switching from 3-4 hours daily (5 days) to 1.5 hours daily much more sustainable — even the same weekly total concentrated into one long block would be unacceptable.
- Peer supervision/group sessions appear to reduce burnout perception: one case manager reported feeling "suddenly less burnt out" after group forums began, though unclear if it was the group itself or the supervision content.
- Working from home at 5 hours (vs. 9) significantly reduces burnout for Rivi personally; empathy and attentiveness noticeably better at lower hours.

### Psychiatrists
- Psychiatrists similarly report burnout, with additional frustration around **difficult/aggressive patients** — they feel unsupported when such patients arrive without adequate screening.
- One psychiatrist described the team as feeling like **"orphaned children without parents"** — a perception that clinical staff manage everything alone without adequate organizational backing.
- Psychiatrists report working **unreported extra hours**: arriving early to review complex cases, reviewing/rewriting AI summaries after sessions, preparing documentation — but they don't report these hours because they know it's "not the Telia'z way" and fear being questioned.
- The transition to **Maccabi** (from post-trauma populations) reduced professional diversity and sense of purpose. One psychiatrist explicitly said: before, the work had social importance (post-October 7 patients, southern residents) and clinical variety; now it feels like "just another HMO clinic" but with worse conditions (isolation at home, documentation overhead).
- Retention risk: psychiatrists appear to stay primarily for **convenience** (work from home, pay) rather than passion. Multiple indications that "if a better offer comes, they'd leave."

### Team Cohesion
- Most staff report **not feeling part of a team**: "I'm alone in front of my computer with patients." Even knowing other colleagues exist doing the same work, there's no real interaction.
- The planned move to an **internal tasks system** (replacing WhatsApp coordination) may inadvertently worsen isolation by reducing the informal social element of WhatsApp groups.
- WhatsApp group messages often go **unanswered** — clinical questions, technical issues, operational matters — creating frustration and reinforcing the sense of abandonment.

### Follow-Up Session Protocol Gap
- Case managers identified a **gap in the 3rd and 4th follow-up sessions**: the first two sessions have clear goals (assess side effects, monitor medication response), but later sessions lack defined objectives.
- Staff improvise ("I always make something up") and each develops their own approach independently.
- A **checklist Rivi created for intake sessions** was well-received — case managers requested something similar for follow-up sessions.
- The pre-treatment patient video should also address follow-up expectations to reduce patient reluctance at being "tracked" repeatedly.

## Role Clarification: Rivi's Position

Rami explicitly defined Rivi's role boundaries:

1. **Researcher and Concept Designer** — not an executor or builder.
2. Deliverables should be **conceptual documents**: problem statement, methodology, findings, recommendations. NOT implementation details (e.g., "is it a filmed video or animation?").
3. Every concept document should follow **academic research structure**: background, rationale, methodology, results, discussion, recommendations.
4. Routing chain for actionable items: Rivi -> (product features) Yael / (existing system improvements) Ohad or Stav / (operational) Shira or Renee.
5. Rivi can add a **"recommendations" appendix** to documents with her implementation ideas, clearly marked as beyond her scope.
6. Need to coordinate with Shira on **staff communication boundaries** to avoid duplication — both talk to staff but with different objectives (Rivi for innovation research, Shira for clinical management).

## Research Methodology Direction

Rami instructed Rivi to:
1. Build a **Google Sheet** with research questions — each row is a topic to investigate. Rami will build an automated system to run overnight literature reviews and return results by morning.
2. Structure all reports like **academic research papers**: background, problem statement, methodology, measurable variables, results, analysis, recommendations.
3. Identify **measurable dimensions**: correlate variables like morning vs. evening shifts, weekend work, hours per day, number of concurrent cases, etc. with satisfaction/burnout outcomes.
4. Build a **periodic staff survey** instrument to track metrics over time, categorized by: financial, work conditions, home-work balance, atmosphere, professional growth, technology, peripheral factors.

## Next-Generation System Prototype

Rami proposed a team-wide initiative to:
1. Survey **all existing clinical systems** in the market and what they offer.
2. Build a **composite model** combining the best features from all.
3. **Brainstorm** innovative/novel features beyond what exists.
4. Create a **full-featured prototype concept** assuming unlimited tools/budget.
5. Use this as the basis for an **annual development roadmap**.

Rivi was asked to lead this effort, starting from existing system analysis.

# UK ADHD Platform Demo Rehearsal Notes 2025-02-11

## Context

Internal rehearsal meeting between Adiel Levin (CBO), Dr. Rami Khouri (CMO), and Dekkel Taliaz (CEO) in preparation for an upcoming platform demo to GP Confederation Leeds stakeholders — specifically Rebecca Wilson (IG/governance perspective) and Jason Brook (clinical workflow perspective). The goal was to script and practice a complete walkthrough of the HealthyMind platform for a UK adult ADHD patient.

## Demo Framing Strategy

Adiel scripted the opening pitch: position the demo as showing how the virtual psychiatry service and technology are utilized by psychiatrists. Key framing points:

- Focus on a UK ADHD patient workflow from beginning to end
- Emphasize that EHR integrations are possible via API — not locked to any single system
- Scheduling does not need to be controlled by Telia'z
- Ask Jason for input on how the workflow relates to GP Confed psychiatry processes and the ADHD tender they are applying for
- Ask Rebecca to validate IG (Information Governance) and DTech compliance criteria

## Platform Workflow Walkthrough

The demo covers four stages of the ADHD assessment workflow:

### Stage 1: Digital Questionnaire
Patient fills out standardized digital questionnaires prior to any clinical session. Scales mentioned include DIVA, ASRS, PHQ, and GAD-7.

### Stage 2: Case Manager Session
A clinical psychologist or clinical social worker (equivalent to a nurse prescriber or PWP in the UK context) conducts a structured pre-assessment session. This collects detailed data around the patient before the psychiatrist appointment. The case manager role is positioned as trainable within the GP Confed structure.

### Stage 3: Psychiatrist Session
The psychiatrist logs in, reviews the pre-collected data (demographics, referral information, examination notes, symptom scales), follows an agenda recommendation (optional), and conducts a one-hour session with the patient.

### Stage 4: Summary and Letter Generation
Post-session, the platform auto-generates a clinical summary. The psychiatrist reviews, adds medication details and notes, then clicks to produce a structured, pre-signed letter that can be sent to the patient. This letter format can be customized to NHS requirements.

## Key Discussion Points

- **ASRS/DIVA in summaries**: Adiel raised whether the demo summary could reference specific ADHD scales (ASRS, DIVA) with scores. Rami noted DIVA is an interview-based assessment, not a scored questionnaire, so it cannot simply show a number. Decision was to verbally explain scale usage during the demo rather than fake data in the summary.
- **Customizable NHS text**: The summary/letter output section can be tailored to NHS-specific requirements — this was flagged as a key selling point for the demo.
- **Patient persona narrative**: Adiel recommended building the demo around a patient story (e.g., "Emily's husband referred for ADHD assessment") to make the workflow more relatable and easier to follow.
- **UK role mapping**: Case managers were mapped to UK equivalents — nurse prescribers or Psychological Wellbeing Practitioners (PWPs) who could be trained within the GP Confed structure.
- **Compliance positioning**: The demo should address CQC, IG, and DTech requirements to satisfy Rebecca Wilson's governance review.

## Gaps Identified

- The current demo environment did not have working buttons for all features — some screens were non-functional
- Standardized scale scores (ASRS, DIVA) were not yet populated in the demo patient data
- Need to create a complete patient persona story for the demo narrative

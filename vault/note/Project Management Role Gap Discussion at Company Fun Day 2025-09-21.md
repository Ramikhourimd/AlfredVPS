# Project Management Role Gap Discussion at Company Fun Day 2025-09-21

A candid conversation between Rami Khouri (Speaker 8) and a colleague (Speaker 6) during the Telia'z Company Fun Day, diagnosing the project management vacuum left by Nadav Blatt's departure and assessing why current team members cannot fill it.

## New Employee Onboarding Gaps

Speaker 6 raised that when a new employee joins, it is unclear from an operational standpoint who needs to handle system access setup. Shira does not know many of the required steps. The lack of a clear onboarding process owner means new hires fall through the cracks on basic access and tooling.

## The Nadav Blatt Void

Rami described how [[person/Nadav Blatt]] used to take full ownership of project management from the start:
- He would sit with [[person/Shachar]] and check everything directly
- He sat with psychiatrists and handled scheduling
- He used Jira, managed tasks, ran meetings
- He functioned as a "joker" -- his role was not clearly defined, but he was the person who made things actually happen operationally

Since Nadav left, no one has stepped into this coordination role. The gap is felt across the organization.

## Adiel Levin's Project Management Weaknesses

Rami assessed [[person/Adiel Levin]]'s limitations as a project owner:
- Adiel is supposed to own new projects, but "on the business side" -- he sees mainly the commercial angle
- He has weaknesses in project management discipline: poor planning, comes in at "minute 90" (too late) with program planning
- He can take things upon himself, but his actual project management execution is not what the role requires
- A current project manager should not be what Adiel is -- he does not do the operational management work

## Rami's Self-Assessment

Rami provided a frank self-evaluation of his own capabilities:
- "I'm bad at execution. I can plan conceptually, but on the execution side I fall apart"
- He sees himself as a conceptual thinker who can envision and architect solutions, but cannot sustain the day-to-day task tracking and follow-through
- He compared this to needing someone like Nadav who would come in, sit down, use Jira, and manage every task and meeting

## The 5% Problem

Rami articulated a structural issue: when projects (like the England expansion or the children's clinic) are assigned to existing staff who each give only 5% of their capacity, things fall through. No single person owns the full lifecycle of a project. This is "unhealthy" and leads to dropped tasks and unclear accountability.

## Product vs. Project Distinction

Rami distinguished between "product" (something you finish and ship) and "project" (something that is constantly changing, evolving -- "it's life"). He noted that most of Telia'z's work is project-oriented, requiring ongoing management rather than one-time delivery.

## Positive Contrast: Adi Lavi

Rami cited [[person/Adi Lavi]] in marketing as a positive example -- she is proactive, does not wait for someone to tell her what to do, and takes initiative. This contrasts with the reactive approach seen in project management.

## Implied Need

The discussion points to a clear organizational need: a dedicated project manager / operations coordinator who can:
- Own the full lifecycle of projects from start to finish
- Manage tasks, meetings, and follow-ups in Jira or equivalent
- Handle operational coordination (onboarding, system access, cross-team communication)
- Be present and proactive rather than coming in at "minute 90"

This aligns with the existing [[assumption/Eitan Can Succeed as Project Manager Replacing Nadav]], which documents Eitan's pilot in this role and the negative feedback received so far.

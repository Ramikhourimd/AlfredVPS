# Organizational Strategy Meeting — 2026-02-22

## Context

In-person strategy meeting at Telia'z offices with key leadership to discuss organizational restructuring. Basel recently joined as Operations Manager and presented a proposed organizational structure developed with Leah. The meeting evolved into a fundamental debate about company governance, clinical authority, and role definitions.

**Participants:** Oren Khouri, Rami Khouri, Basel Khouri, Dekkel Khouri, Ziv, Leah Khouri

## Key Discussion Topics

### 1. Clinic Management Structure ("District Model")

Basel and Leah presented an organizational structure for the clinic with three parallel management tracks:
- **Clinical Operations Manager** (Shira, interim): Day-to-day clinic operations, KPIs, scheduling, workflow management
- **Medical Director** (30% position, to be hired): Clinical protocols, medical consultations, professional oversight of psychiatrists
- **Administrative Manager** (Renee): HR, secretarial staff management, procurement

**Pod Structure:** As the clinic scales, teams will be organized into pods — groups of ~10 psychiatrists or case managers led by a pod leader. Pod leaders handle complaints, training coordination, scheduling, and communicate upward to department heads. Pods are operationally separated but share professional forums (joint clinical meetings between psychiatrists and case managers).

**Key distinction:** Medical Director handles professional/clinical supervision of psychiatrists. Operations Manager handles administrative aspects (scheduling, work hours, coordination). The two collaborate through weekly meetings.

### 2. Secretarial Staff

Current state: 2 full-time secretaries (Alice barely working, Amda going on maternity leave). Renee transitioning out of hands-on secretarial work. Need to hire 2+ additional secretaries. By end of year, expect 5 full-time secretarial positions.

Secretaries serve two functions:
- **Patient-facing:** Appointment scheduling, phone support, system connectivity help, complaint handling
- **Staff-facing:** Calendar management for therapists, schedule changes, technical support

Discussion on whether to assign specific secretaries to specific pods or keep a general pool. Rami suggested 2 secretaries always available for phones/patient scheduling + 1 focused on therapist support.

### 3. CMO vs COO Debate (Central Tension)

**Rami's position:** The person managing the entire clinical operation must be a physician. A Chief Medical Officer should sit above all clinic operations (both clinical and operational), because:
- Medical liability falls on the physician
- Strategic decisions (growth rate, hiring model, marketing approach) have clinical implications
- Israel's High Court has intervened when hospital directors weren't physicians
- The company's unique model (telepsychiatry + AI) requires someone who understands both clinical and operational intersections
- He feels his voice hasn't been heard despite being right on many issues

**Oren's position:** The company should be viewed as one unified entity. The CMO should focus on clinical oversight (protocols, standards, medical veto power) while a COO handles operational management. Key arguments:
- Rami was burning out doing 4 jobs simultaneously (CMO + operations + innovation + clinic management)
- Basel was brought in specifically to take over operational management
- Centralized services (HR, marketing, finance) should come from the parent company, not be duplicated in the clinic
- The "district model" means the clinic is like a regional operation under a corporate headquarters
- Rami's clinical veto power should be formalized through a steering committee

**Ziv's advice:** Drew parallels to multinational corporations with local/global function splits. Cautioned against Rami trying to control everything. Suggested Rami's unique combination of medical and analytical skills is best utilized in a strategic advisory capacity, not day-to-day operations. Key insight: "I grew as a manager when I learned to let go."

### 4. Rami's Role Definition

The group struggled to define Rami's role clearly. Current state: Rami wants to:
- Not manage day-to-day operations
- Not manage people directly
- Focus on strategy and innovation
- Maintain influence over clinical standards

**Proposed resolution:** Rami serves as CMO at the company level (across all clinics), with:
- Clinical veto authority on medical matters
- Seat on the steering committee
- Innovation leadership
- Input into all strategic decisions with clinical implications
- A Medical Director at each clinic level reports to him on clinical standards

**Unresolved:** Rami expressed frustration that even with formal authority, his recommendations are often not implemented quickly enough. He suggested bringing in an external medical consultant to validate his positions.

### 5. Innovation & CTO Relationship

Innovation department (led by Rami) and CTO (Shachar) need better integration:
- Rami has a 70-page innovation plan that Shachar hasn't had capacity to review
- Innovation needs product management to prioritize and translate ideas into development tasks
- Decision to work "in parallel" with tech development, but unclear what that means operationally
- Rami's innovation team (Ravit, Stav) does field research and requirements gathering from therapists
- Need a product manager to mediate between innovation and development

### 6. Resource Sharing / Centralization

Agreement that these functions should remain centralized at the parent company level (not duplicated in clinic):
- HR / People operations (Tal)
- Marketing (Adi)
- Finance / Accounting
- Technical support and training (Roei)
- Business development

The clinic should *consume* these services rather than build its own. Basel, as clinic manager, requests what he needs; the parent company delivers.

### 7. Board & Governance

Current board: Oren, Dekkel, Ilan Rosenfeld (investor representative).
Notable gap: No physician on the board.

**Proposed:** Establish a steering committee (ועדת היגוי) that includes the CMO, ensuring clinical perspective informs all major company decisions.

## Emotional Undercurrents

- Rami feels unheard and frustrated — believes critical clinical perspectives are being dismissed in favor of operational convenience
- Oren is trying to streamline the organization and reduce duplication while acknowledging Rami's concerns are valid
- Ziv serves as a calming mediator, offering corporate analogies
- Basel quietly observes much of the debate, focused on practical clinic operations
- Meeting ended with Rami visibly stressed, acknowledging he needs to think through his role definition

# Psychiatric Session — Medication Transition Plan 2025-12-02

## Context

Voice memo transcript of a psychiatric consultation session conducted on 2025-12-02 (original in Hebrew). The session addresses a patient's recurring depressive and dissociative episodes and outlines a medication change strategy.

## Patient Presentation

- Patient reports recurring episodes of dissociation and depressive states that come and go
- Episodes are not constant — there were periods when everything was fine, particularly during prior treatment
- Patient describes entering a state where they feel disconnected from their surroundings
- This pattern is not lifelong but has been recurring

## Current Medication Regimen

| Medication | Dose | Frequency | Purpose |
|-----------|------|-----------|---------|
| Sertraline (Lustral/Zoloft) | 50mg | Daily | Antidepressant (SSRI) |
| Mondormin | As needed | PRN | Sleep aid |
| Clonex (Clonazepam) | As needed | PRN | Anxiolytic/calming |

- Patient has been on Sertraline for years at 50mg
- Previously tried Cipralex (Escitalopram) — same SSRI family
- Both medications belong to the same drug class (SSRIs), indicating the body has developed tolerance to this mechanism

## Clinical Decision — Switch to Venlafaxine (Effexor)

**Rationale:** The patient has been on SSRIs (Sertraline and previously Cipralex) for an extended period with diminishing therapeutic response. Both medications work exclusively on serotonin. Research shows that when patients stop responding to serotonin-only medications, switching to a drug with a different mechanism of action is effective. Venlafaxine works on both serotonin and noradrenaline (SNRI), offering a "refreshed" pharmacological approach.

**Potential side effects of Venlafaxine:**
- Dry mouth and dry eyes (typically early in treatment)
- Possible blood pressure elevation (usually not clinically significant at low doses)
- Side effect profile differs from SSRI side effects

**Blood pressure monitoring required** — family doctor must track blood pressure during transition.

## Medication Transition Protocol

### Phase 1: Sertraline Taper
| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| Week 1 | Reduce Sertraline to 100mg |
| Week 2 | Reduce Sertraline to 50mg |
| Week 3 | Discontinue Sertraline — allow washout period |

### Phase 2: Venlafaxine Initiation
| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| Week 4 | Start Venlafaxine 37.5mg |
| Week 5+ | Increase to Venlafaxine 75mg |

### Adjunct Medications (Unchanged)
- Mondormin — continue as needed for sleep
- Clonex (Clonazepam) — continue as needed for calming

## Implementation Instructions

1. **Do not change any medication before seeing the family doctor**
2. Psychiatrist will write a referral letter with the full transition plan
3. Family doctor must prescribe Venlafaxine (psychiatrist is not issuing the prescription directly)
4. Family doctor must set up blood pressure monitoring during and after the transition
5. Next psychiatrist appointment is in approximately 2 months
6. The referral letter can be used at the family doctor's clinic same day

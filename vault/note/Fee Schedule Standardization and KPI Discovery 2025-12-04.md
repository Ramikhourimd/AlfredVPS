## Fee Schedule Tier Calculations

### Three-Tier Pricing Structure (Shlavim)

Rami and Basel worked through a structured compensation model for psychiatrist sessions across three tiers, using a 0.75 multiplier (kaf) for follow-up rate derivation:

**Tier 1 (Shlav 1) — Specialist Intake:**
- Intake rate: 225 NIS per 45-minute session (converted to 60-min rate)
- Follow-up (ma'akav) rate with 0.75 multiplier: 148.50 NIS per 30-minute session
- This tier covers specialists ("mutamhim") in psychiatry

**Tier 2 (Shlav 2):**
- Intake rate: Combined Shlav 1 + Shlav 2 intake
- Follow-up rate: 173.25 NIS (with kaf multiplier)
- Total intake per session: 262.50 NIS

**Tier 3 (Shlav 3) — Senior/Expert:**
- Rate: 400 NIS intake, 200 NIS additional
- This maps to current rates for senior psychiatrists like Yael (400+200=600) and Ahmed

**Standardized Follow-up Rate:**
- Decision: All psychiatrists get 250 NIS for standard follow-up sessions ("istradik")
- Children sessions ("yeladim"): Also standardized at 250 NIS follow-up

## Clinical KPI Framework Discovery

Rami discovered a structured KPI framework from a business course resource, relevant to clinic operations:

### Identified KPIs

**Clinical Quality:**
- Retention rate — percentage of patients continuing treatment
- Recovery rate — treatment outcome measurement
- Case size — average treatment duration/sessions
- ER visit rate — emergency room utilization (lower = better clinical outcomes)

**Operational Efficiency:**
- Supervisor to clinician ratio (hours)
- Clinician notes audited — percentage of session notes reviewed
- Time to treatment — appointment wait time
- Time for open roles — recruitment speed

**Stakeholder:**
- Third-party satisfaction — recommendation metrics from HMOs (Maccabi, Clalit)
- Patient satisfaction survey scores
- Treatment time metrics

### Strategic Significance
- Rami noted these KPIs form a causal chain: clinical excellence drives retention, which drives HMO satisfaction, which drives contract renewals and growth
- The "battle" (ma'araka) he described previously — auditing clinician notes — would directly improve the "clinician notes audited" KPI to near 100%
- These KPIs align with the protocol framework being developed for board presentation

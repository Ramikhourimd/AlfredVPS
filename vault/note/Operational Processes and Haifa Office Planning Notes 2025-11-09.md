## 1. Bonus Structure Reform

The team discussed the tension between per-transaction (Makhirot) bonuses and automatic, continuous process-based incentives.

**Key Points:**
- Current per-transaction bonuses (Makhirot) incentivize individual deal closures but create tunnel vision
- When an employee receives a bonus per private patient sale, their entire focus shifts to sales rather than holistic patient care
- Oren argued that bonuses are a tool for directing employee attention: whatever you bonus, that is where the employee will look
- The goal is to transition from per-transaction bonuses to automatic processes where good outcomes happen without individual deal hunting
- This aligns with the existing [[decision/Phase Out Makhirot Incentives After December 2025]]

**Insight:** Bonus structures are not just compensation tools; they are attention-direction mechanisms. Per-transaction bonuses can inadvertently narrow employee focus away from broader operational excellence.

## 2. Maccabi Patient Scheduling Crisis

A critical operational issue was identified regarding Maccabi patient file management.

**Problem:**
- When a Maccabi patient sees a psychiatrist, the psychiatrist should redirect the patient to a case manager for ongoing care scheduling
- Some psychiatrists fail to make this referral
- Without the case manager referral, the patient file is closed by the system
- The patient then falls through the cracks and loses continuity of care

**Impact:** Patients are losing access to ongoing treatment because of a workflow gap between psychiatrist appointments and case manager scheduling.

**Required Action:** Urgent investigation needed. See [[task/Investigate Maccabi Patient File Closure Issue With Psychiatrist Scheduling]].

## 9. Haifa Office Space Planning

- Parking availability

- Financial implications need evaluation

**Arabic Section:**

# Pre-Purchase Vehicle Inspection Report 2025-11-03

## Context

Voice memo recording of a pre-purchase vehicle inspection conducted on a diesel 4x4 vehicle with 153,000 km on the odometer. Rami was present as the prospective buyer along with an associate who brought the vehicle. A professional inspector examined the vehicle and reported findings.

## Vehicle Details

- **Fuel type:** Diesel
- **Drivetrain:** Permanent 4x4 (no warranty on 4WD system)
- **Odometer:** 153,000 km

## Inspection Findings

### Engine — Flagged for Further Investigation
- Excessive oil found covering the engine — oil pooled on the underside of the vehicle
- Inspector flagged the engine as "blowing" — excessive blow-by smoke when pulling the dipstick
- The associate explained the oil was caused by the dipstick being improperly reseated after a previous oil check (the dipstick has an unusually shaped opening, and the air cap was left off, causing oil spray)
- Inspector could not read the engine number due to oil coating
- Signs of engine hood disassembly/reassembly — could indicate engine replacement or front collision repair
- **Recommendation:** Must be checked at a proper garage before purchase. If the garage certifies it as sound, they take responsibility — the inspector does not.

### Front End — Repaired
- Front bumper was repaired/replaced (evidence of bodywork)
- Engine hood shows signs of prior removal and reinstallation
- Multiple possible explanations: front collision repair with bumper/hood replacement, or hood removed to access/replace engine

### Transmission — OK
- Tested during road driving
- Shifts properly, no faults detected
- No transmission warning lights or issues

### Brakes — Minor Issue
- Vibration felt through the steering wheel during braking
- May need brake disc resurfacing or replacement

### Windshield — Minor
- Inspector reported a small crack in the front windshield
- Disputed: the associate and Rami identified it as a surface scratch, not a structural crack
- Inspector maintained that scratches can develop into cracks

### Tires
- Rear tires dated 2021 with some sidewall drying
- Replacement recommended but safe for approximately one more year of use
- Front tires: condition not specifically flagged

### Suspension
- All four shock absorbers checked and found to be in good condition

### Mirrors
- Left side mirror only adjusts horizontally (left-right) — vertical adjustment (up-down) is not functioning

### Service History
- Recommended to verify last service date and continue regular maintenance schedule
- Recommended to monitor oil and water levels between services given the mileage

## Post-Inspection Discussion

After the inspection, Rami expressed frustration privately about the inspector appearing to exaggerate some findings:
- The windshield "crack" appeared to be a minor scratch upon closer examination
- The engine blow-by, while flagged, is expected behavior for diesel engines — the inspector may have overstated the severity
- The associate (who handles vehicle maintenance, not sales) was confident the engine is sound

The group decided to go speak with the sellers/dealership together to address the engine concern and get clarity.

## Key Recommendations

1. **Engine check at a certified garage** — essential before purchase decision
2. **Oil and water monitoring** — check between services given 153,000 km mileage
3. **Brake disc service** — resurface or replace to eliminate braking vibration
4. **Rear tire replacement** — within the next year
5. **Left mirror repair** — vertical adjustment mechanism needs fixing

# Clinic Patient Intake Workflow and Fee Schedule Discussion 2025-12-04

## Context

Voice memo recorded at the Telia'z clinic office on 2025-12-04. Rami and Basel discuss the clinic's patient intake workflow, review staff compensation rates and fee schedule discrepancies, and address operational issues with Li Yamin's handling of employment agreements.

## Patient Intake Workflow (Current Process)

The discussion maps out the clinic's patient intake funnel with the following steps:

1. **Application** — Patient referral arrives (e.g. via Clalit HMO or direct)
2. **First Contact (Rana)** — Rana Khouri conducts the initial screening interview ("riyon mitkadmi"), assessing suitability and gathering basic information
3. **Professional Interview (Shira / Uri / Waseem)** — A clinical professional conducts a professional screening interview. Shira or Uri handles standard cases; Waseem handles couples
4. **Assignment / Scheduling** — Patient is assigned to a psychiatrist based on availability and the applicable fee schedule

Rami notes that under the old scheme he used to conduct both the initial and clinical interviews. Under the current arrangement, if there is no availability on the schedule, Rami can still step in to handle the initial interview himself as an administrative function (assessing applicant suitability, not clinical).

## Staff Compensation Rates Reviewed

Several compensation rates were discussed and discrepancies identified:

- **Kamel** — Paid 350 NIS for 2-hour sessions (per-hour rate). Confirmed as current rate
- **Ahmed Masalha** — Paid 600 NIS per hour, but specifically for night shift coverage. Need to verify and document this rate properly
- **Rate transition issue** — Some staff had a rate of 350 from 2024, but a new rate from 2024 onwards counts differently at 600. Possible missing page in the fee schedule documentation
- **System rates (health fund)** — Current system rate is 150 per session with 177 total. In the Neumit system, an intake is billed at 75 NIS with "cup" (supplement) of 37. Base rate was set at 120 in the appointment system with a note about potential monthly bonuses

## Fee Schedule Issues

A central problem emerged: **fee schedules may be incomplete or inconsistent**.

- Before forwarding fee schedules to Li Yamin, Basel/Rami need to first **ask Shira** whether she has fee schedule documents that were prepared but never sent out, or were sent to staff but never returned signed
- Shira was supposed to handle all fee schedule matters, but there is uncertainty about whether documents reached their recipients
- Some fee schedules dating back more than 10 years are still the operational basis, creating risk of outdated terms

## Li Yamin Performance Concerns

A significant portion of the conversation addresses concerns about Li Yamin's effectiveness:

- Li was creating additional work for Rami, Shira, and Rana rather than reducing it — the opposite of her role's intent
- When agreements were sent to staff through Li and came back unsigned, they returned to Rami's desk rather than being resolved by Li directly
- Li was reportedly reluctant to directly engage with staff to resolve agreement issues, instead escalating complaints to Rami
- Rami decided to "issue" (distance) from Li's process because her involvement made things harder, not easier
- Under the previous arrangement (without Li's intermediation), Shira would directly inform Rami about unsigned agreements, and Rami would handle them — this was described as "much easier"
- The core issue: Li's role should have been to proactively track and resolve agreement logistics, but instead she became a bottleneck that added overhead

## Key Takeaway

The intake workflow itself is clear and functional. The main operational problems are:
1. Fee schedule documentation gaps — need Shira to confirm what exists
2. Li Yamin's intermediation role creating friction rather than reducing it in agreement handling
3. Need to consolidate and standardize all fee schedules into a single authoritative document

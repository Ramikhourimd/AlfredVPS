# New Employee Onboarding Structure and Technology Requirements 2026-02-12

## Context

This is a brief 1:1 clinical management meeting between Rami Khouri and Elias Jahjah (HM-RAMI-ELIAS classification, now discontinued). The discussion focused on establishing a structured onboarding process for new clinic employees, addressing both cadence and content segmentation.

## Onboarding Cadence

Rami proposed a flexible onboarding cadence of once per month or once every two months, depending on the volume of new hires in a given period. Some months may require rapid onboarding when replacements are needed urgently, while others may have no new hires at all. Elias confirmed that from his perspective, running one or two onboarding sessions per month is feasible.

If the cadence is set to bi-weekly (every two weeks), the different onboarding tracks can be sequenced across sessions rather than delivered all at once.

## Three-Track Onboarding Model

Rami outlined a proposal to split onboarding into three distinct tracks:

1. **Operational track** — Clinical operational procedures, workflows, and systems. Can start without a formal session if needed.
2. **Administrative track** — HR, payroll, agreements, and organizational procedures. Can also start independently.
3. **Technology track** — Access to Teams and HoViD (the clinic's clinical platform). This is the **mandatory day-one requirement** — without technology access, the new employee cannot function.

The key insight is that operational and administrative onboarding can be deferred or done informally, but technology onboarding is a hard blocker. A new employee who cannot log into Teams or access HoViD cannot begin clinical work.

## Delivery Responsibility

Rami suggested that either Stav (Zamir) or Shira should handle the combined operational and technology onboarding walkthrough. This would include walking the new employee through HoViD access, which is the most critical system.

## Key Takeaway

Technology access (Teams + HoViD) is the only true "must-have" for day-one onboarding. The operational and clinical components are important but can be delivered incrementally over the first weeks.

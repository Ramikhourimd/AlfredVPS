# Clinic Protocol Framework and Strategic Positioning Brainstorm 2025-12-04

## Context

Rami Khouri and Basel Kanaaneh held an in-person brainstorming session to define the strategic and operational protocol framework for Telia'z Clinic Israel. The goal was twofold: (1) define what the clinic needs to be operationally stable ("yetsivi") in 6 months, and (2) build a compelling strategic argument for leadership to adopt the proposed changes rather than continuing with the current approach focused primarily on patient acquisition metrics.

This session builds on prior operations discussions from 2025-12-02 and feeds directly into the upcoming board presentation and 2026 annual plan.

## Hypergrowth Model Analysis

Rami presented a comparison of two hypergrowth models. The core question is how the clinic scales — not just by hiring more psychiatrists, but by measuring and improving operational efficiency. Key challenge identified: measuring meaningful KPIs beyond simple volume metrics (new patients, Facebook campaign performance).

Rami emphasized: "The important things we are not tracking — if I can define them now, the year will be much easier."

## Protocol Framework Design

The session produced a structured protocol framework with two main branches:

### 1. Clinical Protocols (Klini)

**Clinical Outcomes Chain:**
- Better treatment response (tguvat tipul yoter tova)
- Higher remission rates (ahuzi remissia)
- Reduced hospitalization rates (hordat ahuz ishpuzim)
- Reduced ER visits (hordat pniot lemiyun)
- Reduced burden on public health system (hordat aluyot/netel al ma'arekhet habriut)
- Improved HMO agreements (shipur beheskemim)
- Increased revenue (aliya behakhnasot)

**Clinical-Business Value Chain:** Treatment quality improvements lead to cost savings for the public health system, which strengthens negotiating position with HMOs (Maccabi, Clalit), which leads to better contract terms and higher revenue.

### 2. Administrative/Operational Protocols (Administrativi)

**Protocol Categories Identified:**
1. **Complaints Management (Tluonot):**
   - Professional complaints (miktso'iot)
   - Structural/accumulative complaints (bniyot mitstabrот)
   - Service delivery complaints (shilot dlimot)

2. **Communication Improvement (Shipur Tikshоret):**
   - Communication management (nihul tikshоret)
   - Inter-team communication (tikshоret bein tsvatot)
   - Strategic communication improvement (shipur estrategi)
   - Communication with external bodies (gufim) — HMOs, regulators

3. **Client Satisfaction (Sviut Ratson):**
   - Patient satisfaction (sviut ratson mtupалim)
   - Time-to-resolution (zmаn)
   - Professional satisfaction scores (miktso'iot)
   - Employee satisfaction as attractiveness metric

4. **Training and Onboarding (Hakhshara veTnikha):**
   - Professional training (hakhshara miktso'it)
   - New employee onboarding processes
   - Employee retention programs (shimur ovdim)

**Training Value Chain:** Better training → more professional staff → higher patient/employee satisfaction → better retention → lower turnover costs → more attractive workplace → reduced hiring expenses.

### 3. Operational Efficiency Outcomes

**The protocol framework leads to:**
- Reduced operational expenses (pahot hotsa'a)
- Reduced legal exposure (pahot hashifa mishpatit)
- Reduced risk of litigation costs
- Better staffing outcomes (more attractive employer)

## Strategic Argument for Leadership

Rami articulated the critical gap: there is a disconnect between what he and Basel know needs to happen and what the company leadership has decided to prioritize. The current leadership focus is on acquisition metrics (new patients, Facebook campaigns, monthly revenue), while the operational fundamentals (protocols, training, quality metrics) are not being tracked.

Rami's assessment: "If I cannot explain to them why they need to change their current agenda, they will continue with their agenda. And every Sunday meeting will be frustrating because they are reviewing metrics that don't capture what matters."

The solution: build a documented evidence chain from operational protocols through clinical outcomes to financial impact, then present this to convince leadership to adopt the new operational priorities.

## CMO and Innovation Role Connection

Brief discussion about the VP Medical and Innovation (Smankal Refu'i veHadshаnut) position — connecting the protocol framework work to the broader strategic vision for the clinic.

## Key Insight

The brainstorming revealed that the real challenge is not technical (defining protocols) but political (convincing leadership). The protocol framework must be presented not as operational housekeeping but as a direct driver of financial outcomes — the language leadership understands.

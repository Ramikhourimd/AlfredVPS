# Rami Financial and Expense Planning Discussion 2025-09-23

## Context

In-person Arabic conversation recorded on September 23, 2025, between Rami Khouri and his accountant/financial advisor. Multiple speakers present in a casual setting (coffee offered at start). The discussion covers personal financial structuring related to Telia'z compensation, clinical practice income, and UK business travel expense reimbursement.

## Telia'z Income and Stock Options

- Rami discussed financial arrangements with Telia'z, specifically how income from the company should be structured
- There was a plan to create an additional agreement (heskem) allowing income to flow from Telia'z, but this may not be feasible immediately — the company wants to see how things go first
- Discussion about stock options (optsiyot) as a future consideration
- **Tax concern:** If money is withdrawn from the keren hishtalmut (education fund) or converted to cash, 47% tax applies
- The accountant advised keeping money in the keren hishtalmut rather than withdrawing it
- If Rami opens a separate osek murshe (licensed business), he could route certain income through it, but this has complications if it's the only income source for the company

## Keren Hishtalmut Strategy

- Approximately 20,000 NIS available in the keren hishtalmut, of which ~15,000 NIS is accessible
- Options discussed:
  1. **Keep in keren hishtalmut** (accountant's recommendation) — avoids tax, preserves as savings
  2. **Open osek murshe** and route funds through it — creates a separate business entity
  3. **Transfer to pensions/savings (kranot/amana)** — possible but creates visibility issues if it's the sole income
- Accountant favored keeping money in the keren hishtalmut for tax efficiency

## Clinical Practice Income

- Rami receives a bituh (license) for clinical private practice from his hospital/unit
- Initial allocation: 15 hours/month for 8,000 NIS
- Recent month: 21 hours, still around 8,000 NIS — worked approximately 1.5 days
- Standard schedule: 8 hours per week when available (no leave days, no holidays)
- Expected steady income: 18,000-20,000 NIS/month at current rate
- Combined with company output (osek), this creates a second income stream of roughly 3,000-4,000 NIS additional (from company deductions/benefits)

Rami traveled to the UK for Telia'z business (UK expansion activities in Leeds):

**Route:** Israel → Frankfurt → London → Leeds → Frankfurt → Israel

**Expenses discussed:**
- **KLM flights:** 3,250 NIS total (two segments: Israel-Frankfurt and Frankfurt-Leeds area, plus return)
- **Car rental:** Two charges — 2,000 EUR and 400 EUR
- **Hotels:** At least one hotel expense identified (amount not specified in transcript)
- **Amazon purchases:** Scooter-related items identified on credit card

**Expense submission process:**
- Accountant requested Rami send receipts (kabbalot) for all UK travel expenses
- A tofes (expense form) needs to be filled — similar to the Frankfurt trip form previously submitted
- Accountant needs: conference/trip dates (8th-18th September), itemized expenses, receipts
- Company should ideally pay directly from company account, not personal cards
- Expenses from personal cards can still be submitted but need proper documentation
- Older expenses (up to 3 years) can be submitted but only one-third value is recoverable

## Technology Expenses

## Key Takeaway

The accountant's primary advice was conservative: keep keren hishtalmut funds intact, submit all business travel expenses properly through company forms, and avoid unnecessary tax exposure from premature withdrawals.

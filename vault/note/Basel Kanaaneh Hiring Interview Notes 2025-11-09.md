# Basel Kanaaneh Hiring Interview Notes 2025-11-09

## Interview Setting
- **Date**: November 9, 2025
- **Interviewers**: Rami Khouri (Medical Director & Innovation Head, Telia'z) and Dekkel Taliaz (Founder & Chief Scientist, Telia'z)
- **Candidate**: Basel Kanaaneh, age 30
- **Position**: General Manager, Telia'z Clinic Division
- **Source language**: Hebrew (transcribed from voice recording)

---

## 1. Early Career (Pre-Clalit)

Basel has 8-9 years of experience in the medical field. He began his career at **Bikur Rofeh** (a home doctor visit company, similar to Terem) working as a medical secretary while studying for his first degree in Haifa and the Krayot area. He advanced to **Deputy Manager of Clinics** overseeing Kiryat Shmona, Krayot, and Haifa regions.

## 2. Clalit Health Services (2017–2025)

### 2.1 Clinic Establishment in Shefa-Amr

Basel joined Clalit in 2017 at the onset of Israel's mental health reform. He was tasked with establishing a **child and youth mental health clinic in Shefa-Amr** from scratch:

- Started with nothing — an empty building with only a cleaning staff member
- Handled everything: branding, room allocation, staff recruitment
- Within one month, recruited a psychiatrist (Dr. Olah) as Medical Director
- She in turn recruited the lead psychologist and lead social worker
- Built the full administrative team: secretaries, facilities, marketing

The clinic grew to **25 staff members**.

### 2.2 Expansion to Neurology and Child Development

Within 3-4 months, Basel was asked to also establish a **neurology and child development center** in the same building, creating an integrated center combining both specialties:

- Recruited his sister Ranin (from Macabi) to lead the physiotherapy team
- Combined center total: **52 staff members** across both clinics
- **Clinical staff breakdown**: 8 psychiatrists, 1 neurologist, 6 psychologists, 4 social workers, plus physiotherapists and admin

### 2.3 Eating Disorders Clinic

Approximately 3 years before the interview (~2022), Basel established an additional **eating disorders clinic** under the same umbrella.

### 2.4 Patient Volume and Service Metrics

- **3,800 maintained patients** in the clinic system
- **~2 new patient referrals per day** on average
- This is considered a large clinic by mental health standards
- Service accessibility targets: general patients within 3 months, urgent cases within 3 weeks
- Performance tracked against **80 strategic KPIs** covering phone response, satisfaction surveys, wait times, and referral compliance

### 2.5 Administrative Authority

Basel has authority over:
- Patient intake decisions (who enters the clinic)
- Referral management (Tofes 17 for external hospital referrals — Rambam, etc.)
- Limited external referral quota (both quantity and financial caps)
- Staff scheduling, recruitment, onboarding
- Annual work plans and KPI tracking
- Service quality and customer retention

The administrative manager and medical director work in partnership — medical director handles clinical guidance, training, and case discussions; administrative manager handles operations, budgets, protocols, and work plans.

### 2.6 Budget Management

Basel managed budgets at **district level** for all **13 mental health clinics** in the Haifa and Western Galilee district:

- Annual equipment procurement, therapeutic materials, games
- New clinic establishment funding
- Staff salary budgets with specific caps per physician
- Budget compliance rewarded with additional funding the following year (excellence bonus)
- Innovation: convinced two psychiatrists to switch from salaried to independent contractor status — a first for Clalit in that district — optimizing budget utilization without sacrificing service hours

**Budget philosophy**: Meeting budget targets leads to "excellence" recognition and additional funding the next year, enabling further expansion (new clinics, more staff). Budget discipline is therefore a growth strategy, not just cost control.

### 2.7 Integrated Care Model

Basel's key insight: mental health and child development are deeply interconnected. Most patients who arrive at mental health clinics started their journey in child development. By integrating both under one roof, the district optimized budget utilization and eliminated the surplus budget problem that plagued other districts (who couldn't spend their mental health allocation due to staffing shortages).

### 2.8 Promotion: Field Activity Manager (National Level)

Less than one month before the interview, Basel was promoted to **Field Activity Manager** at Clalit — a **national-level role** covering:
- All service centers nationwide
- Customer retention and acquisition
- Marketing (including marketing to the Arab community)
- Call center operations (Star 2700 and other hotlines)
- Field advisor training across clinics
- Public complaints and satisfaction management
- Hospital-to-community coordination

## 3. Regulatory and Compliance Experience

Through both Clalit and his private venture, Basel worked extensively with:
- **Ministry of Health** — service standards, KPI compliance
- **Ministry of Welfare** — social services coordination
- **Ministry of Defense** — service agreements for defense personnel
- **Multiple health funds** — Clalit, Leumit, Meuhedet agreements
- **Charity organizations** — Keren Yedidut and others

## 4. Private Clinic Venture

Basel established a private clinic company operating two clinics over approximately two years, serving:
- Ministry of Defense patients
- Clalit, Leumit, and Meuhedet health fund patients
- NGO referrals

**Current status**:
- Sold 50% to **Ali Awad** (owner of **Shams Group**, a national healthcare consulting firm)
- Partner **Dr. Wasim Reli** serves as Medical Director
- Basel transferred operational management to Wasim due to conflict of interest with his senior Clalit role
- Basel remains as shareholder/silent partner
- The company has a legal advisor, administrative manager, and medical director operating independently

## 5. Research Coordination

Basel coordinated a **clinical research center** in partnership with **Erasmus MC Rotterdam** (Netherlands):
- **Focus**: Schizophrenia and autism in families with high incidence of both conditions
- **Origin**: An Erasmus MC researcher visited the Shefa-Amr clinic, gave a lecture, and the collaboration developed from there
- **Basel's role**: Recruited Israeli hospitals and psychiatric departments to participate
- **Participating institutions**: Rambam, Nahariya, Carmel, Nazareth, Soroka (Beer Sheva), and others
- **Noteworthy**: Basel successfully recruited clinical staff to participate in unpaid research — demonstrating his persuasive recruitment ability

## 6. Motivation to Join Telia'z

Basel articulated clear frustration with Clalit's organizational inertia:
- "I can fly away for six months and come back to find the same thing. To change something takes 2-3 years."
- "We're like soldiers standing guard — even as a district manager, you can't really enter the battle and fight for the results you want."
- Clalit is "very old and less [innovative]" despite being Israel's largest employer after the IDF (55,000 employees)

**What attracted him to Telia'z**:
- Innovation and AI in mental health
- Building something from the ground up (which he has done before)
- Being part of the future of psychiatry rather than maintaining the status quo
- The startup mentality vs. corporate bureaucracy

**His vision**: AI and innovation will transform healthcare (psychiatry included) just as fintech transformed banking. He wants to be there "from the beginning."

## 7. Commitment and Compensation

- Basel explicitly stated he does **not believe in part-time or fractional roles** — he wants to go "all in"
- He sees this as "laying a foothold, learning the market, preparing strategy and business plan, then going in strong"
- Willing to leave Clalit entirely for this role
- Salary expectations were discussed but not finalized — Basel noted needing to understand the full scope before committing to a number
- **Post-interview**: Rami and Dekkel discussed structuring compensation with options/bonuses tied to KPI achievement, in addition to base salary

## 8. Key Strengths Demonstrated

1. **Clinic builder**: Proven track record of establishing clinics from zero to operational (3 separate clinics)
2. **Recruiter**: Ability to attract and retain clinical talent (52-person team; research recruitment across hospitals)
3. **Budget strategist**: District-level budget management across 13 clinics; innovative cost optimization
4. **Regulatory navigator**: Experience with multiple government ministries and health fund agreements
5. **Entrepreneurial**: Built and ran a private clinic company alongside his Clalit career
6. **Multi-stakeholder manager**: Manages relationships with clinicians, administrative staff, government bodies, external organizations, and patients
7. **Vision-aligned**: Deeply believes in AI/innovation disrupting traditional healthcare delivery

## 9. Post-Interview Assessment

The interviewers expressed a positive impression. Dekkel noted they had met "very interesting people today" and Basel was "one of them." They committed to not making the process lengthy. Basel was subsequently hired as General Manager of the Telia'z Clinic division.

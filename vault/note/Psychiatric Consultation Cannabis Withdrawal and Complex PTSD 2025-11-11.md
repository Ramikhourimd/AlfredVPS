# Psychiatric Consultation: Cannabis Withdrawal and Complex PTSD — 2025-11-11

## Context

Clinical psychiatric consultation conducted by Rami Khouri at Telia'z Clinic Israel with an unnamed female patient. The patient is a sexologist and holistic therapist who self-discontinued heavy medical cannabis use and is experiencing severe withdrawal compounded by Complex PTSD symptom resurgence.

## Patient Background

- **Profession:** Sexologist, lecturer, holistic therapist
- **Personal history:** Left the ultra-Orthodox (Haredi) community approximately 6 years ago with 6 children, 2 with special needs
- **Trauma background:** 16 years in an abusive relationship involving financial, emotional, sexual, and relational violence within a deeply insular religious community. Intergenerational trauma — ex-husband is himself severely post-traumatic and a treatment refuser
- **Psychiatric diagnoses:** Complex PTSD, ADHD
- **Cannabis history:** Heavy daily user (5-6 joints/day) for at least 4 years, self-medicated without official license. Quit abruptly ("cold turkey") approximately 5 weeks prior

## Presenting Complaints

1. **PTSD symptom resurgence:** All previously suppressed traumatic memories returning — 16 years of abuse, childhood-like helplessness, vivid recall of incidents previously forgotten
2. **Severe insomnia:** Nightmares, inability to sleep without weighted blanket (purchased specifically for ADHD)
3. **Physical symptoms:** Hand tremors, elevated resting heart rate (103 BPM at GP visit), restless legs, hyperarousal of nervous system
4. **Functional impairment:** Reduced clinical caseload from 6-8 patients/day down to 2. Cannot drive (suspended driving lessons). Cognitive productivity estimated at 30% of baseline
5. **Emotional dysregulation:** Extreme irritability, touch aversion ("feeling like a hedgehog"), sensory overload in public spaces
6. **Desire for disability recognition:** Wants to file National Insurance (Bituach Leumi) disability claim due to functional impairment affecting self-employed income

## Current Medication Regimen

| Medication | Dose | Purpose | Status |
|-----------|------|---------|--------|
| Wellbutrin (Bupropion) | 150 mg daily | ADHD — stabilizes attention without peaks/troughs of stimulants | Continue — patient reports excellent results |
| Ritalin (Methylphenidate) | 20 mg as needed | ADHD — used on high-demand days | GP advised stopping temporarily due to elevated heart rate; psychiatrist clarified it is not absolutely contraindicated but should be used judiciously |
| Alprazolam (Xanax) | 0.5 mg as needed | Anxiety/withdrawal | GP-prescribed; patient using sparingly to avoid substituting one dependency for another |
| Lorazepam (Lorivan) | As needed | Anxiety | Ineffective for this patient |
| Promethazine (Phenergan) | As needed | Sedation/sleep | Minimal benefit |

## Clinical Decisions Made

1. **Xanax XR prescribed** instead of regular alprazolam — extended release formulation provides more sustained effect with lower addiction potential. Time-limited: 3 months of prescriptions
2. **Ritalin not contraindicated** — psychiatrist clarified that despite elevated heart rate, Ritalin is not absolutely contraindicated. Patient should self-monitor and avoid during peak stress episodes
3. **Trauma-focused therapy referral** — psychiatrist recommended trauma-focused psychotherapy (not psychodynamic). Discussed distinction between trauma-focused therapy and supportive therapy for Complex PTSD
4. **Ongoing psychiatric follow-up** offered even without medication changes — monitoring relationship established
5. **Bituach Leumi letter** — psychiatrist agreed to write disability documentation letter

## Key Clinical Observations

### Cannabis and PTSD Interaction
The patient articulated a critical insight: cannabis did not treat her PTSD — it "closed" the symptoms without processing them. Upon cessation, all suppressed content resurfaced simultaneously. She described it as "a burn victim whose bandages are removed and replaced with a wool sweater."

### Cannabis and Wellbutrin Conflict
Previous psychiatrist explained that cannabis counteracted the stabilizing effects of Wellbutrin — one medication stabilizes attention/mood while the other undermines it. This was a key factor in her decision to quit.

### Dopamine Recovery Timeline
Patient cited the 30-day timeline for natural dopamine production to resume after cannabis cessation. Physical withdrawal has passed but psychological/emotional withdrawal (PTSD resurgence) continues.

### Complex PTSD vs Simple PTSD
Discussion clarified that Complex PTSD (from prolonged relational trauma) requires different therapeutic approaches than single-event PTSD. Many clinicians do not yet recognize C-PTSD as it is relatively new in formal diagnostic frameworks.

### Daughter's Psychiatric Care
Patient's daughter was recently seen at Rambam Medical Center ER for psychiatric evaluation and has been referred to their psychological treatment program. May receive ongoing psychiatric monitoring there.

## Patient's Metaphor

> "Like a person covered in burns, wrapped in bandages in a hospital. One day they remove all the bandages and put a wool sweater on the raw skin. And you go walk around with that, and work with that, and smile at everyone."

This powerfully captures the experience of cannabis withdrawal removing the protective layer that masked unprocessed trauma.

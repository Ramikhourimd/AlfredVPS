# Clinic Protocol Framework and Scaling Strategy 2025-12-04

## Context

Rami and Basel held a brainstorming session to map out what the clinic needs to be "efficient" (yatsiv) within the next 6 months. The critical insight driving this session: there is a **gap between what Rami and Basel know needs to happen** versus **what the company leadership has decided to do**. If Rami cannot articulate and package this knowledge compellingly, the company will continue operating on its current priorities — and every Sunday management meeting will be frustrating because leadership focuses on superficial metrics (new patients, Facebook campaign results, monthly revenue) rather than operational fundamentals.

## Starting Point: Feature Review

The session began with a plan to review all features from the HotSaaS platform, categorizing them into:
- **Clinical** features
- **Administrative** features

However, they quickly pivoted to a more fundamental question: instead of reviewing features, define what the clinic needs to be operationally stable and efficient, then map features to those needs.

## Hypergrowth Model and KPIs

Rami presented a hypergrowth model with two axes:
- **X-axis:** Staffing scale (psychiatrists, hours worked)
- **Y-axis:** Efficiency/output

Key challenge identified: measuring efficiency is difficult. For example, measuring how many psychiatrists are in the "hiring funnel" vs measuring their output per hour. The KPIs need to capture what matters for scaling, not just what is easy to count.

## Protocol Framework

### Why Protocols Are Needed

Basel articulated the core purpose: **protocols define how staff should handle every type of incoming situation.** When a new case arrives, staff currently do not have a clear framework for what to include, what to exclude, what steps to follow. A protocol-driven approach means every staff member knows exactly what to do from first contact through to outcome.

### Two Protocol Categories

**1. Clinical Protocols (בחט מקצועיות)**
- Complaints handling — professional/clinical complaints
- Building cumulative structures (בניות מצטבת) — structural staff development
- Referral/escalation questions (שילות דלימות)
- Communication improvement (שיפור תקשורת)
- Inter-team communication management (ייעול תקשורת בין צוותית)

**2. Administrative/Business Protocols (בחט עסקי)**
- Integration between clinical and administrative operations
- Strategic communication improvement with external bodies (HMOs: Maccabi, Clalit)
- Service terms and conditions management
- Hiring and onboarding framework

### Training and Onboarding (הכשרה ותניכה)

Training leads to:
- More professional staff (מקצועיות)
- Higher patient satisfaction (שביעות רצון)
- Reduced patient attrition (עזיבת מטופלים)
- Staff retention (שימור עובדים)
- Lower expenditure on hiring and training replacements

### Protocol Outcomes Chain

The outcomes form a causal chain:

**Protocols** → Patient satisfaction + Professionalism + Reduced time → Being more attractive to workers → Team efficiency

**Efficiency** produces:
- Surplus capacity (סרו בוט) — more output per time unit
- Revenue from training and guidance
- Administrative hours savings
- Reduced need for external hires

## Clinical-to-Business Outcomes Chain

This is the key argument for leadership:

1. **Better treatment response** (תגובת טיפול) → Better remission rates
2. **Reduced relapse** (הורדה של אחוזי חזרה/רמיסיה)
3. **Reduced hospitalizations** (הורדת אחוז אישפוזים)
4. **Reduced ER visits** (הורדת פניות למיון)
5. **Lower costs/burden on public health system** (שיפור בעלויות הנטל מערכת בריאות)
6. **Better HMO agreements** (שיפור בהסכמים) — proof of value leads to better contract terms
7. **Proof of concept** (הוכחת אנק) — demonstrating reduced costs to the public system

## CMO and Innovation Role

The discussion touched on the **VP Medical and Innovation** (סמנכ"ל רפואי וחדשנות) role — Rami's target position. This role needs to be connected to the broader organizational vision, not just day-to-day clinic operations. The protocol framework and outcomes chain is part of building the case for why this role matters.

## Strategic Communication Problem

**The critical gap:** Rami and Basel have deep operational knowledge about what needs to change (protocols, training, efficiency metrics). But the company leadership (Dekkel, the board) is focused on different priorities. Rami identified that he would need to keep having frustrating alignment meetings every month unless he can package this knowledge convincingly **now**.

**Proposed approach:**
1. Capture everything Rami and Basel know (this brainstorming session)
2. Structure it into a compelling framework
3. Present it to leadership to shift their priorities
4. If successful, the rest of 2026 operations become much easier

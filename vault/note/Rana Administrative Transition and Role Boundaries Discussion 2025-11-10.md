# Rana Administrative Transition and Role Boundaries Discussion 2025-11-10

## Context

Rami met with Shira in person at the clinic to discuss the practical implementation of Rana's role transition from chief secretary to administrative/HR manager. This conversation directly implements [[decision/Expand Rana Role to HR and Office Management]] and addresses operational boundary-setting between the clinical and administrative tracks of [[project/Telia'z Clinic Israel]].

## Key Points

### Handover Scope — What Moves to Rana

Rami outlined several responsibility areas that should transfer from Shira to Rana:

1. **Employee onboarding process** — Rana should own the intake flow for new hires, including coordination with Li Yamin on contracts and communication protocols. Shira can cover temporarily but Rana needs to centralize this.
2. **Receipts and financial reports** — Rami initially suggested Rana handle these, but acknowledged she may not be ready. Left to Shira's judgment on timing.
3. **Administrative staff communication** — All non-clinical staff interactions (leave requests, agreements, signed/unsigned contract follow-ups) should route through Rana, not Shira.
4. **Weekly secretary meetings** — Rana should take over running the weekly secretary team meetings, with Shira stepping back from this coordination role.
5. **HR lifecycle management** — Leave tracking, scheduling, and diary management.

### What Stays With Shira

- All clinical management and clinical communication with staff
- Product development coordination with Shachar
- Clinical flow monitoring and clinical quality
- Ability to request data and reports from the administrative side

### Role Separation Philosophy

Rami articulated a clear vision: the clinic should operate as **two independent functional units** — clinical (under Shira) and administrative (under Rana) — that do not have a hierarchical relationship. Rana should not need to report to Shira for administrative decisions, and Shira should not manage Rana's administrative domain.

Key quote from Rami: "I want to create a separation so that each person can work independently, with less bureaucratic dependency."

### New Secretary Hire Tension

Shira expressed surprise at learning about a new secretary hire that she was not informed about. Rami clarified that in Rana's new role, she has authority to recruit her own team independently. However, Shira felt the information gap was uncomfortable — not because of hierarchy, but because it's a new team member she works alongside. They agreed this was an adjustment-period issue rather than a structural problem.

### Transition Approach

Both agreed the transition should be **gradual**:
- Shira will hand over responsibilities one at a time, waiting for Rana to stabilize on each before adding the next
- Shira noted that current secretarial workload is already at capacity — "there's no room to add new tasks right now because many things I ask for aren't getting done due to insufficient capacity"
- The new clinic manager (Basel, starting December) will eventually oversee Rana and provide accountability that is currently absent

### Shira's Reframing Need

Shira acknowledged she needs to understand her new positioning. She committed to:
1. Have a direct conversation with Rana about mutual expectations
2. Explore how Rana envisions the boundary
3. Hand over the weekly secretary meetings
4. Take "a step back" from administrative coordination

Rami emphasized this should not feel forced — only move forward where Shira agrees it makes sense.

## Implications

- This conversation is a practical implementation step of the broader [[project/Telia'z Organizational Restructuring]]
- The separation into clinical and administrative tracks aligns with the [[assumption/Three Clinic Management Pillars Operate as Parallel Equals]] model
- Basel's arrival in December will provide the missing management layer above both Shira and Rana
- Rana's desire for autonomy (she "keeps asking when she can make decisions independently") suggests readiness for expanded ownership

# Khouri and Shaddad Family Migration History

## Context

This oral history was recorded during a conversation between Rami Khouri and Majdi Shaddad on 2025-12-02. Majdi is a Shaddad family relative from the Rameh branch who has been researching the family tree since approximately 1980. The account traces the Shaddad family's multi-generational migration across the Levant.

## Migration Timeline

### Phase 1: Hauran (c. 1725)
The Shaddad family ("Dar Shaddad") moved to Hauran in southern Syria around 1725. Some accounts suggest the origin may have been Hauran or possibly Zahleh (Lebanon) — the exact starting point is uncertain.

### Phase 2: Hauran to Lebanon
From Hauran, the family migrated to Lebanon. The exact timing is unclear but preceded the major Druze-Christian conflicts.

### Phase 3: Druze-Christian Conflicts (early 1800s)
Conflicts between Druze and Christian communities in Lebanon, beginning around the early 1800s, forced many Christians to migrate southward into Palestine.

### Phase 4: Coastal Settlement and Trade
Christian migrants settled along the Palestinian coast and established themselves in trade. By approximately 1865, there were almost no Christians living in the Galilee villages or inland areas — they were concentrated on the coast.

### Phase 5: Inland Migration to Galilee Villages (c. 1860-1865)
Circumstances (unspecified) forced the coastal Christian communities to move inland to Galilee villages. They settled in:
- **Suata** (السوطه)
- **Rameh** (الرامي) — where the Khouri family became established
- **Eilabun** (إلابون / معلياس)
- **Maghar** (المغار) — church founded 1860-1865
- **Safed** (صفد)
- **Tiberias** (طبريه)

## The Khouri Surname Origin

The name "Khouri" (خوري) derives from "qasis" (قسيس), meaning "priest" in Arabic. A member of the family served as a priest in the village of Rameh. Over time, the children of the priest became known as "Dar Khouri" (the Khouri household), and the name stuck as a family surname. This is a common pattern in Levantine Christian communities where priestly families adopted "Khouri" as a hereditary surname.

## Three Shaddad Family Branches

The Shaddad family split into three cousin branches that settled in different Galilee locations:

1. **Eilabun branch** — Dar Shaddad in Eilabun
2. **Rameh branch** — Dar Shaddad in Rameh (Majdi's branch; where the Khouri name originated)
3. **Nazareth branch** — Dar Shaddad in Nazareth

All three branches consider themselves "awlad al-amm" (paternal cousins).

## Diaspora Connections

Majdi identified two family members who left the region:
- **Yacoub al-Asif** — Connected to a church house ("dar al-kaniseh") in Beirut; possibly served in a religious capacity
- **Boulos** — Currently living in Chile; Majdi has gathered information about his descendants

## The Shahoud Family

Rami asked Majdi about the Shahoud (شحود) family. Majdi recognized the name from a book he encountered during military service but did not know their geographic origin. This remains an open research question.

## Majdi's Genealogy Work

Majdi has been actively researching the family tree since approximately 1980. He:
- Builds and maintains a family tree connecting the branches
- Contacts relatives across villages and diaspora
- Invites family members to visit during holidays to exchange information
- Has consulted written sources (books found during military service)

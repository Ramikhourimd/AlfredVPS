# Personal Assistant Planning Discussion With Family Advisor 2025-11-09

## Context

Voice memo from November 9, 2025. Rami speaks with a close family advisor (speaker 3) about the possibility of bringing [[person/Amani Rohana]] into a personal assistant / organizational support role. The conversation opens with Arabic greetings and a brief joking reference to someone at "Armon clinics in Haifa."

## Key Discussion Points

### Amani as Personal Assistant Candidate

- Rami raises the idea of advancing the "Amani" option — bringing her into his workflow
- The advisor strongly encourages Rami to pursue this, emphasizing: "You need her" (אתה צריך עליה)
- Key advice: Talk to Amani first, set expectations clearly, ensure alignment between them before proceeding
- The advisor stresses the importance of "expectation alignment" (תיאום ציפיות) as the most critical step
- If it works with Amani, this would "close the corner" (סוגר את הפינה) — resolving Rami's need for organizational support while also addressing the additional staffing gap

### Connection to Upcoming Birth

- Rami notes that if Amani is involved in his workflow, it would allow him to continue working during an upcoming birth (לידה)
- Without this support, Rami would need to stop working entirely — "inside the house" (בתוך הבית)
- The advisor suggests a trial period: "Take a week or two to try it out"

### Role Definition and Timeline

- The advisor urges Rami to first define his own role split clearly — how the position divides, time allocation, week-by-week structure
- Start with a self-audit in December or January, then present a clear, ready plan
- The advisor says the target event is in February; Rami wants to act sooner
- Advisor: "From my perspective, as soon as possible. But I haven't sat with her yet."
- The advisor has only mentioned the idea to Amani but has not had a formal discussion yet

### Financial Considerations

- If Rami saves 40,000 NIS (presumably annual savings from role restructuring), this creates budget room
- The discussion references a figure of 13,000 NIS bruto (gross salary) as a reference point
- Rami had previously told the advisor: "I'll take the role, I'll take the budget, and I'll manage it"
- Rami considered taking "at least half" of the budget rather than the full amount, wanting to feel the opportunity while not overstepping
- Rami expresses concern about how this will be perceived — he does not want it to feel like "too much" or like a personal favor
- The advisor notes it should be discussed in terms of employer cost (עלות שכר), since the hire would be a salaried employee
- Reference to 20,000 NIS added to payslip but only yielding approximately 6,000 NIS net — frustrating tax impact
- The 6,000 NIS net is described as going to savings (חיסכונות), not disposable income
- Rami still needs the personal assistant regardless — so there is a real cost that needs to be covered

## Advisor's Approach

The advisor is supportive but cautious:
- Encourages Rami to think carefully ("Think well — how, what, who")
- Trusts Rami's judgment: "You're mature enough, you know what's good for you"
- Wants Rami to present a clear proposal — the advisor can then back it: "I can stand behind it for sure"
- Does not want to create something artificial that just looks like it works
- Emphasizes this should be a genuine working arrangement, not performative

# Clinic Manager KPI Framework and Bonus Structure 2025-03-27

## Context

Management meeting between Alex Taliaz, Rami Khouri, and Oren Taliaz on 2025-03-27 to formalize Shira's expanded role as clinic manager and define measurable KPIs with a bonus compensation structure. This meeting operationalizes the earlier decision to appoint Shira as clinical operations manager.

## Shira's Role Expansion

Shira is formally appointed as clinic manager. Her role remains part-time (half position), working approximately half a day each day except one day off. Key change: responsibilities previously handled by Rami are being redistributed:
- **To Shira:** Primary operational management, KPI ownership, team supervision
- **To Rena, Avital, and Ori:** Day-to-day operational tasks that Rami previously handled as a bottleneck

Rami acknowledged he had been a bottleneck — taking on operational challenges himself but lacking time to resolve them, while Shira lacked authority to handle them independently. The restructuring gives Shira both authority and accountability.

Availability model: Shira is not expected to be available full-time. Staff can send her emails, and she works approximately 30 minutes per day on management tasks. Telia'z (the platform) handles real-time staff availability needs.

## KPI Framework

Rami presented a prepared KPI analysis document with a mind map. Four KPI domains were discussed:

### 1. Patient Transition Rate
Track every patient who needs to transition — to Maccabi, to private care, or to continue through Chosen (the clinic). Measure the percentage of successful transitions within a defined timeframe.

### 2. HMO Commitment Closure Rate
Two problems identified:
- **Pre-treatment hold:** Patients approved but waiting on hold because their HMO commitment (hithayvut) is incomplete
- **Mid-treatment gap:** Patients already in treatment who need a commitment renewal, but treatment continues without authorization

Target: Should be 100% — every patient should have a valid commitment before treatment begins. This is a process discipline KPI, not a stretch goal.

A third sub-KPI emerged: **payment claim submission accuracy.** Ori identified cases where initial commitment verification looked fine, but problems surfaced at the claim submission stage, often due to mismatched dates in the system. This issue has largely been resolved through better coordination between Rena (intake verification) and Ori (claim submission), but ongoing monitoring is needed.

### 3. Financial Utilization Rate (Primary KPI)
The most extensively discussed KPI. Rami showed a per-clinician spreadsheet tracking:
- Total available hours allocated per clinician
- Hours actually utilized (sessions held)
- Hours unutilized (no-shows + unfilled slots)
- Payment model per clinician (some paid per hour worked, some paid for available hours)

**Alex's key insight:** Measure the utilization gap in **monetary terms (NIS)**, not just percentages. Rationale: A clinician at 75% utilization costing 170 NIS/hr matters less than one at 90% utilization costing 430 NIS/hr — the absolute NIS loss is what impacts the bottom line.

Formula: For each clinician, calculate `(actual rate paid per hour - contractual rate per hour) × hours worked`, then sum across all clinicians. The total NIS gap becomes the KPI target to minimize.

### 4. Clinical Compensation as Percentage of Revenue (Top-Level KPI)
Alex established industry benchmarks:
- **35% of revenue** = excellent (best achievable in telemedicine/service industry)
- **50% of revenue** = maximum acceptable
- **Above 50%** = failing

This percentage includes ALL clinical costs: psychiatrist compensation, therapist compensation, insurance, management salaries — everything except Rami's own compensation.

Historical reference: When Rami managed the clinic, the split was approximately 30% to Telia'z overhead and 70% to clinic operations. From the 70%, roughly 65% went to expenses and 35% remained as margin — equivalent to about 45% total clinical cost ratio.

## Bonus Structure Design

Alex proposed a **quarterly bonus** tied to the clinical compensation percentage:
- Shira's target: keep clinical costs at or below 50% of revenue
- Bonus scales linearly between 50% (minimum/no bonus) and 35% (maximum bonus)
- Quarterly measurement avoids gaming that monthly measurement enables
- Shira gets autonomy to decide HOW to achieve the target — she can reduce clinician hours, optimize scheduling, or reallocate staff as she sees fit

**Sub-team KPIs:** Rami suggested that Shira would define sub-KPIs for Rena and Avital. The total bonus pool could be defined for the management team and then distributed among them by Shira.

## Remaining Items

- **Historical baseline needed:** Alex requested historical utilization data to set realistic targets rather than arbitrary ones
- **Bonus amounts not defined:** Alex took this away to "run through his head" — specific bonus NIS amounts not yet set
- **Agreement standardization:** Rami flagged that employment agreement normalization is still pending (deferred to a later discussion)
- **Dekkel discussion:** Alex planned to talk to Dekkel separately after this meeting

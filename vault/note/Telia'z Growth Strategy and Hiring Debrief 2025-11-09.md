# Telia'z Growth Strategy and Hiring Debrief 2025-11-09

## Context

Voice memo recorded on November 9, 2025, capturing a post-interview debrief conversation between Rami Khouri and likely Oren Taliaz / Dekkel Taliaz. The discussion occurred after interviewing candidates for the clinic general manager position (the same day as the Basel Kanaaneh interview) and covers broader strategic planning topics.

## Key Topics

### 1. Clalit Growth Strategy and Marketing Budget Gap

The team discussed how to grow within the new Clalit partnership agreement, which Rami describes as a "watershed moment" (פרשת מים) — securing partnership with another HMO body. However, **no marketing budget has been factored into the financial equations**. This is a critical gap because growth within both Maccabi and Clalit requires active patient acquisition work.

Key points:
- The Clalit agreement provides security but needs active promotion to generate patient volume
- Growth within Maccabi also needs attention — they need someone to "spread the word" inside the HMOs
- HMO operations are highly district-based — each district manager controls their own budget and territory
- When they asked Anat (Maccabi contact) for a list of relevant district contacts, she did not provide names
- A deputy district manager of Maccabi North told them they need to engage directly with relevant district-level people
- Someone needs to be embedded within the HMO organization to drive referrals — this is field-level work

### 2. RACI Task Management Framework

The conversation referenced a task management methodology (RACI) that Basel discussed with Roy Shur:
- Each task should have a task owner, people who need to be part of the discussion, and people who need to be informed
- Alex (Taliaz) previously rejected RACI as "too much" — too structured, putting people in templates
- However, the team now recognizes they've reached a stage where this kind of structure is necessary to "get the most out of people"
- Basel's approach of bringing closed, practical processes was noted positively
- The company has been running in a fast-paced, everyone-busy mode, and new tasks are getting confused across team members

### 3. Competitive Landscape

Brief discussion of competitors in the telepsychiatry space:
- Mentioned names: Elios, Liv, Betrel (phonetic from Hebrew — may be approximate)
- Competition is "actually blessed" because there is enough work for everyone in the market
- Need to map competitors — who does what, relative advantages and disadvantages
- Important to know the competitive landscape thoroughly for investor/board presentations

### 4. Board Presentation Strategy

Discussion about how to present the growth story to investors/board:
- Should show actual processes, not just financial projections or "dreams"
- Show the P&L alongside the story of what happened — how Maccabi growth was achieved, how the next client acquisition will work
- More intuitive for external listeners when you show processes with real data
- Need to demonstrate: what happened within Maccabi, how the customer was acquired, how growth increased, and then project forward

### 5. Hiring Candidate Assessment

After the main strategy discussion, the team debriefed on the day's candidate interviews for the clinic manager role:

| Candidate | Profile | Assessment |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Candidate A (female) | Marketing and sales background, minimal clinical/operational experience | Less relevant — lacks clinical operations depth |
| Candidate B (male) | Very strong operations background, no clinical background at all | Impressive despite no clinical background |
| Candidate C (from Clalit) | Healthcare experience, salary expectation: 35K NIS (currently earning), discussed at 25K initially | Concern about overqualification — may get bored and leave |
| Candidate D (last) | Did not prepare — couldn't name salary figures, works in private sector | Told to do research and come back with numbers |
| Final candidate | Right background, very enthusiastic about Telia'z mission | Most enthusiastic; came from relevant healthcare domain |

The final candidate's enthusiasm about what Telia'z is doing was highlighted as an important positive signal.

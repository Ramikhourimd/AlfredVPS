# Psychiatric Consultation Medication Switch to Venlafaxine 2025-12-02

## Context

Recorded psychiatric consultation session at Telia'z Clinic Israel on 2025-12-02. Rami (psychiatrist) assessed a patient presenting with recurring depressive episodes and dissociative experiences, and recommended a medication change due to diminished efficacy of the current SSRI regimen.

## Patient Presentation

- **Chief complaint:** Recurring episodes of emotional disconnection and depersonalization — patient describes feeling detached from surroundings, sensing that familiar places have changed, with episodes that come and go
- **History:** Symptoms are not lifelong but have been present for a significant period; there was a period of relative stability during prior treatment
- **Current status:** Partial response to current medication — not fully controlling symptoms
- **Sleep:** Disturbed sleep, part of the depressive picture
- **Concurrent therapy:** Receiving ongoing psychological treatment

## Current Medication

| Medication | Class | Dose | Duration | Schedule |
|-----------|-------|------|----------|----------|
| Sertraline (Lustral) | SSRI | 50mg | Years | Daily |
| Mondormin | Hypnotic | PRN | Ongoing | As needed for sleep |
| Clonazepam (Clonex) | Benzodiazepine | PRN | Ongoing | As needed for calming |

## Medication History

- Previously tried Cipralex (Escitalopram) — another SSRI from the same family
- Body has habituated to the SSRI class (both Sertraline and Cipralex)
- Need to switch to a different mechanism of action

## Clinical Decision

**Switch from Sertraline (SSRI) to Venlafaxine/Effexor (SNRI)**

**Rationale:** Patient has stopped responding to serotonin-only medications. Venlafaxine works on both serotonin and norepinephrine (dual mechanism), which research supports as effective when patients stop responding to SSRIs alone.

## Tapering and Transition Plan

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| Week 1 | Reduce Sertraline to 100mg |
| Week 2 | Reduce Sertraline to 50mg |
| Week 3 | Stop Sertraline; allow washout |
| Week 3-4 | Start Venlafaxine 37.5mg |
| Week 4+ | Increase Venlafaxine to 75mg |
| Ongoing | Mondormin and Clonex continue as needed |

## Monitoring Requirements

- **Blood pressure monitoring** — Venlafaxine can potentially raise blood pressure; requires tracking
- **Family doctor involvement** — Rami wrote a referral letter for the family doctor to manage the transition, prescribe the medication, and monitor blood pressure during the switch
- **No changes before seeing family doctor** — patient instructed not to alter any medication before the family doctor appointment
- **Follow-up with Rami** — next psychiatric appointment in approximately 2 months

## Potential Side Effects Discussed

- Initial dry mouth and dry eyes possible
- Theoretical blood pressure elevation (generally not significant at low doses)
- Side effect profile different from previous SSRI experience
- Patient has history of some issues (referenced but not detailed)

# Psychiatric Consultation: SSRI to SNRI Medication Switch

## Context

Voice memo recording of a psychiatric consultation session on 2025-12-02. The psychiatrist (likely [[person/Rami Khouri]]) meets with a patient presenting recurring depressive and dissociative symptoms. The session focuses on medication review and a planned switch from SSRI to SNRI class antidepressant.

## Patient Presentation

- **Chief complaint:** Recurring episodes of dissociation and emotional detachment — feeling disconnected from surroundings, a sense that "things weren't always like this"
- **Course:** Episodic, not lifelong. The patient has periods where everything is fine, alternating with episodes of detachment
- **Current functioning:** Sleep disturbances are part of the depressive picture

## Current Medication Regimen

| Medication | Class | Dose | Frequency | Duration |
|------------|-------|------|-----------|----------|
| Sertraline | SSRI | 50mg | Daily | Years (stable dose) |
| Mondormin | Hypnotic | PRN | As needed for sleep | Ongoing — helps somewhat |
| Clonazepam (Clonex) | Benzodiazepine | PRN | As needed for anxiety | Not regular |

## Medication History

- Previously prescribed **Cipralex (Escitalopram)** — another SSRI
- Both Sertraline and Cipralex belong to the same SSRI family
- The psychiatrist concludes the patient's body has adapted to this drug family and a different mechanism of action is needed

## Treatment Decision: Switch to Venlafaxine (SNRI)

**Rationale:** When patients stop responding to serotonin-only medications (SSRIs), research supports switching to a medication with a dual mechanism — working on both serotonin and noradrenaline. Venlafaxine (Effexor) operates differently from the SSRI class and can "refresh" the therapeutic response.

**Taper and Transition Schedule:**

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| Week 1 | Reduce Sertraline to 100mg |
| Week 2 | Reduce Sertraline to 50mg |
| Week 3 | Stop Sertraline — allow washout period |
| Week 4 | Start Venlafaxine 37.5mg |
| Week 5 | Increase Venlafaxine to 75mg |

**Adjunct medications:** Mondormin and Clonazepam to continue as needed (sleep and anxiety support remain unchanged).

## Monitoring Requirements

- **Blood pressure:** Venlafaxine can theoretically elevate blood pressure. Patient needs BP monitoring — the family doctor will manage this
- **Referral letter:** Psychiatrist is writing a detailed letter for the family doctor (first name "Yehuda" mentioned) with the full switch plan
- **Important:** Patient must NOT change any medications before seeing the family doctor. The family doctor will write the prescription and set up BP monitoring
- **Follow-up:** Psychiatrist's next appointment is in 2 months — family doctor covers interim monitoring
- **Side effects to watch:** Possible initial dry mouth and dry eyes; generally mild and transient

## Ongoing Treatment

- Patient confirmed receiving concurrent **psychological therapy** (psychotherapy)
- The combined pharmacological and psychological approach continues

## Additional Context

- Final segment discusses managing expectations around significant life changes with someone referred to as "Yehuda" — suggesting either a family member or the family doctor should be informed about the medication transition and its implications
- Emphasis on setting realistic expectations and getting agreement before proceeding

## Key Topics Discussed

### Patient Growth Projections
- Year-end 2026 projection: 7,786 active patients (Maccabi data from May onwards)
- Staffing need: 44 FTE positions — 24 psychiatrists, 21 case managers
- Current recruitment: quarter-positions (40 hrs/month), so multiply FTE x4 for headcount
- Monthly patient churn rate: ~15% average (range 11.6%–18.9% across months)

### Session-to-Patient Ratio Anomaly
- Case manager ratio: 1.38 sessions per patient per month
- Psychiatrist ratio: >1.0 sessions per patient per month (unexpected — should be lower)
- Likely cause: active patient count definition excludes new intake patients
- Decision: Investigate and recalculate together (Rami + Dekkel + Alex)

### 60+ Clinical KPIs Framework
- Rami developed ~60 KPIs across all clinic departments
- Categories: growth readiness, risk assessment, clinical documentation, triage, treatment outcomes
- Key innovation: establishing a "danger threshold" below which the organization is at risk
- KPIs include: wait times, staff satisfaction, burnout indicators, employee churn, documentation quality, symptom tracking, remission rates, ER/hospitalization referrals

### Brand Strategy for Cost Savings
- Basel's insight: Clalit attracts workers at 50-60 NIS/hr because of brand reputation
- Proposal: build clinical reputation (national impact on mental health burden) to attract salaried case managers
- Projected savings: ~7,000 NIS/year (~6% of expenses) by converting case managers to salaried employees

### Core Conflict: Financial vs Clinical Strategy
- Rami argued previous week's presentation focused exclusively on financial targets
- "Clinical excellence" was defined by sub-40% profitability — no actual clinical KPIs
- Shachar spent 3 hours trying to link R&D features to financial targets only
- Marketing (Adi) focused solely on bringing more patients, not clinical quality
- Rami's position: a clinical operation cannot be governed by financial targets alone without risking collapse in 2027
- Rami expressed deep frustration at organizational misalignment across all departments

### Resolution
- Oren and Dekkel acknowledged they had "thrown Rami into the water" without support
- Oren proposed Rami focus on clinical requirements and let leadership handle operations/finances
- Rami rejected this — cannot separate clinical decisions from operational execution
- Agreed: Sunday meeting to hear Rami's full clinical strategy proposal
- Dekkel committed to a detailed walkthrough of Rami's plan

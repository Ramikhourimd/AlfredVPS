## Summary

This is a deeply personal family conversation (recorded as a voice memo in Palestinian/Levantine Arabic) between Rami Khouri and his mother Leah Khouri, with Rana Khouri discussed extensively.

### Rami's ADHD and Medication

Rami describes persistent fatigue, low energy, and unhappiness. He connects this to ADHD — explaining that during six years of medical school he could not read a single textbook, could not sit still for 45 minutes, and used to bring Sudoku puzzles to lectures. He did not know he had ADHD at the time and thought he was simply inadequate. He notes that "the difference between us and other people with ADHD is that we're intelligent, so we found ways to compensate." Medication allows him to focus "like a good student," but when unmedicated the baseline deficit becomes more visible.

### Rana's Situation

Rana (Rami's sister) is approximately 39-40 years old, single, with no children. She works as an administrative manager overseeing 9 staff members, earning a bonus of approximately 3,000 NIS (~30% of her salary). She has dogs. Leah is critical of Rana for losing her engagement ring during a move and a necklace (which Rana claimed was stolen by a friend). Rami defends Rana, arguing that if she lies to their mother, it is because she does not feel safe telling the truth.

### Fertility Discussion

Leah mentions that Rana asked for 10,000 NIS, apparently for fertility-related procedures (egg freezing / IVF). Rami notes the low probability of success at age 40 but argues the money and emotional support matter more than the odds — that Rana's request was really about wanting to share and connect with her mother. Rami advises Leah to give without conditions and follow up with Rana about actual medical testing.

### Mother's Loneliness

Leah describes feeling isolated. When the children visit on Saturdays, she feels reduced to cooking, serving food, cleaning, and making coffee while her daughters-in-law chat among themselves and manage children. She says she wants to go out, shop together, or simply spend time — but everyone is busy. She concludes from their unavailability that she should not bother them.

### Rami's Emigration Decision

In one of the conversation's most striking moments, Rami declares: "Do you know why I haven't emigrated? I have every reason in the world to emigrate. I could work remotely. There is no reason to live among these dogs [referring to the political situation]. Nor for my daughter to grow up in this country. The only reason I haven't left is because I am in Haifa and I still can't see you enough — if I move abroad, my daughter might not even know you." He explicitly ties his decision to stay in Israel to proximity to his mother.

### Family Dynamics

Rami acts as mediator between Leah and Rana. He urges his mother to accept her children as they are ("We're your children — accept us as we are"), to stop attaching conditions to gifts, and to stop trying to prove who is right. He also gently challenges her to stop making unilateral decisions about visiting ("Don't decide for yourself that I'm busy — I'd rather see you even when I'm exhausted than not see you at all").

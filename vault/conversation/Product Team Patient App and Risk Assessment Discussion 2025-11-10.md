## Topic 1: Patient App Launch Readiness

Rami raised concerns about Dekkel's push to launch the patient-facing mobile application to all patients immediately. Key issues identified:

- **Summary download gap**: Patients currently cannot download session summaries from the app
- **Complaint handling**: No protocol exists for handling patient complaints
- **UX characterization needed**: The app needs proper UX review before broad rollout
- **Secretary protocol**: Secretaries need a support protocol since they will be first point of contact for patient issues
- **In-app support**: The app should have a chat or support feature so patients can report issues directly through the application rather than calling the clinic
- **Focused notifications**: The app could alert patients about issues within the app interface

Rami proposed involving Rivi Idelman for UX evaluation and characterizing what features exist, what patients can see, and what actions are possible.

## Topic 2: Suicide Risk Assessment and Emergency Contact Integration

Eitan (Adiel Levin) reported the suicide risk assessment feature is nearly complete — handed to Shmulik for the final integration piece (estimated half a day of work). Noa proposed integrating the emergency contact person field (from Ori Shukron's protocol) alongside the risk assessment, so patients fill both at the same intake point.

Key decisions:
- **Emergency contact is one-time**: Filled only at the first meeting with the case manager, not recurring
- **Prospective only**: Will apply only to new patients going forward, not retroactively
- **Bundled with risk assessment**: Emergency contact field to be placed with the suicidality screening questionnaire

Ori to forward her emergency contact protocol to Eitan so he can implement the integration.

## Summary

In-person voice memo recording of a conversation between Rami Khouri and Majdi Shaddad about the Khouri/Shaddad family genealogy. Majdi has been researching the family tree since approximately 1980. Key topics covered:

1. **Shaddad family migration timeline**: Left Hauran (southern Syria) around 1725, moved to Lebanon
2. **Druze-Christian conflicts**: Around 1800s, conflicts in Lebanon drove Christians to migrate to Palestine
3. **Coastal settlement**: Christians settled on the Palestinian coast and worked in trade
4. **Galilee settlement (c. 1865)**: Circumstances forced coastal families into inland Galilee villages — Suata, Rameh, Mi'ilya, Maghar, Safed, Tiberias
5. **Three Shaddad branches**: Eilabun, Rameh, and Nazareth — all are cousins
6. **Khouri surname origin**: Derives from "qasis" (priest) — a family member served as a priest in Rameh
7. **Diaspora connections**: Yacoub al-Asif at a church house in Beirut; Boulos (Paul) in Chile
8. **Shahoud family**: Rami asked about this family; Majdi heard the name in military records but has limited information about their origins

# Rami Overview of Telia'z for Firas 2025-09-01

## Context

Informal voice memo conversation on 2025-09-01 in which [[person/Rami Khouri]] explains his work at [[org/Telia'z]] to an acquaintance named Firas. At least one additional person is present and asks clarifying questions about the company. The conversation is conducted in Levantine Arabic.

## Company Origins and Evolution

Rami describes how [[org/Telia'z]] began as an algorithm company around 2010, developing predictive models for psychiatric medication responses. The algorithms used three data sources: clinical data, demographic data, and DNA samples. The research was based on a study called "Study" (a longitudinal research project). They later integrated genetic components into their algorithmic models.

Rami joined the company during his psychiatric residency ("Tmahut") as a clinical consultant. The company needed clinical data from within Israel, so they established a virtual clinic. Originally the clinic was named [[asset/HealthyMind Platform]], but after merging with the parent startup, it was rebranded to Telia'z Healthcare (though it can still be found under the HealthyMind name).

## Current Scale and Operations

The virtual clinic now has approximately 5,000 active patients across Israel. The entire operation is virtual — no physical clinic locations. They have their own HR platform, and clinical assessments are conducted via Microsoft Teams.

During the war period, they worked with:
- Resilience centers ("Merkazei Chosen") — community mental health support centers for people affected by security situations. These existed before the war but expanded significantly, operating in both the north and south of Israel.
- Coalition Ultra
- [[org/Maccabi Healthcare Services]] RCI — the psychiatric shortcut referral pathway

## Patient Acquisition and Payment Model

The patient funnel evolved over time:

1. **Initial phase**: Patients came via resilience center referrals. When a psychologist or social worker at a resilience center identified a patient needing psychiatric assessment, they would refer them to Telia'z.
2. **Tofes 17 phase**: The first formal agreement with Maccabi was through Tofes 17 (Form 17 — a special authorization form for external provider services). However, Maccabi discontinued this after observing high volume and costs.
3. **Current phase**: Telia'z is now a regular Maccabi provider ("Rofe Maccabi"). Patients only need to be Maccabi members to access services for free — no special referral or form required.

At peak volume, the clinic handled approximately 1,500 new patients per month, with wait times of around 10 days.

## Clinical Workflow

The clinical flow is divided into two stages:

1. **Case Manager assessment**: The first evaluation is performed by a Case Manager — either a psychologist or a social worker ("Oved Sotziali Refui"). This is a comprehensive initial assessment.
2. **Psychiatrist assessment**: The second evaluation is conducted by a psychiatrist. Based on questionnaire data from stage one, the system builds a structured AI-generated report providing clinical insights for the psychiatrist.

The entire clinical flow, scheduling, and assessments run on the platform. Questionnaire data feeds into automated processes that generate reports based on patterns — partly AI-driven, partly rule-based.

## Rami's Roles

Rami holds two titles at [[org/Telia'z]]:

1. **Global Medical Director**: Overseeing medical operations across Israel and the planned UK clinic. In Israel there is also a Chief Medical Officer below him; in the UK (not yet launched) there will be a separate medical lead.
2. **Chief of Innovation**: Leading the [[project/Telia'z Innovation Program]] with a team of software engineers working on clinical process development and automation.

Rami works 8 hours per week in direct clinical practice and spends the rest of his time on Telia'z operations and innovation.

## UK Expansion

Rami mentions that [[org/Telia'z]] is also building a clinic in England ([[project/Telia'z UK Expansion]]), though at the time of this conversation it had not yet launched.

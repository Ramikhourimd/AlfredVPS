## Summary

Follow-up psychiatric consultation with a female outpatient on Clonazepam (Clonex). Patient reports feeling significantly better. Doctor reviewed medication regimen — patient takes Clonex 1 tablet morning and 1 tablet evening, though a noon dose was also prescribed. Doctor decided against any medication changes, noting even small adjustments could destabilize her current improvement.

Key clinical issue: patient has been absent from work for 2 months. Doctor strongly counseled that work serves a therapeutic function — "we need work not for money but for your brain's health" — framing occupational engagement as complementary to pharmacotherapy.

Patient mentioned a recent burn incident while cooking bread. She also expressed interest in secretarial/computer work. A family member was present and echoed the therapeutic value of work, also raising a religious perspective on medication from a Christian viewpoint.

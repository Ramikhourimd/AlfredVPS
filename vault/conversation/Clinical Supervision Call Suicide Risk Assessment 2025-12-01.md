## Summary

A clinician called Rami for supervision regarding a patient case involving suicidal risk. The patient is connected to military/security service. Rami provided comprehensive clinical guidance covering:

1. **Risk assessment framework**: Assessing immediate danger (סכנה מיידית) using the suicidality scale — ideation, thoughts, intention, plan, act

3. **Documentation requirements**: Importance of documenting what was assessed, what was not assessed and why, and the clinician's clinical impression

5. **Reporting protocols**: Military (IDF) vs. civilian channels — if patient is active military, can report to military mental health officer; if civilian, cannot report without consent
6. **Action plan**: Contact patient's therapist, request urgent psychiatric evaluation, take to ER if signs of deterioration

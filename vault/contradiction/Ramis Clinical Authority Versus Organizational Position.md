# Rami's Clinical Authority Versus Organizational Position

## Claim A

Rami possesses deep clinical expertise and operational knowledge, functioning as de facto CMO — he drives clinical direction, innovation strategy, and has expressed that clinical perspectives are insufficiently integrated into company decision-making. He proposed a steering committee to ensure clinical input into strategic decisions.

## Claim B

The proposed organizational structure does not assign Rami a clear executive position. The meeting "struggled to define Rami's role clearly" and a task was created for him to propose his own role — suggesting the organization doesn't have a natural slot for his multi-domain contribution. Basel's arrival as Operations Manager and the three-pillar clinic structure may reduce Rami's operational authority.

## Analysis

This tension reflects a common founder-scaling challenge: a founding team member who built the operation now needs to be "slotted" into a structured hierarchy. Rami's value spans clinical, operational, and innovation domains — but structured organizations typically require focused roles. The CMO role at parent company level could resolve this, but only if Rami wants that scope and is willing to release operational and innovation responsibilities.

## Resolution

Pending — Rami has been tasked with defining his preferred role. The resolution will depend on whether the CMO role (if created) matches his aspirations and whether innovation leadership can be separated from clinical authority.

# Secretary Assignment Model — Pod-Dedicated vs Shared Pool

## Claim A

Secretaries should be assigned to specific pods as dedicated diary managers. Each pod of ~10 clinicians would have a designated secretary who knows their scheduling patterns, patient preferences, and workflow. This ensures consistent, responsive support and reduces errors from unfamiliarity.

## Claim B

Secretaries should operate as a shared pool with rotating availability across all pods. This provides better coverage during absences (sick leave, maternity), avoids bottlenecks when one pod's demand spikes, and is more efficient at current scale (5 FTE secretaries for potentially 3+ pods).

## Analysis

This question was explicitly raised in the Feb 22 leadership meeting but no final decision was reached. The answer depends partly on scale — at 5 secretaries and 2-3 pods, dedicated assignment may leave gaps; at 8+ secretaries and 3+ pods, dedication becomes more feasible. The dual function of secretaries (patient-facing scheduling AND staff-facing diary management) complicates the choice: patient-facing work may benefit from pooling while staff-facing diary management may benefit from dedication.

## Resolution

Unresolved. Listed as an open item from the Feb 22 meeting. Likely depends on hiring pace and final pod structure decisions.

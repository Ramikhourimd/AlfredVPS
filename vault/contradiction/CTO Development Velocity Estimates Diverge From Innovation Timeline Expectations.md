# CTO Development Velocity Estimates Diverge From Innovation Timeline Expectations

## Claim A

Shachar (CTO) estimates that development requests from Rami's innovation function require approximately 8 person-months of engineering effort. This reflects the CTO's assessment based on traditional development practices and the current platform roadmap capacity.

## Claim B

Rami believes the same work could be completed in approximately 2 weeks using AI-assisted development tools and approaches. He is frustrated that Shachar took 2 years to start using AI in his coding environment, suggesting the CTO's estimates reflect outdated development practices rather than actual task complexity.

## Analysis

This is a 16:1 disagreement on effort estimates for the same scope of work. The divergence likely stems from:
1. Different assumptions about development methodology (traditional vs. AI-augmented)
2. Different definitions of "done" (production-ready vs. proof-of-concept)
3. Platform roadmap competition (Shachar may be including capacity constraints, not just task effort)
4. Technology adoption gap (Shachar's slower adoption of AI development tools)

This contradiction directly blocks innovation execution — innovation ideas cannot be prototyped quickly because the CTO pipeline bottleneck prevents rapid iteration.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The proposed product manager role (task/Establish Innovation-CTO Coordination Mechanism) may help mediate this by creating shared effort estimation frameworks and prioritization criteria.

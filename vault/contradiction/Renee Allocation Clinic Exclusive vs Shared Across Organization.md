# Renee Allocation Clinic Exclusive vs Shared Across Organization

## Claim A

Renee should serve the clinic exclusively as its Administrative Manager. The clinic needs a dedicated administrative leader who is fully embedded in day-to-day operations, not split across multiple organizational units.

## Claim B

Renee should be a shared resource at the parent company level, consistent with the shared services model. Administrative support is a centralized function, and dedicating Renee to one clinic contradicts the principle of centralized shared services.

## Analysis

This contradiction directly tests the boundary of the "shared services" decision. The meeting revealed tension about where the line falls between clinic-level staff and parent-company shared services. If administrative management is clinic-level (like Clinical Director and Operations Manager), then Renee is clinic-dedicated. If it falls under shared services (like HR, marketing, finance), she serves the parent company.

The "Clinic District Model With Three Parallel Management Tracks" decision includes an Administrative Manager as one of the three clinic-level roles — which implies Renee should be clinic-dedicated. However, the shared services model pulls in the opposite direction for cost efficiency.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The task "Define Role Expectations for Existing Clinic Staff" was created specifically to address this, but no outcome has been recorded.

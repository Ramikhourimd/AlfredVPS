# Founders Satisfied With Li Yamin Performance Despite Documented Obstruction

## Claim A

Oren and Dekkel are "generally satisfied" with Li Yamin's work. Despite the friction reported by multiple colleagues, the founders view her as performing adequately in her role. This satisfaction was noted during the 2025-12-02 management discussion even as Rami was presenting evidence of repeated obstruction.

## Claim B

Li Yamin has a documented pattern of blocking organizational progress across multiple colleagues over at least six months:
- **Shachar** (~6 months prior): Found her difficult to work with, experienced similar friction
- **Rami** (2025-12-02): Sent email requesting document handoff; Li pushed back claiming "not her responsibility"
- **Basel** (2025-12-02): Requested employment agreements; Li redirected him to Shira instead of providing them
- **Self-reported**: Li told Rami "If I decide not to do it, I won't do it"

## Analysis

This contradiction suggests a visibility gap in founder oversight. The founders interact with Li Yamin primarily on tasks she chooses to engage with (where she performs well), while the obstruction occurs on tasks she deflects — which the founders don't directly observe. The colleagues who experience the obstruction (Shachar, Rami, Basel) are not in a position to escalate effectively because each incident appears isolated and minor.

The 1-3 month "patience window" decision reflects this contradiction: Oren acknowledges the problem enough to intervene but not enough to act decisively — because his overall satisfaction with Li's work moderates his response.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The contradiction persists and directly affects the timeline of the Li Yamin role evaluation. If founders remain generally satisfied, the "root cause" solution Rami seeks will be indefinitely deferred in favor of incremental workarounds (routing through Tal, coaching conversations).

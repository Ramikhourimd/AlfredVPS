# Growth Strategy — Immediate Expansion vs Stabilization First

## Claim A

Leadership (Alex, Oren, Dekkel) designed 2026 goals with overwhelming financial focus: expense ratio targets, patient volume expansion, expansion to additional HMOs (specifically Clalit and Leumit), new patient acquisition numbers, and UK expansion preparation. These targets were set without meaningful clinical input. Weekly Sunday meetings are entirely marketing and business-development oriented — no space for clinical operations, quality metrics, or staff wellbeing.

## Claim B

Rami argues the clinic needs stabilization before growth: recruit better psychiatrists, train staff, build ~60 operational KPIs and clinical protocols, and reach a quantitative organizational health score threshold. He estimates 6 months to growth readiness. He warns premature growth will increase wait times, reduce the clinic to "just another clinic," lead to lower-quality staff on small contracts, and expose the organization to legal risk.

## Analysis

This is the central strategic tension at Telia'z. Leadership sees market opportunity (e.g., 350 unserved patients from Clalit South psychiatrist departure) and focuses on capturing share. Rami sees operational fragility and focuses on building infrastructure to sustain growth. Both positions have merit — the question is sequencing, not direction. Neither side has produced a model showing the consequences of their preferred approach vs the other's.

## Resolution

Unresolved. Rami states he plans to "follow leadership's financial plans while building his own clinical plan independently" — a parallel-track approach that avoids confrontation but does not resolve the fundamental disagreement.

# Internal Task System Improves Efficiency But May Worsen Staff Isolation

## Claim A

Replacing WhatsApp-based communication with a structured internal task system improves operational efficiency: tasks are tracked, assigned, and resolved through proper channels rather than lost in chat threads. Staff complaints about unanswered WhatsApp messages support the need for better task routing.

## Claim B

WhatsApp groups, despite their inefficiency for task management, serve an informal social function — staff see each other's messages, share frustrations, and maintain a sense of ambient connection. Replacing this with transactional task routing removes the last informal social channel, potentially deepening the isolation that staff already report as a significant problem.

## Analysis

Both claims are well-supported by different evidence streams:
- Operational data shows WhatsApp messages going unanswered, creating frustration (supports Claim A)
- Staff interviews reveal that isolation and lack of team cohesion are primary complaints, and even imperfect social channels have value (supports Claim B)

The tension is real because the same tool (WhatsApp) simultaneously fails at task management and partially succeeds at social connection. Replacing it optimizes for one function while degrading the other.

## Resolution

Unresolved. Possible approaches:
1. **Dual-channel design** — Implement structured task system AND maintain a separate informal social channel
2. **Social features in task system** — Build social/community features into the new internal system
3. **Compensating interventions** — Accept the task system reduces social contact and introduce separate peer interaction programs (group supervision, team meetings)

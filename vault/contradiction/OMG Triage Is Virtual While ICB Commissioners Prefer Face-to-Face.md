# OMG Triage Is Virtual While ICB Commissioners Prefer Face-to-Face

## Claim A

The NHS ADHD patient pathway designed with OMG specifies that the initial triage (inclusion/exclusion screening) is "conducted by phone or virtual (not in-person at clinic)." This was explicitly designed into the pathway during the July 2025 meeting between Rami and Adiel.

## Claim B

ICB commissioners in Leeds "heavily weight procurement bids that offer face-to-face clinical services over purely virtual." Sean flagged this during the GP Confed meeting as a key factor in tender evaluation. The bid must address face-to-face service preference to score well.

## Analysis

These claims create a structural tension: the operational design optimises for virtual delivery (scalability, cost efficiency), while the procurement evaluation rewards face-to-face presence. OMG has physical clinic infrastructure that could be used for face-to-face, but the pathway was explicitly designed as virtual.

The tension may be manageable if face-to-face is offered as an option (hybrid model) while defaulting to virtual, or if face-to-face is provided at later stages (psychiatrist session) while triage remains virtual. However, the current pathway design does not address this tension.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The pathway design and tender evaluation criteria have not been reconciled. Rebecca Wilson's clinical pathway design task was meant to address the "face-to-face vs. virtual split" but completion status is unknown.

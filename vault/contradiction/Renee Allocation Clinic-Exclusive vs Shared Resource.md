# Renee Allocation — Clinic-Exclusive vs Shared Resource

## Claim A

Under the district model, each clinic has its own Administrative Manager. Renee, currently serving in an administrative role at the clinic, should be dedicated to the Israel clinic district. This aligns with the agreed-upon three-pillar management structure (Clinical Director, Operations Manager, Administrative Manager).

## Claim B

Renee's skills and capacity should be shared across the organization rather than locked into a single clinic. The parent company also needs administrative support, and with limited headcount, sharing resources is more financially efficient. This aligns with the centralized shared services philosophy.

## Analysis

This contradiction sits at the intersection of two agreed decisions that pull in opposite directions: the district model (each clinic is self-contained with its own management triad) vs the shared services model (centralize support functions at parent level). Administrative management falls into a grey zone — is it a "district function" or a "shared service"? The Feb 22 meeting surfaced this tension explicitly but did not resolve it.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The task "Define Role Expectations for Existing Clinic Staff" was created specifically to address this ambiguity, but no resolution has been documented.

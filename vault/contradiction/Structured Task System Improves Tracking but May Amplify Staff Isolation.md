# Structured Task System Improves Tracking but May Amplify Staff Isolation

## Claim A

The move from WhatsApp to an internal tasks system will improve operational communication — better task tracking, clearer accountability, reduced message loss, and more structured workflows. This is the operational rationale driving the transition.

## Claim B

The WhatsApp groups, despite being inefficient for task tracking, provided informal social connection between otherwise isolated remote workers. Staff who work alone from home facing a screen described WhatsApp group chats as one of their few points of human contact with colleagues. Replacing this with a structured system may eliminate the informal social layer that partially counteracted isolation.

## Analysis

This is a classic efficiency-vs-cohesion trade-off. The task system solves a real operational problem (unanswered messages, unclear accountability), but the cure may worsen a different problem (staff isolation and lack of team feeling). The two claims operate on different dimensions — operational efficiency vs. social cohesion — and both appear to be valid simultaneously.

The risk is especially acute given that most staff already report not feeling part of a team. Removing informal communication channels without replacing the social function they served could accelerate disconnection.

## Resolution

Not yet resolved. Possible approaches:
1. Implement task system but maintain a separate informal/social channel
2. Increase group supervision frequency to compensate for lost informal contact
3. Add social features to the task system (team check-ins, casual threads)
4. Monitor isolation metrics after transition and adjust

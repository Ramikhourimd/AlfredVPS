# Rent vs Buy Path Unresolved

## Claim A

The family is actively viewing rental properties in the 1,200-1,500 NIS/month range, evaluating lease terms, and assessing rental-specific criteria (landlord stability, lease duration). This suggests renting is the primary strategy.

## Claim B

Simultaneously, the family evaluates a property for purchase at ~850K NIS (plus renovation costs), discusses the Maghar family land valued at ~4M NIS with options to sell or develop, and engages in detailed property market price analysis (4.3-5.2M NIS for houses). This suggests purchase — potentially leveraging the Maghar land — is also being seriously considered.

## Analysis

These two paths are not inherently contradictory (one could rent short-term while planning a purchase), but no source record indicates a clear timeline, priority ordering, or decision framework for choosing between them. The rental viewings suggest immediate housing needs, while the purchase/land discussions suggest longer-term planning. The gap between the apparent purchase budget (~850K) and market prices (4.3-5.2M) may be bridgeable only through the Maghar land asset, but no decision has been made on liquidating that asset.

Key tension: The family is spending energy on both paths without a clear strategy for which to prioritize, creating risk of decision fatigue and missed opportunities on either front.

## Resolution

Unresolved. A clear decision on rent-first vs. buy-first (with or without Maghar land liquidation) would focus the search significantly.

# Internal Task System Improves Operations But May Worsen Staff Isolation

## Claim A

The internal task system replacing WhatsApp will improve operational clarity — messages will have owners, deadlines, and accountability. The current WhatsApp-based communication is unstructured and messages frequently go unanswered, creating confusion and perceived abandonment.

## Claim B

WhatsApp groups, despite their operational limitations, serve as the only informal social channel for remote clinical staff. Replacing them with a task-oriented system eliminates this incidental social layer. Staff already report isolation as a major problem; further formalizing communication removes the last informal touchpoint.

## Analysis

Both claims are simultaneously true — they address different dimensions of the same system change. The operational improvement (Claim A) and the social cost (Claim B) are not mutually exclusive. The contradiction is that the same change is both beneficial and harmful, depending on the dimension measured.

The resolution likely requires a dual approach: implement the task system for operational accountability AND explicitly add social/community features (virtual coffee, peer channels, team events) to replace the informal connection WhatsApp provided.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The task system migration is proceeding, but no explicit plan exists to mitigate the social isolation risk.

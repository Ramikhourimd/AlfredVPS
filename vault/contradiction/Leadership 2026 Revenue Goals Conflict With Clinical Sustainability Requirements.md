# Leadership 2026 Revenue Goals Conflict With Clinical Sustainability Requirements

## Claim A

Telia'z company leadership (via Alex's board presentation the week before 2025-12-04) presented 2026 goals structured around financial targets and patient acquisition metrics. Under the "clinical excellence" category, the primary metric was profitability below 40% — indicating that even clinical goals were framed in financial terms. Weekly Sunday leadership meetings focus on surface metrics: new patient counts, Facebook campaign performance, and monthly revenue.

## Claim B

Rami Khouri presented data showing that the clinic's growth trajectory (from ~1,000 to ~7,786 active patients) requires approximately 176 individual clinician hires, formalized protocols, and operational stabilization before aggressive growth targets can be sustainably pursued. He described the prior week's presentation as a "crossroads" (tzomet) and argued that clinical sustainability — treatment response rates, remission rates, hospitalization reduction — must be the foundation on which financial targets are built, not the reverse.

## Analysis

The conflict is not about whether growth or quality matters, but about sequencing and measurement. Leadership measures success in revenue and patient volume; Rami measures it in clinical outcomes that eventually drive revenue. Both paths lead to revenue, but Rami's path requires upfront investment in protocols, staffing stability, and outcome tracking before pursuing volume. The current approach risks scaling an operationally fragile system.

The 2025-12-04 meeting was Rami's attempt to reframe the conversation. The related assumption "Protocol Framework Will Convince Leadership to Change Priorities" (status: challenged) suggests this argument has not yet succeeded.

## Resolution

Unresolved as of the source dates. The protocol framework document and board presentation are the intended resolution mechanism. Basel's onboarding as Operations Manager provides operational capacity to implement whichever direction is chosen.

# Roy Shur Holds COO Title While Absent From Operations Restructuring

## Claim A

Both the December 2025 and February 2026 HR system exports list Roy Shur as "Chief Operating Officer" — a full-time, hybrid employee with an active status. This is the highest operational authority title in the company, sitting alongside Dekkel (CEO), Oren (CFO), Rami (CMIO), Adi Lavi (CMO), and Alex (Chief Strategy & Growth Officer) in the leadership tier.

## Claim B

The February 22, 2026 restructuring meeting — the pivotal session defining operational leadership for the clinic and company — discusses at length: Basel's Operations Manager role, the three-pillar district model, CMO vs COO authority for Rami, shared services boundaries, and clinic-to-company reporting lines. Roy Shur is not mentioned in either the detailed meeting note or the strategy meeting summary. Basel, a recent hire, is being positioned as the operational leader for the clinic while Roy Shur's existing COO mandate goes entirely unaddressed.

## Analysis

Several possible explanations exist:
1. Roy Shur's COO title is nominal or refers to a different operational domain (e.g., technology operations, not clinic operations)
2. Roy Shur's authority has been informally diminished and the restructuring formalizes a de facto reality
3. The restructuring focuses on clinic operations specifically, while Roy oversees company-level operations — but the meeting explicitly discusses company-level C-suite roles without referencing him
4. There is an unspoken understanding about Roy's role that doesn't appear in these records

The complete absence — not disagreement, not redefinition, but total invisibility — of an active COO from a meeting about operational restructuring suggests either a deep disconnect between formal titles and organizational reality, or a deliberate avoidance of a sensitive topic.

## Resolution

Unresolved. No source record addresses Roy Shur's relationship to the restructuring or explains the gap between his title and his absence from operational leadership discussions.

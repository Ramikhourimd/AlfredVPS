# Oren Repeatedly Commits to Including Rami But Exclusion Pattern Persists

## Claim A

Oren verbally commits to including Rami in organizational decisions. On December 2, 2025, during a private management discussion, Oren "acknowledged authority vacuum, committed to keeping Rami in the loop." On February 22, 2026, at the leadership meeting, Oren again committed to including Rami in cross-functional meetings. (Sources: [[conversation/Telia'z Management Discussion HR Leadership and CEO Role 2025-12-02]], [[decision/Oren Commits to Including Rami in Cross-Functional Meetings]])

## Claim B

Despite these commitments, Rami's exclusion from cross-functional planning persists. On December 5, 2025 — three days after Oren's first commitment — Rami reports that Sunday leadership meetings remain entirely marketing/business-development oriented with no clinical operations slot. By February 22, 2026, the same exclusion issue resurfaces as a discussion point, indicating no structural change occurred in the intervening three months. (Sources: [[conversation/Rami Personal Debrief on Leadership Frustrations 2025-12-05]], [[note/Telia'z Leadership Meeting 2026-02-22 Organizational Structure and Roles]])

## Analysis

This is not a single broken promise but a repeating pattern: commitment → no structural follow-through → same problem resurfaces → new commitment. The contradiction suggests that verbal commitments without structural mechanisms (agenda changes, formal role authority, governance committees) are insufficient to change organizational behavior. Oren may be genuinely well-intentioned but lacks either the follow-through or the authority to change how the leadership team operates.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The proposed steering committee ([[decision/Steering Committee for Strategic Governance]]) could address this structurally if implemented. Without a formal governance mechanism, this pattern is likely to continue.

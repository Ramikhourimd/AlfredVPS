# Combined vs Separated CMO-COO Authority

## Claim A

Rami argues that clinical leadership requires operational authority. A CMO without operational control cannot effectively ensure clinical quality, because clinical outcomes are inseparable from how operations are run (scheduling, staffing ratios, workflow design). He advocates for a combined CMO/COO role with full authority over the clinic.

## Claim B

Oren argues that clinical oversight and operational management are distinct competencies that should be held by different people. Separating them allows for professional management (Basel as operations) while preserving clinical governance (Rami as CMO). He sees combined authority as a bottleneck and potential conflict of interest.

## Analysis

This is the central unresolved tension in the restructuring. Both positions have merit:
- Clinical quality IS affected by operational decisions (Rami's point)
- Concentration of authority in one person creates bottlenecks and dependencies (Oren's point)

The district model partially addresses this by creating parallel management tracks (Clinical Director, Operations Manager, Administrative Manager), but the question of who has final authority when clinical and operational priorities conflict remains unresolved. The Define CMO Role Proposal task was created specifically to bridge this gap.

## Resolution

Unresolved. Rami is tasked with creating a formal role proposal that delineates responsibilities. The outcome will likely determine the power dynamics of the restructured organization.

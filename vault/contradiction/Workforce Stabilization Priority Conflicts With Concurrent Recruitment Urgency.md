# Workforce Stabilization Priority Conflicts With Concurrent Recruitment Urgency

## Claim A

Rami Khouri explicitly stated during the 2026-02-15 team meeting that the priority should be stabilizing the current workforce before aggressively recruiting. The rationale: current staff are dissatisfied and actively telling prospective applicants not to work at the clinic (reported by Adi Lavi via Rivi Idelman's feedback). Recruiting into a dissatisfied workforce risks accelerating turnover rather than building capacity — new hires arrive into a negative environment and leave quickly, wasting recruitment investment.

## Claim B

At the same 2026-02-15 meeting and in parallel task records, the team is pushing urgently to launch the careers page and recruitment campaigns immediately. Dekkel pushed for same-day launch "even imperfect." Multiple task records frame the careers page as a critical blocker for capacity expansion. The underlying position: the clinic cannot wait for stabilization because the 12x demand-capacity gap (945 patients vs. 77 intakes) is an existential operational risk requiring immediate hiring action.

## Analysis

Both claims address the same capacity crisis from different temporal perspectives. Rami's position is sequential: fix retention first, then recruit. The leadership/operational position is parallel: recruit now while also addressing satisfaction. The conflict is whether recruitment into an unstable workforce is net positive (even if some new hires churn) or net negative (churn costs plus cultural damage outweigh marginal capacity gain).

The existing staff satisfaction data is qualitatively alarming (staff actively deterring applicants) but quantitatively weak (survey response rates too low for statistical validity, per separate constraint record). This data quality gap makes it difficult to resolve the contradiction empirically.

## Resolution

Unresolved as of 2026-02-26. A possible resolution path: launch recruitment campaigns now but invest simultaneously in structured staff engagement (the employee experience research initiated 2026-02-18 may provide the data needed to design retention interventions).

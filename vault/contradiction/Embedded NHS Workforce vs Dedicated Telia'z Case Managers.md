# Embedded NHS Workforce vs Dedicated Telia'z Case Managers

## Claim A

In the GP Confederation partnership model (Jan 2025), case managers were positioned as trainable within existing GP Confed workforce. Nurse prescribers, PWPs, or similar NHS staff would be upskilled to perform the structured pre-assessment. The demo rehearsal (Feb 2025) reinforced this by describing the role as "trainable within GP Confed structure."

## Claim B

In the Leon track (Feb 2026 team meeting), the plan is fundamentally different: start with Israeli case manager (Bassem) as stopgap, then hire dedicated UK-based case managers employed by Telia'z. Adiel was firm that UK case managers should be hired locally — but as Telia'z employees, not NHS embedded staff.

## Analysis

These represent two different workforce models for the same role, driven by different partnership contexts. GP Confed model leverages existing NHS workforce (lower cost, less control). Leon model requires Telia'z to build its own workforce (more control, higher cost). Both are active simultaneously across different market entry tracks, creating potential confusion about long-term staffing strategy.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The two models may coexist across different tracks, but no explicit decision has been made about preferred long-term model or whether they should converge.

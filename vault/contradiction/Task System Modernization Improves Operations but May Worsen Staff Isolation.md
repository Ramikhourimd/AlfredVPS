# Task System Modernization Improves Operations but May Worsen Staff Isolation

## Claim A

Replacing WhatsApp with an internal task system improves operational efficiency. Tasks get tracked, assigned, and resolved systematically rather than buried in informal group chat threads. Messages no longer go unanswered without accountability. Operational communication becomes structured and reliable.

## Claim B

Multiple staff members report that isolation and lack of team cohesion are primary contributors to burnout. WhatsApp groups, despite being operationally inefficient, provided the only informal social connection among remote clinicians. Some staff expressed that "things they struggle with, others also experience, but nobody knows because they don't see each other's messages." An internal task system optimized for task tracking may further reduce the informal social interaction that partially mitigated isolation.

## Analysis

This is a genuine tension between operational efficiency and social cohesion in a remote clinical workforce. The task system solves a real problem (unanswered messages, lost tasks) but risks amplifying another real problem (isolation, feeling like "orphaned children"). The two goals are not inherently contradictory — a system could be designed to support both — but the current trajectory prioritizes operational structure without an explicit social cohesion mechanism to replace what WhatsApp informally provided.

## Resolution

Unresolved. Possible approaches include: adding a social/informal channel alongside the task system, expanding group supervision as a social connection mechanism, or designing the task system with visibility features that let staff see others' work and struggles.

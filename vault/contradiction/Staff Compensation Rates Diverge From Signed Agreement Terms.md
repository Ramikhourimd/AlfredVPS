# Staff Compensation Rates Diverge From Signed Agreement Terms

## Claim A

Signed employment agreements (heskemim) specify particular hourly or monthly rates for each staff member. Shira sent agreements and fee schedules that define the contractual terms.

## Claim B

Actual payment data in the Excel spreadsheets shows different amounts being paid. Specific discrepancies identified during the 2025-12-04 review:

- One psychiatrist receiving **400 NIS/hour** when their tier places them at **350 NIS/hour**
- Lori/Ori listed at **100 NIS/hour** in Excel but agreement shows base **1,350** with additions to **1,650** plus a **6,000** component — and a 75% position calculation yields **13,500 NIS** which doesn't reconcile with the hourly rate
- A base rate listed as **9,000 NIS** was actually **6,300 NIS** real plus **2,700 NIS** overage
- Sarah has no Excel file from Shira despite being on the payroll

## Analysis

The discrepancies appear to stem from multiple causes: (1) informal rate adjustments made without updating agreements, (2) different calculation methods (hourly vs monthly vs percentage position), and (3) missing documentation for some staff entirely. The lack of a standardized template meant each person's compensation evolved independently.

## Resolution

Basel has been tasked with cross-referencing every agreement against actual payments. Rami's policy is to grandfather higher rates rather than reduce them. The unified agreement template (4 new templates from Shira) should prevent future divergence, but existing discrepancies need individual resolution.

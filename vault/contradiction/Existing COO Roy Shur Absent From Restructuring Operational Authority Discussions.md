# Existing COO Roy Shur Absent From Restructuring Operational Authority Discussions

## Claim A

Roy Shur holds the official title of "Chief Operating Officer" in the Telia'z HR system. Both the December 2025 and February 2026 employee roster exports confirm this title. Roy is listed as a full-time hybrid employee in the Leadership Team section alongside the CEO, CFO, and other C-level executives.

## Claim B

The organizational restructuring discussions — both the December 2025 private management conversations and the February 2026 leadership meeting — debate operational authority as if no COO exists. Basel is positioned as Operations Manager for the clinic. The CMO-vs-COO debate centers on whether Rami should hold operational authority alongside clinical authority. Neither the meeting notes nor the task definitions reference Roy Shur's existing COO role or explain how it relates to the restructured operational hierarchy.

## Analysis

This disconnect suggests one of several possibilities: (1) Roy Shur's COO role is scoped to the parent company (technology/product) and is considered separate from clinic operations, (2) the COO title is nominal and does not carry the operational authority being debated, or (3) the restructuring team has an implicit assumption that clinic operations are a distinct domain from the company-level COO function. The absence of any explicit discussion about Roy's role relative to the new structure is itself significant — it implies the team treats parent company C-suite and clinic management as entirely separate organizational domains without acknowledging this boundary.

## Resolution

Unresolved. No source record addresses how Roy Shur's COO title relates to the clinic operational authority being restructured.

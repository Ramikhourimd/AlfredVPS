# Roy Shur COO Title Absent From Restructuring Discussions

## Claim A

Both the December 2025 and February 2026 HR system exports list Roy Shur as "Chief Operating Officer" — a full-time, hybrid position. This is not an interim or honorary title; it appears in the formal employee roster alongside other C-suite members (Dekkel as CEO, Oren as CFO, Rami as CMIO). Roy Shur is one of six leadership-level employees.

## Claim B

The February 22, 2026 leadership meeting extensively discusses operational authority, the CMO-vs-COO debate, clinic operational management, and whether clinical leadership requires operational authority. At no point do the meeting notes reference Roy Shur's existing COO title, his operational responsibilities, or how the proposed restructuring interacts with his current role. The discussion proceeds as if the COO position is vacant or undefined.

## Analysis

Two explanations are possible: (1) Roy Shur's COO title is nominal or scoped to parent company/technology operations, not clinic operations, making it irrelevant to the clinic restructuring discussion. (2) The team has implicitly excluded Roy from the clinic restructuring scope, treating parent-company COO as a separate domain. Either way, the disconnect reveals that Telia'z existing C-suite structure is not treated as authoritative when planning operational changes. This reinforces the pattern identified in the "Formal HR Titles Misalign With Operational Role Reality" contradiction — titles exist in HR but do not reflect real authority distribution.

## Resolution

Unresolved. Clarification needed on whether Roy Shur's COO authority extends to clinic operations or is limited to parent company operations. If the latter, the restructuring discussions are internally consistent but the HR titles remain misleading.

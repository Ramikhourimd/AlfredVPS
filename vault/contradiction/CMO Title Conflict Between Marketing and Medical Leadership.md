# CMO Title Conflict Between Marketing and Medical Leadership

## Claim A

The HR system (both December 2025 and February 2026 exports) lists Adi Lavi as "Chief Marketing Officer" (CMO) — a full-time, hybrid position based in Tel Aviv. This is an active, formal title in the organization.

## Claim B

The organizational restructuring proposes a "Chief Medical Officer" role at the company level — also abbreviated CMO. The task to define Rami's role explicitly references CMO authority, and the Feb 22 meeting agreed that a CMO role is required at the company level for clinical governance.

## Analysis

Two C-level roles would share the same abbreviation (CMO) within the same organization. Rami's current HR title is "CMIO (Chief Medical Officer & Head of Innovation)" which avoids the collision, but restructuring discussions consistently use "CMO" for the medical role. This creates potential confusion in governance documents, reporting structures, and external communications. It also raises the question of whether the marketing CMO title will be retained, modified, or whether the medical role will use a different abbreviation.

## Resolution

Not yet addressed. The restructuring discussions have not acknowledged the naming collision with Adi Lavi's existing title.

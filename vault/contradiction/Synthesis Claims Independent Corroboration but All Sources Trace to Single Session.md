# Synthesis Claims Independent Corroboration but All Sources Trace to Single Session

## Claim A

The existing synthesis record "Consistent Four-Phase Migration Pattern Across All Sources" states that six vault records were "created independently at different times" and that their consistency across independently authored records strengthens confidence in the migration narrative.

## Claim B

All six source records — three conversations and three research notes — trace to the same single oral history session on 2025-12-02 between Rami Khouri and Majdi Shaddad. The records were created as multiple vault entries capturing the same conversation, not as independent observations from different sources or timepoints. The consistency therefore reflects one informant's internal coherence within a single account, not independent corroboration from multiple sources.

## Analysis

The distinction matters for evidence assessment:
- **Internal consistency** (one account retold consistently) is weaker evidence than **independent corroboration** (multiple sources converging on the same conclusion)
- The migration pattern may still be historically accurate — it aligns with documented events like the 1860 Mount Lebanon civil war — but the vault's evidence base for it is narrower than the synthesis suggests
- True corroboration would require a second independent source: church records, Ottoman registers, another family historian, or academic research on Christian migration patterns in the Galilee

This does not invalidate the four-phase migration model, but it recalibrates confidence: the model rests on one informant's consistent oral account plus alignment with known historical events, not on six independent sources.

## Resolution

Could be resolved by:
1. Updating the synthesis record to clarify that consistency is within one informant's account, not across independent sources
2. Seeking a second independent source to provide genuine corroboration
3. Cross-referencing with academic literature on Christian migration to the Galilee

# Formal HR Titles Misalign With Operational Role Reality

## Claim A

The HR system (both December 2025 and February 2026 exports) shows clear title assignments: Dekkel Taliaz is CEO and Head Scientist, Roy Shur is Chief Operating Officer, and Rami Khouri is CMIO (Chief Medical Officer and Head of Innovation). These titles imply defined roles with clear boundaries.

## Claim B

In practice, restructuring discussions treat these roles as vacant or contested. The team discusses a "CEO vacuum" despite Dekkel holding the title. Roy Shur as COO is invisible in restructuring conversations — the COO function is debated as if no one occupies it. Rami performs four overlapping functions (CEO substitute, Medical Director, Clinical Operations Head, Innovation Lead) that far exceed his CMIO title. The Feb 22 meeting debates CMO vs COO authority as if these positions are open.

## Analysis

The gap between formal titles and operational reality suggests the organization has outgrown or diverged from its original structure without updating it. This creates multiple problems: accountability confusion (who is actually responsible for what), authority gaps (title holders not exercising authority), and restructuring discussions that ignore existing assignments. Notably, Roy Shur as COO does not appear in any restructuring conversation despite holding the title most relevant to operational discussions.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The restructuring aims to create a new organizational chart, but it is proceeding without first reconciling existing titles with actual roles. This risks creating a new structure layered on top of an unacknowledged existing one.

# Rami Role Definition Pending While Structure Finalizes Around Him

## Claim A

The leadership team is actively finalizing the organizational structure — district model, three-pillar management, shared services, CMO role, Medical Director hire. Decisions are being made and roles are being assigned. Basel is confirmed as Operations Manager, Renee as Administrative Manager, a Medical Director will be hired.

## Claim B

Rami — whose current responsibilities span four distinct functions and whose future role (CMO + Innovation) is central to the entire restructuring — has not yet defined what he wants his role to be. The meeting outcome explicitly states "Rami to articulate his preferred role definition" as a next action. The conversation record flags this as high risk: "organizational ambiguity is causing operational friction and role confusion."

## Analysis

There is a sequencing contradiction: the structure is being built around a role (CMO) whose scope and boundaries are not yet defined by the person who will fill it. This creates a risk that decisions made now (Medical Director scope, Operations Manager boundaries, shared services allocation) may need to be revisited once Rami articulates his preferences. The structure may be optimized for a version of the CMO role that Rami does not ultimately accept.

## Resolution

Unresolved. Rami needs to articulate his role definition before the org chart can be truly finalized. The follow-up meeting should prioritize this.

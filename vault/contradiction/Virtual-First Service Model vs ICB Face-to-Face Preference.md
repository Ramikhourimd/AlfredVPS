# Virtual-First Service Model vs ICB Face-to-Face Preference

## Claim A

Telia'z's entire service model is built around virtual-first telepsychiatry. The Israel operation — 2,700+ patients, 9,600+ sessions — is delivered entirely remotely. The technology platform (AI transcription, real-time summarization, digital questionnaires) is optimized for virtual delivery. The demo rehearsal and GP Confed presentations showcase a fully virtual workflow. The economic model depends on virtual delivery for the efficiency gains (60% psychiatrist time savings, 3.5-day referral-to-assessment).

## Claim B

ICB commissioners in Leeds "heavily weight procurement bids that offer face-to-face clinical services over purely virtual ones." Sean Rejabris explicitly flagged this in the GP Confed meeting (2025-01-22) as a key factor in the tender evaluation. The clinical pathway design task includes addressing "face-to-face vs. virtual split" as a required element.

## Analysis

This creates a fundamental tension: the service that Telia'z has built and proven at scale is virtual, but the buyers (ICB commissioners) prefer face-to-face. The OMG partnership partially addresses this — OMG has physical clinic infrastructure in Leeds — but the Telia'z-delivered psychiatric assessment component remains virtual. The question is whether a hybrid model (OMG face-to-face triage + Telia'z virtual assessment) satisfies the ICB preference, or whether the psychiatric consultation itself must offer a face-to-face option.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The pathway design work with Rebecca Wilson and GP Confed is expected to address this, but no documented resolution exists yet. The OMG partnership provides physical infrastructure that could enable hybrid delivery, but the implications for Telia'z's technology platform (which is optimized for virtual) and psychiatrist staffing model (Israeli psychiatrists cannot provide face-to-face UK services) remain unaddressed.

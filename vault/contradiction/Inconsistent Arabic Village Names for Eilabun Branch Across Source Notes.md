# Inconsistent Arabic Village Names for Eilabun Branch Across Source Notes

## Claim A

Two of the three research notes use Arabic village names that actually refer to different Galilee villages when identifying the Eilabun branch:
- "البعنة" (al-Ba'na) is a separate village in the Galilee, not Eilabun
- "إعبلين" (I'billin) is also a distinct village, not Eilabun
- "اللبيا" and "الجبيا" do not correspond to standard Arabic names for Eilabun

## Claim B

The third note correctly identifies the village as "إلابون" (Eilabun), which is the standard Arabic name. This suggests the other notes contain transcription or transliteration errors introduced during voice memo processing.

## Analysis

All three notes derive from the same 2025-12-02 voice memo conversation with Majdi Shaddad. The inconsistency likely arose during transcription — Arabic village names spoken in dialect may have been misheard or incorrectly mapped to written Arabic. This is significant for a genealogy project because:
1. It creates ambiguity about which village the Eilabun branch actually settled in
2. Future researchers consulting these notes may be misled
3. The correct village name needs to be confirmed with Majdi

## Resolution

Needs clarification from Majdi Shaddad. The standard identification is Eilabun (إلابون), but the voice memo should be re-listened to confirm what Majdi actually said.

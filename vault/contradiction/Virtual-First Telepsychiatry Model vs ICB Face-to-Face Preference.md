# Virtual-First Telepsychiatry Model vs ICB Face-to-Face Preference

## Claim A

Telia'z's core service model is virtual-first telepsychiatry. All psychiatric assessments are delivered remotely via video. The entire technology platform — AI transcription, session summarization, report generation — is built around virtual consultations. This is the model that achieved 2,700+ patients, 81.3% NPS, and 3.5-day average access time in Israel.

## Claim B

ICB commissioners in Leeds "heavily weight procurement bids that offer face-to-face clinical services over purely virtual" (per Sean Rejabris at the GP Confederation meeting). The tender response must address the face-to-face preference explicitly. This reflects broader NHS policy direction favoring in-person care for complex conditions.

## Analysis

This is a fundamental tension between Telia'z's product architecture and the UK buyer's stated preference. Two potential resolutions are being explored:

1. **OMG hybrid model**: OMG Healthcare has physical clinics across England. By partnering with OMG, the initial triage could happen in-person at OMG facilities while the psychiatric assessment remains virtual via Telia'z. However, OMG's own triage is currently designed as phone/virtual (not in-person), so this resolution itself requires operational changes.

2. **Reframing**: Position the service as "digital-first with face-to-face capability" rather than "virtual-only." The GP Confederation pathway could include optional in-person touchpoints using OMG or GP Confed clinic space.

Neither resolution has been validated with ICB commissioners.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The OMG partnership is the leading hypothesis for bridging this gap, but the specific hybrid model has not been designed or approved by commissioners.

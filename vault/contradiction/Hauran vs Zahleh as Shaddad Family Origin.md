# Hauran vs Zahleh as Shaddad Family Origin

## Claim A

The Shaddad family left Hauran (southern Syria) around 1725, with Hauran being their point of origin or at least a major settlement before the westward migration to Lebanon.

## Claim B

The Shaddad family may have originated from or passed through Zahleh (Lebanon) before settling in Hauran, meaning the migration direction in the earliest phase could be reversed or more complex than the primary narrative suggests.

## Analysis

Majdi Shaddad himself raised this uncertainty during the 2025-12-02 conversation — he was told it was "either Hauran or Zahleh." The three research notes all acknowledge this ambiguity. The contradiction may reflect: (1) incomplete oral tradition that lost precision over ~300 years, (2) multiple migration steps that got simplified in retelling, or (3) different branches of the family preserving different versions of the origin story.

This is significant because it affects the earliest phase of the migration timeline and could indicate connections to the Zahleh Christian community that are currently unexplored.

## Resolution

Unresolved. Would require documentary evidence such as Ottoman registers, church records from Hauran or Zahleh, or corroborating accounts from other Shaddad branches to determine the actual origin point.

# Dedicated vs Shared Administrative Staff Allocation

## Claim A

The clinic needs dedicated administrative staff (specifically Renee) to ensure consistent, responsive support for clinical operations. Sharing staff across the organization creates availability gaps and divided loyalties that directly impact patient care and psychiatrist scheduling.

## Claim B

Administrative staff should be part of the centralized shared services model, serving multiple districts and functions. Dedicating staff to a single clinic is inefficient, especially at current scale, and contradicts the shared services decision already made for HR, marketing, and finance.

## Analysis

This contradiction exposes a tension within the shared services model decision. While the group agreed that HR, marketing, finance, and tech should be centralized, the boundary between "shared" and "dedicated" staff becomes contentious for roles that directly support clinical operations. The administrative manager role sits at the intersection of operational support (clinic-specific) and organizational efficiency (shared across districts).

The Define Role Expectations task was created to resolve this, but the underlying philosophical disagreement about where shared services end and clinic-specific resources begin remains unaddressed.

## Resolution

Unresolved. The task to define role expectations for Shira and Renee is intended to address this, but no framework for making this decision has been established.

# Rami Perceived as Impractical Dreamer Despite Highest Operational Output

## Claim A

Management (Alex, Oren, Dekkel) perceives Rami as an impractical dreamer. Rami reports in his therapy session: "He senses that management views him as a dreamer ('he dreams, he floats') because he always has ideas." This perception means his proposals — regardless of their content — are received with skepticism about their feasibility rather than evaluated on their merits.

## Claim B

Rami has produced more concrete operational artifacts than any other team member. He has defined nearly 60 KPIs for clinic operations ("it's already basically a hospital"), built a quantitative organizational health score model with burnout-risk indicators and growth-readiness thresholds, produced a 70-page innovation plan, and performs over 50% of CEO functions (by his own assessment, partially corroborated by Oren's acknowledgment of the authority vacuum).

## Analysis

This contradiction reveals a perception-reality gap that may be the most damaging dynamic in the organization. Two mechanisms may explain it:

1. **Volume effect**: Rami generates so many ideas and frameworks that the sheer volume overwhelms recipients, making each individual contribution appear less substantial than it is.

2. **Presentation mismatch**: Alex's immediate challenge of data methodology (2025-12-04 meeting) suggests Rami's presentation style may not match his audience's expectations — rigorous quantitative content delivered in a way that triggers methodological skepticism rather than strategic engagement.

3. **Identity protection**: If management acknowledged Rami's operational output honestly, they would also have to acknowledge their dependence on him — which undermines the narrative that the founders are running the company.

## Resolution

Unresolved. This perception gap persists and actively blocks Rami's ability to influence organizational strategy. It is distinct from (but compounds) the "personal motives" attribution pattern captured in the existing synthesis.

# CEO Role Incompatible With Rami's Personal Sustainability

## Claim

Rami taking the CEO role at Telia'z would result in burnout within approximately one month. The position requires three days per week on-site, distances him from technology/innovation work, distances him from family, and eliminates the creative thinking time that sustains his mental health. Despite having the professional capability, the personal cost makes it unsustainable.

## Basis

Rami has experienced the three-days-per-week on-site pattern before and describes it as having "finished him." He has recognized through self-reflection that he is not suited to constant external engagement and needs quiet time for creative work. He describes taking the role as "entering something you know you'll fail at" — not from competence, but from burnout.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-05: Explicit statements in therapy session about burnout risk, personal cost, and distance from technology/home
- Prior experience with intensive on-site work "finishing" him
- Self-awareness about needing personal space for creative thinking

## Impact

This constrains restructuring options — a physician CEO must be found externally rather than defaulting to Rami. It also explains why management's focus on "what Rami wants" misses the point: his advocacy is professional, not personal ambition.

Bringing in an external medical consultant to validate Rami's clinical positions can break the internal credibility deadlock where leadership (Alex, Oren, Dekkel) dismisses Rami's data-driven clinical arguments. The assumption is that external professional validation will carry weight that internal advocacy has failed to achieve.

During the Feb 22 strategy meeting, Rami — frustrated that his recommendations are often not implemented quickly enough — explicitly suggested bringing in an external medical consultant to validate his positions. This follows a pattern where Rami's internal presentations are challenged on methodology (by Alex) or dismissed as impractical (by Oren/Dekkel), despite being grounded in clinical evidence and healthcare literature.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-04: Rami's analytical presentation to Alex was shut down before completing 10% of prepared material
- 2026-02-22: Rami proposes external consultant after pattern of internal dismissal
- Pattern: Internal data and arguments are challenged on methodology; external validation may bypass this dynamic

## Impact

If it fails (i.e., leadership also dismisses external consultants), it would indicate the resistance is not about credibility but about strategic priorities — a deeper misalignment.

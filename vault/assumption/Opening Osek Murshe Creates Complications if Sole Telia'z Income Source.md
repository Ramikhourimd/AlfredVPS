# Opening Osek Murshe Creates Complications if Sole Telia'z Income Source

## Claim

If Rami opens a separate osek murshe (licensed business entity) to route income from Telia'z, it creates tax visibility and structural complications — particularly if Telia'z is the only income source flowing through the entity. The accountant flagged this as problematic.

## Basis

During the 2025-09-23 financial planning discussion, the accountant discussed three options for handling keren hishtalmut and income routing. Opening an osek murshe was one option, but the accountant noted complications arising when the entity has only one client (Telia'z), which can trigger tax authority scrutiny as it may be reclassified as an employer-employee relationship rather than a genuine business.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-09-23: Accountant advised against osek murshe if sole income source is Telia'z

## Impact

- Limits viable options for income structuring from Telia'z
- Reinforces the accountant's recommendation to keep funds in keren hishtalmut
- Future income diversification (e.g., clinical practice income routed through the same osek murshe) could mitigate this concern

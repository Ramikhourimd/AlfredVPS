# Secretary Roles Should Be Differentiated Into Patient-Facing and Staff-Facing

## Claim

The secretary team structure should distinguish between patient-facing roles (scheduling, patient communication, intake) and staff-facing roles (internal coordination, psychiatrist support, administrative tasks). This differentiation is treated as a design principle for the new team structure.

## Basis

The acceptance criteria for the secretary team structure task explicitly include "Roles defined (patient-facing vs. staff-facing)" as a required outcome, implying the team already assumes this is the right axis for role differentiation rather than, for example, geographic, shift-based, or psychiatrist-aligned splits.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-25: Identified in acceptance criteria of [[task/Finalize Secretary Team Structure and Hiring]]

## Impact

This assumption shapes hiring criteria (different skill profiles for each role type), training requirements, and potentially the pod-dedicated vs. shared pool model decision. If invalidated, the entire secretary team design would need rethinking.

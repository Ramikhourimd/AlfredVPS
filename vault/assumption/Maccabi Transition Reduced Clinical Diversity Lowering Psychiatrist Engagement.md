# Maccabi Transition Reduced Clinical Diversity Lowering Psychiatrist Engagement

## Claim

The transition from Clalit South (post-October 7 trauma work) to Maccabi (general psychiatry) significantly reduced clinical case diversity and professional challenge for psychiatrists. This shift removed both the social mission element (treating trauma survivors) and the clinical variety (diverse PTSD presentations vs. routine general psychiatry), making the work feel like "just another kupat cholim."

## Basis

Psychiatrist Ilia explicitly told Rivi that the Clalit South work provided both professional challenge and a sense of social importance. After the Maccabi transition, neither remained. The documentation burden (AI summaries) is unchanged, but the work itself is less rewarding. Ilia characterized the cost-benefit as unfavorable: the work is no longer "worth it" given the conditions.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Ilia's feedback relayed by Rivi during one-on-one with Rami

## Impact

This is a significant insight for psychiatrist retention strategy. Re-introducing clinical diversity (e.g., specialized programs, complex case tracks, or research involvement) could counterbalance the convenience-only retention pattern. Without it, Telia'z competes purely on pay and flexibility — a losing proposition against well-funded competitors.

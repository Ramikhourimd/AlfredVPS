# CMO Concurrent Clinical Practice Provides Firsthand Evidence for Organizational Policy

## Claim

Rami Khouri simultaneously maintains active clinical practice (direct patient consultations, medication management, psychiatric assessments at residential facilities) alongside his CMO role. This concurrent practice creates a direct feedback loop where individual clinical encounters generate evidence that informs organizational clinical policy — medication philosophy, risk assessment protocols, treatment guidelines, and discharge criteria.

## Basis

Multiple psychiatric consultation records from 2025-12-02 show Rami personally conducting clinical sessions whose outcomes map directly to organizational policy decisions:

- **Residential patient medication simplification** (Seroquel monotherapy consolidation) → organizational medication philosophy favouring simplification and monotherapy over polypharmacy
- **SSRI-to-SNRI switch protocol** (Sertraline to Venlafaxine transition) → standardized guidance on medication class switching when patients habituate to a drug family
- **Clonazepam maintenance decision** (maintain without changes for stable patient) → conservative philosophy of not disrupting functioning regimens
- **Suicidal risk assessment** during case supervision → structured five-level risk protocol adopted clinic-wide
- **Protocol framework brainstorming** (2025-12-04) → operational knowledge grounded in clinical experience, packaged for leadership presentation

The pattern suggests organizational clinical policy is not derived from literature review or external consultancy alone, but is actively informed by firsthand clinical experience at the point of care.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Four psychiatric consultations conducted by Rami, producing clinical decisions later reflected in organizational policies
- 2025-12-04: Protocol framework session where Rami drew on clinical experience to define clinical vs administrative protocol categories
- Ongoing: Case manager supervision sessions where acute risk protocols were developed from direct clinical encounters

## Impact

This assumption has significant implications for succession planning. If the CMO's clinical policy authority derives partly from active practice, a replacement Medical Director who does not maintain clinical practice may produce less grounded policies. The planned Medical Director role (30% position) would need to maintain sufficient clinical load to preserve this evidence-to-policy feedback loop. Without it, clinical policy risks becoming disconnected from frontline reality — the same pattern Rami identified in leadership's disconnection from operational fundamentals.

# Majority of Patients Have PTSD Diagnosis

## Assumption

Approximately 65-67% of the 3,100+ patients in the dataset were diagnosed with PTSD. Among first-line exposure patients, the PTSD diagnosis rate was approximately 70%, while second-line exposure patients showed a rate of approximately 40-50%.

## Basis

Rami reported these figures based on data he had already extracted from the database. Dekkel confirmed the general proportions. The remaining patients had diagnoses including depression and other conditions.

## Impact on Paper

This high PTSD prevalence supports the decision to focus the paper's clinical analysis specifically on PTSD outcomes. The diagnosis distribution will be presented as a pie chart, with subsequent analysis focusing on the PTSD subgroup.

## Confidence

High — based on actual data extraction from the clinical database, not estimates.

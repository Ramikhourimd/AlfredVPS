# Company Identity Must Be Defined Before Organizational Structure

## Claim

The company's core identity — what Telia'z fundamentally is beyond psychiatric services — must be defined before organizational structure decisions (org chart, CEO role, department boundaries) can be meaningfully made. Structure should follow identity, not precede it.

## Basis

Rami argues in the Dec 2 management discussion and the company identity task that team members don't know what the company truly is — each interprets identity based on their own role. Adi Lavi branded everything as "Telia'z Health" (psychiatric services), but Rami believes the identity should be broader to accommodate future expansion beyond psychiatry. He states: "Company identity must precede structural decisions."

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Rami raises identity vacuum in management discussion with Oren
- Task created: "Define Telia'z Company Identity and Strategic Vision" — still open
- Meanwhile, structural decisions (district model, three-pillar management) are proceeding without identity resolution

## Impact

If this sequencing is correct, the current restructuring may need to be revisited once identity is defined. If wrong, the team is unnecessarily delaying structural decisions that could proceed independently. The restructuring is currently advancing without waiting for identity resolution — creating a potential misalignment.

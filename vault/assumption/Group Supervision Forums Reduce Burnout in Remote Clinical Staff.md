# Group Supervision Forums Reduce Burnout in Remote Clinical Staff

## Claim

Peer supervision sessions (hadracha/group forums) have a measurable positive effect on reducing burnout perception among clinical staff working in telepsychiatry. The effect may come from the group contact itself, the professional supervision content, or both.

## Basis

At least one case manager reported that burnout "suddenly decreased" after group supervision sessions started. She was unsure whether the improvement came from the group social contact, the clinical supervision content, or the combination. The finding was reported across multiple overlapping meeting notes from the 2026-02-18 session.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Single case manager's self-reported experience (strong signal but n=1)
- Confidence set to low because: (1) single data point, (2) self-reported, (3) confound between social contact and supervision content not resolved

## Impact

- If confirmed, group supervision could be a cost-effective burnout intervention
- The staff satisfaction survey should measure burnout before and after supervision periods
- Supports the hypothesis that isolation is a key driver of burnout (group contact as partial remedy)
- May inform whether more frequent group sessions should be scheduled

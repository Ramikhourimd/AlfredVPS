# Later Follow-Up Sessions Lack Defined Clinical Protocol Creating Patient Disengagement

## Claim

The 3rd and 4th follow-up sessions between case managers and patients have no defined clinical protocol. While 1st and 2nd sessions have clear objectives (baseline assessment, side effects monitoring), later sessions are unstructured — case managers improvise their own approach, and patients perceive follow-ups as unnecessary, creating disengagement risk.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman reported that after creating a successful intake checklist (with Shira's input), case managers explicitly requested a similar structured protocol for follow-up sessions. The intake checklist was well-received, confirming that structured protocols improve case manager confidence and patient engagement. The absence of equivalent guidance for later sessions creates a quality gap.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi reports case managers request follow-up protocol similar to intake checklist
- Intake checklist created with Shira's input — confirmed useful by case managers
- 3rd and 4th sessions identified as the gap — 1st and 2nd have defined objectives

## Impact

Without structured follow-up protocols: (1) case managers develop inconsistent approaches, (2) patients question the value of later sessions and may disengage prematurely, (3) clinical quality varies by individual case manager rather than standardized practice. This directly affects patient retention metrics and treatment completion rates.

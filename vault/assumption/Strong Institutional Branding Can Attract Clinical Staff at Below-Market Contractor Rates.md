# Strong Institutional Branding Can Attract Clinical Staff at Below-Market Contractor Rates

## Claim

If Telia'z builds strong institutional branding (comparable to established health systems like Clalit), case managers would be willing to accept employment at 50-60 NIS/hour rather than demanding contractor-market rates. Basel Kanaaneh estimated this could save approximately 7,000 NIS/year per case manager and 6% in total expenses.

## Basis

Basel Kanaaneh proposed this during the 2025-12-04 strategic goals meeting, drawing on his experience at Clalit Health Services. At Clalit, the brand reputation attracted clinical staff willing to accept standard employment terms. Basel suggested that Telia'z could replicate this effect once the brand is established, shifting the hiring model from expensive individual contractor negotiations to standardized employment offers at lower rates.

## Evidence Trail

- Basel's suggestion was made in the context of a broader discussion about clinical sustainability vs financial targets
- No pilot or validation has been conducted to test whether Telia'z brand recognition is sufficient to support this assumption
- Current evidence suggests the opposite: psychiatrists like Atef frame participation as "doing Rami a favor" (November 2025 staffing update), and the child psychiatrist Ahmad Masalha departed over compensation dissatisfaction
- Staff satisfaction data (February 2026) indicates some current staff would not recommend working at the clinic

## Impact

If validated, this assumption could significantly reduce the per-clinician cost of scaling to 176 individual hires. If invalidated, the clinic must accept market-rate compensation or find alternative recruitment levers (e.g., the psychiatry academy, residency pipeline).

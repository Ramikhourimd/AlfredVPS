# Khouri Surname Derives From Priestly Role in Rameh

## Claim

The surname "Khouri" (خوري) derives from "qasis" (قسيس), meaning "priest" in Arabic. A member of the Shaddad family served as a priest in Rameh, and over time his descendants became known as "Dar Khouri" (the Khouri household), adopting this as a family surname.

## Basis

Majdi Shaddad provided this etymology during the 2025-12-02 conversation. The pattern of occupational/religious surnames is well-documented in Levantine Christian communities — "Khouri" is one of the most common such surnames. However, which specific ancestor served as priest, and when exactly the surname was adopted, remain unspecified in the oral account.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi explained the surname origin in recorded conversation
- All three research notes consistently describe this etymology
- The pattern is consistent with known Levantine Christian naming conventions
- No church records or documentary evidence cited to identify the specific priest

## Impact

This assumption links the Khouri family identity directly to the Rameh settlement and to the Shaddad family. If confirmed through church records, it would pinpoint when the Khouri branch formally separated from the broader Shaddad family. It also implies the family had sufficient standing in Rameh for a member to serve as priest.

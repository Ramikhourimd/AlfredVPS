# Case Manager Role Includes Nurse Practitioners and Junior Psychiatrists as Eligible Professionals

## Claim

The case manager role in the three-step clinical workflow can be filled by four professional categories: clinical psychologists, social workers, nurse practitioners, and junior psychiatrists. This is broader than the psychologist-and-social-worker scope described in later UK planning documents and assumptions.

## Basis

During the GP Confederation meeting on January 22, 2025, Adiel presented the Israel service model and described the case manager session (Step 2) as conducted by a "psychologist, social worker, nurse practitioner, or junior psychiatrist." This four-category definition comes directly from Israel operational experience where all four professional types perform the role.

## Evidence Trail

- **2025-01-22**: GP Confederation meeting — Adiel lists four eligible professional types for case manager role
- **2025-07-03 onward**: UK planning assumptions consistently narrow to "clinical psychologists or social workers" only
- The narrowing may reflect UK-specific recruitment realities or regulatory constraints, but no explicit decision to exclude nurse practitioners and junior psychiatrists has been recorded

## Impact

- UK recruitment pool is potentially larger than current planning assumes — nurse practitioners and junior psychiatrists could also fill the case manager role
- If UK regulations permit these professional categories for ADHD intake assessments, the staffing bottleneck may be less severe than assumed
- The OMG partnership model (where OMG has existing clinical staff) could leverage nurse practitioners already in their workforce

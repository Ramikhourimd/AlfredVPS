# Arab Community Proximity Is Primary Housing Selection Criterion

## Claim

The family's housing search is primarily filtered by proximity to an Arab community. Having Arab neighbors and community integration is more important than property specifications such as size, price, or amenities.

## Basis

In the neighborhood evaluation conversation (2025-10-09), Rami's wife explicitly stated: "This is the thing that bothers me the most honestly" — referring to the lack of Arab community/neighbors in their current area. Streets feeling empty at night and limited walkability compounded the isolation concern. Separately, the neighborhood assessment from 2025-09-23 documented warm integration of Arab families in a mixed neighborhood as a strong positive signal (neighbors bringing food after childbirth, genuinely friendly relationships).

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-10-09: Wife explicitly names isolation from Arab community as top concern
- 2025-09-23: Neighborhood with warm Arab-Jewish integration described positively
- 2025-10-09: Kiryat Haim evaluated favorably partly due to past community experience

## Impact

This assumption drives the geographic scope of the housing search. Neighborhoods without Arab community presence will likely be rejected regardless of property quality. This significantly narrows the candidate pool in the Haifa metropolitan area.

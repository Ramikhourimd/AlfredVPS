# 6-8 Weekly Hours Is the Sustainable Threshold for Telepsychiatry Staff

## Claim

Clinical staff (both psychiatrists and case managers) at Telia'z can sustain telepsychiatry work at approximately 6-8 hours per week without significant burnout. Beyond this threshold, exhaustion escalates markedly. The intensity per hour — not just total hours — is the critical factor.

## Basis

Multiple independent staff reports converge on this pattern:
- One psychiatrist (Sa'id) found 3-4 hours/day across 5 days draining, but 1.5 hours/day sustainable — even if total weekly hours were compressed into one block, he would not do it
- A case manager described leaving Telia'z shifts feeling more exhausted than hospital residency days, despite residency having longer hours
- Rivi personally noted significantly better empathy and attentiveness at 5 hours vs 9 hours
- Staff working 6-8 hours/week reported managing intensity reasonably well

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Multiple staff interviews by Rivi Idelman confirmed this pattern across both psychiatrists and case managers

## Impact

This threshold has direct implications for:
- Scheduling and staffing models — may need more clinicians at fewer hours each rather than fewer clinicians at more hours
- Recruitment messaging — should emphasize flexible, limited-hour positions
- Burnout prevention — scheduling beyond 8 hours/week should require explicit justification
- The employee satisfaction survey instrument being designed by Rivi should include hours-per-week as a key segmentation variable

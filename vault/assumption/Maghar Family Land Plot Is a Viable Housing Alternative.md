# Maghar Family Land Plot Is a Viable Housing Alternative

## Claim

The Khouri family's ~1 dunam land plot in Maghar (Khallet al-Sharif neighborhood, parcels 80 and part of 78) could be developed for family housing instead of renting or purchasing an existing property. The plot is valued at approximately 4 million NIS and has existing planning documentation specifying building rights, number of floors, setbacks, and permitted building area.

## Basis

During a family gathering on 2025-09-22, multiple family members discussed development options for the plot including: (1) building apartments for family use, (2) selling the land, or (3) partnering with a developer. The plot's location on a main road between Eilabun and Maghar, its corner position near a roundabout, and existing zoning documentation suggest it is developable. Historical buyer interest (up to 2.5-3M NIS per dunam) confirms market viability.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-09-22: Family discussion identified build-on-land as one of several housing options
- No survey (mitstafit) has been conducted yet
- No takhrit (planning document) has been obtained (cost ~250 NIS)
- Olive tree clearance issue remains unresolved with authorities

## Impact

If viable, this option would resolve the [[contradiction/Rent vs Buy Path Unresolved]] by introducing a third path: build on family land. However, development timelines, costs, and regulatory approvals make this a long-term option rather than an immediate housing solution. The family's current rental search suggests this is not being treated as the primary path.

# Jewish-Arab Psychiatric ER Utilization Patterns Diverge During Conflict

## Claim

Jewish and Arab populations show divergent psychiatric emergency room utilization patterns during periods of military conflict. Rambam Medical Center data covering all psychiatric ER visits over two years reveals measurable ethnic disparities in presentation timing, diagnosis patterns, and utilization rates between pre-war and wartime periods.

## Basis

A researcher at Rambam presented this line of investigation at a psychiatric conference on 2025-11-13. The analysis examines all ER psychiatric visits from the past two years, with specific attention to Jewish-Arab disparities and pre-war vs. war-period patterns. The research is ongoing but has already identified divergent patterns.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-13: Rambam researcher describes active analysis of ethnic disparities in psychiatric ER utilization during the current conflict period

## Impact

This external research validates and complements Telia'z's own patient cohort data. If Jewish and Arab patients present differently during conflict, the AI diagnostic pipeline's post-October 7 bias findings may be partially explained by ethnic-specific trauma patterns rather than purely contextual priming. This has implications for both Paper 1 methodology (subgroup analysis by ethnicity) and Paper 2 bias analysis (distinguishing population-level patterns from diagnostic bias). Also relevant to Rami's broader interest in Arab minority mental health services.

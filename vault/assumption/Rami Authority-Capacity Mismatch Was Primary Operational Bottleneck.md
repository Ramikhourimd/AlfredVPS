# Rami Authority-Capacity Mismatch Was Primary Operational Bottleneck

## Claim

The primary cause of operational stagnation at Clinic Israel was a structural mismatch: Rami had the authority to resolve operational problems but lacked the time (due to CMO duties, clinical work, and multiple responsibilities), while Shira had the proximity and availability but lacked the authority to make decisions independently. This created a deadlock where problems were identified but not resolved.

## Basis

Rami explicitly acknowledged this during the 2025-03-27 KPI meeting with Alex Taliaz. He stated he had been "a bottleneck" — absorbing operational challenges but unable to resolve them due to time constraints, while Shira could not act because she lacked formal authority. The restructuring (giving Shira the clinic manager title with KPI-linked accountability) was designed specifically to break this deadlock.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-03-27: Rami explicitly acknowledges bottleneck role in KPI meeting
- 2025-03-27: Redistribution decision made — authority transferred to Shira
- Ongoing: Shira operates as clinic manager with KPI accountability
- Status: Confirmed — the restructuring directly addressed this assumption

## Impact

This assumption explains why many earlier operational initiatives stalled despite being identified as priorities. It also validates the restructuring approach: the problem was structural (authority-capacity mismatch), not a personnel performance issue. Future scaling decisions should monitor for similar mismatches as new roles are created.

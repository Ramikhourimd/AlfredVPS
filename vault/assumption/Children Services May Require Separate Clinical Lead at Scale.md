# Children Services May Require Separate Clinical Lead at Scale

## Claim

Children's psychiatric services — currently approximately 10% of clinic activity — may need a separate clinical lead if volumes grow. Child psychiatry has distinct protocols, regulatory requirements, and practitioner qualifications that differ from adult services.

## Basis

Listed as an unresolved item from the Feb 22 leadership meeting. The current organizational design (pods, medical director, operations manager) is implicitly structured around adult telepsychiatry. No discussion of how children's services fit into the pod model, whether child psychiatrists would form their own pod, or whether the Medical Director position requires child psychiatry expertise.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Mentioned as unresolved item #7. No detailed discussion recorded.

## Impact

If children's services grow beyond 10%, the organizational structure may need a fourth management track or a specialized sub-pod. This affects Medical Director hiring criteria (does the MD need child psychiatry competency?), pod composition, and potentially regulatory compliance. Deferring this question is reasonable at current volumes but creates risk if growth is faster than expected.

# Oren Defaults to Patience Over Structural Solutions for Conflicts

## Claim

When interpersonal or role conflicts arise, Oren's default resolution strategy is to ask for patience and time rather than implementing structural changes. This manifests as: (1) asking Li Yamin to "be patient for 1-3 months" and cooperate rather than addressing her role misfit, (2) the Feb 22 meeting concluding with "continue discussions" rather than binding decisions on contested topics, (3) committing to "keeping Rami in the loop" rather than formalizing Rami's authority.

## Basis

The Dec 2, 2025 conversation records Oren speaking with Li Yamin at 5 PM, asking her to be patient for 1-3 months and cooperate with Basel. The Feb 22, 2026 meeting notes indicate "No final decisions were made" on several key topics despite lengthy discussion. Both Rami and Oren acknowledge in their Dec 2 discussion that Li Yamin's behavior is a pattern, not a one-time issue, yet Oren's intervention remains conversational rather than structural.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Oren asks Li Yamin for 1-3 months patience instead of role restructuring
- 2025-12-02: Oren commits to "keeping Rami in the loop" rather than defining formal authority
- 2026-02-22: Leadership meeting on restructuring ends with continuation rather than decisions on contested items

## Impact

This pattern creates a governance environment where structural problems are deferred rather than resolved, allowing dysfunction to persist. It explains why the restructuring has generated extensive discussion but limited implementation. It also means that when Oren "agrees" to something in a meeting, it may not translate to action — contributing to the pattern identified in "Team Treats Tentative Agreements as Final Decisions."

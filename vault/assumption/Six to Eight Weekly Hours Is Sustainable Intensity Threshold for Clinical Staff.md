# Six to Eight Weekly Hours Is Sustainable Intensity Threshold for Clinical Staff

## Claim

Clinical staff (both psychiatrists and case managers) at Telia'z can sustain telepsychiatry work at 6-8 hours per week without significant burnout escalation. Beyond this threshold, burnout increases markedly regardless of how the hours are distributed.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman's qualitative interviews with multiple staff members during early 2026 consistently pointed to this threshold:
- Staff working 6-8 hours/week reported manageable intensity
- One psychiatrist (Sa'id) found 3-4 hours/day across 5 days draining, but 1.5 hours/day sustainable
- A case manager reported being much more empathetic and attentive at 5 hours versus 9 hours
- Multiple staff described burnout as present and significant at higher hour counts

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Multiple staff interviews reported via Rivi to Rami, consistent pattern across case managers and psychiatrists

## Impact

- Scheduling decisions should respect this threshold when possible
- Recruitment may need to target more part-time clinicians rather than fewer full-time ones
- The staff satisfaction survey instrument should include hours-per-week as a key segmentation variable
- If confirmed quantitatively, this could reshape the clinic's workforce planning model

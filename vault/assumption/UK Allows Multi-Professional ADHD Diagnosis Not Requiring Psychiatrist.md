# UK Allows Multi-Professional ADHD Diagnosis Not Requiring Psychiatrist

## Claim

UK regulatory framework permits ADHD diagnosis by a multi-professional team, not exclusively by psychiatrists. This means nurse practitioners, clinical psychologists, or other qualified professionals can participate in the diagnostic process under appropriate governance.

## Basis

During the GP Confederation meeting on 2025-01-22, Rebecca Wilson and the clinical team discussed designing a "multi-professional diagnosis model" for the ADHD pathway. This was presented as a UK regulatory reality that differs from the Israel model where psychiatrists perform all diagnoses.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: GP Confed meeting explicitly references "multi-professional diagnosis model (UK allows non-psychiatrist diagnosis)" as a pathway design element
- Pending: Confirmation of exact scope — which non-psychiatrist roles can diagnose, under what supervision

## Impact

Significantly affects UK staffing model and cost structure. If non-psychiatrist professionals can diagnose ADHD, Telia'z can scale faster and at lower cost than if limited to psychiatrists only. This also affects the AI-assisted workflow design — case manager sessions may carry more diagnostic weight in the UK than in Israel.

# Balcony Privacy Is a Housing Selection Criterion

## Claim

Properties with balconies that feel too exposed to neighbors or passersby will be viewed negatively. Rami raised this as a concern during the 2025-10-09 apartment viewing, noting that the balcony felt too open.

## Basis

During the apartment viewing at a family-owned building on 2025-10-09, Rami specifically raised concerns about the balcony being too exposed. This suggests privacy — particularly visual privacy from the street and neighbors — is an important factor in housing selection, consistent with cultural expectations around family privacy.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-10-09: Concern raised about exposed balcony during apartment viewing

## Impact

Properties with ground-floor or street-facing balconies lacking privacy screening may be rejected or require modification (screens, plants, barriers). This criterion narrows the acceptable housing stock, particularly in dense village settings where buildings are close together.

# Physician Must Lead Medical Company as CEO

## Claim

Rami holds a governance principle that a medical company — especially one providing psychiatric services — should be led by a physician as CEO. This goes beyond the legal requirement for physician oversight of clinical operations (Bagatz rulings); it is a belief about organizational identity and governance philosophy. A company whose core business is medical services should have its highest authority understand medicine, not merely delegate clinical matters to a subordinate.

## Basis

Rami expressed this as a "desire for physician CEO" during his therapy session on 2025-12-05. The position is reinforced by his frustration that leadership decisions (growth targets, marketing strategy, HMO expansion) are made from a purely financial lens without clinical input. He observes that the weekly Sunday meetings have no space for clinical operations, quality metrics, or staff wellbeing — evidence that non-physician leadership structurally deprioritizes clinical concerns.

The Israeli legal framework (Bagatz rulings on hospital director qualifications) provides partial support, establishing that medical operations require physician oversight. Rami extends this principle to the CEO level for a company whose primary value proposition is medical care.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-05: Explicitly stated in therapy session as governance position
- 2025-12-05: Reinforced in personal debrief — frustration that financial-only leadership misses clinical needs
- 2026-02-22: Implicitly reinforced in CMO-vs-COO debate — Rami argues clinical authority requires operational authority

## Impact

This assumption directly informs Rami's position on the CMO/COO debate: if he believes physician leadership is essential but rejects the CEO role for personal sustainability reasons, the organizational design must find another mechanism to ensure physician authority at the highest level — whether through board representation, CMO veto power, or a steering committee structure.

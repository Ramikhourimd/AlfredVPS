# Clinical Practice Payment Does Not Scale with Hours Worked

## Claim

The clinical private practice bituh (license) payment appears to be a fixed allocation rather than strictly hourly. When Rami worked 15 hours in the initial month, revenue was approximately 8,000 NIS. When he worked 21 hours in a subsequent month (a 40% increase in hours), revenue remained approximately 8,000 NIS. This suggests the payment model has a cap or fixed component that does not automatically increase with additional hours worked.

## Basis

During the September 23, 2025 financial planning discussion, the accountant reviewed Rami's clinical practice numbers. The data shows:
- Initial allocation: 15 hours/month → ~8,000 NIS
- Recent month: 21 hours → still ~8,000 NIS

A 40% increase in hours produced no measurable increase in revenue, indicating a non-linear or capped payment structure.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-09-23: Initial data point — 15hrs/8K and 21hrs/8K discussed with accountant

## Impact

This assumption directly affects the pathway to the projected 18-20K NIS/month clinical income stabilization target. If payment is capped or fixed per allocation period, reaching 18-20K would require either:
1. Renegotiating the bituh terms or payment rate
2. Obtaining additional allocation slots
3. Supplementing with other patient streams outside the bituh framework

Understanding this cap is critical for realistic financial planning and the broader income diversification strategy.

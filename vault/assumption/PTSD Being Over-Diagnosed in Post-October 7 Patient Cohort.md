# PTSD Being Over-Diagnosed in Post-October 7 Patient Cohort

## Claim

Multiple clinicians at Telia'z Clinic Israel have raised concerns that PTSD is being over-diagnosed in the post-October 7 patient cohort. Patients presenting with general anxiety, depression, or trauma-related distress that does not meet full DSM-5 PTSD criteria (flashbacks, nightmares, intrusive re-experiencing) are receiving PTSD diagnoses. This concern is shared by senior case managers Sarah Pardes and Rivi Idelman.

## Basis

During the January 2025 HealthyMind Academy training session, Sarah Pardes raised the central concern: increasing cases where patients have trauma-related symptoms but lack hallmark PTSD features. Rivi Idelman observed a temporal shift — early post-October 7 patients showed clear PTSD presentations, while more recent arrivals present with milder symptoms and secondary-gain motivations. Maram Faris offered a counterpoint that PTSD exists on a spectrum and under-diagnosis is equally risky.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-13: Multiple clinicians raised over-diagnosis concern during HealthyMind Academy training
- Pattern shift observed: early post-Oct 7 patients had clear PTSD; later arrivals show milder, less specific presentations
- Counter-argument acknowledged: PTSD is a spectrum and under-diagnosis also carries risk

## Impact

If PTSD is being over-diagnosed, the clinic risks: (1) diluting the clinical seriousness of the diagnosis, (2) channeling patients into PTSD-specific treatment tracks when anxiety or adjustment disorder interventions may be more appropriate, (3) contributing to systemic diagnostic inflation that affects Bituach Leumi (National Insurance) assessments and HMO reporting. This directly affects the quality of clinical outcomes the clinic can report — a key metric in the clinical KPI cascade.

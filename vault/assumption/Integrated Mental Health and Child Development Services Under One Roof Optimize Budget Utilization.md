# Integrated Mental Health and Child Development Services Under One Roof Optimize Budget Utilization

## Claim

Mental health and child development services are deeply interconnected — most patients who arrive at mental health clinics started their journey in child development. Integrating both services under one organizational umbrella optimizes budget utilization and eliminates the surplus budget problem that plagued Clalit districts that couldn't spend their mental health allocation due to staffing shortages.

## Basis

Basel Kanaaneh described this as a key operational insight from his Clalit experience during the November 9, 2025 hiring interview. At Shefa-Amr, he integrated a child/youth mental health clinic (25 staff) with a neurology and child development center (combined 52 staff) in the same building. This integration model helped the Haifa/Western Galilee district fully utilize its mental health budget allocation while other districts returned unspent funds.

## Evidence Trail

- 2017-2025: Basel's integrated Shefa-Amr center successfully combined both service lines
- District-level comparison: Basel's district fully utilized budget while others had surplus (due to inability to recruit enough mental health staff independently)
- Budget philosophy: full utilization earned "excellence" recognition and additional funding the following year

## Impact

Telia'z Clinic Israel currently operates both adult and child psychiatry services. If the clinic expands into additional developmental services or deepens the children's clinic, Basel's integrated model could inform service architecture. The insight also applies to the UK expansion where ADHD services (a neurodevelopmental condition) bridge both mental health and child development domains.

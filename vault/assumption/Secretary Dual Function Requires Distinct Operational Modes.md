# Secretary Dual Function Requires Distinct Operational Modes

## Claim

The leadership team identifies two fundamentally different functions within the secretary role — patient-facing (appointment scheduling, reminders, complaints, tech support) and staff-facing (diary management for clinicians, shift coordination, schedule changes) — and treats these as requiring different operational approaches. This distinction drives the unresolved debate about whether secretaries should be assigned to specific pods (optimizing staff-facing diary management) or operate as a general pool (optimizing patient-facing availability).

## Basis

The Feb 22 meeting note explicitly separates secretarial work into two categories and frames the pod-dedicated vs. shared-pool question around this dual function. The assumption is that these two functions have different optimization requirements: staff-facing work benefits from relationship continuity (knowing "your" clinicians), while patient-facing work benefits from pooled availability (no single point of failure for incoming calls).

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Meeting note categorizes secretary functions into patient-facing and staff-facing, with no final decision on assignment model.

## Impact

This assumption shapes hiring criteria (do you hire for patient service skills or diary management skills?), training programs, scheduling of secretary shifts, and the ultimate pod assignment decision. If the dual function can actually be served by a single operational mode (e.g., all-purpose secretaries in a pool), the team may be over-engineering the solution. If the functions truly require specialization, the team may need to hire specifically for each function rather than treating secretaries as interchangeable.

# Existing Staff Can Absorb Onboarding Delivery Without Additional Resources

## Claim

Stav Zamir or Shira can deliver the combined technology and operational onboarding for new employees using their existing capacity, without requiring additional dedicated onboarding staff or significant time allocation. The assumption is that one or two sessions per month, led by current staff, is sufficient to onboard all new hires.

## Basis

During the 2026-02-12 onboarding discussion, Rami proposed that Stav or Shira would handle the combined technology + operational onboarding track. Elias confirmed he could support one or two sessions per month from his side. The proposal assumes this cadence and staffing model is sufficient without evaluating the cumulative time burden.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-12: Rami proposes Stav or Shira as onboarding delivery leads (four separate note records document this discussion)
- 2026-02-12: Elias confirms monthly/bi-monthly cadence is feasible from his perspective
- Pending: No validation against projected hiring volume or existing staff workload data

## Impact

If the clinic scales toward 176 quarter-position hires (as projected), the onboarding cadence would need to handle potentially 10-15+ new hires per month. At this volume, one or two sessions per month delivered by staff who have other primary responsibilities may become a bottleneck. Shira is already operating at half-position capacity as clinic manager. If this assumption fails, it creates a cascading delay: new hires arrive but cannot be onboarded promptly, which delays their clinical availability, which perpetuates the capacity crisis.

# PPF Applied to LED Headlights Causes Heat-Related Deterioration

## Claim

Paint Protection Film (PPF) applied to LED headlights generates trapped heat that causes the headlights to deteriorate and eventually burn out. A service center technician stated he has seen multiple cases of this pattern and advised never to apply PPF to headlights.

## Basis

During a vehicle service visit on 2025-10-09, headlight damage was discovered after PPF removal from a vehicle approximately 3 years old. The service center classified it as non-warranty "impact damage." The technician explained that PPF traps heat against LED headlights, causing progressive deterioration — a pattern he has observed across multiple vehicles.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-10-09: Technician at service center confirmed pattern across multiple vehicles
- Rami's vehicle showed headlight damage appearing after 3 years under PPF
- Service center refused warranty coverage, classifying as impact damage

## Impact

Future vehicle PPF installations should exclude headlight lenses. This is a maintenance decision relevant to any vehicle with LED headlights and aftermarket PPF application.

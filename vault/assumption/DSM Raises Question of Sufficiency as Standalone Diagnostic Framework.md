# DSM Raises Question of Sufficiency as Standalone Diagnostic Framework

## Claim

The bias finding — that psychiatrists diagnose PTSD based on contextual knowledge rather than strict DSM symptom criteria — raises fundamental questions about whether the DSM is sufficient as a standalone diagnostic tool, or whether clinical context necessarily supplements formal criteria in real-world psychiatric practice.

## Basis

The LLM experiment demonstrated that applying DSM criteria alone (unbiased expert agent) produces different diagnoses than applying DSM criteria with contextual awareness (biased expert agent), and that real psychiatrists align with the context-aware version. This implies that practicing psychiatrists routinely go beyond DSM criteria when diagnosing, incorporating environmental and population-level knowledge.

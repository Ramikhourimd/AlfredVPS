# Rami Wants Authority-Influence Alignment Not the CEO Title

## Claim

Rami's core motivator in the restructuring is not obtaining the CEO title — it is aligning his formal authority with his actual influence and contribution level. He explicitly stated his key motivator is "authority to match his actual influence, not the title itself." He is not fully committed to wanting the CEO role and acknowledges he loves innovation work more than operational leadership. What frustrates him is making decisions that affect the organization without the organizational standing to enforce them.

## Basis

During the December 2, 2025 private discussion with Oren, Rami explicitly distinguished between wanting the title and wanting decision-making power. He proposed: "If there's ever a decision about replacing Alex, at least discuss it with me before bringing someone external" — a governance request, not a title claim. In the Feb 22 meeting, he reiterated the same position: wanting formal mechanisms (steering committee, CMO veto power) rather than the CEO title.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Rami self-assesses as doing >50% of CEO work but distinguishes between wanting authority and wanting the title
- 2025-12-02: Rami says he "loves innovation work more" than operational CEO duties
- 2026-02-22: Rami pushes for steering committee and CMO veto power — structural authority, not title
- 2026-02-22: Rami was asked to define his preferred role — consistent with accepting a non-CEO title if authority is adequate

## Impact

This assumption is critical for the CMO role proposal (task/Define CMO Role Proposal). If the restructured CMO role provides genuine decision-making authority on clinical and strategic matters — through steering committee membership, veto power, and cross-functional inclusion — Rami may find it sustainable. The risk is creating a CMO title that carries nominal authority but no real influence, which would recreate the current frustration.

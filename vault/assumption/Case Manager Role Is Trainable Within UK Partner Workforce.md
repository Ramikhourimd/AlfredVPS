# Case Manager Role Is Trainable Within UK Partner Workforce

## Claim

The case manager role in Telia'z's three-step ADHD assessment workflow can be filled by training existing UK partner staff — specifically nurse prescribers, Psychological Wellbeing Practitioners (PWPs), clinical psychologists, or clinical social workers within GP Confederation or OMG Healthcare's workforce. The role does not require importing Israeli-trained staff long-term.

## Basis

During the UK ADHD platform demo rehearsal (2025-02-11), Adiel explicitly positioned the case manager role as "trainable within the GP Confed structure." The GP Confederation meeting (2025-01-22) discussed the case manager session as a 30-45 minute structured pre-assessment conducted by a psychologist, social worker, nurse practitioner, or junior psychiatrist — roles that already exist in UK NHS workforce pools.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: GP Confed meeting — case manager role described as fillable by multiple professional types
- 2025-02-11: Demo rehearsal — role explicitly positioned as trainable for GP Confed staff
- 2026-02-15: Team meeting — interim Israeli stopgap (Bassem) while UK recruitment proceeds

## Impact

If this assumption holds, Telia'z can scale UK operations without importing staff, reducing costs and regulatory complexity. If it fails — if the AI-assisted workflow requires specialized training that UK NHS staff resist or cannot absorb quickly — the staffing bottleneck could delay service scaling significantly.

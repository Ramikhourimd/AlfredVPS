# Basel-Leah Proposals Accepted as Design Authority Without Institutional Validation

## Claim

The leadership team accepted Basel and Leah's district model and three-pillar structure as the primary organizational framework without formally stress-testing it against institutional knowledge held by longer-tenured staff (Shira, Renee, the clinical team). Both Basel (recently onboarded Operations Manager) and Leah (organizational consultant/partner) bring fresh perspective but limited exposure to the clinic's operational history, legacy commitments, and informal workflows that have developed over years of organic growth.

## Basis

Both the detailed and analytical notes from the Feb 22 meeting describe Basel and Leah as the primary presenters of the district model. The proposal was discussed constructively and reached "conceptual agreement" — but the notes record no systematic validation step where existing staff (Shira managing Maccabi operations, Renee managing clinical staff, secretaries handling daily workflows) were consulted about whether the proposed structure matches operational reality.

The existing synthesis "Operational Framing Gains Traction Where Analytical Framing Was Dismissed" confirms that Basel's operational framing succeeded where Rami's analytical approach was dismissed — but success of framing does not equal validation of content.

## Evidence Trail

- Feb 22 meeting: Basel/Leah present district model; discussed favorably
- No recorded validation session with Shira, Renee, or clinical staff
- Rami's counter-proposals exist but remain unmerged (per "Operational and Clinical Proposal Tracks Remain Unmerged" synthesis)

## Impact

If the district model contains assumptions that conflict with operational reality (e.g., secretarial workflows, Maccabi-specific constraints, clinical forum logistics), these will surface during implementation rather than during design — increasing cost of correction and risk of staff resistance.

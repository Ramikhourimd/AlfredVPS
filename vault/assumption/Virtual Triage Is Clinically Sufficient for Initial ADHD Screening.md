# Virtual Triage Is Clinically Sufficient for Initial ADHD Screening

## Claim

Phone or virtual triage (not in-person) is clinically adequate for the initial inclusion/exclusion screening of ADHD patients entering the NHS pathway. OMG staff can perform health inequity and high-risk factor screening remotely without compromising clinical safety or accuracy.

## Basis

The OMG pathway design explicitly specifies triage as "conducted by phone or virtual (not in-person at clinic)." This was designed during the Rami-Adiel pathway mapping session where each stage was systematically defined.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-07-03: OMG pathway design meeting — triage explicitly designed as phone/virtual
- Existing constraint: ICB commissioners prefer face-to-face services over virtual (tension with this assumption)

## Impact

This assumption is in tension with the known constraint that ICB commissioners in Leeds prefer face-to-face services. If virtual triage is challenged during tender evaluation or CQC inspection, the pathway may need redesign with physical clinic touchpoints at the triage stage, increasing operational costs and reducing Telia'z's virtual-first advantage.

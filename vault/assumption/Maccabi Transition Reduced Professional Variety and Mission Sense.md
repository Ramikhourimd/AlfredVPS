# Maccabi Transition Reduced Professional Variety and Mission Sense

## Claim

The transition from serving post-October 7th southern trauma patients (Clalit South) to routine Maccabi HMO patients reduced psychiatrists' sense of professional challenge and social mission, making the work feel like "just another HMO clinic" with the added downsides of remote work and documentation burden.

## Basis

Psychiatrist Ilya reported this directly to Rivi: serving southern patients provided professional variety (complex PTSD cases) and a sense of social importance. The Maccabi caseload is routine and lacks this dimension, making the job purely transactional (convenience + pay) rather than intrinsically motivating.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Reported by Rivi from staff interview with Ilya
- Correlates with broader finding that psychiatrists are retention risks if better offers appear

## Impact

If professional variety and mission are key retention drivers, the clinic may need to:
- Actively create professional development opportunities
- Ensure diverse caseloads where possible
- Communicate the broader social impact of accessible mental health care
- Consider specialty tracks or complex case exposure as retention tools

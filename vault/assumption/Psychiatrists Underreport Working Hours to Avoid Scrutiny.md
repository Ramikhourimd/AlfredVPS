# Psychiatrists Underreport Working Hours to Avoid Scrutiny

## Claim

Psychiatrists at Telia'z regularly work unreported extra hours — arriving early to review complex cases, reviewing AI summaries after sessions, doing additional documentation — but do not report these hours. They believe reporting would result in questions about why they need extra time, since the official model assumes tasks fit within scheduled hours. Case managers are less affected because their extra hours are not as far above expectations.

## Basis

Rivi's qualitative interviews with multiple psychiatrists. They explicitly described not reporting preparation and post-session review time. The pattern appears systematic, not individual.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Initial finding (high confidence — multiple independent sources)
- Improvement in AI summaries may partially reduce post-session documentation time

## Impact

- Actual labor cost per session is higher than what payroll data shows
- Burnout metrics based on reported hours underestimate true workload
- Any scheduling optimization must account for this hidden labor
- If AI summary quality improves, this specific time sink may decrease

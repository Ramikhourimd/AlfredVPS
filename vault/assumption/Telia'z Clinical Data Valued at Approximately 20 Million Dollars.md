## Statement

Telia'z clinical data is internally valued at approximately $20 million, described as "golden data." The strategic thesis is that the data asset is becoming the primary product, with the clinical service as the data generation engine.

## Evidence

- Stated by Rami at the November 2025 conference
- Data includes: clinical transcriptions, AI-generated summaries, structured outcome metrics, questionnaire responses, session metadata
- Scale: ~6,000 active patients, 200 new patients/week, 10 psychiatrists generating continuous data
- Currently at estimated <5% of potential Maccabi market penetration

## Conditions for Validity

- Valuation depends on data quality, structure, and regulatory compliance (de-identification)
- Requires established data licensing or partnership frameworks
- Value increases with scale (more patients, longer longitudinal records)
- Competitive landscape for clinical mental health datasets affects pricing

## Risks if Wrong

- If data is not monetizable due to regulatory constraints, the valuation is theoretical
- If data quality is insufficient for research/pharma use cases, value drops significantly
- Building strategy around data-as-product while clinical service generates losses could be risky

## Open Questions

- What valuation methodology was used to arrive at ~$20M?
- Is this based on comparable dataset transactions in mental health?
- What de-identification and consent frameworks are in place for data commercialization?

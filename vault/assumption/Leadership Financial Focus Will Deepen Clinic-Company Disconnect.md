# Leadership Financial Focus Will Deepen Clinic-Company Disconnect

## Claim

If Telia'z leadership (Alex, Oren, Dekkel) continues setting 2026 goals with a purely financial lens — patient volumes, expense ratios, HMO expansion — without integrating clinical operations metrics (psychiatrist wellbeing, protocol compliance, quality KPIs), the disconnect between the company's strategic layer and the clinic's operational reality will widen. This will lead to commitments the clinic cannot fulfill (e.g., new HMO agreements without capacity to serve patients).

## Basis

- Rami's direct experience: 2026 goal-setting session focused entirely on financial targets
- Rami attempted to present ~60 operational KPIs; Alex derailed by questioning basic numbers
- Sunday leadership meetings consist entirely of marketing/business metrics
- Rami predicts signing a Leumit agreement within months that the clinic cannot operationally support
- The pattern is consistent: leadership decides, then hands targets down without clinical input

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-04: Leadership meeting where Alex, Oren, and Dekkel set 2026 financial targets. Rami stopped presenting after ~10% of material due to frustration.
- 2025-12-05: Rami debriefs in personal conversation, predicting growing disconnect.

## Impact

If true: the clinic will face a capacity crisis when business development produces commitments (new HMOs, patient volume) that operations cannot fulfill. Legal exposure increases as growth outpaces protocol development. Staff burnout accelerates.

If false: leadership may have heard more than Rami perceives, and subsequent meetings may show course correction.

# Psychiatry Academy Serves Dual Purpose of Recruitment and Quality

## Claim

The Psychiatry Academy (structured training program for tech-enabled psychiatry) will simultaneously serve as a recruitment value proposition and a quality improvement mechanism. Specifically: (a) it will position Telia'z as an attractive workplace for clinicians seeking cutting-edge practice, and (b) it will materially improve clinical quality through structured onboarding and upskilling.

## Basis

Rami proposed the academy during the 2026-02-15 team meeting. Adi Lavi confirmed the marketing narrative is shifting toward innovation/technology positioning. The first cohort enrollment was targeted for early March 2026 with training starting mid-March. Rami explicitly cautioned that the program content must be built before it's marketed externally — indicating awareness that the recruitment benefit depends on actual delivery.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: Academy concept introduced during team meeting. Adi enthusiastic about marketing angle.
- Rami cautioned: content must exist before external marketing claims are made (aligns with existing assumption that innovation marketing must deliver value before promotion)

## Impact

If the recruitment assumption holds, the academy becomes a key differentiator for clinician hiring — especially valuable given existing staff burnout and retention challenges. If the quality assumption holds, it addresses concerns about inconsistent clinical practice identified through staff interviews.

Risk: If the academy is launched primarily as a marketing tool without substantive clinical content, it could damage credibility with both prospective and existing staff. The dual-purpose framing may create tension between marketing timelines (wanting content fast) and quality requirements (needing rigorous curriculum development).

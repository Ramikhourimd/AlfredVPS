# Payment Increase Primarily Driven by Prime Rate Rises

## Claim

The 1,200 NIS monthly payment increase (3,400 → 4,600 NIS) is primarily caused by Prime rate increases affecting the variable-rate (Prime-linked) track of the mortgage.

## Basis

Both consultation notes describe the increase as "likely driven by Prime rate increases" and "likely driven by Prime rate rises affecting the variable-rate track." The mortgage has a Prime-linked variable rate track, which would be directly affected by Bank of Israel rate changes since the mortgage originated in May 2020 (when rates were historically low).

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-01-26: RiseUp consultation identified Prime-linked track as probable driver of payment increase
- No formal breakdown of per-track payment changes has been documented yet

## Impact

This assumption underpins the refinancing strategy. If the Prime rate is the primary driver, restructuring away from Prime exposure would stabilize payments. If other factors contribute significantly, the savings estimate may change.

# Case Manager Role Is Trainable Within UK Local Healthcare Workforce

## Claim

The case manager role in Telia'z's three-step clinical workflow does not require specialist psychiatric training. It can be filled by nurse prescribers, psychological wellbeing practitioners (PWPs), clinical psychologists, or clinical social workers already available in the UK healthcare workforce. The demo rehearsal explicitly positioned this role as "trainable within the GP Confed structure."

## Basis

During the UK ADHD Platform Demo Rehearsal (2025-02-11), Adiel positioned the case manager session as conductable by staff equivalent to "a nurse prescriber or PWP in the UK context." The GP Confederation meeting (2025-01-22) also discussed the case manager role as a structured pre-assessment that collects data before the psychiatrist — implying it is protocol-driven rather than requiring deep clinical judgment.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: GP Confed meeting — three-step workflow presented, case manager described as psychologist/social worker/nurse practitioner
- 2025-02-11: Demo rehearsal — case manager role explicitly "positioned as trainable within the GP Confed structure"
- 2026-02-15: Team meeting — Israeli case manager (Bassem) used as stopgap, suggesting the role can be performed with training rather than specialist background

## Impact

If confirmed, this significantly reduces UK recruitment difficulty and cost. Rather than importing Israeli staff or hiring specialist psychiatric nurses, Telia'z/partners can train existing NHS workforce (PWPs, practice nurses) for the case manager role. This directly affects scalability and the GP Confed partnership model where Confed provides the workforce.

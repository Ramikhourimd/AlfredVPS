# Diaspora Relatives in Beirut and Chile Connect to Shaddad Family

## Claim

Two specific diaspora individuals are claimed as Shaddad family relatives: Yacoub al-Asif (يعقوب الأصيف), associated with a church house in Beirut, and Boulos (Paul / بولس), who emigrated to Chile. These connections extend the Shaddad family network beyond the three Galilee branches (Eilabun, Rameh, Nazareth) to an international diaspora.

## Basis

Majdi Shaddad mentioned both individuals during the 2025-12-02 conversation as family connections. The Beirut and Chile diaspora claims appear in two of the three conversation records. No documentation of the specific family relationship (which branch, what generation) has been provided.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi mentioned Yacoub al-Asif at a church house in Beirut
- 2025-12-02: Majdi mentioned Boulos (Paul) in Chile
- Both conversation records that include detail sections reference these diaspora connections
- No genealogical documentation linking these individuals to specific Shaddad branches

## Impact

If verified, these connections would expand the family tree significantly and could provide additional sources of family records and oral history. The Chile connection is particularly interesting as it aligns with well-documented Levantine Christian emigration to Latin America in the late 19th/early 20th century. However, without specific genealogical links, these remain unverified claims at low confidence.

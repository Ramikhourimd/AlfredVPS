# AI-Generated Clinical Reports Acceptable to UK Regulators

## Claim

The team assumes that AI-generated clinical reports (produced in ~10 seconds post-session) will be accepted by UK regulatory bodies including CQC, NICE, and NHS commissioners. The Israel system generates psychiatrist summaries, diagnostic reports, and treatment plans using AI, and this capability is being presented to UK partners as a core differentiator without explicit validation that UK regulators permit AI-authored clinical documentation.

## Basis

In the GP Confederation meeting (2025-01-22), Adiel presented the AI report generation as a key capability — "AI generates final report in ~10 seconds post-session." In the demo rehearsal (2025-02-11), the team scripted a walkthrough showing AI-generated summaries as a standard part of the clinical workflow. Neither session included discussion of whether UK clinical governance frameworks explicitly permit AI-authored reports or require human attestation/review steps beyond what exists in the Israel model.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: AI report generation presented to GP Confed without regulatory challenge raised
- 2025-02-11: Demo rehearsal includes AI summary generation as standard workflow stage
- New NICE digital/AI guidance for neurodevelopmental services released ~Dec 2024 — not yet reviewed against this assumption

## Impact

If UK regulators require additional human review steps or prohibit AI-generated clinical documentation, the core efficiency proposition (60% psychiatrist time savings) would be significantly reduced. Report generation workflow would need redesign. Decisions depending on this: the entire service model's economic viability at NHS tariff pricing.

# Evening and Night Clinical Practice From Home Is Part of Housing Vision

## Claim

Beyond simply having a room suitable for clinical practice, Rami envisions conducting evening and night psychiatric sessions from a home-based clinic. The ability to "work at night from a home clinic" was specifically cited as a significant advantage when evaluating the investment property on 2025-10-09.

## Basis

During a family conversation evaluating a property with ground-floor clinic potential ([[note/Property Investment Discussion Family Conversation 2025-10-09]]), the ability to work at night from the home clinic was explicitly called out as a benefit. This goes beyond the general preference for clinical practice space (captured in [[assumption/Home-Based Clinical Practice Space Is a Housing Selection Factor]]) by adding a temporal dimension.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-10-09: Night clinical work mentioned as property advantage during investment property evaluation

## Impact

If confirmed, this assumption materially changes property requirements beyond just "a room for a clinic":
- **Separate entrance**: Patients arriving at night should not disturb sleeping family members
- **Sound insulation**: Clinical conversations must not carry to bedrooms
- **Exterior lighting and safety**: Patients need safe access after dark
- **Quiet neighborhood**: Residential calm for both practice and family
- **Parking**: Evening patients need available parking
- **Ground floor preferred**: To avoid patients traversing residential floors

This elevates properties with genuine ground-floor clinical layouts (like the ~850K investment property) relative to those where a bedroom could theoretically serve as an office.

# Psychiatrist Retention Driven by Convenience Not Organizational Commitment

## Claim

Psychiatrists at Telia'z remain primarily because of convenience factors — work-from-home flexibility and competitive pay — rather than organizational commitment or professional satisfaction. If a comparable or better offer appeared, many would likely leave.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman's qualitative interviews with psychiatrists revealed that intrinsic motivation is low. Staff describe feeling like "orphaned children without parents," unsupported by management, burdened by unreported extra hours, and frustrated by difficult patients arriving without screening. The transition from mission-driven trauma work (post-October 7th) to routine Maccabi HMO caseloads further eroded the sense of professional purpose. Convenience (WFH, pay) is the primary anchor.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi reported this pattern across multiple psychiatrist interviews
- Case managers show stronger commitment by contrast — most plan to stay, though one left due to workload

## Impact

If convenience-based retention is the dominant driver, any market shift (competitor offering WFH + higher pay, or reduced Telia'z compensation) could trigger rapid attrition. Retention strategy must either increase intrinsic motivation (mission, professional growth, community) or maintain a significant convenience premium.

# Private Patient Track and NHS Track Can Operate Simultaneously Without Conflict

## Claim

The team is pursuing both a private patient track (via Leon's CQC, targeting March 2026 launch) and an NHS patient track (via OMG/GP Confed CQC, targeting NHS tender cycle) simultaneously. The implicit assumption is that these two tracks serve different patient populations, operate under different commercial models, and can run in parallel without regulatory, operational, or reputational conflicts.

## Basis

- The Leon track targets private patients who find the service through Google Ads and Leon's practice website
- The OMG/GP Confed track targets NHS patients referred through GP pathways and Right to Choose
- Different CQC registrations underpin each track (Leon's personal CQC vs OMG's organizational CQC)
- Different revenue models: Leon receives a percentage of patient revenue; GP Confed receives a share from NHS tariff
- Team meetings discuss both tracks without surfacing potential conflicts

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: GP Confed meeting establishes NHS partnership pathway
- 2025-07-03: OMG pathway meeting designs NHS ADHD clinical flow
- 2026-02-15: Team meeting confirms Leon Track 1 launching imminently while Track 2 (NHS) continues in parallel

## Impact

If this assumption is wrong, several risks emerge:
- **CQC conflict:** Operating the same clinical service under two different CQC registrations could raise regulatory questions about governance boundaries
- **Brand confusion:** Patients seeing "Healthy Mind" through Leon's website AND through NHS referral may create confusion about the service identity
- **Resource competition:** Same psychiatrists, case managers, or platform capacity shared across both tracks could create bottlenecks
- **Commercial tension:** If GP Confed discovers Telia'z is also running a parallel private service in the same geography, it could undermine the NHS partnership trust
- **Pricing arbitrage:** Different pricing between private (uncapped) and NHS (tariff-capped) tracks could create perverse incentives

# Preemptive Fee Schedule Standardization Can Prevent Ad-Hoc Rate Negotiations

## Claim

By issuing unified, standardized fee schedules (tariffs) and requiring all clinicians to sign updated agreements simultaneously, the clinic can prevent the pattern of individual psychiatrists demanding ad-hoc rate adjustments based on peer comparisons or external market information.

## Basis

When Elinor and Noa demanded rate increases after hearing about expert rates (450 NIS/session), management chose not to negotiate individually. Instead, they decided to issue standardized new fee schedules for all clinicians and have everyone sign updated agreements. The logic is that a formal, published rate structure leaves less room for individual negotiation.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-04: Individual rate demands from Elinor and Noa triggered the standardization decision
- Decision to unify all employment agreements already in progress (decision/Unify Staff Employment Agreements)
- Three-tier structure (decision/Adopt Three-Tier Psychiatrist Compensation Structure) provides the framework

## Impact

- If this assumption holds, it significantly reduces management time spent on compensation negotiations
- If it fails, standardized rates may create a different set of grievances (clinicians feeling locked into tiers)
- The assumption is untested — no previous attempt at clinic-wide fee schedule standardization
- Success depends on the tier structure being perceived as fair by all clinician groups

# 6-8 Weekly Hours Is Sustainable Threshold for Telepsychiatry Staff

## Claim

Clinical staff (both case managers and psychiatrists) working 6-8 hours per week at Telia'z manage the intensity of telepsychiatry work reasonably well. Beyond this threshold, burnout increases significantly and staff report disproportionate exhaustion.

## Basis

Multiple staff interviews conducted by Rivi Idelman in early 2026 converge on this finding:
- One psychiatrist (Sa'id) found 3-4 hours daily across 5 days draining, but 1.5 hours daily sustainable — even compressing the same weekly total into one long day would be unacceptable
- A case manager reported feeling "much more empathetic and attentive" at 5 hours versus 9 hours of work
- Multiple staff described a nonlinear relationship between hours and exhaustion — it is the intensity per hour, not total hours, that drives burnout
- One case manager described leaving Telia'z shifts more exhausted than hospital residency shifts, despite residency having longer hours

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi reports consistent pattern across multiple psychiatrists and case managers

## Impact

This assumption directly affects scheduling policy, recruitment promises, and workforce planning. If validated, it suggests Telia'z should optimize for shorter, more frequent shifts rather than longer consolidated blocks. It also implies current staff working beyond 8 hours/week may be at elevated burnout risk.

# Three Shaddad Branches Share Common Ancestry

## Claim

The Shaddad families in Eilabun, Rameh, and Nazareth are all cousins (awlad al-amm) — branches of the same original Shaddad family that split during settlement in the Galilee.

## Basis

Majdi Shaddad, from the Rameh branch, has been tracing these connections since approximately 1980. All source records consistently identify three branches: Eilabun, Rameh, and Nazareth. The Rameh branch is where the Khouri surname originated (from a family member who served as priest). The branching appears to have occurred during the post-1865 settlement period in the Galilee.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi explicitly described three cousin branches in conversation with Rami
- All three research notes consistently list the same three branches
- Based on oral tradition and Majdi's genealogical research — no documentary verification noted

## Impact

This assumption is foundational to the genealogy project. It defines the scope of the family tree — connecting Khouri (Rameh), Shaddad (Eilabun), and Shaddad (Nazareth) as one extended family. If any branch is not actually related, the family tree structure would need significant revision.

# Role Restructuring Savings Can Offset Personal Assistant Hiring Cost

## Claim

If Rami saves approximately 40,000 NIS annually through role restructuring (consolidating his four roles into two defined positions), those savings can fund or substantially offset the cost of hiring a personal organizational assistant (Amani Rohana). The financial model treats the PA hire as budget-neutral by redirecting savings from role consolidation.

## Basis

During the November 9, 2025 planning discussion with a family advisor, Rami mentioned that 40,000 NIS in savings from role restructuring could cover the PA cost. The advisor's market salary estimate for Amani is 10,000-13,000 NIS gross monthly, which translates to roughly 120,000-156,000 NIS annually — significantly more than 40,000 NIS. This gap has not been explicitly addressed in the source records.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-09: Family advisor discussion references 40K savings as funding mechanism
- 2025-11-09: Compensation discussion with Dekkel explores salary restructuring details
- Amani market salary estimated at 10K-13K NIS/month (120K-156K annually)
- Gap between 40K savings and 120K+ annual cost not addressed

## Impact

If this assumption is incorrect, the PA hire either requires additional budget approval from Oren/Dekkel or must be justified on independent grounds. The financial viability of the entire PA plan depends on this calculation holding up.

# Organizational Health Score Must Clear Threshold Before Growth

## Claim

Clinic growth (adding patients, onboarding new psychiatrists) should only begin after a quantitative organizational health score — incorporating patient satisfaction, staff workload, and burnout indicators — crosses a defined threshold. Rami estimates this threshold can be reached within six months. Premature growth risks increased wait times, quality erosion, and loss of the clinic's premium positioning.

## Basis

Rami built a quantitative model that calculates an organizational health score as a growth readiness indicator. He views this as a critical safeguard against the kind of reactive expansion that management pushes (e.g., absorbing 350 patients from a departed Clalit South psychiatrist without preparation).

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-05: Rami describes the model in therapy session, expresses frustration that management ignores it and pushes growth immediately
- Management pushed to contact Adi about absorbing Clalit South patients despite Rami's objections

## Impact

If this assumption holds, the clinic should delay patient acquisition pushes until the health score clears the threshold. If management overrides it, Rami predicts quality degradation and loss of differentiation.

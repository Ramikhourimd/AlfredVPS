# Maccabi Transition Reduced Psychiatrist Professional Motivation

## Claim

The transition from serving post-October 7th trauma patients to standard Maccabi HMO patients reduced psychiatrists' sense of professional purpose and clinical variety. Before the transition, work had social importance (helping displaced southern residents) and unique clinical challenge (PTSD, acute trauma). Now it feels like "just another HMO clinic" but with worse conditions (remote isolation, summary burden).

## Basis

Psychiatrist Ilya explicitly described this to Rivi Idelman. The broader pattern — that psychiatrists stay primarily for convenience rather than engagement — was a recurring theme across multiple staff conversations.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Ilya's direct feedback to Rivi, corroborated by general psychiatrist sentiment
- This aligns with the broader retention risk identified by Rivi

## Impact

- Psychiatrist retention is a convenience-based equilibrium, not engagement-based — fragile to competitive offers
- Restoring professional meaning may require: clinical variety programs, specialized caseloads, research involvement, or professional development pathways
- Recruitment messaging that emphasizes "innovative telepsychiatry" rings hollow if daily work feels routine

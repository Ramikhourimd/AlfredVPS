# Israeli District Model is Replicable for International Expansion

## Claim

The organizational structure being designed for the Israel clinic — the district model with Clinical Director, Operations Manager, and Administrative Manager plus centralized shared services — can be replicated in other countries (starting with the UK) with minimal adaptation beyond local regulatory compliance.

## Basis

The UK Expansion project explicitly states the structure "must support replicating the Israeli clinic model internationally." The district model and shared services decisions are being designed with this replicability as a core design constraint. However, no formal analysis of UK healthcare regulations, market differences, or cultural factors has been conducted to validate this assumption.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: UK Expansion project references replication of Israeli model
- 2026-02-22: District model and shared services decisions shaped by scaling intent
- No UK regulatory analysis or market study found in records

## Impact

If the Israeli model does not translate well internationally, the entire organizational restructuring may need rework. Key risks include: different regulatory environments requiring fundamentally different management structures, cultural differences in remote psychiatry adoption, and shared services that cannot effectively support multiple time zones or regulatory regimes.

# Scaling Requires Approximately 176 Individual Quarter-Position Hires

## Claim

Based on Maccabi data analysis, reaching the projected year-end active patient count of ~7,786 requires approximately 44 FTE positions (24 psychiatrists + 21 case managers). Because most clinicians work quarter-positions (approximately 25% time), this translates to roughly 176 individual hires (44 FTE × 4). This staffing math has significant implications for recruitment pipeline capacity, onboarding processes, and administrative overhead.

## Basis

Rami presented this analysis at the 2025-12-04 strategic meeting using clinic database data from Maccabi. The calculation chain: ~7,786 projected patients → required clinical capacity → ~44 FTE → ~176 individuals at quarter-positions. The current active patient base is ~1,000, meaning the clinic needs to scale staffing roughly 4x from current levels.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-04: Strategic meeting — Rami presented patient projections and staffing requirements derived from Maccabi database
- The 4x multiplier (quarter-positions to FTE) is based on the current staffing model where most clinicians work part-time

## Impact

This assumption drives the urgency of the organizational restructuring. If the quarter-position model holds, the clinic's HR, onboarding, payroll, and supervision infrastructure must handle 176 individual relationships — not 44. This multiplier effect explains why payroll fragmentation is critical to resolve early, why the district model with a Medical Director is needed for clinical supervision at scale, and why administrative protocols must be standardized before growth accelerates.

The assumption is vulnerable if: (a) the patient growth projection is revised, (b) the staffing ratio changes (e.g., through efficiency gains), or (c) the clinic shifts toward larger position sizes per clinician.

# Medical Director Will Have Dual Reporting Lines

## Claim

The new Medical Director will operate under a dual reporting structure: reporting to the CMO on clinical matters, and coordinating with the Operations Manager on day-to-day clinic operations. This is a matrix management arrangement, not a simple hierarchical one.

## Basis

The acceptance criteria for the Medical Director hiring task explicitly state: "Reporting lines defined (to CMO on clinical matters, coordination with Operations Manager)." This specifies two distinct authority relationships — clinical governance flowing upward to the CMO, and operational coordination flowing laterally to the Operations Manager.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Hiring task acceptance criteria specify dual reporting structure

## Impact

This dual reporting structure:
- Requires clear delineation of clinical vs operational authority to avoid conflict
- Means the Medical Director must navigate two chains of command
- Could create tension if CMO clinical priorities conflict with operational efficiency goals
- Needs explicit documentation of which decisions fall under which reporting line
- Implies the Operations Manager role has sufficient authority to coordinate with a physician leader

# Israel Discharge Framework Can Free Case Manager Capacity for UK Launch

## Claim

Rami proposed a patient discharge framework during the February 15, 2026 team meeting to address the Israel clinic's 8% intake conversion rate (945 new patients, only 77 receiving intake appointments). The implicit assumption is that implementing systematic discharge processes will free sufficient case manager bandwidth to support UK launch staffing needs.

## Basis

The Israel clinic has accumulated patients who may no longer need active management, consuming case manager capacity that could otherwise be redirected. By implementing structured discharge criteria, some case managers could be partially reallocated to UK operations during the stopgap phase.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: Rami presented dashboard showing 945 new patients, only 77 (8%) receiving intakes. Proposed discharge framework as response.
- No evidence yet on discharge framework implementation timeline or expected capacity recovery.

## Impact

If this assumption holds, it provides an alternative path to UK staffing without external vendors. If it fails (discharge is slow or frees insufficient capacity), the UK launch timeline depends entirely on the vendor/call-center option or delayed local hiring.

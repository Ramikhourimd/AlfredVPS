# Renee Is the Right Fit for Administrative Manager

## Claim

The leadership team maps Renee to the Administrative Manager role (HR, secretarial oversight, onboarding, compliance) in the three-pillar district model without explicit discussion of her qualifications for this expanded management position. Renee is "currently mapped to" this role based on her existing administrative involvement, not a formal assessment.

## Basis

The Feb 22 meeting note identifies Renee as the Administrative Manager in Basel and Leah's proposed structure. Renee currently handles administrative functions, making her the natural candidate. However, the role as defined — HR oversight, compliance, onboarding processes — represents a significant scope expansion from hands-on secretarial and administrative work to a management position overseeing these functions.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Meeting note states Administrative Manager "currently mapped to Renee."
- Existing contradiction records note tension about whether Renee should be clinic-exclusive or shared across the organization.

## Impact

The assumption that Renee can transition from hands-on administrative work to a management role affects: (1) the viability of the three-pillar model, (2) hiring plans for secretarial replacements, and (3) the timeline for the restructuring. If Renee struggles with the management scope, the administrative pillar becomes a weak link in the district model.

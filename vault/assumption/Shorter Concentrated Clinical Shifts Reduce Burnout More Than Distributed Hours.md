# Shorter Concentrated Clinical Shifts Reduce Burnout More Than Distributed Hours

## Claim

For remote telepsychiatry work at Telia'z, shorter daily shifts (e.g., 1.5 hours/day) produce significantly less burnout than longer shifts (e.g., 3-4 hours/day spread across 5 days), even when total weekly hours are comparable. Staff working 6-8 hours/week manage better than those with higher weekly totals.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman's qualitative interviews. One psychiatrist (Said) reported that switching from 3-4 hours/day across 5 days to 1.5 hours/day dramatically reduced burnout, and he would not consolidate those hours into a single long day. Rivi also confirmed this pattern on herself — her burnout is noticeably lower at 5 hours than at 9 hours daily.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi reported this pattern from multiple staff interviews during meeting with Rami

## Impact

If validated quantitatively (through the planned satisfaction survey), this could inform scheduling policy — recommending shorter, more frequent shifts over longer concentrated ones. This would be a structural intervention against burnout that doesn't require hiring more staff.

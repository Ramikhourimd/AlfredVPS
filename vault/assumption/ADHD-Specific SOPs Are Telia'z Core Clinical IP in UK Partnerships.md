# ADHD-Specific SOPs Are Telia'z Core Clinical IP in UK Partnerships

## Claim

The ADHD-specific standard operating procedures (SOPs) — not just the AI platform or technology — are Telia'z's proprietary clinical intellectual property and primary differentiator in UK partnerships. In the Leon partnership, "all other SOPs are his" — only the ADHD clinical flow SOPs come from Telia'z. This positions the ADHD workflow methodology, not the technology alone, as the core value Telia'z brings to clinical partnerships.

## Basis

In the task record for sharing SOPs with Leon, the ADHD flow is explicitly described as "Telia'z's proprietary development and differentiation in the UK market." The Leon partnership structure makes this especially clear: Leon provides his own CQC registration, his own clinical SOPs for general practice, and his own governance — the ONLY clinical artifact Telia'z contributes is the ADHD-specific SOP set.

This contrasts with existing learning records that position the AI three-step workflow and technology platform as the core differentiator. In reality, the clinical SOPs (which define HOW the three steps are executed, what instruments are used, what clinical decisions are made at each stage) are the foundational IP that the technology merely supports.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: Task created to share ADHD SOPs with Leon — explicitly calls them "proprietary development and differentiation"
- 2026-02-15: Team meeting confirms "all other SOPs are his [Leon's]" — only ADHD SOPs from Telia'z
- 2025-07-03: OMG pathway design meeting shows Rami defining detailed clinical pathway stages — these clinical decisions form the SOP content

## Impact

If the ADHD SOPs are the true differentiator (not just the AI), this affects:
- IP protection strategy — SOPs need formal documentation and protection
- Partnership negotiations — Telia'z's leverage depends on SOP exclusivity, not just technology licensing
- Scalability — SOPs are transferable without technology, meaning partners could potentially replicate the workflow independently
- Competitive moat — Technology can be replicated; clinical methodology validated across 2,700+ patients is harder to reproduce

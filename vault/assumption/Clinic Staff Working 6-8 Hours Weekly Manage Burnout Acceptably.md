# Clinic Staff Working 6-8 Hours Weekly Manage Burnout Acceptably

## Claim

Clinical staff (both psychiatrists and case managers) who work 6-8 hours per week at Telia'z Clinic Israel report manageable levels of burnout. Beyond this threshold, burnout increases significantly and becomes a retention risk.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman's qualitative interviews with multiple staff members during Q1 2026. One psychiatrist (Said) specifically reported that reducing from 3-4 hours daily (across 5 days) to 1.5 hours daily dramatically improved his experience. Case managers report similar patterns — those at lower weekly hours cope better.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Initial qualitative finding from Rivi's interviews with case managers and psychiatrists
- Needs quantitative validation via structured survey (see task: Build Structured Staff Satisfaction Survey)

## Impact

If confirmed, this has direct implications for:
- Clinician scheduling and hiring strategy (prefer more clinicians at fewer hours each)
- Scaling model (need more quarter-position hires rather than fewer full-time)
- Retention strategy (monitor hours as leading indicator of attrition risk)

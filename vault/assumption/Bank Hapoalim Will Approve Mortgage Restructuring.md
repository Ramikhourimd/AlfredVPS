# Bank Hapoalim Will Approve Mortgage Restructuring

## Claim

The refinancing project implicitly assumes that Bank Hapoalim will approve the mortgage restructuring under Option A terms. Neither source record discusses bank approval as a risk or prerequisite — the consultation focuses entirely on which option Rami prefers, treating bank cooperation as a given.

## Basis

Both consultation summaries proceed directly from option selection to service tier payment, skipping any discussion of whether the bank might decline, impose different terms, or require additional conditions. RiseUp presents the options as achievable outcomes rather than proposals subject to bank approval.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-01-26: RiseUp consultation presents Option A and Option B as available choices, with no mention of bank approval risk.

## Impact

If Bank Hapoalim declines the restructuring or offers materially different terms, the projected ~200,000 NIS savings would not materialize. The RiseUp service fee (2,400 or 6,000 NIS) would be spent without achieving the desired outcome. This assumption underpins every other assumption and decision in the refinancing project.

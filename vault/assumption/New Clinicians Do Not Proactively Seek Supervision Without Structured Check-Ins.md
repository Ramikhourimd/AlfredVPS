# New Clinicians Do Not Proactively Seek Supervision Without Structured Check-Ins

## Claim

Newly onboarded clinicians at Telia'z Clinic Israel do not proactively reach out for clinical consultation or supervision support, even when such support is available. Jenny had spoken with the senior clinician before starting (when she was at Rambam) but had not consulted about any cases after being assigned patients. Both Rami and the senior clinician had to agree to proactively follow up with her.

## Basis

- Jenny case (2025-11-10): Despite having a prior relationship with the senior clinician, Jenny did not initiate consultation about assigned cases
- The fully remote model eliminates the informal hallway conversations that naturally prompt supervision-seeking in physical clinics
- New clinicians may feel reluctant to appear uncertain or may not recognize when consultation is needed

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-10: Rami and senior clinician agreed to proactively check on Jenny (task/Check on Jenny Case Consultation and Wellbeing)

## Impact

The onboarding process must include structured, scheduled check-ins during the first weeks — not just availability of supervision. Relying on new clinicians to self-identify consultation needs creates clinical risk gaps, especially in a remote setting where isolation compounds the problem.

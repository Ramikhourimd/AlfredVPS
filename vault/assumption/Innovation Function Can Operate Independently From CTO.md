# Innovation Function Can Operate Independently From CTO

## Claim

Rami's innovation and AI work can operate as a separate function from Shachar's CTO responsibilities, with collaboration on shared projects but distinct reporting lines and authority. The two domains — clinical innovation and technology infrastructure — are assumed to be separable without creating organizational friction.

## Basis

The restructuring meeting discussed "Innovation & CTO Relationship" as a distinct topic and concluded that "Rami's innovation work to remain separate from Shachar's CTO responsibilities, with collaboration on shared projects." The board presentation task lists "CTO-CMO collaboration model" as a topic requiring articulation, suggesting the boundary is not yet clearly defined.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Meeting agreed innovation stays separate from CTO scope
- Board presentation planned to articulate the CTO-CMO collaboration model

## Impact

If innovation and CTO responsibilities overlap more than assumed — particularly around AI product development and technology infrastructure — this could create authority conflicts and duplicated effort. The need to present a "CTO-CMO collaboration model" to the board suggests this boundary is recognized as fragile.

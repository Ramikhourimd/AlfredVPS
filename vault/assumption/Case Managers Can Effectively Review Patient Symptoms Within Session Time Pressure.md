# Case Managers Can Effectively Review Patient Symptoms Within Session Time Pressure

## Claim

The redesigned case manager screen (tab-based navigation with a dedicated symptoms tab) assumes case managers can effectively review patient symptoms alongside questionnaire data within their available session time. Shira Lachman clarified during the January 2025 sprint meeting that case managers also review symptoms — not just psychiatrists — but flagged that they operate under significant time constraints.

## Basis

During the UX design review for the tab-based case manager screen, Shira noted that case managers review symptoms too and face time pressure doing so. The tab-based redesign gives symptoms its own tab, implicitly assuming this additional review responsibility is feasible within session windows. No time-motion study or workflow analysis has validated this.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-16: Shira raised time constraint concern during sprint meeting UX review

## Impact

If this assumption is wrong, the redesigned screen may add cognitive/time burden that reduces case manager effectiveness or gets skipped entirely. The tab-based design decision rests partly on this assumption. May need workflow timing validation before full rollout.

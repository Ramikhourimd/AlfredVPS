# Walkability and Neighborhood Liveliness Are Housing Selection Criteria

## Claim

Beyond proximity to an Arab community, the family requires a neighborhood with active street life and walkability. Rami's wife specifically expressed frustration that streets feel empty — especially at night — and that the current area is hard to reach on foot. She described this isolation as "the thing that bothers me the most honestly." This is a distinct criterion from Arab community proximity: a neighborhood could have Arab residents but still feel dead or unwalkable.

## Basis

During the 2025-10-09 neighborhood evaluation, the wife raised multiple related concerns:
- Streets feel empty, especially at night
- Limited walkability — hard to reach places on foot
- Feeling of isolation despite being in a residential area

These concerns were expressed with strong emotional weight, suggesting this is a high-priority filter.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-10-09: Wife explicitly stated isolation and empty streets are her top concern

## Impact

Properties in quiet, car-dependent suburbs may be rejected even if they meet other criteria (size, price, community). Neighborhoods like Kiryat Haim — described as "essentially part of Haifa" — may score better on this criterion than more suburban or peripheral locations.

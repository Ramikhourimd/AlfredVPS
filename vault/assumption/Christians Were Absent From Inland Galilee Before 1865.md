# Christians Were Absent From Inland Galilee Before 1865

## Claim

By approximately 1865, there were "almost no Christians living in the Galilee villages or inland areas" — the Christian community was concentrated on the Palestinian coast. This implies the current Christian presence in villages like Rameh, Eilabun, Maghar, and others dates only from the mid-1860s onward, not earlier.

## Basis

Majdi Shaddad stated this during the 2025-12-02 conversation, and the claim is repeated consistently across all three research notes. It serves as the foundational premise for Phase 4 of the migration timeline — without this claim, the narrative of coastal-to-inland migration loses its basis.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi stated "by 1865, there were almost no Christians in the Galilee villages" across multiple retellings
- All source notes reproduce this claim without qualification
- No primary historical sources have been consulted to verify or challenge this claim

## Impact

This is a strong historical claim with significant implications. If Christians were already present in Galilee villages before 1865, the entire Phase 3→Phase 4 transition in the migration narrative would need rethinking. Historical scholarship on Ottoman-era demographics in the Galilee could confirm or challenge this. The claim's confidence is rated low because it is an extraordinary demographic assertion relying solely on oral tradition.

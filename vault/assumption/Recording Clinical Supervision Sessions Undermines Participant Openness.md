# Recording Clinical Supervision Sessions Undermines Participant Openness

## Claim

Recording clinical supervision sessions (audio or video) fundamentally changes the group dynamic, reducing participant willingness to share personal experiences, acknowledge vulnerability, and engage in the emotional processing that makes supervision therapeutically effective. The presence of a recording device transforms a safe emotional space into a documented performance, undermining the core purpose of the session.

## Basis

During the 2025-11-10 debrief following the first Moli Lahad burnout supervision session, the team debated whether to record future sessions. The conclusion was that recording would compromise the emotional safety that made the session valuable. Key observations:

- Participants shared deeply personal experiences about burnout, isolation, and boundary struggles in remote telepsychiatry work
- The session included guided meditation and emotional exercises that require vulnerability
- Shira emphasized that "the value of the session is in being there, not in reading a summary afterward"
- Prof. Moli Lahad took handwritten notes capturing key themes — this was deemed the appropriate level of documentation
- The decision was made not to record sessions, codified in a separate decision record

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-10: First session held, debrief confirmed high emotional engagement and team cohesion impact
- 2025-11-10: Recording policy debated and rejected based on therapeutic value assessment
- Ori Shukron reported the session "strongly united" the case manager team — an outcome that depends on open sharing

## Impact

This assumption underpins the no-recording decision and has broader implications:
- Clinical supervision knowledge cannot be captured and redistributed through recordings
- New staff who miss sessions cannot "catch up" via playback — they must attend
- The clinic's clinical knowledge transfer model depends on synchronous presence, creating a scaling constraint
- Any future attempt to systematize supervision content (e.g., for onboarding materials) must find alternatives to recording

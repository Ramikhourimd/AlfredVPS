# Six to Eight Weekly Hours Is Sustainable Threshold for Telepsychiatry Staff

## Claim

Clinical staff (both case managers and psychiatrists) working 6-8 hours per week at the Telia'z telepsychiatry clinic manage the work intensity reasonably well. Beyond this threshold, burnout increases significantly. Additionally, distributed shorter blocks (e.g., 1.5 hours/day) are more sustainable than concentrated longer blocks (e.g., 3-4 hours/day), even with the same total weekly hours.

## Basis

Qualitative data collected by Rivi Idelman from multiple staff conversations:
- Psychiatrist Said: reported 3-4 hours daily across 5 days was very draining; 1.5 hours daily works much better
- Case manager: described leaving Telia'z shifts feeling more exhausted than residency despite sometimes shorter hours
- Multiple staff confirmed burnout correlates with hours beyond 6-8/week
- Rivi herself observed significant empathy/attentiveness improvement at 5 hours vs. 9 hours

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Initial qualitative finding reported by Rivi Idelman during meeting with Rami
- Needs validation through structured survey (see [[task/Build Structured Employee Satisfaction Survey Instrument]])

## Impact

If confirmed, this has major implications for:
- Scheduling optimization (favor distributed short blocks over concentrated long ones)
- Clinician contract structuring (may need more clinicians at fewer hours each)
- Capacity planning (4x hiring volume multiplied by sustainability constraints)
- Understanding why the quarter-position employment model may actually be a strength, not a limitation

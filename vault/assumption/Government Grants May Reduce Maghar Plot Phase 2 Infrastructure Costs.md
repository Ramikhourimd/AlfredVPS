# Government Grants May Reduce Maghar Plot Phase 2 Infrastructure Costs

## Claim

Government grants are potentially available for EV charging infrastructure and tourism facility development on the Maghar plot, which could significantly reduce Phase 2 investment requirements in the phased development strategy.

## Basis

The 2025-09-23 feasibility analysis mentions two grant sources:
1. **EV infrastructure grants**: "Government grants available for EV infrastructure deployment" — referenced in the EV charging station option (~50,000–150,000 NIS per charger unit)
2. **Tourism ministry grants**: "Tourism ministry may offer grants" — referenced in the caravan park/camping option

These grants could offset the medium-investment tier costs that currently separate Phase 1 (low-investment uses) from Phase 2 (infrastructure-dependent uses).

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-09-23: Feasibility analysis references government grants for EV and tourism infrastructure

## Impact

If grants are accessible, the Phase 2 investment barrier drops, potentially allowing faster progression from passive income (parking, storage) to higher-value uses (EV charging, caravan park). Confidence is low because: (1) grant availability, eligibility criteria, and amounts are unverified; (2) application process timeline is unknown; (3) grants may have geographic, business-type, or scale requirements that exclude this plot.

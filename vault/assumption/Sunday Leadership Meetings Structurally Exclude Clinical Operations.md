# Sunday Leadership Meetings Structurally Exclude Clinical Operations

## Claim

The weekly Sunday leadership meetings at Telia'z are exclusively oriented around marketing and business development: new patient acquisition counts, referral source analysis (family doctors, campaigns), marketing initiatives, secretary staffing, UK expansion progress, and Clalit agreement status. There is no standing agenda item and no recurring space for clinical operations, quality metrics, staff wellbeing, training, or clinical protocol development.

## Basis

Rami describes the Sunday meeting pattern explicitly in his 2025-12-05 debrief: the meetings are "entirely marketing and business-development oriented." He lists the recurring topics (new patient counts, referral sources, campaigns, new secretaries, UK expansion, Clalit) and explicitly notes the absence of clinical operations, quality metrics, or staff wellbeing from the agenda. This is not an occasional oversight but a structural pattern in the meeting cadence.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-05: Rami describes the pattern as established and persistent, not a recent change
- The meeting agenda mirrors the leadership's financial tunnel vision documented in the same period (Alex, Oren, Dekkel focused on expense ratios, patient volumes, HMO expansion)
- No source record from any period describes clinical topics being discussed in Sunday meetings

## Impact

This structural exclusion has cascading effects:
1. Clinical concerns have no regular forum at the leadership level, forcing ad hoc escalation
2. Rami must fight for inclusion rather than clinical topics having a guaranteed slot
3. The meeting structure reinforces the financial-first culture that Rami identifies as problematic
4. Quality, safety, and clinical development decisions happen outside the primary leadership rhythm, making them feel secondary
5. This directly feeds the constraint where clinical perspective is excluded from cross-functional planning decisions — the Sunday meeting IS the primary cross-functional planning venue

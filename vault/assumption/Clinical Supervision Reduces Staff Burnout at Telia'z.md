# Clinical Supervision Reduces Staff Burnout at Telia'z

## Claim

Structured clinical supervision sessions (group "hadracha") have a measurable protective effect against burnout among case managers. One case manager reported that the feeling of burnout noticeably decreased after group supervision began.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman's qualitative interviews with case managers in early 2026. One case manager explicitly stated that "something suddenly became less draining" after supervision sessions started, though she was unsure whether it was the group interaction itself or the clinical guidance.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: First reported by Rivi during one-on-one with Rami

## Impact

If confirmed, this suggests that increasing clinical supervision frequency and quality could be a cost-effective intervention for staff retention. It also suggests that the burnout problem is partly driven by professional isolation, not just workload volume.

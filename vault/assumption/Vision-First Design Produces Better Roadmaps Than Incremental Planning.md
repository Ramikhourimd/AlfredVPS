# Vision-First Design Produces Better Roadmaps Than Incremental Planning

## Claim

Designing a system prototype by first assuming unlimited technical capability — then constraining that vision into a phased development roadmap — produces a better roadmap than incrementally planning from current constraints. The team's survey methodology explicitly calls for building an ideal combined model before deriving realistic phases.

## Basis

Rami's proposed approach in the 2026-02-18 meeting follows a specific sequence: (1) survey existing systems, (2) combine best features, (3) brainstorm next-generation additions assuming no limits, (4) produce a concept document, (5) derive a phased roadmap from that concept. This is a deliberate choice to separate ideation from constraint application.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Approach defined in task assignment to Rivi — steps 4 and 5 explicitly state "assuming unlimited technical capability" then "derive a phased development roadmap"

## Impact

This assumption shapes the entire yearly development planning process. If vision-first design does NOT produce better roadmaps (e.g., if unconstrained ideation leads to unrealistic expectations or scope creep), the derived roadmap may be poorly calibrated to actual delivery capacity. The assumption also implies that the survey output is a maximum-ambition document, not a pragmatic plan.

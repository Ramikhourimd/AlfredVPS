# Centralization First Then Localize as Scale Demands

## Claim

The correct scaling strategy is to start centralized — shared services, unified processes, single management structure — and build local functions only as scale demands. Ziv drew parallels to multinational corporations that begin with centralized control and gradually localize as each market reaches sufficient size.

## Basis

Ziv explicitly advised this approach during the Feb 22 meeting, cautioning against copying hospital structures blindly or prematurely building local capabilities in each clinic. Oren's "one company" stance aligns with this philosophy. The shared services model decision reflects this assumption in practice.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Ziv presented the multinational parallel. Oren endorsed centralization. Shared services decision reflects this approach.

## Impact

This assumption shapes every structural decision: whether the UK clinic gets its own HR, whether marketing is replicated or centralized, whether the pod model is managed locally or coordinated across clinics. If the assumption is wrong — if clinics need local autonomy earlier than expected due to regulatory, cultural, or operational differences — the centralized model becomes a bottleneck rather than an efficiency gain.

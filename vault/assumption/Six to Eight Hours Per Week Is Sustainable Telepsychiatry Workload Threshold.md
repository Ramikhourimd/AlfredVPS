# Six to Eight Hours Per Week Is Sustainable Telepsychiatry Workload Threshold

## Claim

Clinical staff (both psychiatrists and case managers) working 6-8 hours per week at Telia'z manage burnout reasonably well, but beyond this threshold, exhaustion escalates significantly. This threshold is being used implicitly to evaluate scheduling sustainability.

## Basis

Multiple staff interviews conducted by Rivi Idelman in early 2026 consistently point to this range:
- One psychiatrist (Sa'id) found 3-4 hours/day across 5 days draining, but 1.5 hours/day sustainable
- A case manager reported being "much more empathetic and attentive" at 5 hours versus 9 hours
- Multiple staff described burnout as manageable within the 6-8 hour/week range but escalating beyond it

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi reported qualitative findings from case manager and psychiatrist interviews supporting this threshold

## Impact

This assumption shapes scheduling decisions, workload allocation, and the design of the employee satisfaction survey instrument. If invalidated (e.g., burnout is more about session intensity than total hours), staffing models would need revision.

# Ambidextrous Organization Model Required for Innovation-R&D Coexistence

## Claim

The structural friction between Rami's innovation work and Shachar's R&D/CTO function is inherently structural, not personal, and requires an "ambidextrous organization" model to resolve. This model gives the company "two hands" — one for executing today's business (R&D), one for building tomorrow's (Innovation) — coordinated by senior leadership as the "same brain." Research cited claims organizations using this model achieve 90% of their innovation goals.

## Basis

On 2025-12-01, Rami consumed structured research content (likely via NotebookLM) presenting two competing models for innovation department design: one prioritizing internal structure and lifecycle efficiency, the other prioritizing external authority and organizational independence. The core structural prescription: the head of innovation must report directly to the CEO, establishing innovation as a peer to R&D. The recommended core team is five people across three arms: Clinical & Insights, AI & Data, and Projects & Implementation.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-01: Three related recordings on innovation department design captured
- 2025-12-01: Ambidextrous model research reviewed — 90% innovation goal achievement cited
- 2025-12-01: 83% of physician informatics officers now report directly to C-suite cited as industry trend
- 2025-12-01: Five-person core team blueprint defined (innovation lead, product manager, clinical informatics specialist, AI/ML engineer, platform engineer)

## Impact

Rami is building his innovation department proposal on this specific theoretical framework. It directly supports his positions on CEO-level reporting for innovation, separation from CTO, and the need for a dedicated product manager hire. If this model is accepted, it provides a research-backed justification for organizational choices that might otherwise appear self-serving. If challenged, the entire innovation structure proposal loses its theoretical foundation.

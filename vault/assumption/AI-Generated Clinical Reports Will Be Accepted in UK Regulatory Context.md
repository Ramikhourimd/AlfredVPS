# AI-Generated Clinical Reports Will Be Accepted in UK Regulatory Context

## Claim

AI-generated psychiatric assessment reports — produced by Telia'z's platform in approximately 10 seconds post-session — will be accepted by UK GPs, CQC inspectors, and NHS governance frameworks as valid clinical documentation. The assumption extends to the AI-generated session summaries, agenda recommendations, and diagnostic support outputs that psychiatrists rely on during consultations.

## Basis

The Israel service model generates AI reports that are already accepted in the Israeli healthcare system. During the demo rehearsal (2025-02-11), the team prepared to showcase this capability to GP Confederation stakeholders without apparent concern about regulatory acceptance. The GP Confed meeting (2025-01-22) discussed NICE digital/AI guidance for neurodevelopmental services (released ~Dec 2024) but did not flag it as a blocker.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: GP Confed meeting — AI capabilities presented; Rebecca flagged NICE compliance as needing mapping but not as a dealbreaker
- 2025-02-11: Demo rehearsal — AI report generation featured prominently as key value proposition
- No evidence yet of explicit CQC or NICE validation of AI-generated clinical reports

## Impact

If this assumption fails, Telia'z's core efficiency advantage (60% operational time savings for psychiatrists, 10-second report generation) could be undermined. UK regulators might require human-authored or human-verified reports, adding significant time and cost to each assessment. This would directly impact the commercial viability of operating within NHS tariff pricing.

# Facebook and Online Listings Supplement Personal Network Property Discovery

## Claim

While personal networks remain the dominant property discovery channel, the family also uses Facebook and online property listings as a supplementary source. Properties found online are discussed casually during social gatherings and may prompt physical viewings, but online discovery has not yet led to a serious property pursuit on its own.

## Basis

- On 2025-09-23, a speaker "discusses properties found on Facebook" during a social gathering, including a rental offer of 600 (currency unspecified).
- On 2025-10-09, a "Coastal Villa (Facebook Listing)" was noted — described as beachfront, possibly Atlit/Neve Yam area — but characterized as "brief mention, unclear if a serious candidate."
- Both instances show online browsing as a casual, low-commitment activity embedded in social contexts rather than a systematic search method.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-09-23: Facebook property browsing mentioned during social visit (low engagement, casual)
- 2025-10-09: Facebook listing of coastal villa noted but not pursued (very low engagement)
- No records show a property discovered purely online progressing to a serious viewing or negotiation

## Impact

This modifies — but does not contradict — the existing synthesis that housing search relies on social networks rather than formal channels. Online platforms serve as a supplementary awareness layer but have not yet produced actionable leads. If the family wanted to broaden their search funnel, increasing engagement with online platforms (Yad2, Facebook groups, real estate sites) could surface options outside their current social network radius.

# Asynchronous Digital Announcements Insufficient for Clinician Feature Adoption

## Claim

WhatsApp messages, PDF guides, and other asynchronous digital communications do not result in meaningful feature adoption by psychiatrists at Telia'z Clinic Israel. Clinicians who receive feature announcements through these channels consistently continue using legacy workflows (e.g., editing summaries in Google Docs instead of the in-app summary tool) until they receive frontal (synchronous, in-person or video) training sessions.

## Basis

Across two separate operational meetings (January 12, 2025 and August 1, 2025), Rami observed that psychiatrists were not using the new in-app summary editing feature despite receiving WhatsApp announcements. Psychiatrists expected their Google Docs edits to propagate to the system — indicating they had not read or understood the feature change communication. The same non-adoption pattern was observed seven months apart with no improvement from additional async messaging.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-12: Feature communication discussed; psychiatrists still using Google Docs workflow
- 2025-08-01: Same non-adoption pattern observed; Rami raised concerns about WhatsApp channel effectiveness
- Multiple decisions created requiring frontal training before deprecating old workflows — confirming async announcements alone are insufficient

## Impact

This assumption underpins four separate decisions requiring frontal training for feature changes. It means every significant platform change requires scheduling synchronous training sessions, which creates a dependency on available training time and limits the speed of feature rollouts. It also implies that the planned internal task system (replacing WhatsApp) may face similar adoption challenges unless paired with synchronous onboarding.

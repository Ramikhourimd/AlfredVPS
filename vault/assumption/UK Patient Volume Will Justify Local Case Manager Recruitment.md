# UK Patient Volume Will Justify Local Case Manager Recruitment

## Claim

The team assumes UK patient volume will grow to a level that justifies hiring locally based UK case managers. Until that volume materialises, Israeli staff (Basem) will serve as a stopgap.

## Basis

Adiel stated the team "won't hire UK case manager until patient volume is confirmed." This implies an expectation that volume will eventually reach a threshold, but that threshold is undefined. The entire staffing transition plan — from Israeli stopgap to local UK hires — rests on this assumption.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: Adiel firm that UK case managers should ultimately be hired locally, but only after volume is confirmed. No volume projections or thresholds discussed.

## Impact

If UK patient volume remains low or grows slowly, the team may be stuck in "stopgap mode" longer than anticipated, with Basem splitting time between Israeli and UK case management. The decision to recruit locally (already recorded as final) depends on this assumption being validated by actual patient numbers.

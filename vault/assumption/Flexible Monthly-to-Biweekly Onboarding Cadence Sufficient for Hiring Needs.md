# Flexible Monthly-to-Biweekly Onboarding Cadence Sufficient for Hiring Needs

## Claim

A flexible onboarding cadence of one to two structured sessions per month can accommodate the clinic's unpredictable hiring volume. Some months may require rapid orientation when replacing departing staff, while other months may have no new hires. The cadence should flex between monthly and bi-weekly based on actual hiring activity.

## Basis

Rami proposed and Elias confirmed the feasibility of this cadence during a 2026-02-12 onboarding structure discussion. The reasoning: not every month produces new hires, and when it does, the three-track model (operational, administrative, technology) allows the technology track to run independently as a day-one requirement while the other tracks can be batched into scheduled sessions.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-12: Both onboarding discussion notes confirm the monthly-to-biweekly proposal
- No historical data yet on actual onboarding session frequency or throughput

## Impact

This cadence assumption underpins the clinic's ability to scale hiring. If the target of ~176 quarter-position hires is pursued aggressively, bi-weekly sessions accommodating 4-5 new hires each would process ~8-10 per month — taking 18+ months to fill all positions. The assumption has not been stress-tested against the aggressive hiring targets set by leadership.

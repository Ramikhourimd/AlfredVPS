# CQC Paperwork Can Be Exchanged and Processed Rapidly After Leon Contract Signing

## Claim

The team assumes that once the Leon contract is signed, the CQC-related paperwork (managed by Susan on the regulatory side, with Navin's documents already prepared) can be exchanged and processed quickly enough to not delay the March 2026 launch timeline. The paperwork is described as "ready" on Navin's side, implying it's a formality rather than a substantive process.

## Basis

In the Feb 15 meeting notes, Adiel reported: "Navin's paperwork is ready" and documents "will exchange after contract signing." The framing treats contract signing as the only gate — once that clears, paperwork flows immediately. No discussion of CQC processing times, regulatory review periods, or potential back-and-forth with the CQC office.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: "CQC registration paperwork is managed by Susan; Nevin's paperwork is ready"
- 2026-02-15: Documents to be exchanged "after contract signing"
- No mention of CQC processing timeline or potential delays
- March 31 launch target assumes this is not a bottleneck

## Impact

If CQC paperwork exchange involves a regulatory review period (which CQC variations or additions sometimes do), the assumed rapid turnaround could add weeks to the timeline. With the March 31 system launch and mid-March campaign start already aggressive, any CQC processing delay would cascade into the entire launch schedule.

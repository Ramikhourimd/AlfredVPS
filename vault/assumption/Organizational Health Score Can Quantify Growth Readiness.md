# Organizational Health Score Can Quantify Growth Readiness

## Claim

Rami has built a quantitative model that calculates an organizational health score as a growth readiness indicator. The model incorporates patient satisfaction scores, staff workload metrics, and multiple burnout-risk indicators. A defined threshold must be crossed before growth should begin — Rami estimates this could be reached within approximately six months.

## Basis

Described in Rami's therapy session on 2025-12-05. He frames this as an evidence-based alternative to leadership's revenue-driven growth targets. The model has approximately 60 KPIs. However, it is unclear whether the model has been validated, peer-reviewed, or presented formally to the board.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-05: Rami describes the health score model in therapy session
- Model status: Built by Rami, not reviewed by leadership
- Leadership has not engaged with or acknowledged this framework

## Impact

If this model is sound, it provides an objective basis for timing growth decisions. If leadership continues to ignore it, growth timing remains a subjective disagreement. The model's credibility depends on whether it is presented and validated — currently it exists only in Rami's domain.

# Pre-Treatment Patient Video Will Reduce Intake Dropout

## Claim

Creating a pre-treatment orientation video that explains the treatment process, sets patient expectations, and communicates the value of follow-up sessions will reduce patient dropout during the intake phase. The assumption is that patients disengage partly because they do not understand what to expect from the treatment journey.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman gathered initial requirements from Renee about common friction points during first phone calls — where patients get stuck, what information is missing, and what expectations are unclear. This suggests an information gap exists at the pre-treatment stage that may contribute to dropout.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi discusses patient video concept during meeting with Rami; directed to produce concept document (not production spec)
- No quantitative data yet on intake dropout rates attributable to information gaps

## Impact

If confirmed, this intervention could improve intake conversion rates at relatively low cost. However, the causal link between information gaps and dropout has not been validated — dropout may be driven by other factors (wait times, cost, stigma). The concept document phase will help clarify the problem before committing to production.

# Most Medical Professionals Have Not Adopted AI Tools Creating a Widening Competency Gap

## Claim

The majority of medical professionals have not meaningfully adopted AI tools beyond casual ChatGPT use, and the gap between basic use and building automations/agents for clinical workflows is widening. Early adopters gain a compounding advantage that will create an "intellectual barrier" for non-adopters.

## Basis

During the 2025-09-01 networking conversation, an early AI-adopting medical professional (who took a "Prompt Master" course and has been deeply involved in AI forums since ChatGPT launch) expressed surprise at how few doctors have adopted even basic AI tools. They identified the real gap as being between casual ChatGPT use and building automations for daily medical workflows, and predicted an emerging "intellectual barrier" for non-adopters.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-09-01: Direct observation from AI-adopting medical professional in the field
- Training program existence (3 cohorts) confirms demand for structured AI education among healthcare professionals

## Impact

This assumption validates Telia'z investment in the Psychiatry Academy and AI training initiatives. If correct, Telia'z early AI integration gives it a significant competitive advantage in clinician recruitment and clinical quality. It also justifies the innovation program's focus on AI-enabled clinical workflows.

# Patient Work Attendance Is a Clinical Intervention Not Just Administrative Compliance

## Claim

Work attendance for psychiatric patients is prescribed as an active therapeutic intervention — a form of occupational therapy — rather than merely an administrative or regulatory compliance requirement. Rami frames return-to-work directives as clinically necessary for patient recovery, not just as responses to employer or Ministry of Health pressure.

## Basis

Across multiple outpatient consultations on 2025-12-02, Rami consistently directed patients to resume work attendance (specific days: Monday and Wednesday), framing workplace activity as part of the treatment plan. The clinical rationale: structured daily activity, social interaction, and routine prevent regression and reinforce the stability achieved through medication. The directive is given alongside — not instead of — pharmacological treatment.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Outpatient follow-up — patient absent from work for 2 months, Rami prescribed Monday/Wednesday attendance as clinical requirement
- 2025-12-02: Medication review session — work return framed as integral to treatment stability

## Impact

This assumption shapes how the clinic handles patient absence cases. Rather than treating work return as an external compliance issue (Ministry of Health, employer demands), it is integrated into clinical treatment plans. Case managers and psychiatrists are expected to actively manage patient work attendance as a therapeutic outcome, not delegate it to administrative staff.

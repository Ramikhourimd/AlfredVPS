# Clinic Staff Need a Local HR Address Not Just Parent Company

## Claim

Clinic staff — particularly in a hybrid/remote telepsychiatry environment — need a local administrative "address" for HR issues, onboarding, and day-to-day people management. Centralizing HR entirely to the parent company creates a disconnect that impacts staff retention and responsiveness.

## Basis

Rami pushed back on fully outsourcing HR during the Feb 22 meeting, arguing that staff need to feel they have someone local to turn to. Basel supported having local administrative presence. This is especially relevant for a clinic where many practitioners work remotely and may never visit a central office — their relationship with management is mediated through the administrative team.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Rami and Basel argued for local admin presence despite shared services model. Oren acknowledged the concern but maintained "one company" stance.

## Impact

This assumption directly challenges the shared services model for HR. If valid, the Administrative Manager role (Renee) becomes more critical as the local HR interface, even if formal HR processes run through the parent company. It affects how Renee's role is scoped and how shared services SLAs are designed.

# Internal Task System May Amplify Staff Isolation

## Claim

Replacing WhatsApp group communication with a structured internal task system will improve task tracking and operational clarity, but may worsen staff isolation by eliminating the informal social connection that WhatsApp groups provided.

## Basis

Staff interviews revealed that WhatsApp groups, despite their operational limitations (unanswered messages, lack of structure), served as the primary informal social channel. Moving to a task-oriented system removes this incidental social layer without replacing it. Staff already report feeling isolated working from home; further formalizing communication could deepen this.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi reported that staff fear losing the informal social aspects of WhatsApp when the task system launches
- Multiple staff interviews confirmed isolation as a pre-existing concern

## Impact

The next-generation clinical system design and communication platform decisions should explicitly include social/community features to compensate. Without mitigation, the task system migration may inadvertently worsen the isolation problem it partially addresses.

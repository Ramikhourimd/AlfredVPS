# Existing DTAC Compliance Covers UK Partnership Service Configurations

## Claim

Telia'z obtained DTAC (Digital Technology Assessment Criteria) compliance with help from Rebecca Wilson at DigiSave prior to the January 2025 GP Confederation meeting. The team assumes this existing DTAC certification covers the service configurations being proposed across all UK partnership tracks — Leon private patients, GP Confederation NHS tender, and OMG NHS pathway — without requiring re-assessment or additional DTAC work.

## Basis

During the 2025-01-22 GP Confederation meeting, the DTAC compliance was referenced as an existing credential. DigiSave (Rebecca Wilson) facilitated the process. DTAC is the NHS-mandated assessment for digital health technologies and is typically required for any digital service used within NHS pathways. The team treats it as a completed checkbox rather than an ongoing compliance requirement.

## Evidence Trail

- Pre-2025-01: DTAC compliance obtained via DigiSave (Rebecca Wilson)
- 2025-01-22: Referenced as existing credential in GP Confed meeting
- No mention of DTAC renewal, re-assessment, or scope review in any subsequent records

## Impact

DTAC compliance is a prerequisite for NHS digital technology adoption. If the original DTAC assessment covered only the Israel-configured platform (Hebrew-primary, Israel clinical workflows), it may not automatically cover UK-adapted configurations — English-language AI reports, UK-specific questionnaires, Leon website integration, OMG data handoff. A gap in DTAC coverage could block the GP Confederation tender bid and any NHS referral pathway. This assumption should be verified with DigiSave.

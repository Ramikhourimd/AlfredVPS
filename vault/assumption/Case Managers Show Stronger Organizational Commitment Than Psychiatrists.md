# Case Managers Show Stronger Organizational Commitment Than Psychiatrists

## Claim

Case managers at Telia'z demonstrate stronger organizational commitment than psychiatrists. Most case managers plan to continue working at the clinic, with only one departure attributed to workload. However, this commitment may be temporary — it could shift once case managers complete their professional training programs, at which point external opportunities become more attractive.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman's comparative interviews with both role types during the 2026-02-18 research period. Psychiatrists were assessed as staying primarily for convenience (WFH, pay), while case managers showed more intrinsic commitment. The key caveat is that case manager commitment may be partially driven by the value of current training/experience for their career development.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi reported this distinction across multiple interviews with both groups
- One case manager left due to workload — exception rather than pattern

## Impact

Differential retention profiles require different retention strategies. Psychiatrist retention depends on maintaining convenience premium; case manager retention depends on continued professional growth opportunities. The training-completion risk for case managers suggests a future attrition cliff that should be anticipated.

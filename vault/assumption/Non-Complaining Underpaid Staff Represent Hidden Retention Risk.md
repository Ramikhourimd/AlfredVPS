# Non-Complaining Underpaid Staff Represent Hidden Retention Risk

## Claim

Staff members who are underpaid relative to peers but do not raise the issue themselves represent a higher long-term retention risk than vocal negotiators. Their silence does not indicate satisfaction — it indicates a different coping strategy that may culminate in sudden departure rather than gradual negotiation.

## Basis

During the 2025-12-04 payroll review, Rami and Basel discovered that Atef is paid below the standard scale for intake sessions, despite working more hours than other psychiatrists. Unlike Elinor and Noa — who heard about expert rates (450 NIS/session) and immediately demanded increases — Atef did not raise the issue himself. Management identified this as both a fairness issue and a retention risk, prioritizing a proactive rate adjustment.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-04: Atef underpaid at below-scale rate, doing more hours than peers, did not complain
- 2025-12-04: Elinor and Noa demanded rate increases after learning about expert rates (vocal negotiators)
- Management chose to proactively raise Atef's rate rather than wait for him to ask

## Impact

Compensation audits should specifically flag staff members who are underpaid AND have not made rate requests — these are higher flight risks than vocal negotiators. The absence of complaints should not be interpreted as satisfaction with compensation.

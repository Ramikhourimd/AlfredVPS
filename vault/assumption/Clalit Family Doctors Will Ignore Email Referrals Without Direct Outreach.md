# Clalit Family Doctors Will Ignore Email Referrals Without Direct Outreach

## Claim

If the Clalit district contact sends referral emails to family doctors (GPs) about the Telia'z telepsychiatry service, the GPs will largely ignore these emails, resulting in near-zero patient referrals until Telia'z pursues direct marketing or alternative patient acquisition strategies.

## Basis

Rami Khouri's direct experience with the Maccabi partnership: when referrals depended on Form 17 (a gated authorization process through family doctors), patient volume was stuck at approximately 13 patients per month for several months. Once Form 17 was eliminated and patients could access the service directly, volumes jumped to hundreds per month. This 10x+ difference demonstrates that GP-mediated referral gates are a severe bottleneck for patient acquisition.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-09: Rami states prediction during team discussion, citing Maccabi Form 17 experience as basis
- Maccabi historical data: 13 patients/month with Form 17 vs. hundreds/month without

## Impact

If this assumption holds, Telia'z will need to invest in direct marketing to Clalit patients (adding costs not yet reflected in profitability calculations) or negotiate a self-referral pathway. The entire Clalit partnership business case depends on whether this referral barrier can be overcome.

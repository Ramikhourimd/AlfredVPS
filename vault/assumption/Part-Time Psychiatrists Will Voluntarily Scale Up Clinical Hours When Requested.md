# Part-Time Psychiatrists Will Voluntarily Scale Up Clinical Hours When Requested

## Claim

The clinic's workforce scaling model implicitly assumes that psychiatrists hired at reduced hours (e.g., 20-25 hours/month) will voluntarily increase their clinical hours over time when asked by management. This assumption underpins the quarter-position recruitment model — hire many part-timers and grow them into larger positions.

## Basis

The assumption is embedded in operational planning conversations. When onboarding Eliezer (November 2025), Rami approved a 25-hour start but required a "commitment to a timeline for reaching 40 hours." The expectation is that verbal commitment translates to actual hour increases.

## Evidence Trail

- **2025-11-10**: Atef logged only 20 hours despite Rami "previously asking him to increase." Atef frames his participation as "doing Rami a favor" — suggesting he views the position as discretionary, not primary employment.
- **2025-11-10**: Eliezer requested to "start as late as possible while keeping the position reserved" and could only commit to 25 hours, citing research in China, an innovation center, and Stage B exam preparation. Multiple concurrent commitments prevent clinical focus.
- **Pattern**: Both psychiatrists demonstrate reluctance to increase hours despite management requests, suggesting the assumption that part-timers will scale up is unreliable.

## Impact

If this assumption is invalid, the clinic's scaling model (targeting ~176 quarter-position hires to reach 7,786 patients) requires even more individual hires than projected, because existing staff won't grow into larger positions as expected. This compounds the already severe recruitment bottleneck.

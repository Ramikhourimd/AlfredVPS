# Six to Eight Weekly Hours Is Sustainable Threshold for Telepsychiatry Clinicians

## Claim

Clinicians (both psychiatrists and case managers) working approximately 6-8 hours per week at Telia'z report manageable burnout levels. Above this threshold, burnout increases significantly. Distributed shorter sessions (e.g., 1.5 hours daily) are more sustainable than concentrated blocks (e.g., 3-4 hours daily).

## Basis

Rivi Idelman's qualitative interviews with multiple case managers and psychiatrists, reported in the 2026-02-18 working session. Specific example: one case manager switched from 3-4 hours/day over 5 days to 1.5 hours/day and reported dramatically improved sustainability. Staff universally say they would not concentrate the same weekly hours into a single long block.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Initial finding from Rivi's staff interviews (medium confidence — qualitative, no systematic measurement yet)
- Pending: Employee satisfaction survey instrument to quantify this with hard data

## Impact

If confirmed, this has direct implications for scheduling policy, hiring volume calculations (more clinicians needed at lower hours each), and financial modeling of per-clinician capacity.

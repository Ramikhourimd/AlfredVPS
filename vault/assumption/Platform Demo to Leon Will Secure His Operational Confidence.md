# Platform Demo to Leon Will Secure His Operational Confidence

## Claim

The team assumes that demonstrating the clinical system (admin views and psychiatrist views) to Leon will be sufficient to secure his operational buy-in and willingness to proceed with the partnership. Leon specifically requested to see the system before finalizing the arrangement.

## Basis

During the February 15, 2026 team meeting, Adiel reported that Leon "also wants to see the clinical system — admin and psychiatrist views" and that Stav is preparing a demo. The team treats this as a straightforward step rather than a potential risk point. However, Leon is a practicing family doctor evaluating whether to attach his CQC registration to a foreign technology platform — his comfort level with the system is not guaranteed by a single demo.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: Leon requests system demo; Stav assigned to prepare it
- 2026-02-13 (Thursday before meeting): Yael met Leon and showed initial patient flow design
- No record of Leon's reaction to the patient flow design

## Impact

If the demo raises concerns for Leon (about usability, clinical appropriateness, data handling, or integration with his existing practice), contract signing could be delayed or derailed. Given that the entire Track 1 launch depends on Leon's CQC, this is a single point of failure that the team appears to be treating as routine.

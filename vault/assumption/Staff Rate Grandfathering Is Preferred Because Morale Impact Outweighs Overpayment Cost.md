# Staff Rate Grandfathering Is Preferred Because Morale Impact Outweighs Overpayment Cost

## Claim

Reducing a staff member's pay rate — even when the current rate exceeds their correct tier — causes disproportionate morale damage relative to the financial cost of maintaining the overpayment. Rami's explicit operating principle: "better to carry someone than to reduce them."

## Basis

During the 2025-12-04 staff rate review, Rami and Basel identified multiple cases where staff were being paid above their correct rate tier. Rather than correcting rates downward, Rami articulated a deliberate policy preference: absorb the overpayment cost and allow natural correction over time (via new rate scales applied to future hires), rather than reducing existing staff rates and risking morale damage, grievances, or turnover.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-04: Rami stated principle explicitly during rate review with Basel
- Applied to specific case: one psychiatrist at 400 NIS/hr when tier is 350 NIS/hr

## Impact

This assumption drives the clinic's entire approach to rate standardization. Rather than immediate cost corrections, the strategy relies on new unified agreements applied to future contracts while grandfathering existing overpayments. This means short-term payroll costs remain higher than optimal, but staff retention and morale are preserved during the restructuring period.

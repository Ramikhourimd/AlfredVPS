# Majdi Shaddad Research Is Reliable Foundation for Genealogy

## Claim

Majdi Shaddad's approximately 45 years of genealogical research (since ~1980) provides a reliable foundation for the Khouri Family Genealogy project, despite being based on oral tradition rather than primary historical documents.

## Basis

The project currently relies almost entirely on Majdi's oral history as its primary source. Multiple notes explicitly acknowledge this is "not verified against primary historical sources." However, Majdi's account is internally consistent across the recorded conversation, and the broad migration pattern aligns with known historical events (Druze-Christian conflicts in Mount Lebanon, 1860 civil war). His long research tenure and connections across the three family branches (Eilabun, Rameh, Nazareth) and diaspora (Beirut, Chile) suggest significant accumulated knowledge.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi shared a detailed, internally consistent migration timeline
- Multiple vault notes corroborate the same narrative without significant contradictions
- Known historical events (1860 Mount Lebanon civil war) align with the migration timeline
- Limitation: No primary documentary sources cited

## Impact

All current genealogy findings depend on this assumption. If Majdi's account contains significant errors, the migration timeline, branch connections, and surname origin would all need independent verification. The project should consider cross-referencing with church records, Ottoman registers, or other documentary sources as they become available.

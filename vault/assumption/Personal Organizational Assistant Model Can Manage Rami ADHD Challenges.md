## Assumption

A personal organizational assistant (human, AI, or hybrid) functioning as an ADHD buddy can effectively compensate for [[person/Rami Khouri]]'s executive function challenges, resulting in measurably improved productivity and follow-through.

## Basis

- ADHD coaching and accountability partner models have documented effectiveness in clinical literature
- Rami identified specific executive function gaps: task tracking, priority management, schedule adherence, commitment follow-through
- External structure is a well-established compensatory strategy for ADHD
- The concept maps directly to Rami's self-identified needs

## Evidence Supporting

- ADHD coaching effectiveness is supported by research
- Rami's self-awareness about his challenges suggests high receptivity to external support
- Similar models work in corporate settings for executives with ADHD

## Risks If Wrong

- Cost of human assistant without proportional productivity gain
- Dependency on external structure without building internal habits
- Potential mismatch between assistant style and Rami's working preferences
- AI tools may lack the contextual awareness needed for effective support

## Validation Approach

- Pilot period (1-3 months) with measurable outcomes
- Track completion rates on committed tasks
- Compare scheduling adherence before and after
- Rami self-assessment of organizational improvement

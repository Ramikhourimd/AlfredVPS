# Clalit Follow-Up Oversight Negotiable Via Time-Limited Pilot Period

## Claim

The Clalit district contact's demand to personally review patient summaries and approve whether patients proceed to follow-up care can be negotiated down through a time-limited pilot period (1-2 months), after which bulk or automatic authorization would replace per-patient oversight.

## Basis

During the 2025-11-09 preparation meeting, the team planned to propose a pilot period as a compromise: the Clalit contact gets hands-on oversight initially (1-2 months), allowing him to build confidence in Telia'z's clinical quality, after which the process transitions to a more scalable model. This mirrors the general pattern in HMO relationships where initial oversight relaxes as trust is established.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-09: Strategy formulated during preparation meeting — untested assumption at this point
- Pending: outcome of actual negotiation with Clalit contact

## Impact

If this assumption holds, the Clalit pipeline can scale after the pilot period. If the contact insists on permanent per-patient oversight, the partnership becomes operationally unscalable — every follow-up patient would require manual approval, creating a permanent bottleneck that worsens with volume growth.

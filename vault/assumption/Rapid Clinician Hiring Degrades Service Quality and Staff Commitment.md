# Rapid Clinician Hiring Degrades Service Quality and Staff Commitment

## Claim

Aggressive hiring to meet patient volume targets produces lower-quality staff on small contracts with minimal organizational commitment. Rami states this is "well-documented in healthcare literature" — rapid hiring to fill capacity leads to clinicians who treat the position as temporary, invest less in institutional knowledge, and deliver inconsistent care quality.

## Basis

During the 2025-12-05 debrief, Rami argued that leadership's push for immediate HMO expansion (Clalit, Leumit) and patient volume growth would force rapid hiring. His specific concern: the clinic would recruit psychiatrists willing to take small-hours contracts rather than committed professionals who invest in the institution. This directly undermines the premium clinic positioning he advocates.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-05: Rami explicitly cites healthcare literature on rapid hiring leading to lower-quality staff
- 2025-12-05: Links this to leadership's 2026 financial targets (expense ratios, patient volumes)
- 2025-12-05: Warns rapid hiring reduces the clinic to "just another clinic" without differentiation
- Ongoing: Leadership (Alex, Oren, Dekkel) pushing for expansion to Clalit South (350 unserved patients)

## Impact

This assumption directly supports Rami's "stabilization before growth" position and challenges leadership's expansion timeline. If correct, the financial targets set by Alex, Oren, and Dekkel for 2026 may produce short-term revenue gains at the cost of long-term institutional quality — the opposite of the premium branding strategy Rami advocates.

# Maccabi Transition Reduced Professional Diversity and Staff Motivation

## Claim

The transition from serving post-October 7 trauma populations (with social importance and clinical variety) to regular Maccabi HMO patients has reduced psychiatrists' sense of professional purpose and clinical challenge. The work now feels like "just another HMO clinic" but with worse conditions (isolation, documentation overhead). This contributes to retention risk.

## Basis

Specific feedback from at least one psychiatrist (via Rivi's interviews), who explicitly stated that before Maccabi, the work had social importance (southern residents, post-October 7 patients) and clinical diversity. Now it's routine, and combined with working-from-home isolation and documentation burden, feels like a poor trade-off.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: First explicit articulation of this dynamic via Rivi's interviews

## Impact

If widespread, this suggests Telia'z needs to proactively create professional engagement mechanisms (case complexity programs, research involvement, supervision forums) to compensate for the loss of intrinsic motivation from the post-trauma mission.

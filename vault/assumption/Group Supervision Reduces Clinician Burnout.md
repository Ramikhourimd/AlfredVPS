# Group Supervision Reduces Clinician Burnout

## Claim

Group supervision sessions (hadracha) meaningfully reduce clinician burnout. At least one case manager reported that burnout "suddenly decreased" after peer supervision sessions began.

## Basis

Single case manager's self-report during Rivi's staff interviews. She was unsure whether the reduction was from the group contact itself or the supervision content. No controlled measurement or comparison across staff.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi reported one case manager's experience of burnout decreasing after hadracha sessions started

## Impact

This assumption is being used to justify expanding group supervision programs. If the effect is placebo, novelty-based, or specific to one individual, resource allocation toward supervision may be misdirected. The planned employee satisfaction survey should include questions to validate this across a larger sample.

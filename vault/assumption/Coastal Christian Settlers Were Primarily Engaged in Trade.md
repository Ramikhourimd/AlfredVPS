# Coastal Christian Settlers Were Primarily Engaged in Trade

## Claim

Christians who migrated from Lebanon to Palestine in the early-to-mid 1800s initially settled along the Palestinian coast, where their primary economic activity was trade. This coastal-trade period preceded their later migration inland to the Galilee villages around 1865.

## Basis

Multiple independent records of the same oral history from Majdi Shaddad consistently describe the coastal period as trade-focused. The conversation record states Christians "settled on the Palestinian coast and worked in trade." All three research notes independently preserve this same claim: "settled along the Palestinian coast and established themselves in trade" / "worked primarily in trade."

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi Shaddad described Christians settling on the coast and working in trade (voice memo conversation)
- All three independently-created research notes preserve this same claim

## Impact

If accurate, this trade-based coastal period explains how displaced Lebanese Christians established economic viability before circumstances drove them inland. It positions the Galilee settlement not as direct migration from Lebanon but as a two-stage process with an intermediate economic establishment phase on the coast. Understanding the economic context may help identify the specific "circumstances" that later forced the move inland.

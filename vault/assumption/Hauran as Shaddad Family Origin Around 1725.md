# Hauran as Shaddad Family Origin Around 1725

## Claim

The Shaddad family ("Dar Shaddad") originated from or settled in Hauran (southern Syria) around 1725, before migrating to Lebanon and eventually to the Galilee.

## Basis

Majdi Shaddad, who has been researching the family tree since approximately 1980, shared this account during a recorded conversation on 2025-12-02. Multiple independently created notes in the vault consistently cite Hauran as the origin point with the ~1725 date. However, Majdi himself acknowledged uncertainty — some accounts within the family suggest Zahleh (Lebanon) rather than Hauran as the starting point.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi Shaddad shared migration timeline starting from Hauran ~1725 (voice memo recording)
- All three research notes in the vault cite this date and location consistently
- Oral tradition only — no primary historical documents have been consulted

## Impact

This date and origin anchors the entire migration timeline. If the origin is Zahleh rather than Hauran, the early migration phases would need to be reinterpreted. The ~1725 date determines how far back the genealogy can be traced.

# Monthly Patient Churn Rate Is Approximately 15 Percent

## Claim

The Telia'z Clinic Israel experiences a monthly patient churn rate of approximately 15%, with observed range of 11.6% to 18.9% across months May through December. This means roughly one in seven active patients drops off each month.

## Basis

Rami presented this analysis based on Maccabi data from the clinic database during the 2025-12-04 strategic goals meeting. The range (11.6%–18.9%) was derived from actual monthly data, giving the 15% figure reasonable empirical grounding. This is not a target — it is an observed operational reality.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-04: Data presented at strategic meeting showing 11.6%–18.9% monthly churn across May–December
- This churn rate directly feeds the staffing projection model (44 FTE required for ~7,786 year-end patients)

## Impact

- Staffing projections depend heavily on this rate — a 5% swing in churn dramatically changes required FTE
- Marketing and patient acquisition must account for churn replacement before counting net growth
- The churn rate may indicate treatment discontinuation, which has clinical implications beyond the operational ones
- If churn is reducible through better follow-up protocols, the clinic could grow without proportionally increasing intake volume

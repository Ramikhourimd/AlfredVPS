# Patient App Passive Metrics Can Scale Clinical Research Beyond Traditional Prospective Studies

## Claim

The existing Telia'z patient app can be leveraged for passive metric collection (screen time, phone usage, walking patterns, GPS location, heart rate, breathing, sleep) combined with Ecological Momentary Assessment (EMA — once or multiple daily sampling). This would provide high-resolution therapeutic data at a scale traditional prospective research cannot match: while a conventional study might recruit ~100 patients, Telia'z can deploy to 5,000 patients who already use the app as part of their care workflow.

## Basis

At the November 2025 conference, Rami described the patient app's potential for passive data collection, referencing a conference peer who already collects phone data (screen time, GPS, walking) combined with clinical data. He noted that distributing wearables (e.g., Garmin sport watches, as another company has done) could add biometric data streams. The 5,000-patient deployment figure comes from the existing active patient base using the app.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-13: Rami presents this vision at psychiatric conference, distinguishing prospective research scale (~100 patients traditional vs ~5,000 via app)
- Conference peer reference: Another company distributes Garmin watches for patient data collection
- EMA methodology referenced as providing higher-resolution data than periodic clinical assessment

## Impact

- Could fundamentally change the research output model from retrospective data analysis to prospective real-world evidence
- Passive metrics combined with clinical data would strengthen the data valuation thesis (~$20M)
- Patient consent requirements and privacy regulations would need careful navigation
- Wearable distribution costs would need to be budgeted

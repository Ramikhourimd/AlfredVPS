# No Significant Christian Presence in Galilee Villages Before 1865

## Claim

By approximately 1865, there were almost no Christians living in the inland Galilee villages — the Christian population was concentrated on the Palestinian coast. The settlement of Christians in Galilee villages (Rameh, Eilabun, Maghar, Safed, Tiberias, etc.) occurred only after ~1865.

## Basis

Majdi Shaddad stated this explicitly during the 2025-12-02 discussion, and it appears consistently across all three research notes. The claim is used as the foundation for dating the Shaddad family's arrival in Rameh. The Maghar church founding (~1860-1865) is cited as corroborating evidence.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi Shaddad stated "by 1865, there were almost no Christians in the Galilee" (oral history)
- Maghar church founding (~1860-1865) cited as physical evidence of Christian arrival timing
- No primary historical sources have been consulted to verify this claim

## Impact

This assumption establishes the timeline for when the Khouri/Shaddad family arrived in Rameh. If Christians were present in Galilee earlier, it could mean the Shaddad family arrived earlier than claimed, or that the coastal-to-inland migration narrative is incomplete. The Maghar church date is the only semi-verifiable anchor point mentioned.

# Competitive Clinical Platform Survey Will Yield Actionable Design Insights

## Claim

Surveying existing clinical management platforms used by competitors and peers will produce design patterns and feature ideas directly applicable to the next-generation system. The planned methodology is: survey existing systems, build a combined model of best features, brainstorm innovative additions, create a concept document, then derive a phased development roadmap.

## Basis

Two tasks define this methodology: the next-generation system prototype brainstorm and the competitive system survey, both emerging from the 2026-02-18 innovation team meeting. The approach assumes external platforms have solved problems relevant to the clinic, and studying them will accelerate design compared to starting from first principles.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rami proposed the survey-first approach during the innovation team meeting
- Rivi assigned as lead researcher with Ohad, Yael, and Rami supporting
- No survey results yet — assumption remains untested

## Impact

If this assumption holds, the next-generation system design will be grounded in proven patterns rather than speculative design. If it fails (e.g., psychiatric clinical platforms are too specialized or different in scope), the team would need to pivot to first-principles design, delaying the yearly development roadmap.

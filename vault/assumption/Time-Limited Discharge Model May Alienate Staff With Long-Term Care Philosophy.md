# Time-Limited Discharge Model May Alienate Staff With Long-Term Care Philosophy

## Claim

Basel Kanaaneh's patient lifecycle plan — with expected discharge percentages at defined time intervals — may alienate clinical staff who view psychiatry as requiring long-term therapeutic relationships. Rivi expressed concern that a time-limited treatment model conflicts with the professional values of clinicians who open extra slots specifically to maintain continuity of care.

## Basis

Rivi raised this during the 2026-03-17 meeting in the context of the patient capacity bottleneck. She referenced Basel's discharge model as potentially incompatible with how clinicians think about patient care. The concern is implicit but significant: staff who already feel like "orphaned children" may perceive a discharge-focused model as further deprioritizing clinical judgment.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-03-17: Rivi flags concern about discharge model alienating staff
- Multiple 2026-02-18 session records document clinician frustration with feeling unsupported
- Psychiatrist retention already described as fragile and convenience-driven (existing synthesis)

## Impact

If this assumption is correct, the discharge model could accelerate psychiatrist attrition — the one factor class sustaining retention (convenience) could be undermined by perceived philosophical misalignment. Needs validation through staff survey data.

# Premium Clinic Branding Attracts Better Clinicians at Lower Cost

## Claim

If the marketing department brands the clinic as a premium, prestigious psychiatric service rather than pushing pure revenue growth, it would attract higher-quality salaried clinicians at lower recruitment cost. The current marketing mandate ("increase revenue") misaligns with this vision for sustainable clinic positioning.

## Basis

Rami expressed this in his therapy session on 2025-12-05. He wants marketing to position the clinic as prestigious to attract clinicians who prefer salaried employment at a reputable institution, rather than the current approach of revenue-maximization marketing. This is an untested hypothesis — no evidence of market research or recruitment data supporting the claim.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-05: Rami describes premium branding strategy in therapy session
- Current state: Marketing operates under "increase revenue" mandate (set by leadership)
- No market validation or recruitment cost comparison exists

## Impact

If correct, this strategy could reduce recruitment costs while improving clinical quality — a double benefit. It directly conflicts with the current marketing mandate and Adi Lavi's branding direction ("Telia'z Health" = psychiatric services). Adoption would require leadership buy-in and marketing strategy pivot.

# Pods Are Operational Units Not Clinical Governance Units

## Claim

Pods in the clinic scaling model are strictly operational units responsible for scheduling, workload distribution, and internal communications. Clinical matters — training, protocols, case consultations, and professional development — are explicitly excluded from pod scope and instead handled through shared forums that span all pods. Pod leads report to the Operations Manager for operational matters and to the Medical Director for clinical matters, but the pod itself is not a clinical governance boundary.

## Basis

The Feb 22 leadership meeting explicitly stated: "Pods are operational (scheduling, workload, communications), not clinical. Clinical matters (training, protocols, case discussions) handled through shared forums across all pods." This distinction was a deliberate design choice to prevent clinical knowledge silos from forming within pod boundaries.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Basel and Leah's pod model proposal explicitly separates operational and clinical functions. The decision "Clinical Forums Shared Across All Pods" reinforces this boundary.

## Impact

This assumption is foundational to the pod architecture. If violated — if pods begin handling clinical governance — the shared forums become redundant and clinical practices may diverge between pods. Conversely, if the operational/clinical boundary proves too rigid (e.g., pod leads need clinical authority to make scheduling decisions that affect patient care), the model may need revision. The dual reporting line (Operations Manager for operational, Medical Director for clinical) depends on this boundary being clear and maintainable.

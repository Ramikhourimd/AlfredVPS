# Dekkel Operationally Capable But Lacks Strategic Planning Ability

## Claim

Both Rami and Oren assess that Dekkel Taliaz is effective at operational execution — managing defined tasks and day-to-day processes — but lacks long-term strategic planning capability. This candid assessment was made jointly during a private management discussion and has direct implications for the CEO role vacancy and organizational governance.

## Basis

During the 2025-12-02 management discussion, Rami and Oren explicitly evaluated Dekkel's strengths and weaknesses. They agreed his operational competence does not extend to strategic vision or long-term planning — capabilities expected of someone holding a CEO title (which Dekkel formally holds per HR records).

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Rami and Oren jointly assess Dekkel as having "operational strengths, no long-term planning" during private HR discussion
- HR records show Dekkel holds CEO and Head Scientist title despite this assessment

## Impact

This assumption directly affects:
- Whether Dekkel can credibly serve as CEO beyond formal title
- The urgency of defining a governance structure that compensates for this gap (steering committee, CMO authority)
- Why the "authority vacuum" persists — the titled CEO lacks strategic capacity while the de facto strategic leader (Rami) lacks the title

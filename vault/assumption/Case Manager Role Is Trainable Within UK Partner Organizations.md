# Case Manager Role Is Trainable Within UK Partner Organizations

## Claim

The team assumes that the case manager role — which in Israel is performed by clinical psychologists or clinical social workers — can be trained into UK-based staff within GP Confederation or OMG partner structures. The demo rehearsal positioned this role as "trainable within the GP Confed structure," equating it to nurse prescribers or psychological wellbeing practitioners (PWPs) in the UK context.

## Basis

During the demo rehearsal (2025-02-11), the case manager role was explicitly "positioned as trainable within the GP Confed structure." The GP Confed meeting (2025-01-22) described the role as conducted by "psychologist, social worker, nurse practitioner, or junior psychiatrist" — suggesting flexibility in who can fill it. The team has not validated whether UK professional registration requirements constrain who can perform structured clinical intake for ADHD assessment.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: Case manager described as flexible role (multiple professional backgrounds)
- 2025-02-11: Role positioned as trainable within GP Confed structure
- No evidence of UK professional body validation (e.g., NMC, HCPC) for this role definition

## Impact

If UK professional registration requirements restrict who can perform structured clinical intake, the staffing model and cost assumptions would need revision. The decision to recruit locally depends on finding appropriately qualified UK professionals willing to work in this role. Training timeline affects launch readiness.

# Unconstrained Prototype Design Produces Better Strategic Direction Than Incremental Planning

## Claim

Designing a clinical system prototype with no budget or technical constraints — imagining the ideal system first — produces more innovative and strategically useful roadmaps than incremental feature-by-feature planning. The dream prototype approach starts from "what would the best possible system look like?" rather than "what can we build next quarter?"

## Basis

Rami proposed the next-generation clinical system initiative with an explicit methodology: (1) survey all existing systems, (2) build a composite of best features, (3) brainstorm novel additions beyond the market, (4) create a full prototype concept assuming unlimited capability, (5) use that prototype to inform the annual development roadmap. The deliberate removal of budget constraints is a methodological choice — the assumption being that constraint-free ideation surfaces innovations that incremental planning would miss.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rami proposed the dream prototype initiative during the working session with Rivi, explicitly stating "no budget constraints" for the concept phase and framing the output as the basis for annual roadmap planning.

## Impact

This assumption shapes how the innovation team approaches product strategy. If valid, it means the annual development roadmap will be derived from an idealized vision rather than accumulated feature requests. If invalid (i.e., unconstrained designs produce impractical roadmaps disconnected from real capabilities), the team risks spending significant research time on concepts that cannot inform practical planning.

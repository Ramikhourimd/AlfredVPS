# Mortgage Will Be Held to Full Term for Projected Savings to Materialize

## Claim

The projected ~200,000 NIS total cost savings under Option A assumes the mortgage will be held to maturity (or close to it). If the property is sold, the mortgage is repaid early, or significant lump-sum payments are made, the actual savings will be materially different from the projection. The decision to optimize for total cost savings over monthly reduction only makes sense if the loan runs long enough for the compounding benefit to accumulate.

## Basis

Both source notes frame the refinancing choice as "total cost savings over the life of the loan" versus "immediate monthly relief." Rami explicitly chose the total-cost approach (Option A), which front-loads costs in exchange for long-term benefit. This framing implicitly assumes a long holding period. No discussion of early exit scenarios, property sale timelines, or partial prepayment plans appears in either consultation record.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-01-26: Option A chosen specifically for ~200,000 NIS total savings framing — no break-even analysis or minimum holding period discussed

## Impact

If Rami sells the apartment or makes significant extra payments within 5-7 years, the actual savings may be substantially less than 200,000 NIS. The RiseUp advisory fee (6,000 NIS) and any refinancing costs must also be recovered over the holding period. A break-even timeline analysis would validate or challenge this assumption.

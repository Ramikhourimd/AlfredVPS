# Single Person Bridge Between Clinical and Innovation Is Sustainable

## Claim

The innovation team's operating model assumes that a single person (Stav Zamir) can simultaneously serve as: (1) primary clinical viability validator for all new technology, (2) clinical needs analyst and "field sensor," (3) implementation coordinator and trainer, (4) active clinician maintaining hands-on patient work, (5) prompt engineering pipeline owner, and (6) stakeholder feedback collector. This multi-hat arrangement is assumed to be sustainable long-term.

## Basis

Stav's role manual explicitly defines three responsibility pillars plus additional duties that span clinical operations, innovation validation, implementation, training, and documentation. The role design concentrates bridge functions in one person rather than distributing them across a team.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-25: Stav's Comprehensive Role Manual documents the full scope of the bridge role
- The role identifies three potential career paths (Director of Clinical Innovation, PM Clinical AI, Head of Clinical Informatics), all of which would represent scope reduction from the current combined role
- No evidence of delegation planning or backup training for any of these functions

## Impact

If this assumption is wrong, the innovation pipeline faces cascading failures: clinical viability validation stops, prompt engineering becomes unowned, clinical needs go undetected, and implementation coordination stalls. The concentration is similar to the Ohad OPTICA concentration but affects the clinical side of every project.

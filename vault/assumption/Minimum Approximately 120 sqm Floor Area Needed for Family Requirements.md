# Minimum Approximately 120 sqm Floor Area Needed for Family Requirements

## Claim

Given the family's requirement for 3-4 bedrooms plus a home office, properties below approximately 120 sqm are unlikely to be viable. The pattern across multiple viewings suggests an implicit minimum floor area threshold.

## Basis

Reactions to properties viewed on 2025-10-09 reveal a size preference gradient:
- **75 sqm**: Dismissed — bedroom described as small, price called "not logical" for the space
- **112 sqm**: Considered but on the lower end — standard layout with limited flexibility
- **115 sqm + 20 sqm balcony**: "Apartment is small" — brief mention, less interest
- **126 sqm + 37 sqm balcony**: Received more detailed evaluation (rejected on kitchen/light issues, not size)
- **140 sqm**: The more attractive option at the family building — better fit for family needs
- **163 sqm**: Large house, described positively as "big"

The 3-4 bedroom + office requirement mathematically requires substantial floor area. Properties under 120 sqm struggle to accommodate this.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-10-09: 75 sqm dismissed, 115 sqm described as small, 140 sqm preferred over 112 sqm

## Impact

This implicit threshold should narrow the property search. Listings under 120 sqm can likely be filtered out early unless they have exceptional layouts or expansion potential (e.g., large convertible balconies).

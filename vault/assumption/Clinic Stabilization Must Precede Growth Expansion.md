# Clinic Stabilization Must Precede Growth Expansion

## Claim

The clinic must reach a defined quality and operational stability threshold before pursuing growth (new HMOs, patient volume expansion, geographic expansion). Rapid growth without stabilization will erode clinical quality, increase staff burnout, and expose the organization to legal and reputational risk.

## Basis

Rami articulates this consistently across multiple conversations. In the Dec 5 debrief, he explicitly states the clinic needs to recruit better psychiatrists, train staff, and build operational protocols before expansion. He has defined nearly 60 KPIs and built a quantitative organizational health score model with a threshold that must be crossed before growth should begin. He estimates this threshold could be reached within six months.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-05: Rami states clinic needs stabilization — defined ~60 KPIs, built health score model (debrief recording)
- 2025-12-05: Therapy session — Rami describes growth readiness threshold, estimates 6-month timeline
- Leadership (Alex, Oren, Dekkel) set 2026 goals with expansion-first approach, directly challenging this assumption

## Impact

This assumption drives Rami's opposition to the leadership's 2026 growth targets. If correct, premature expansion risks quality erosion and legal exposure. If wrong, the company may miss market opportunities (e.g., Clalit South psychiatrist departure creating 350 unserved patients).

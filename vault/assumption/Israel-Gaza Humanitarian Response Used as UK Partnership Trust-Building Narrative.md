# Israel-Gaza Humanitarian Response Used as UK Partnership Trust-Building Narrative

## Claim

The team assumes that Telia'z's decision to provide free psychiatric services to displaced populations during the Israel-Gaza conflict (which paused UK expansion for ~18 months) strengthens rather than weakens their position with UK partners. Rather than explaining the 18-month gap as a business interruption, the team deliberately frames it as evidence of social mission and clinical commitment.

## Basis

In [[note/GP Confederation ADHD Partnership Meeting Notes 2025-01-22]], the context section explicitly notes: "The UK expansion effort was paused for ~18 months during the Israel-Gaza conflict, during which Telia'z pivoted to provide free psychiatric services to displaced populations. Now re-engaging with UK partners." This framing was presented directly to NHS stakeholders at GP Confederation offices in Leeds — a context where social mission and public service values carry significant weight.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: Narrative used in GP Confederation in-person meeting (first confirmed use)
- No evidence yet of how UK partners received this framing

## Impact

- If this narrative resonates, it differentiates Telia'z from purely commercial ADHD providers
- If it raises concerns (geopolitical sensitivity, business continuity risk), it could undermine partner confidence
- The assumption remains untested — no documented partner feedback on this specific framing

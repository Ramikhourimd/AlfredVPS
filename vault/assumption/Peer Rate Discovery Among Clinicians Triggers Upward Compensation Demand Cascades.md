# Peer Rate Discovery Among Clinicians Triggers Upward Compensation Demand Cascades

## Claim

When clinicians at Telia'z learn about peer compensation rates — particularly higher-tier specialist rates — they demand equivalent rates regardless of their own tier placement. This creates an upward cascade: one rate disclosure triggers multiple renegotiation demands.

## Basis

During the 2025-12-04 fee schedule discussion, Elinor and Noa heard that expert psychiatrists (Mumkhim) earn 450 NIS per session and immediately demanded their intake rates be raised accordingly. Rather than negotiate individually, management decided to issue standardized fee schedules and new agreements for all clinicians preemptively.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-04: Elinor and Noa demand rate increases after learning about 450 NIS expert rate
- 2025-12-04: Management responds with plan to issue unified tariffs and updated agreements for all staff
- Pattern connects to earlier compensation discrepancies discovered during same-day payroll review

## Impact

- Forces preemptive standardization rather than allowing organic rate evolution
- Makes compensation transparency a double-edged sword — necessary for fairness but triggers cascading demands
- Unified fee schedule issuance (task/Issue Updated Fee Schedules) is a direct consequence of this dynamic
- Future rate changes for any tier must consider information propagation to all tiers

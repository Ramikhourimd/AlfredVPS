# Basel Clinic-Building Methodology Starts With Medical Director as First Clinical Hire

## Claim

Basel Kanaaneh's proven clinic establishment methodology — demonstrated across three separate Clalit clinics — follows a consistent pattern: recruit a Medical Director first, who then recruits clinical leads (psychologists, social workers), after which the administrative team is built. At the Shefa-Amr mental health clinic, Basel hired Dr. Olah as Medical Director within one month of starting from an empty building; she recruited the lead psychologist and lead social worker, and the clinic grew to 25 staff.

## Basis

This pattern was explicitly described during the November 9, 2025 hiring interview. Basel repeated the same approach across three clinic establishments at Clalit (child/youth mental health, neurology/child development, eating disorders). Each time, the Medical Director served as the recruitment anchor for the clinical team.

## Evidence Trail

- 2017: Shefa-Amr mental health clinic — MD first, then clinical leads, grew to 25 staff
- 2017-2018: Neurology and child development center — same pattern, combined to 52 staff
- ~2022: Eating disorders clinic — same pattern under existing umbrella

## Impact

This methodology directly informs how Telia'z should approach new clinic establishment — both for UK expansion and for new Israeli districts under the restructuring. The district model's requirement for a dedicated Medical Director at each location aligns with Basel's proven approach. The assumption is that this "MD-first" pattern will replicate successfully in Telia'z's telepsychiatry context, where the physical co-location advantage Basel had at Clalit does not exist.

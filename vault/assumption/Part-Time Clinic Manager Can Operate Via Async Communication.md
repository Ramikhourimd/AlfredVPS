# Part-Time Clinic Manager Can Operate Via Async Communication

## Claim

Shira can effectively manage the clinic on a part-time (half-position) basis by operating primarily through asynchronous communication (email). She works approximately 30 minutes per day on management tasks, with one day completely off. The Telia'z platform handles real-time staff availability needs, so Shira does not need to be constantly reachable.

## Basis

Rami argued that with proper expectation-setting among staff about when and how to reach Shira, and with the Telia'z platform handling immediate operational needs, part-time management is viable. Alex explicitly raised the concern: "from our perspective, the half-salary thing — is it not problematic? Because whoever manages day-to-day might need her when she's not available."

Rami's response was pragmatic rather than definitive — he acknowledged situations may arise but believed proper coordination could manage them.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-03-27: Alex raised concern; Rami defended with async communication model
- This remains untested at the time of this meeting — Shira's expanded role is new

## Impact

If this assumption proves false, the clinic may need to increase Shira's position to full-time, significantly increasing management overhead costs. This directly affects the 35-50% clinical compensation target.

# Single Israeli Case Manager Can Handle Initial UK Patient Volume

## Claim

The team plans to launch UK clinical operations with a single Israeli case manager (Bassem) as a stopgap. The implicit assumption is that one part-time case manager — who also has responsibilities in the Israel clinic — can absorb the initial UK patient volume without creating a bottleneck in the three-step clinical workflow.

## Basis

Bassem was identified in the February 15, 2026 team meeting as capable and having prior experience conducting sessions in English. Adiel stated UK case managers should ultimately be hired locally, and the team will not recruit until patient volume is confirmed. No capacity modeling was discussed — the assumption appears to rest on an expectation of low initial volume.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: Bassem named as stopgap case manager; Basel (his manager) absent from meeting and not yet consulted on time allocation
- Rami suggested coordinating with Basel two weeks before launch to prepare
- No discussion of what happens if volume exceeds one person's capacity

## Impact

If UK patient volume ramps faster than expected (especially given the 25-year Leeds ADHD waiting list creating pent-up demand), a single case manager becomes a bottleneck at step 2 of the clinical workflow. Patients who complete the digital questionnaire would face delays before psychiatric consultation, undermining the 3.5-day referral-to-psychiatrist metric that is central to the UK sales pitch. The decision to not recruit until volume is confirmed creates a chicken-and-egg risk.

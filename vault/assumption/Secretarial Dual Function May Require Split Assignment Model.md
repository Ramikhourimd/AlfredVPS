# Secretarial Dual Function May Require Split Assignment Model

## Claim

Medical secretaries at the clinic perform two functionally distinct roles: (1) patient-facing work — appointment scheduling, reminders, complaints handling, tech support for connecting to the platform, and general service; and (2) staff-facing work — diary management for clinicians, shift coordination, and schedule changes. These two functions have different optimal organizational models: patient-facing work benefits from pooled availability (any secretary can handle any patient call), while staff-facing diary management benefits from dedicated assignment (a secretary who knows a clinician's patterns and preferences).

## Basis

The Feb 22 meeting explicitly identified these two functions and discussed — without resolving — whether to assign secretaries to specific pods (dedicated diary managers) or maintain a general pool with rotating availability. The unresolved contradiction (Pod-Dedicated vs Shared Pool) may be a false binary: a hybrid model where patient-facing duties are pooled while diary management is dedicated could satisfy both requirements.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Meeting note Section 3 lists both functions explicitly. No final decision reached on assignment model.

## Impact

If this assumption holds, the secretary assignment debate should be reframed: instead of choosing between dedicated and pooled models wholesale, the team should consider splitting the two functions and applying different assignment strategies to each. This would affect job descriptions, training, scheduling, and the target ratio of secretaries to clinicians. At 5 FTE secretaries and 2-3 pods, a hybrid model may be the only way to provide both dedicated diary support and adequate patient-facing coverage.

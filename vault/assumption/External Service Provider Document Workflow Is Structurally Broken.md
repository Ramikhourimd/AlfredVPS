# External Service Provider Document Workflow Is Structurally Broken

## Claim

External service providers (lawyers, accountants, back-office services) repeatedly lose documents that Li Yamin sends them, creating redundant work where she must resend the same materials multiple times. This suggests the document handoff workflow between Telia'z and its external providers is structurally broken — not solely a consequence of Li Yamin's interpersonal difficulties.

## Basis

During the December 2, 2025 HR discussion, while Rami and Oren were assessing Li Yamin's pattern of obstruction, they acknowledged a legitimate complaint from Li: external providers repeatedly lose documents she sends. This counter-evidence was noted by both parties even as they concluded her overall behavior was problematic. The decision to reroute external provider communication through Tal was the interim solution.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Li Yamin reports external providers lose documents she sends, creating redundant work
- 2025-12-02: Rami and Oren acknowledge this as a legitimate complaint alongside her behavioral issues
- 2025-12-02: Decision made to reroute external communication through Tal (addressing the relationship, not the root cause)

## Impact

If the external provider workflow is genuinely broken, then: (1) some portion of Li Yamin's perceived "obstruction" may be rational frustration with a broken process, (2) rerouting communication through Tal changes the person but not the process — providers may continue losing documents, (3) Basel's operations work should include standardizing document handoff procedures with external providers, not just internal role clarification.

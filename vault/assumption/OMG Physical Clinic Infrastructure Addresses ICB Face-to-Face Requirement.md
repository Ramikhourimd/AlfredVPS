# OMG Physical Clinic Infrastructure Addresses ICB Face-to-Face Requirement

## Claim

OMG Healthcare's existing physical clinic premises in Leeds and elsewhere in England can satisfy the ICB commissioners' preference for face-to-face services, even though Telia'z's core model is virtual/telepsychiatry. The team assumes that having OMG's physical locations available for in-person appointments resolves the face-to-face concern raised during the GP Confederation meeting.

## Basis

OMG Healthcare operates ~100,000 GP patients across their clinic network and ~400,000 acute care patients annually through physical premises. The July 2025 pathway design meeting documented OMG's dual business: real estate/facilities (building and operating NHS clinic premises) and clinical services. The January 2025 GP Confed meeting explicitly flagged that "ICB commissioners will heavily weight procurement bids that offer face-to-face clinical services over purely virtual."

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: GP Confed meeting — ICB face-to-face preference explicitly raised as procurement risk
- 2025-07-03: OMG partnership meeting — OMG's physical clinic infrastructure documented, positioned as complementary to Telia'z virtual model

## Impact

If this assumption holds, the OMG partnership directly neutralizes the biggest procurement risk for the Leeds tender. The joint bid can offer a hybrid model (virtual + face-to-face) that satisfies ICB requirements while maintaining Telia'z's efficiency advantages. If OMG's premises are not suitable or available for ADHD services, the face-to-face gap remains a vulnerability.

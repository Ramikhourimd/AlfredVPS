# Shorter Daily Session Blocks More Sustainable Than Longer Ones at Same Weekly Total

## Claim

For telepsychiatry clinicians, distributing the same total weekly hours into shorter daily session blocks (e.g., 1.5 hours/day) is significantly more sustainable than longer concentrated blocks (e.g., 3-4 hours/day), even when total weekly hours remain constant. This is distinct from the total weekly hours threshold — it is about how those hours are distributed across the week.

## Basis

One psychiatrist (Sa'id) previously worked 3-4 hours/day across 5 days and found it very draining. After reducing to 1.5 hours/day (due to exam preparation), he found it much more sustainable. He explicitly stated he would NOT want the same total weekly hours compressed into one long day. This suggests session distribution, not just total volume, is a key factor in sustainability.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Sa'id's self-report via Rivi's staff interviews — 3-4 hrs/day draining, 1.5 hrs/day sustainable, compression into one day rejected
- Corroborated by case manager reports that intensity escalates non-linearly with session length

## Impact

Scheduling policy implications: if session distribution matters independently of total hours, optimal scheduling should favor shorter daily blocks distributed across more days rather than concentrated shifts. This affects both psychiatrist and case manager rostering.

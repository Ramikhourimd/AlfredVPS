# Druze-Christian Conflicts Drove Migration From Lebanon to Palestine

## Claim

The Druze-Christian sectarian conflicts in Mount Lebanon during the early 1800s (historically linked to the 1860 Mount Lebanon civil war and preceding tensions) were the primary driver that forced Christian families, including the Shaddads, to leave Lebanon and migrate southward into Palestine.

## Basis

All three conversation records and all three research notes consistently cite "Druze-Christian conflicts" as the causal mechanism for Phase 3 of the migration. Majdi Shaddad presented this as established family knowledge during the 2025-12-02 discussion. The 1860 Mount Lebanon civil war is a documented historical event, lending plausibility, but the specific claim that the Shaddad family was directly displaced by these conflicts (rather than other factors) rests on oral tradition.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi Shaddad described the conflicts as driving Christians from Lebanon to Palestine (all three conversation records)
- Cross-referenced in all three research notes as the accepted cause of migration

## Impact

This assumption underpins the entire Phase 2-to-Phase 3 transition in the migration narrative. If the actual cause of departure from Lebanon was different (economic, family dispute, opportunity-seeking), it would reframe the family story from "displaced refugees" to a different narrative.

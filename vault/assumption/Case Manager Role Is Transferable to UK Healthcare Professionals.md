# Case Manager Role Is Transferable to UK Healthcare Professionals

## Claim

The case manager role performed by clinical psychologists and social workers in Israel can be adapted to UK healthcare professionals — specifically nurse prescribers, psychological wellbeing practitioners (PWPs), or similar mid-level clinical staff. The team positions this role as "trainable within the GP Confed structure" rather than requiring Israel-trained specialists.

## Basis

In the GP Confederation meeting (Jan 2025), the case manager session was described as a structured pre-assessment conducted by a psychologist or social worker — positioned as equivalent to a UK nurse prescriber or PWP. During the demo rehearsal (Feb 2025), Adiel framed the case manager role as trainable within GP Confed's existing workforce. The role involves conducting a 30-45 minute structured intake using AI-supported tools, not independent clinical judgment.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: GP Confed meeting — case manager positioned as equivalent to nurse prescriber/PWP
- 2025-02-11: Demo rehearsal — role described as trainable within GP Confed structure
- 2026-02-15: Team meeting — separate discussion about hiring dedicated UK case managers (different model)

## Impact

If this assumption holds, Telia'z can leverage existing NHS workforce rather than building a separate clinical team. If it fails, the GP Confed partnership model requires significant workforce investment that may not be available within NHS staffing constraints.

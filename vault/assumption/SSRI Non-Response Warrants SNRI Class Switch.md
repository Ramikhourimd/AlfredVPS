# SSRI Non-Response Warrants SNRI Class Switch

## Claim

When a patient fails to respond adequately to two or more SSRI medications (e.g., Sertraline and Escitalopram), switching to an SNRI class medication (e.g., Venlafaxine) — which acts on both serotonin and noradrenaline — is the appropriate next pharmacological step, rather than trying a third SSRI.

## Basis

- Established psychopharmacology principle: repeated failure within a drug class suggests class-level resistance
- Venlafaxine's dual mechanism (serotonin + noradrenaline reuptake inhibition) provides a mechanistically distinct approach
- Referenced by the treating psychiatrist during consultation as supported by research evidence
- Consistent with STAR*D trial findings and standard switching algorithms

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Applied in clinical practice during patient consultation — psychiatrist explicitly cited research supporting SNRI switch after SSRI non-response

## Impact

- Guides medication switching decisions in clinical practice at the clinic
- Relevant to the AI diagnostic platform's treatment recommendation algorithms
- Informs clinical training content on antidepressant management

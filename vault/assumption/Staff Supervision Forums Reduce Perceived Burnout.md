# Staff Supervision Forums Reduce Perceived Burnout

## Claim

Clinical supervision forums (hadracha/guidance groups) at Telia'z Clinic Israel reduce perceived burnout among case managers. After supervision forums began, at least one case manager reported that the work suddenly felt less draining, possibly due to the sense of collective support and shared experience.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman reported that after clinical supervision forums started, a case manager mentioned feeling noticeably less burned out. The case manager was uncertain whether this was due to the forum itself (group support, shared experience) or coincidental timing, but the correlation was noted. Rivi also observed this effect in her own experience — being in a supported environment makes the workload more bearable.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Initial finding reported by Rivi Idelman based on case manager interviews

## Impact

If confirmed, this supports:
- Expanding supervision forum frequency and scope
- Including psychiatrists in similar group support structures
- Using forums as a burnout mitigation tool alongside workload management
- Addressing the isolation problem reported by remote clinical staff

# Three Clinic Management Pillars Operate as Parallel Equals

## Claim

The three clinic management roles — Clinical Operations Manager (Shira), Medical Director (to be hired), and Administrative Manager (Renee) — are explicitly designed as parallel peers with no internal hierarchy among them. None reports to any other. All three report upward to the clinic-level leadership (Basel as integration point).

## Basis

The Feb 22 leadership meeting notes state explicitly: "These three roles are parallel — none reports to the other. They all report upward to the clinic-level leadership." This is a deliberate design choice by Basel and Leah to prevent any single function from dominating the others.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Both meeting notes confirm the parallel structure as proposed by Basel and Leah
- Coordination between pillars happens through weekly meetings (Medical Director and Operations Manager), not through reporting lines

## Impact

- Requires strong horizontal coordination mechanisms (weekly meetings, shared forums) since there is no vertical authority to resolve disputes between pillars
- Basel's role as integration point above the three pillars becomes critical for conflict resolution
- If coordination breaks down between pillars, the escalation path is unclear beyond Basel
- The parallel design assumes all three role-holders can collaborate as equals — a significant organizational requirement given the current dynamics

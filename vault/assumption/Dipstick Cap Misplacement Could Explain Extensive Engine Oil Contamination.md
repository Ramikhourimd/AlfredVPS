# Dipstick Cap Misplacement Could Explain Extensive Engine Oil Contamination

## Claim

The vehicle handler claims that all oil contamination on the diesel 4x4 engine and undercarriage was caused by a worker leaving the dipstick air vent cap improperly seated after a routine oil check. The dipstick has an unusually shaped, non-round opening, and the loose cap caused oil to spray across the engine during driving. If true, this means the engine is fundamentally sound despite the alarming visual appearance.

## Basis

The handler's explanation rests on the non-standard dipstick design making it easy to improperly reseat the cap. He claimed their team inspected the vehicle thoroughly and found no underlying mechanical issue.

## Evidence Trail

- **Challenged (2025-11-03):** The inspector noted oil on the opposite side of the engine from the dipstick and along the entire undercarriage — difficult to explain from a single point source. Additionally, the inspector identified excessive crankcase blowby (fumes from the dipstick opening), which is an engine-internal condition unrelated to external cap placement. Signs of prior hood removal add further uncertainty.
- **Pending:** Professional garage inspection needed to confirm or invalidate.

## Impact

If this assumption is valid, the vehicle is a sound purchase at the offered price. If invalid, the engine may have serious internal damage, prior replacement history, or front-end collision damage — making the vehicle a poor investment.

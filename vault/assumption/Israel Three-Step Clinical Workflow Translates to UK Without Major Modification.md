# Israel Three-Step Clinical Workflow Translates to UK Without Major Modification

## Claim

The team assumes the Israel clinical workflow — (1) digital questionnaire, (2) case manager intake session, (3) psychiatrist consultation with AI support — can be deployed in the UK with only surface-level adaptations (report formats, questionnaire language) rather than fundamental restructuring. This is distinct from the organizational/district model assumption; this specifically concerns the clinical assessment pathway and its compatibility with UK NICE guidelines, CQC standards, and NHS commissioning expectations.

## Basis

The GP Confederation meeting (2025-01-22) presented the Israel three-step workflow as the UK service model. The demo rehearsal (2025-02-11) scripted the same workflow for UK partners. The OMG pathway design (2025-07-03) added an OMG triage layer before the Telia'z steps but preserved the core three-step flow. The adaptations discussed have been cosmetic (report formats, prescription module) rather than structural (reordering steps, adding clinical governance checkpoints, modifying the AI's role).

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: Three-step workflow presented to GP Confed as-is
- 2025-02-11: Same workflow demonstrated in demo rehearsal
- 2025-07-03: OMG pathway design preserves core three steps with triage prepended
- No evidence of systematic comparison against NICE ADHD assessment guidelines

## Impact

If UK clinical governance requires additional steps (e.g., mandatory GP consultation before diagnosis, multi-disciplinary team review, specific consent processes for AI involvement), the workflow would need restructuring. This could affect system architecture, timeline, and the 3.5-day referral-to-assessment promise.

# Basel Clalit Mental Health System Experience Maps Directly to Telia'z Scaling Requirements

## Claim

Basel Kanaaneh's eight-year tenure at Clalit Health Services (2017-2025) provides directly transferable operational experience for Telia'z's scaling challenges. His specific accomplishments — building a 52-person child/youth mental health clinic from scratch in Shefa-Amr, managing 3,800 maintained patients with ~2 new referrals per day, overseeing budgets for all 13 mental health clinics across the Haifa and Western Galilee district, and pioneering the conversion of salaried psychiatrists to independent contractors — map almost precisely to Telia'z's current operational requirements.

## Basis

During his November 9, 2025 hiring interview, Basel described:
- Building a 25-person child/youth mental health clinic plus a neurology/child development center (52 staff total)
- Managing 8 psychiatrists, 1 neurologist, 6 psychologists, 4 social workers, plus admin/support staff
- District-level budget management for all 13 mental health clinics in Haifa and Western Galilee
- Converting salaried psychiatrists to independent contractors to optimize budget and capacity — the same staffing model Telia'z uses
- Running a private clinic venture serving multiple health funds simultaneously (sold 50% to Ali Awad of Shams Group; partner Dr. Wasim Reli served as Medical Director)
- Coordinating clinical research with Erasmus MC Rotterdam on schizophrenia and autism
- Most recent role: Field Activity Manager at Clalit (national level) covering marketing, customer service, and retention

Notably, Basel was only 30 years old at time of interview — a remarkably compressed career trajectory for this scope of experience.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-09: Hiring interview confirms experience metrics and direct parallels to Telia'z challenges
- Late 2025: Basel immediately took operational ownership upon joining, validating the transferability assumption
- 2026-02-22: Basel actively participating in leadership restructuring meetings as operational authority

## Impact

This assumption underpins all operational scaling plans for Clinic Israel. If his Clalit management experience does not transfer to Telia'z's fully remote telepsychiatry model (e.g., because clinic-based management patterns diverge from virtual-first operations), the scaling assumptions for the district model are at risk. His experience with 3,800 patients is comparable to Telia'z's current ~2,700 patients and target of 7,000+.

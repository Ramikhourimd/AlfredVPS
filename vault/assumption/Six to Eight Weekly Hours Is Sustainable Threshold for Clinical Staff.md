# Six to Eight Weekly Hours Is Sustainable Threshold for Clinical Staff

## Claim

Clinical staff (both psychiatrists and case managers) working approximately 6-8 hours per week at Telia'z Clinic Israel report manageable burnout levels, while those working significantly more hours experience disproportionate exhaustion and dissatisfaction. Concentrated long sessions are more draining than shorter distributed sessions, even when total weekly hours are equivalent.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman's qualitative interviews with multiple staff members revealed a consistent pattern: staff at the 6-8 hour/week level cope well, while those above this threshold report significantly higher burnout. One psychiatrist (Sa'id) specifically noted that 3-4 hours daily across 5 days was far more draining than 1.5 hours daily — shorter concentrated sessions work better. Rivi herself observed that working 5 hours feels substantially different from working 9 hours in terms of empathy and attentiveness.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Initial finding reported by Rivi Idelman based on staff interviews

## Impact

This assumption, if confirmed by structured research, would influence:
- Session scheduling policies (shorter concentrated slots preferred over long blocks)
- Staff recruitment targets (may need more clinicians working fewer hours each)
- Workload distribution models
- Burnout prevention protocols

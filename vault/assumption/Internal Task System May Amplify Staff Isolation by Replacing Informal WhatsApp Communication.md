# Internal Task System May Amplify Staff Isolation by Replacing Informal WhatsApp Communication

## Claim

The planned transition from WhatsApp groups to a structured internal task system for clinical operations will improve task tracking and accountability, but may inadvertently amplify staff isolation by removing the informal social interaction that WhatsApp group chats provided.

## Basis

Multiple meeting notes from 2026-02-18 capture this concern:
- Staff already report not feeling part of a team and experiencing isolation working from home
- WhatsApp groups, despite their operational shortcomings (unanswered messages, no task tracking), provide a form of informal social connection
- Some staff expressed that problems they struggle with are shared by others, but nobody knows because they don't see each other's messages
- Moving to structured task-based communication removes the serendipitous social contact of group chat

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Concern raised during Rami-Rivi meeting, based on Rivi's staff interviews

## Impact

- The internal task system design should consider preserving informal communication channels alongside structured task workflows
- Staff satisfaction survey should measure sense of belonging before and after the transition
- May need to create alternative social touchpoints (virtual coffee chats, informal channels) to compensate

# Innovation and CTO Functions Remain Separate With Selective Collaboration

## Claim

Rami's innovation work (AI integration, product development) and Shachar's CTO responsibilities (technology infrastructure, engineering) are treated as distinct domains. They collaborate on shared projects but neither reports to nor subsumes the other.

## Basis

The restructuring meeting discussed "Innovation & CTO Relationship" and concluded that Rami's innovation work should "remain separate from Shachar's CTO responsibilities, with collaboration on shared projects." The task defining Rami's role for 2026 frames Innovation Lead as one of his two target roles, distinct from CTO.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Meeting established separation principle between Innovation and CTO
- Rami's role consolidation targets CMO + Innovation Lead (not CTO)
- UK expansion project lists CTO and CMO as separate company-level roles

## Impact

This assumption shapes hiring decisions (need for a product manager under Innovation), reporting lines (Innovation Lead reports to CEO, not CTO), and budget allocation (separate innovation budget vs technology budget). If this assumption changes, the entire C-level structure needs revisiting.

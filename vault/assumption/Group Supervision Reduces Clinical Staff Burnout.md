# Group Supervision Reduces Clinical Staff Burnout

## Claim

Peer group supervision sessions (hadracha) have a measurable positive effect on reducing perceived burnout among clinical staff at Telia'z.

## Basis

One case manager reported that burnout "noticeably decreased" after group supervision sessions began. She was unsure whether the effect came from group contact itself or from the supervision content specifically. Multiple notes from the 2026-02-18 meeting record this finding independently.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Single case manager reports reduction in burnout after hadracha sessions started
- Mechanism unclear: could be social contact, professional development content, or both

## Impact

If confirmed, this would support expanding group supervision as a burnout intervention. However, current evidence is anecdotal (single respondent, self-reported, no controlled comparison). Needs structured measurement via the planned staff satisfaction survey to validate.

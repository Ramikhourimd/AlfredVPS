# Rami Preferred Leadership Style Is Strategic Oversight Not Direct Management

## Claim

Rami's self-assessed leadership style is "manage goals, not people" — he prefers to hire competent managers and oversee their output rather than directly managing teams or handling operational details (budgets, schedules). He acknowledges he does not love operational details but can perform them when necessary. This preference directly shapes what role he can sustainably occupy in the restructured organization.

## Basis

During the December 2, 2025 private management discussion with Oren, Rami explicitly described his leadership philosophy. He stated he has strong vision for company identity and direction but prefers strategic oversight. He specifically said he does not want to manage people directly and does not want to manage day-to-day operations — positions he reiterated in the Feb 22 meeting.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Rami self-describes as "manage goals, not people" during CEO role discussion with Oren
- 2026-02-22: Rami confirms he does not want to manage day-to-day operations or manage people directly
- Pattern consistent across both sessions, three months apart

## Impact

This assumption constrains viable role definitions for Rami. The CMO role at company level (clinical veto, strategic input, innovation leadership) aligns with this preference. A combined CMO/COO role or any position requiring direct reports would conflict with it. This directly informs task/Define CMO Role Proposal.

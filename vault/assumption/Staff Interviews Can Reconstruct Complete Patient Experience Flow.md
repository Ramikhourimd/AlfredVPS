# Staff Interviews Can Reconstruct Complete Patient Experience Flow

## Claim

Interviewing key staff members (Shira for the overall workflow, Renee for inter-session patient experience, Roy for follow-up questionnaires) will produce a complete and accurate map of the patient journey from intake through follow-up, including all automated communications.

## Basis

Rami assigned Rivi to investigate by interviewing specific staff members, each chosen for their domain knowledge of different parts of the patient flow. The approach also includes Rivi experiencing the flow herself as a pseudo-patient.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-11: Task created for Rivi to map patient journey via staff interviews and self-experience.

## Impact

If staff knowledge is fragmented or outdated (e.g., automated messages changed without staff awareness), the resulting patient journey map will have gaps. This matters because:
- Follow-up feature design depends on understanding the current patient experience
- Automated communications may exist that no single staff member is fully aware of
- The pseudo-patient experience component partially mitigates this risk by providing direct observation

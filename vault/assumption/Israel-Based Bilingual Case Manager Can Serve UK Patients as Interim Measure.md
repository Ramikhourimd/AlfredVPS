# Israel-Based Bilingual Case Manager Can Serve UK Patients as Interim Measure

## Claim

An existing Israel-based case manager (Bassem, suggested due to prior English-language experience) can serve UK patients as a stopgap until UK-based clinical staff are hired. This assumes that remote cross-border case management is clinically appropriate, legally permissible under UK CQC rules, and that time zone differences will not impede patient care.

## Basis

During the 2026-02-15 team meeting, the team discussed staffing the UK launch. Rather than delaying until UK-based case managers are hired, the team proposed using Bassem from the Israel team. This follows the clinic's pattern of using existing resources as stopgaps for new service launches.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: Bassem named as interim UK case manager candidate due to English experience

## Impact

If this assumption holds, UK launch can proceed without UK-based clinical hiring. If it fails (due to regulatory, clinical, or practical barriers), UK launch timeline extends significantly. The assumption also risks stretching Israel operations further, given existing capacity constraints documented in multiple records.

# Dotted-Line Reporting Expected to Resolve Clinical-Operational Authority Conflicts

## Claim

The team expects that defining formal dotted-line reporting relationships in the organizational chart will resolve the ambiguous authority boundaries between clinical and operational tracks. The org chart task explicitly includes "Dotted-line relationships" as a deliverable alongside direct reporting lines.

## Basis

The "Finalize Organizational Chart" task lists "Reporting lines and dotted-line relationships" as a key deliverable. This implicitly assumes that the deep disagreement about whether clinical authority should encompass operational authority (Rami's position) vs. keeping them separate (Oren's position) can be resolved through a structural mechanism — lines on a chart.

## Evidence Trail

- Org chart task deliverables: "Reporting lines and dotted-line relationships"
- The three-pillar management model creates parallel authority tracks requiring formal coordination
- No evidence of alternative conflict resolution mechanisms (e.g., decision rights matrices, escalation protocols)

## Impact

If dotted-line relationships prove insufficient to resolve the clinical-operational tension — which is fundamentally a disagreement about philosophy, not just reporting — the org chart will be a cosmetic solution that masks ongoing authority conflicts. The team may need more robust governance mechanisms (decision rights matrices, escalation protocols, defined veto domains) to actually resolve the authority question.

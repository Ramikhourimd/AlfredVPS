# Psychiatrists Naturally Compress Session Duration Below Scheduled Time

## Claim

Psychiatrists at Telia'z naturally shorten their session durations over time — from the originally scheduled 40 minutes down to approximately 27 minutes — without informing management. This compression happens organically as clinicians become more efficient with the AI-prepared patient information system.

## Basis

Rami described this pattern at the November 2025 psychiatric conference: sessions started at 40 minutes, but data showed psychiatrists were running approximately 27-minute sessions. Rather than enforcing the scheduled duration, Rami used this insight to propose an incentive restructuring: clinicians earning 450 NIS/hour could earn 600 NIS/hour by doing more sessions at 25 minutes, increasing throughput without pushing reluctant clinicians.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-13: Rami describes the pattern to a conference peer, citing data-driven discovery of the compression
- The AI preparation system (where psychiatrists receive all patient information before the session) reduces in-session documentation burden, enabling shorter effective sessions

## Impact

- Session length data can be used as a management tool for incentive restructuring rather than enforcement
- The natural compression suggests that AI-prepared sessions genuinely reduce clinician workload per session
- Throughput can be increased by aligning incentives with observed behavior rather than mandating changes
- This data-driven approach to workforce management may be replicable for UK operations

# Early Treatment Seekers Have Higher Recovery Rates

## Assumption

Patients who sought treatment within 3 months of the October 7th trauma had a higher likelihood of clinical improvement compared to those who waited longer. This was a preliminary finding from Rami's earlier data analysis.

## Basis

Rami reported this observation during the meeting based on his previous analysis of the patient data. The specific threshold of 3 months emerged from the data as a meaningful cutoff point. This finding will be a central component of the paper's clinical analysis (time-to-treatment correlation).

## Validation Needed

This assumption needs to be rigorously validated through the formal statistical analysis that Noam will conduct. The per-symptom scoring methodology may refine this finding.

## Impact on Paper

This is the primary clinical finding the paper aims to demonstrate — that earlier treatment correlates with better outcomes. It will form the core of the Results section.

## Confidence

Medium — based on preliminary analysis, pending formal statistical validation.

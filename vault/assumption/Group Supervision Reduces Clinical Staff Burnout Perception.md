# Group Supervision Reduces Clinical Staff Burnout Perception

## Claim

Group peer supervision sessions (hadracha) for case managers at Telia'z clinic appear to reduce the subjective perception of burnout. One case manager reported that burnout "suddenly decreased" after group supervision began.

## Basis

Single case manager report to Rivi Idelman. The case manager herself was uncertain whether the effect came from (a) the group social element itself, (b) the supervision content, or (c) coincidental timing. Rivi also noted from her own experience that reduced hours (5 vs. 9) improve her empathy and attentiveness.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Initial anecdotal report from one case manager
- Confidence: Low — single data point, confounded by multiple variables
- Needs structured measurement via satisfaction survey

## Impact

If confirmed, this supports investment in:
- More frequent group supervision sessions
- Expanding supervision to psychiatrists (who currently lack equivalent forums)
- Using supervision as a retention tool, not just a clinical quality tool

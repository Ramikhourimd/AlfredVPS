# Psychiatrists Staying at Telia'z Primarily for Convenience Not Engagement

## Claim

Most psychiatrists at Telia'z Clinic Israel are staying primarily because of convenient working conditions (remote work, pay) rather than genuine professional engagement or loyalty. If a slightly better offer appeared, most would leave.

## Basis

Rivi Idelman's assessment based on qualitative interviews with psychiatrists in early 2026. Since the transition from Clalit South (post-trauma, socially meaningful work) to Maccabi (general psychiatry), the clinical diversity and sense of mission decreased significantly. One psychiatrist (Ilia) explicitly described the current work as "just another kupat cholim but from home."

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-18: Rivi reported this assessment during meeting with Rami. She stated that while she heard this pattern more from psychiatrists than case managers, the retention risk is higher among psychiatrists.

## Impact

This creates a significant retention risk for the clinic. If Telia'z raises its profile through marketing/recruitment campaigns without first improving the employee experience, new hires will encounter the same disengagement and the clinic will face a churn cycle. This assumption directly supports the urgency of the employee satisfaction research initiative.

# Oren Functions as Mediator Rather Than Decisive Authority

## Claim

Oren Taliaz (CFO/Co-founder) consistently operates as a mediator between stakeholders rather than exercising decisive authority to resolve organizational disputes. When conflicts surface, he acknowledges problems, makes conciliatory commitments, and seeks to keep all parties satisfied — but does not make the structural decisions needed to resolve root causes.

## Basis

Multiple source records reveal this pattern:

- **Li Yamin obstruction (Dec 2025)**: When Rami raised Li's refusal to cooperate with Basel, Oren responded by speaking to Li and "asking her to be patient for 1-3 months." He did not restructure her role, set accountability expectations, or create consequences. The intervention was a mediation conversation, not a management decision.
- **Authority vacuum acknowledgment (Dec 2025)**: Oren acknowledged the CEO authority vacuum and "committed to keeping Rami in the loop" — a relationship commitment, not a structural resolution. The vacuum persists months later.
- **Feb 2022 meeting outcomes**: The meeting produced no final decisions. Tasks were assigned for further definition and follow-up meetings. Oren's role in the meeting was facilitative rather than directive.
- **Rami inclusion commitment**: Oren committed to including Rami in cross-functional meetings — addressing the symptom (exclusion) rather than the cause (structural separation of clinical from business planning).

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Mediates Li Yamin situation with patience request rather than structural change
- 2025-12-02: Acknowledges authority vacuum, commits to communication rather than resolution
- 2026-02-22: Facilitates restructuring discussion that ends with no final decisions

## Impact

This mediation pattern contributes to the restructuring implementation stall identified in other synthesis records. If the most senior active authority figure (Oren, as CFO/Co-founder) defaults to mediation over decision-making, structural changes require either external pressure (board intervention, crisis) or consensus among all stakeholders — which the circular dependency pattern makes difficult to achieve. It also explains why Rami functions as de facto CEO: someone must make decisions, and Oren's mediation style creates a vacuum that Rami fills by default.

# Re-intake After Six Months Can Be Billed as New Intake

## Claim

When a patient returns after 6+ months of inactivity and undergoes a full re-intake, the clinic can bill the HMO (Maccabi/Clalit) for a standard intake session rather than a follow-up session.

## Basis

This assumption underpins the financial viability of the re-intake policy established on 2025-11-10. Rami directed that billing implications be confirmed with Nirit, indicating the answer is currently unknown.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-10: Re-intake policy established; billing question raised but not resolved
- Pending: Confirmation from Nirit on billing codes and HMO rules

## Impact

If re-intake cannot be billed as a new intake:
- The clinic absorbs the cost of full re-intake at follow-up rates
- May need to adjust the policy or negotiate with HMOs
- Financially impacts the children clinic where re-intake volume could be significant

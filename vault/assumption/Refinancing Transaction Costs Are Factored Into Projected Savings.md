# Refinancing Transaction Costs Are Factored Into Projected Savings

## Claim

The ~200,000 NIS projected savings under Option A implicitly assumes that all transaction costs — including early repayment penalties, bank processing fees, legal fees, and the RiseUp advisory fee — are already factored into the savings calculation. Neither consultation note clarifies whether these costs are deducted from the headline savings figure or are additional expenses.

## Basis

Both consultation summaries present the ~200,000 NIS savings as a net figure without breaking down what costs are included or excluded. The RiseUp service fee (6,000 NIS for full service) is discussed separately as a prerequisite payment, suggesting it may not be included in the savings calculation. Early repayment penalties for Israeli mortgages can be significant, particularly for fixed-rate tracks.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-01-26: RiseUp consultation presents ~200,000 NIS savings without itemizing included/excluded costs.
- 2026-01-26: RiseUp service fee discussed as a separate decision, not deducted from savings figure.

## Impact

If transaction costs are NOT included in the ~200,000 NIS figure, the actual net savings could be materially lower. Early repayment penalties on the fixed non-indexed track and the short 14-year track could reduce savings by tens of thousands of NIS. This should be clarified with RiseUp before proceeding.

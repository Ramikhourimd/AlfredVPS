# Israeli Staff English Proficiency Sufficient for UK Clinical Interactions

## Claim

The Israeli case management team includes staff members with sufficient English proficiency to conduct clinical intake sessions with UK patients. Specifically, Bassem was identified as capable because he "has done it before in English." The team assumes this English-language clinical capability is adequate for patient-facing interactions in the UK — not just conversational English, but clinical interview quality.

## Basis

During the 2026-02-15 team meeting, when discussing the UK case manager stopgap, Bassem was specifically named as an Israeli case manager who can operate in English. Adiel confirmed using him as a temporary measure. However, the evidence is limited to a single team member's assessment — no formal language proficiency testing, patient feedback from English-language sessions, or clinical quality comparison between Hebrew and English interactions was referenced.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: Bassem identified by name as English-capable for UK stopgap role
- No formal assessment of clinical English proficiency referenced
- No patient satisfaction data from English-language sessions cited

## Impact

This assumption underpins the entire UK launch timeline for Track 1 (Leon partnership). If Israeli staff cannot deliver clinical intake at the quality UK patients expect — accounting for accent, clinical terminology, cultural rapport — it could damage early patient satisfaction, NPS scores, and Leon's willingness to continue the partnership. The decision to delay local UK hiring until patient volume is confirmed depends entirely on this stopgap working.

# Role Incumbent Defining Own Position Can Produce Objective Proposal

## Claim

The team assumes that Rami — the person whose role scope is actively contested — can write an objective proposal defining his own CMO position. The task "Define CMO Role Proposal" is assigned to Rami himself, asking him to "clarify the boundaries between CMO and COO responsibilities" and produce a proposal that should "bridge the gap" between his preference for broad authority and Oren's preference for separation.

## Basis

This assignment follows from the Feb 22 meeting where Rami and Oren disagreed fundamentally about the scope of Rami's role. Rather than appointing a neutral party or engaging the full leadership team in role design, the task was assigned to the person with the strongest stake in the outcome.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Task assigned to Rami to "prepare a formal proposal defining his role"
- The conversation records Rami arguing for combined CMO/COO authority, Oren arguing for separation
- The proposal is expected to "bridge that gap" — a diplomatic outcome that may be difficult for the person on one side of the debate

## Impact

- If the proposal tilts toward combined authority (Rami's preference), Oren may reject it, creating another iteration
- If the proposal tilts toward separation (Oren's preference), Rami may feel forced to argue against his own proposal
- A neutral third-party review of the proposal may be needed before it can be accepted as a basis for decision
- This assumption's validity directly affects whether the CMO role definition task can actually resolve the underlying contradiction

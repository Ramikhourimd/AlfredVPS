# Physician CEO Required for Medical Company Governance

## Claim

Rami holds the governance philosophy that a company whose core business is medical services should be led by a physician CEO, not a business executive. This is distinct from the legal requirement for physician oversight of clinical operations — it is a belief about organizational identity and strategic leadership. A non-physician CEO will inherently prioritize financial metrics over clinical quality because they lack the domain understanding to evaluate clinical trade-offs.

## Basis

In his December 5, 2025 therapy session, Rami explicitly expressed a "desire for physician CEO" as part of his vision for Telia'z governance. This connects to his broader frustration that leadership (Alex, Oren, Dekkel) focuses overwhelmingly on financial metrics while treating clinical operations as a cost center rather than the core competency. The therapy session context suggests this is a deeply held conviction, not a negotiating position.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-05: Rami expresses desire for physician CEO during therapy session
- 2025-12-05: Personal debrief records frustration that leadership's financial tunnel vision lacks clinical understanding
- 2025-12-02: CEO discussion with Oren — Rami acknowledged doing 50%+ of CEO work but rejected the title for sustainability reasons
- The constraint "No Physician on Board of Directors" reinforces that clinical perspective is structurally absent from top governance

## Impact

This assumption shapes Rami's evaluation of all restructuring proposals. If he believes only a physician can properly lead the company, then: (1) any COO or CEO appointment of a non-physician will face his implicit resistance, (2) his advocacy for CMO authority is partly about ensuring physician voice at the top when a physician CEO is absent, (3) the long-term governance question — who leads Telia'z — remains unresolved because no current candidate satisfies both Rami's physician-CEO criterion and practical sustainability constraints.

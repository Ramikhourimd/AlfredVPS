# Case Manager Role Is Trainable From Existing UK Healthcare Workforce

## Claim

The team assumes the Telia'z case manager role — a clinical psychologist or social worker who conducts a 30-45 minute structured pre-assessment using the AI platform — can be filled by training existing UK healthcare workers (nurse prescribers, psychological wellbeing practitioners, or GP Confed staff) rather than requiring specialised psychiatry backgrounds.

## Basis

In the demo rehearsal (2025-02-11), the case manager role was explicitly "positioned as trainable within the GP Confed structure." In the GP Confed meeting (2025-01-22), the workflow was presented showing case managers as interchangeable clinical staff guided by the AI platform. The Israel model uses psychologists and social workers, but the UK positioning assumes a broader pool of trainable professionals.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-01-22: Case manager workflow presented to GP Confed as part of the three-step process
- 2025-02-11: Demo rehearsal explicitly positions role as "trainable within the GP Confed structure"
- 2026-02-15: Decision to hire UK case managers locally (not Israeli staff) implicitly assumes available local talent pool

## Impact

If the case manager role requires more specialised training than anticipated, recruitment timelines extend and the cost-efficiency advantage of the Telia'z model diminishes. The GP Confed partnership value proposition partly rests on their ability to supply this workforce.

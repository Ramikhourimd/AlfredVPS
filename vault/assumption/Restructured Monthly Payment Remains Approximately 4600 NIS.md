# Restructured Monthly Payment Remains Approximately 4600 NIS

## Claim

Under Option A, the restructured mortgage will maintain monthly payments at approximately the same level as the current ~4,600 NIS. The ~200,000 NIS savings come entirely from total cost reduction (interest optimization, elimination of negative amortization), not from any change in monthly cash flow.

## Basis

Both RiseUp consultation notes describe Option A as: "Keep monthly payment roughly the same as current (~4,600 NIS)" while achieving ~200,000 NIS in total savings. This positions Option A as a pure efficiency restructure — same monthly outflow, dramatically lower total cost.

The family's decision to choose Option A over Option B (which would have reduced monthly payments by ~800-850 NIS) explicitly depends on this assumption holding true. If the restructured payment significantly exceeds 4,600 NIS, the rationale for choosing total cost savings over monthly relief weakens.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-01-26: RiseUp consultation presented Option A with "monthly payment stays roughly the same as current" — this is consultant-level projection, not a bank-confirmed offer

## Impact

This assumption is foundational to two linked decisions:
- [[decision/Refinance for Total Cost Savings Over Monthly Reduction]] — chosen because monthly stays manageable
- [[decision/Engage RiseUp Full-Service Refinancing]] — engaged to execute this specific outcome

If the actual restructured payment exceeds ~4,600 NIS significantly (e.g., 5,000+ NIS), the family may need to revisit whether Option A remains preferable to Option B's monthly reduction approach.

# Maghar Church Founding Dates Christian Settlement in Galilee

## Claim

The establishment of a church in Maghar around 1860-1865 serves as a reliable marker for when Christians first settled in the inland Galilee villages. This date is used across multiple source records as corroborating evidence for the broader coastal-to-inland migration timeline.

## Basis

Majdi Shaddad cited the Maghar church founding during the 2025-12-02 discussion. All three research notes include this detail as supporting evidence. However, the church founding date itself comes from oral tradition — no parish records, architectural survey, or historical documentation has been cited to verify it.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-02: Majdi Shaddad mentioned Maghar church founded ~1860-1865 (oral history)
- Used as anchor date in all three research notes

## Impact

This is the only semi-verifiable physical evidence anchor in the migration narrative. If the Maghar church date could be confirmed through parish records or historical archives, it would strengthen the entire Phase 4 timeline. If it proves inaccurate, the ~1865 settlement date loses its only corroborating evidence.

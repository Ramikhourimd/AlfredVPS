# Each Clinic District Must Be Financially Self-Sustaining

## Claim

Each clinic operating as a "district" under the Telia'z parent company must demonstrate standalone financial viability. The district model implicitly assumes that a clinic's revenue can cover its own management triad (Clinical Director, Operations Manager, Administrative Manager) plus practitioners, while also contributing to shared services costs at the parent level.

## Basis

The financial review task explicitly mentions "the clinic's standalone financial viability" as a key validation point. The task also requires "shared services cost allocation across districts," indicating each district bears a proportional share of centralized costs. Combined with the budget constraints limiting new management hires, this creates a financial self-sufficiency requirement for each district.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Financial plan review task created with explicit reference to standalone viability
- Budget constraint acknowledged as limiting factor for new management positions

## Impact

This assumption is critical for the UK expansion. If the Israel clinic cannot demonstrate financial self-sustainability under the district model, the replicability of the model internationally is undermined. It also constrains how quickly new management positions can be added — each hire must be justified against clinic revenue.

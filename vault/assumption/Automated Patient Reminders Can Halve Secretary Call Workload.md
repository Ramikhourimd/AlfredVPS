# Automated Patient Reminders Can Halve Secretary Call Workload

## Claim

An automated patient reminder system (outbound calls or SMS confirming upcoming appointments, similar to standard HMO/kupat cholim systems) would eliminate approximately 50% of secretarial workload currently spent on manual reminder calls.

## Basis

Shira Lachman proposed this during the 2025-07-14 management meeting with Rami. She estimated that secretaries spend roughly half their time calling patients to remind them of appointments. When patients don't answer, secretaries call repeatedly, consuming significant time. Shira had discussed initial concepts with Shachar before the meeting, but progress paused due to Shachar's vacation.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-07-14: Shira proposed automated reminders, claimed ~50% workload reduction potential
- No implementation or pilot data yet to confirm the 50% estimate
- Standard HMO reminder systems exist as reference implementations

## Impact

If validated, this assumption would fundamentally change the secretary hiring calculus. Instead of hiring 2+ additional secretaries to meet demand, automation could free existing capacity equivalent to one full-time secretary. This intersects with the known constraint of immediate secretary hiring needs (maternity leave) and the broader synthesis that secretary capacity is a compounding bottleneck.

# Telemedicine Cannot Meaningfully Shorten Psychotherapy Session Duration

## Claim

Psychotherapy sessions cannot be meaningfully shortened via telemedicine technology without compromising clinical quality. Unlike other clinical services that can be compressed or streamlined through remote delivery, psychotherapy requires sustained therapeutic time that resists optimization through technology.

## Basis

During the 2025-11-09 partnership evaluation discussion, Rami expressed explicit skepticism about a company (suggested by Shachar) that claimed to shorten psychotherapy session times via telemedicine. Rami's clinical judgement is that psychotherapy is fundamentally different from other services — the therapeutic relationship and processing time cannot be compressed. He was curious whether the company had rebranded the approach (e.g., calling it "cognitive training" instead of "psychotherapy") to sidestep this limitation.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-11-09: Rami stated psychotherapy "cannot be meaningfully shortened the way other services can" during partnership discussion
- The team agreed to attend the meeting to learn the company's approach, suggesting openness to being proven wrong but baseline skepticism

## Impact

This assumption constrains how Telia'z designs any future psychotherapy service offering:
- Session duration planning cannot assume telemedicine efficiency gains
- Staffing models for psychotherapy must account for full standard session lengths
- Any partnership claiming session shortening should be evaluated with clinical skepticism
- Revenue models for psychotherapy must use standard session durations, not compressed ones

# Case Manager-Psychiatrist Professional Hierarchy Creates Latent Workplace Tension

## Claim

A latent professional hierarchy tension exists between case managers and psychiatrists at Telia'z Clinic Israel. Case managers perceive that psychiatrists "look down on psychologists," while psychiatrists feel administrative hierarchy is being imposed on them. This tension surfaces as playful banter in social settings but may reflect deeper role-identity friction that affects cross-role collaboration and staff satisfaction.

## Basis

During the Telia'z Company Fun Day (2025-09-21), a lively exchange between a case manager and Rami revealed:
- Case manager: "Managing a case next to a psychiatrist, I always win" — playful rivalry about influence over patients
- Case managers feel psychiatrists are intimidating: "They try to program us"
- The dynamic described: case managers feel psychiatrists look down on psychologists, while psychiatrists feel administrative hierarchy is imposed on them
- Staff asked Renee whether they feel intimidated by psychiatrists — suggesting this is a known topic among staff
- Rami acknowledged having "many psychological tricks" — treating the exchange as good-natured but not dismissing it

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-09-21: Company Fun Day ambient recording captures extended banter about professional hierarchy between case managers and psychiatrists

## Impact

- In the remote telepsychiatry model, where case managers and psychiatrists rarely interact except through platform handoffs, this latent tension may go unaddressed
- The existing staff isolation findings (systemic across all clinical roles) may be partially reinforced by inter-role hierarchy perceptions
- Pod model design should consider whether pod leaders can bridge this professional hierarchy gap
- Group supervision forums (hadracha) may need to include cross-role sessions to address this dynamic
- Onboarding programs should explicitly frame the case manager-psychiatrist relationship as collaborative partnership, not hierarchy

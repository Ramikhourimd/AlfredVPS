# Clinical and Administrative Work Hours Compensated on Separate Rate Scales

## Claim

The Telia'z Clinic Israel operates with distinct compensation tracks for clinical work versus administrative/coordination work. Clinical hours (patient sessions, consultations) are compensated at higher rates (150+ NIS/hour) while administrative hours (office coordination, training, hadrakha) are compensated at a lower tier (120 NIS/hour for Rivi's admin work). This dual-track structure means staff performing mixed roles have blended compensation that can obscure their true cost.

## Basis

During the 2025-12-04 payroll review, Rami and Basel reviewed individual staff rates and identified clear rate tiers: 120 NIS/hour for administrative work (Rivi), 150 NIS/hour as starting clinical rate (Manal, Naomi), 157 NIS/hour (Bassam), and higher rates for senior psychiatrists. The distinction between "hatshnut" (clinical hours) and "avoda masredit" (office/admin work) was explicitly discussed as separate compensation categories.

## Evidence Trail

- 2025-12-04: Staff rate review confirmed Rivi at 120 NIS/hr for admin + training hours, separate from clinical rates
- 2025-12-04: Starting clinical rate identified at 150 NIS/hr (Manal, Naomi as first hires at this tier)
- 2025-12-04: Bassam at 157 NIS/hr clinical rate, distinct from admin compensation

## Impact

This dual-track structure affects payroll calculations, hiring budget projections, and role design. Mixed-role staff (who do both clinical and admin work) require careful hour tracking to ensure correct compensation. The assumption also implies that as the clinic scales, role specialization (pure clinical vs pure admin) will simplify payroll.

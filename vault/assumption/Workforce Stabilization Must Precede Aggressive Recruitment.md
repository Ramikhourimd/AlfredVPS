# Workforce Stabilization Must Precede Aggressive Recruitment

## Claim

The clinic's existing workforce must be stabilized — addressing satisfaction issues, workload concerns, and cultural problems — before aggressively scaling recruitment of new psychiatrists and case managers. Recruiting new staff into a dissatisfied environment risks both new-hire attrition and accelerated departure of existing staff.

## Basis

During the 2026-02-15 team meeting, Adi Lavi reported that Rivi Idelman's feedback collection revealed some staff (including case managers) are actively telling prospective applicants not to work at the clinic. Rami confirmed this was "not surprising" given the strain on existing staff and emphasized that stabilizing the current workforce should take priority over aggressive recruitment.

This directly conflicts with the concurrent push to launch careers pages and recruitment campaigns — creating a tension between growth velocity and organizational health.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-15: Adi Lavi reports staff actively discouraging applicants (confirmed by Rivi Idelman's data)
- 2026-02-15: Rami states priority should be stabilization before recruitment
- Ongoing: Careers page and recruitment campaigns proceeding in parallel regardless
- Staff satisfaction risk already documented as a constraint

## Impact

If this assumption is correct, the current parallel recruitment push risks wasted marketing spend (recruiting into a leaky bucket) and reputational damage if new hires leave quickly. If wrong, delaying recruitment may worsen the capacity crisis documented elsewhere (945 patients/month, 8% conversion).

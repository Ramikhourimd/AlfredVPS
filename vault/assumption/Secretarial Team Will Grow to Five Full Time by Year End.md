# Secretarial Team Will Grow to Five Full Time by Year End

## Claim

The medical secretary team will grow from 2 full-time positions to 5 full-time positions by end of 2026. This projection drives hiring plans and budget allocation for the administrative function.

## Basis

Both meeting notes reference the current state (2 full-time secretaries, with Alice barely working and Amda going on maternity leave) and the need to hire 2+ additional secretaries. The projection to 5 full-time positions by year-end appears linked to the expected clinic growth in psychiatrist and case manager headcount. Secretaries serve dual functions — patient-facing (scheduling, phone support) and staff-facing — and the current team cannot sustain either function adequately.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-22: Both meeting notes state current team of 2 FTEs, need for 2+ hires, projection of 5 FTEs by year-end.

## Impact

This assumption drives budget allocation for administrative staff, Renee's role transition (moving out of hands-on secretarial work into management), and workspace/equipment planning. If growth is slower, over-hiring creates unnecessary cost. If faster, under-staffing creates patient-facing service failures.

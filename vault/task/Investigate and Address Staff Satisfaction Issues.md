# Investigate and Address Staff Satisfaction Issues

Reports emerged at the 2026-02-15 team meeting that some Telia'z clinic staff are not satisfied and would not recommend working at the clinic to others.

## Context

Adi Lavi reported receiving information from Rivi Idelman, who is collecting feedback, that some staff members (including case managers) told prospective applicants not to work at the clinic. This was flagged as a critical issue given the concurrent push to recruit more clinicians.

Rami confirmed this is not surprising given the strain on existing staff and emphasized that the priority should be stabilizing the current workforce before aggressively recruiting. Key concerns:
- Staff feeling overworked and disconnected
- Lack of personal connection with management
- No clear channel for raising concerns
- Shira handles regular check-ins but capacity is limited

## Action Plan
1. Gather structured feedback from Rivi's data collection
2. Identify specific pain points (workload, management, systems, culture)
3. Develop internal marketing/engagement plan for clinicians
4. Create accessible channel for staff to raise concerns
5. Coordinate with Shira on expanded outreach
